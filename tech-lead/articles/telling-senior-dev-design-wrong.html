Telling a Senior Dev Their Design is Wrong

Article 15 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Senior 🎯 Disagreeing without demeaning, principle-based pushback, private conversations

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday, 11:00 AM HKT. Mei (senior dev, 8 years experience) proposed a design for the new event-sourcing ledger using a single Kafka topic with 50 partitions and no schema registry. You've reviewed it; you see 3 concerns: (a) coupling 50 services to one schema, (b) no evolution strategy, (c) operational risk if partitions need to grow.

周二上午11点香港时间。Mei（senior dev，8 年经验）为新事件溯源账本提了个设计：单一 Kafka topic，50 个分区，无 schema registry。你 review 后看到 3 个问题：(a) 50 个服务耦合到同一个 schema，(b) 无演进策略，(c) 分区需要扩容时的运维风险。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Mei (design doc):Wei, I've drafted the event-sourcing design. Single Kafka topic, 50 partitions, no schema registry — keeps it simple. Can you review?Wei，我把事件溯源设计起草好了。单一 Kafka topic、50 分区、无 schema registry——保持简单。能帮我 review 吗？
You (TL, in 1:1):Mei, thanks for the design — it's clean and the single-topic approach is a valid trade-off. I have 3 concerns I'd like to walk through with you. None of them are about you — they're about the design.Mei，谢谢你的设计——很干净，单一 topic 路径是合理的取舍。我有 3 个关切想跟你过一下。跟你本人无关——是关于设计的。
You (TL):Concern 1: 50 services coupled to one schema. If we change the schema, we touch 50 services at once. What's our migration path?关切 1：50 个服务耦合到同一个 schema。如果改 schema，要同时动 50 个服务。我们的迁移路径是什么？
Mei:We'll version the events in the payload. Backward-compatible for 2 versions.我们在 payload 里版本化事件。向后兼容 2 个版本。
You (TL):OK, that works for 2 versions. What about version 3? Do we deprecate, dual-write, or migrate?好，2 个版本能行。第 3 个版本呢？弃用、双写、还是迁移？
Mei:Hmm, I hadn't thought that far.嗯，没想那么远。
You (TL):That's a concern, not a criticism. It's a 1-day exercise to write a 3-version evolution plan. Want to pair on it tomorrow?这是关切，不是批评。写 3 版本演进计划是 1 天的活儿。明天配对写？
Mei:Sure. What's concern 2?好。关切 2 呢？
You (TL):Concern 2: no schema registry. Schema registry gives us a contract test in CI — if a producer breaks the schema, the build fails. Without it, we find out in production.关切 2：无 schema registry。Schema registry 让 CI 有契约测试——如果 producer 破坏 schema，构建会失败。没它就只能在生产发现问题。
Mei:Schema registry is extra ops complexity.Schema registry 是额外的运维复杂度。
You (TL):Valid concern. Can we use the managed Confluent Schema Registry? Group Cyber already operates it for the other tribes. Zero additional ops for us.合理的关切。我们能用托管的 Confluent Schema Registry 吗？Group Cyber 已经在为其他 tribe 运维。我们零额外运维。
Mei:Oh, I didn't know that. That changes my view.哦，我之前不知道。这改变我的看法。
You (TL):Concern 3: 50 partitions. If we hit 10x growth, do we add partitions? Adding partitions to an active topic is risky — rebalancing causes consumer lag spikes. What's the headroom plan?关切 3：50 个分区。如果业务涨 10 倍，加分区吗？在用 topic 上加分区有风险——rebalance 导致 consumer lag 尖峰。容量余量怎么规划？
Mei:I assumed we'd just add partitions.我以为直接加分区就行。
You (TL):Adding partitions mid-flight is a 2-day ops exercise — coordination with all 50 consumers. Better to start with 100 partitions and over-provision. Disk cost is 2x, but ops risk is 0.在用 topic 加分区是 2 天的运维活儿——和 50 个 consumer 协调。不如一开始用 100 分区过度配置。磁盘成本翻倍，但运维风险归零。
Mei:OK, I'm convinced on all 3. Let me revise: schema registry from Group Cyber, 100 partitions, 3-version evolution plan. I'll have it by Thursday.好，3 个我都被说服了。我改一下：用 Group Cyber 的 schema registry、100 分区、3 版本演进计划。周四前出。
You (TL):Perfect. And Mei — thanks for taking the feedback in the spirit it was given. It's a strong design, just with 3 risk-mitigation tweaks.好。Mei——谢谢你按原意接住反馈。设计本身很强，只是 3 处风险缓解微调。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Frame None of these are about you — they're about the design.跟你本人无关——是关于设计的。Open by separating the person from the work. Disagreement on the design is not disagreement on them.
  • Concern, not criticism That's a concern, not a criticism.这是关切，不是批评。Use 'concern' instead of 'problem' or 'issue'. Concerns are discussable; problems are negotiable; criticisms are personal.
  • Elicit What's our migration path?我们的迁移路径是什么？Elicit their answer before offering yours. The senior's answer is part of the design; your job is to make the gap visible.
  • Info-bridge Oh, I didn't know that. That changes my view.哦，我之前不知道。这改变我的看法。Often the senior doesn't push back — they just don't know. Info-bridge is a non-confrontational way to share context.
  • Cost-frame Disk cost is 2x, but ops risk is 0.磁盘成本翻倍，但运维风险归零。Pair every over-engineering concern with the cost trade-off. The cost is real; the risk is also real.
  • Close on praise It's a strong design, just with 3 risk-mitigation tweaks.设计本身很强，只是 3 处风险缓解微调。End on the strength of the design, not the gaps. The senior leaves the room with their reputation intact.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Event sourcing n.. An architecture pattern where state changes are stored as a sequence of events. 事件溯源
  • Schema registry n.. A service that stores and validates event schemas. Schema 注册中心
  • Backward-compatible adj.. A change that doesn't break existing consumers. 向后兼容
  • Deprecation n.. Marking a feature as outdated, with a removal date. 弃用
  • Dual-write n.. Writing to two systems at once during a migration. 双写
  • Partition rebalance n.. Kafka's mechanism for redistributing partitions across consumers. 分区再平衡
  • Consumer lag n.. The delay between a producer writing an event and a consumer reading it. Consumer 滞后
  • Headroom n.. Extra capacity reserved for future growth. 容量余量
  • Over-provision v.. To allocate more resources than current need, for future safety. 过度配置
  • Person vs work n.. Separating feedback about the person from feedback about the work. 对人 vs 对事

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'None of these are about you — they're about the design' — separating person from work

Open design feedback with none of these are about you — they're about the design / the work, not the person. Disagreement on the work is not disagreement on them.

用 none of these are about you — they're about the design 区分。

② 'That's a concern, not a criticism' — softer vocabulary

Use 'concern' instead of 'problem' or 'issue'. That's a concern, not a criticism / that's a risk, not a fault. Concerns are discussable; problems are negotiable; criticisms are personal.

用 'concern' 代替 'problem'/'issue'/'criticism'。

③ 'Info-bridge: I didn't know that' — the non-confrontational update

When you have new info that changes the design, share it as info-bridge: FYI, Group Cyber already operates Confluent Schema Registry. Oh, I didn't know that.

用 info-bridge 分享新信息。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Open by separating the person from the work. 'None of these are about you — they're about the design'.
  • Use 'concern' instead of 'problem' or 'issue'. Concerns are discussable; problems are negotiable; criticisms are personal.
  • Elicit their answer before offering yours. The senior's answer is part of the design.
  • Info-bridge is non-confrontational. 'I didn't know Group Cyber operates it' is gentler than 'you should have known'.
  • Pair every over-engineering concern with the cost trade-off. The cost is real; the risk is also real.
  • End on the strength of the design, not the gaps. The senior leaves the room with their reputation intact.
  • Always in private, never in public. Design feedback is 1:1, never in front of the squad.

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these is the most 'TL-as-design-mentor' (not gate-keeper, not people-pleaser)?

  1. 'Mei, this design is wrong. Rewrite it.'
  2. 'Mei, this design is great. Ship it.'
  3. 'Mei, the design is strong. I have 3 concerns about the design (not about you). Concern 1: 50 services coupled to one schema. What's our migration path?'
  4. 'Mei, I would have done it differently.'

③ is the TL-as-mentor. ① is gate-keeping. ② is people-pleasing. ④ is ego. A common mistake is either gate-keeping (imposing your design) or people-pleasing (rubber-stamping). The TL's job is to make the gaps visible, then trust the senior to close them.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

Your senior dev proposed using MongoDB for a new transaction ledger. Your concerns: (a) no ACID transactions, (b) no regulatory audit trail, (c) operational team has zero MongoDB experience. Write the design pushback in English. Use 'concerns, not criticisms', elicit the migration path, info-bridge, cost-frame, end on the strength of the design.

"Mei, thanks for the design — it's a clean MongoDB proposal. I have 3 concerns about the design — none about you, all about the tech fit. Concern 1: no ACID transactions. A transaction ledger needs cross-document atomicity. What's the migration path if we hit a partial-write failure? Concern 2: no regulatory audit trail. HKMA expects an immutable audit log. Can we layer a Kafka append-log for the audit, or do we need a different store? Concern 3: operational team has zero MongoDB experience. The bank has PostgreSQL expertise across 5 tribes. Going MongoDB means we hire 3 DBAs or retrain the team. Cost-frame: $300k/year for 3 DBAs vs. $0 retraining. Disk cost is 0, but ops risk is high. Info-bridge: PG has a JSONB extension — we can keep the document flexibility AND keep ACID. Oh, I didn't know that. That changes my view. It's a strong design, just with 3 risk-mitigation tweaks."
