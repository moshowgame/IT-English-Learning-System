Choosing Between Two Tech Approaches

Article 07 · Module 2: Design & Code Quality · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Trade-off framing, evaluation criteria, decision ownership

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Wednesday, 3:00 PM HKT. The squad needs to choose between two technical approaches for the new real-time fraud detection pipeline: Approach A: Kafka Streams (already in the bank, mature) and Approach B: Apache Flink (newer, more powerful, but no operational expertise). The squad is split: Raj and Mei prefer Flink for exactly-once semantics; Lin and James (PO) prefer Kafka Streams for speed-to-market.

周三下午3点香港时间。Squad 需要为新的实时反欺诈管道在两个技术方案间选：方案 A：Kafka Streams（银行内已有，成熟）和方案 B：Apache Flink（较新，更强大，但银行内无运维经验）。Squad 有分歧：Raj 和 Mei 因 exactly-once 偏好 Flink；Lin 和 James（PO）因上市速度偏好 Kafka Streams。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Raj:Wei, I strongly recommend Flink. The exactly-once semantics are critical for fraud detection — we cannot miss a chargeback.Wei，我强烈推荐 Flink。Exactly-once 语义对反欺诈很关键——我们不能漏掉一笔 chargeback。
Lin:But Flink has a steep learning curve and no operational expertise in the bank. Kafka Streams is already in production — we can ship in 2 sprints. Flink is 5 sprints.但 Flink 学习曲线陡，银行内无运维经验。Kafka Streams 已经生产——2 个 Sprint 就能上。Flink 要 5 个 Sprint。
You (TL):OK, let's frame the trade-off properly. Three evaluation criteria: time-to-market, exactly-once guarantee, and operational risk. Raj, score Flink on each.好，我们正经地框定一下取舍。三个评估标准：上市时间、exactly-once 保证、运维风险。Raj，给 Flink 在每个上打分。
Raj:Time-to-market: 2 out of 5. Exactly-once: 5 out of 5. Operational risk: 2 out of 5.上市时间：2 / 5。Exactly-once：5 / 5。运维风险：2 / 5。
You (TL):Lin, score Kafka Streams.Lin，给 Kafka Streams 打分。
Lin:Time-to-market: 5 out of 5. Exactly-once: 3 out of 5. Operational risk: 5 out of 5.上市时间：5 / 5。Exactly-once：3 / 5。运维风险：5 / 5。
You (TL):OK. Weighted score: time-to-market 30%, exactly-once 50%, operational 20%. Flink: 3.5. Kafka Streams: 4.0. Kafka Streams wins on weighted score.好。加权分：上市 30%，exactly-once 50%，运维 20%。Flink: 3.5。Kafka Streams: 4.0。Kafka Streams 加权分胜出。
Raj:But Wei, the chargeback risk if we miss exactly-once — that's regulator territory.但 Wei，如果我们漏掉 exactly-once 的 chargeback 风险——那是监管问题。
You (TL):Good point. Let me re-weight: for fraud, exactly-once should be 70%. Flink: 4.1. Kafka Streams: 3.6. Now Flink wins. And the regulator risk outweighs time-to-market. My recommendation: Flink.好观点。我重新加权：反欺诈 exactly-once 应该 70%。Flink: 4.1。Kafka Streams: 3.6。现在 Flink 胜。监管风险压倒上市时间。我建议：Flink。
James (PO):But 5 sprints is too long. The business wants fraud detection live by Q3.但 5 个 Sprint 太久了。业务希望反欺诈Q3 上线。
You (TL):Compromise: Flink, but in two phases. Phase 1: ship Kafka Streams in 2 sprints to hit Q3 — with at-least-once + 24-hour dedup. Phase 2: re-platform to Flink in 3 sprints. Hits Q3 and de-risks regulator exposure. Anyone object?折中：用 Flink，但分两期。阶段 1：2 个 Sprint 先上 Kafka Streams 保 Q3——配至少一次+ 24 小时去重。阶段 2：3 个 Sprint 切到 Flink。既保 Q3 又降监管风险。有反对吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Frame Let's frame the trade-off properly.我们正经地框定一下取舍。'Frame the trade-off' is TL vocabulary. It signals: we're going to be rigorous, not emotional.
  • Criteria Three evaluation criteria.三个评估标准。Always name your criteria upfront.
  • Score Score this on each.在每个上打分。Forcing people to score reduces opinion-driven arguments.
  • Weight Weighted score: time-to-market 30%, exactly-once 50%.加权分：上市 30%，exactly-once 50%。Weights force you to declare what matters most.
  • Reweight Let me re-weight.我重新加权。Don't be afraid to re-weight when new info arrives.
  • Recommend My recommendation: Flink.我建议：Flink。TLs recommend. They don't vote.
  • Compromise Compromise: Flink, but in two phases.折中：用 Flink，但分两期。A good TL always has a compromise ready.
  • Consent Anyone object?有反对吗？End with a consent check.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Trade-off n.. The explicit comparison of options with pros, cons, and the chosen one. 取舍
  • Evaluation criteria n.. The dimensions you score an option against (e.g., time, cost, risk). 评估标准
  • Weighted score n.. A score where each criterion is multiplied by its importance (weight). 加权分
  • Exactly-once semantics n.. A guarantee that each event is processed exactly one time. 精确一次语义
  • Operational risk n.. The risk of running the system in production, including on-call pain. 运维风险
  • Time-to-market n.. How long until the feature is live in production. 上市时间
  • Chargeback n.. A reversal of a credit card transaction, often triggered by fraud. 退款请求
  • Regulator risk n.. The risk of non-compliance with HKMA, MAS, FCA, etc. 监管风险
  • Phased approach n.. Rolling out a solution in two or more stages. 分期实施
  • De-risk v.. Reduce the worst-case outcome without solving the whole problem. 降低风险

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'Let's frame the trade-off properly' — the meta-frame

Use let's frame X properly / let's be rigorous about this to set the tone.

用 let's frame X properly 定调。

② 'My recommendation: X' — the TL's signature phrase

Use my recommendation / I recommend in technical decisions. Avoid I think / I vote for.

用 my recommendation / I recommend。

③ 'Compromise: X, but in Y phases' — the de-risk pattern

Use compromise: X, but in Y phases when stakeholders disagree.

用 compromise: X, but in Y phases 处理分歧。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • TLs drive technical decisions, not vote on them. Your job is to recommend, with reasoning, and own the outcome.
  • Frame the trade-off, don't argue the option. Frames win.
  • Always name the criteria upfront. Once named, you can't sneak in a new criterion mid-debate.
  • Weights are the real trade-off. Stakeholders often argue about the weights, not the scores.
  • Re-weight when new info arrives. 'Let me re-weight' is honest.
  • Always have a compromise ready. The first phase hits the deadline; the second solves the long-term risk.
  • End with a consent check. 'Anyone object?' beats 'agreed?'.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the trade-off

Translate the following trade-off discussion into natural Chinese.

"OK, let's frame the trade-off properly. Three evaluation criteria: time-to-market, exactly-once guarantee, and operational risk. Score Flink: time-to-market 2/5, exactly-once 5/5, operational 2/5. Score Kafka Streams: 5/5, 3/5, 5/5. Weighted: time-to-market 30%, exactly-once 50%, operational 20%. Flink: 3.5. Kafka Streams: 4.0. Kafka Streams wins. But the chargeback risk if we miss exactly-once — that's regulator territory. Good point. Let me re-weight: for fraud, exactly-once should be 70%. New scores: Flink 4.1. Kafka Streams 3.6. Now Flink wins. My recommendation: Flink. But 5 sprints is too long. Compromise: Flink, but in two phases. Phase 1: ship Kafka Streams in 2 sprints. Phase 2: re-platform to Flink in 3 sprints. Anyone object?"

"好，我们正经地框定一下取舍。三个评估标准：上市时间、exactly-once 保证、运维风险。Flink 打分：上市 2/5，exactly-once 5/5，运维 2/5。Kafka Streams：5/5、3/5、5/5。加权：上市 30%，exactly-once 50%，运维 20%。Flink: 3.5。Kafka Streams: 4.0。Kafka Streams 胜。但 chargeback 风险——监管问题。好观点。我重新加权：反欺诈 exactly-once 应该 70%。新分数：Flink 4.1。Kafka Streams 3.6。现在 Flink 胜。我建议：Flink。但 5 个 Sprint 太久了。折中：用 Flink，但分两期。阶段 1：2 个 Sprint 上 Kafka Streams。阶段 2：3 个 Sprint 切到 Flink。有反对吗？"

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these is most 'TL-decision-driver-like'?

  1. 'Flink is better. Let's do Flink.'
  2. 'Let's frame the trade-off. Three criteria. Here's the score, here's the weight, here's the recommendation.'
  3. 'What do you all think?'
  4. 'My recommendation: Flink, in two phases, with a re-evaluation in Q4. Anyone object?'

② and ④ are TL-decision-driver-like. ① is opinion-only. ③ is abdication. A common mistake new TLs make is asking the team to vote on a technical decision. Your job is to recommend with reasoning.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

Choose between PostgreSQL (mature, 2 sprints) and Cassandra (5 sprints, scales 10x) for a global transaction ledger. Write your TL trade-off in English. Include a compromise and a consent check.

"OK, let's frame the trade-off properly. Three criteria: time-to-market, write throughput, operational risk. Score PostgreSQL: 5/5, 3/5, 5/5. Score Cassandra: 2/5, 5/5, 2/5. Weighted: 30%, 50%, 20%. PostgreSQL: 4.0. Cassandra: 3.6. PostgreSQL wins on weighted score. But for a global fraud-detection ledger, throughput is critical — re-weight: throughput 70%. New scores: PostgreSQL 3.6, Cassandra 4.1. Now Cassandra wins. My recommendation: Cassandra, but in two phases. Phase 1: PostgreSQL in 2 sprints with sharding. Phase 2: re-platform to Cassandra in 3 sprints. This hits the deadline and de-risks the 10x growth. Anyone object?"
