Handling a Production Incident Escalation

Article 17 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Senior 🎯 Incident command, war-room discipline, calm under pressure, post-mortem ownership

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday, 9:47 PM HKT. You are at home. Your phone rings. Diego (UK on-call) is on the line. The payment service is down. 3,000 transactions queued. P1 incident. You are the most senior engineer online in HK.

周五晚9:47香港时间。你在家。电话响了。Diego（英国 on-call）在线上。支付服务挂了。3000 笔交易排队。P1 事件。你是香港能联系上的最资深工程师。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Diego (UK on-call, phone):Wei, P1 incident. Payment service is down. 3,000 transactions queued, growing at 200/min. I am the incident commander. Need you as engineering lead.Wei，P1 事件。支付服务挂了。3000 笔交易排队，每分钟涨 200。我是 incident commander。需要你做工程 lead。
You (TL):On it. Opening my laptop. What is the war-room channel? Who is in it?马上。开电脑。War-room 频道是哪个？谁在里面？
Diego:War-room: #incident-pay-2024-07-04. Me, SRE James from London, the SRE from Shanghai. I have notified the bank comms team.War-room：#incident-pay-2024-07-04。我、伦敦 SRE James、上海 SRE。已经通知银行 comms 团队。
You (TL, joining war-room):I am in. Status snapshot: payment service down, 3k queued, 200/min growth. Diego is IC. I am engineering lead. Three things in parallel: (1) stop the bleed — is the upstream still sending? (2) customer comms — what is the public-facing message? (3) root cause hypothesis — what changed in the last hour?到了。状态快照：支付服务挂，3000 排队，每分钟涨 200。Diego 是 IC。我是工程 lead。三件事并行：(1) 止血——上游还在发吗？(2) 客户沟通——对外公开说什么？(3) 根因假设——过去一小时变了什么？
SRE Shanghai:Upstream is still sending. We cannot stop it without coordination with the upstream team.上游还在发。不跟上游团队协调停不下来。
You (TL):OK, don't stop upstream. We'll let them queue. SRE Shanghai, throttle the consumer to 50/min so the queue doesn't grow. Diego, can you Slack the upstream team to throttle at source?好，不停上游。让它们排队。上海 SRE，把 consumer 限流到 50/分钟，别让队列再涨。Diego，你能 Slack 上游团队源头限流吗？
Diego:Done. Upstream team acknowledged. Throttling in 5 min.好了。上游团队确认。5 分钟内限流。
You (TL):Good. Root cause: SRE Shanghai, what changed in the last hour? Deployment? Config change? Traffic spike?好。根因：上海 SRE，过去一小时变了什么？发版？配置变更？流量尖峰？
SRE Shanghai:PR #442 — the thread pool config change — deployed 30 min before the incident.PR #442——线程池配置变更——事件前 30 分钟发版。
You (TL):That's our prime suspect. Roll back PR #442 now. SRE Shanghai, can you do the rollback in 2 min?头号嫌疑。立即回滚 PR #442。上海 SRE，2 分钟内能回滚吗？
SRE Shanghai:Rolling back. Done in 90 seconds.回滚中。90 秒内完成。
You (TL, 10 min later):Status?状态？
SRE Shanghai:Queue draining. Down to 800 transactions. Throughput back to normal.队列在排空。降到 800 笔。吞吐回到正常。
You (TL):Incident contained. Diego, can you mark P1 → P3 and post the all-clear? Post-mortem owner: me. Post-mortem due: Wednesday. All-clear to comms team.事件已遏制。Diego，能把 P1 → P3 标一下并发全清通知吗？Post-mortem owner：我。Post-mortem 截止：周三。通知 comms 团队。
Diego:Done. Thanks all. War-room closing in 5 min.好了。谢谢各位。War-room 5 分钟后关。
You (TL, post-war-room, Slack DM to Diego):Diego — clean incident command. Three things I want to add to the post-mortem: (1) why did PR #442 skip the canary? (2) why did the thread-pool config not have a circuit breaker? (3) why did upstream not have backpressure? Wednesday, post-mortem doc.Diego——incident command 很干净。post-mortem 里要加三件事：(1) 为什么 PR #442 跳过了金丝雀？(2) 为什么线程池配置没有熔断器？(3) 为什么上游没有背压？周三，post-mortem 文档。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open On it. Opening my laptop. War-room channel?马上。开电脑。War-room 频道是哪个？When paged at 9:47 PM, the first 30 seconds set the tone. Calm, on it, war-room channel.
  • Status snapshot Status snapshot: payment service down, 3k queued, 200/min growth.状态快照：支付服务挂，3000 排队，每分钟涨 200。Open with a status snapshot. Everyone in the war-room is now on the same page.
  • Roles Diego is IC. I am engineering lead.Diego 是 IC。我是工程 lead。IC = incident commander. The IC owns the war-room; engineering lead owns the technical decisions.
  • Three things in parallel Three things in parallel: stop the bleed, customer comms, root cause hypothesis.三件事并行：止血、客户沟通、根因假设。Three parallel tracks. The war-room does not wait for one to finish before starting the next.
  • Stop the bleed Stop the bleed. Throttle, not stop.止血。限流，不是停。Stop the bleeding first. Throttling is the safest stop.
  • Prime suspect That's our prime suspect. Roll back.头号嫌疑。回滚。Frame the suspicion as 'prime suspect', not 'I think this is the cause'. Then roll back. The post-mortem confirms.
  • Roll back Roll back PR #442 now. Can you do the rollback in 2 min?立即回滚 PR #442。2 分钟内能回滚吗？Roll back first, debug later. The cost of a wrong rollback is small; the cost of a 4-hour P1 is huge.
  • Post-mortem owner Post-mortem owner: me. Post-mortem due: Wednesday.Post-mortem owner：我。Post-mortem 截止：周三。Always name the post-mortem owner. 'Me' is fine — it shows ownership.
  • Three things to add Three things I want to add to the post-mortem: why, why, why.post-mortem 里要加三件事：为什么、为什么、为什么。Post-mortem is blameless, but it is also curious. Three 'why' questions beat one.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • P1 incident n.. A highest-severity production incident with major customer impact. P1 事件
  • Incident commander (IC) n.. The person who owns the war-room and the decisions. 事件指挥官
  • War-room n.. A dedicated chat channel for real-time incident handling. 战时频道
  • Status snapshot n.. A 30-second summary of where the incident stands. 状态快照
  • Stop the bleed v.. To take the immediate action that stops the incident from growing. 止血
  • Throttle at source v.. To reduce the rate at the upstream service, not at the consumer. 源头限流
  • Backpressure n.. A mechanism where a slow consumer signals the producer to slow down. 背压
  • Roll back v.. To revert a deployment to the previous version. 回滚
  • Post-mortem n.. A blameless document analyzing what happened and what to fix. Post-mortem 复盘
  • Blameless post-mortem n.. A post-mortem focused on systems, not individuals. 无指责复盘

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'On it. Opening my laptop.' — the calm opener

When paged, open with on it / I am in / I am here. Calm beats panicky. The first 30 seconds set the war-room tone.

被呼叫时用 on it / I am in 开头。冷静胜过慌张。

② 'Three things in parallel: X, Y, Z' — the parallel tracks

Use three things in parallel: stop the bleed, customer comms, root cause hypothesis to structure a war-room. The war-room does not wait.

用 三件事并行 结构化战时沟通。

③ 'That is our prime suspect. Roll back.' — rollback framing

Frame the suspected cause as prime suspect, not I think this is the cause. Then roll back. The post-mortem confirms.

把怀疑的原因框定为 头号嫌疑，然后回滚。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Calm sets the tone. The first 30 seconds of the war-room set the tone. On it beats Oh no.
  • Status snapshot in 30 seconds. Everyone in the war-room is on the same page.
  • Three parallel tracks. Stop the bleed, customer comms, root cause hypothesis. Do not wait.
  • Throttle, not stop. Throttling is the safest stop. Stopping requires coordination.
  • Roll back first, debug later. The cost of a wrong rollback is small; the cost of a 4-hour P1 is huge.
  • Always name the post-mortem owner and due date. Me, Wednesday shows ownership.
  • Post-mortem is blameless, but it is also curious. Three why questions beat one.

Exercise 2 — Spot the war-room mistake

Which of these is the most TL-as-war-room-lead (calm, structured, owns the post-mortem)?

  1. Oh no. What do we do? Someone help!
  2. Status: P1, payment down. I am IC. Three things in parallel: stop the bleed, customer comms, root cause. Diego, throttle upstream. SRE Shanghai, throttle consumer. Roll back PR #442 now. Post-mortem owner: me, due Wednesday.
  3. I think it is the database. Let me check.
  4. Wait for senior to come online. Do not roll back yet.

② is the war-room lead. ① is panic. ③ is debugging during the war-room (wrong order — stop the bleed first). ④ is paralysis. A common mistake is debugging during the war-room. The war-room is for stopping the bleed, not for understanding. The post-mortem is for understanding.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You are paged at 11:00 PM. The mobile app is throwing 500 errors on login. Diego is the IC, you are the engineering lead. Last hour: 2 deployments (PR #501 and PR #503). PR #501 is a config change to the auth service. PR #503 is a UI bug fix. War-room is #incident-mobile-2024-08-01. Write your war-room opening lines and the 3 parallel tracks in English.

"On it. Opening my laptop. War-room channel: #incident-mobile-2024-08-01. Status snapshot: mobile app throwing 500 on login. Diego is IC. I am engineering lead. Three things in parallel: (1) stop the bleed — can we route mobile traffic to the old auth endpoint while we debug? (2) customer comms — what is the public-facing message? The status page should say we are investigating login issues. (3) root cause hypothesis — what changed in the last hour? Two deployments: PR #501 (auth config) and PR #503 (UI bug fix). PR #501 is the prime suspect. Diego, can you throttle mobile traffic to the old auth endpoint? SRE Shanghai, roll back PR #501 now. Post-mortem owner: me, due Wednesday."
