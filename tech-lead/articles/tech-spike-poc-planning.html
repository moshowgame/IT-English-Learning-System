Tech Spike / POC Planning

Article 08 · Module 2: Design & Code Quality · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Time-boxed investigation, hypothesis-driven POC, evidence over opinion

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Monday, 10:00 AM HKT. The squad is considering a tech spike to evaluate Redis vs Memcached for a new session cache. There is strong opinion in the team but no data. You, as TL, propose a 3-day time-boxed spike with clear hypotheses. The squad needs to align on what success looks like.

周一上午10点香港时间。Squad 在考虑用 tech spike 评估新 session 缓存：Redis vs Memcached。团队里意见强烈但没数据。你作为 TL，提出一个3 天时间盒的 spike，带着明确的假设。Squad 需要对齐"成功"长什么样。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (PO):Wei, before we commit to Redis, can we do a quick spike? The team is divided.Wei，在我们选定 Redis 之前，能不能做个快速 spike？团队里意见有分歧。
You (TL):Good call. I propose a 3-day time-boxed spike. Not 5 days, not 1 week. 3 days max, with a clear deliverable at the end.好提议。我建议 3 天时间盒的 spike。不要 5 天，不要 1 周。最多 3 天，最后产出明确的交付物。
Raj:What hypothesis are we testing?我们要测什么假设？
You (TL):Three hypotheses. H1: Redis single-node throughput is at least 2x Memcached on our workload. H2: Redis persistence overhead is under 15% latency cost. H3: Redis operational complexity is manageable for the team. If all three hold, we go with Redis.三个假设。H1: 在我们工作负载下，Redis 单节点吞吐至少是 Memcached 的 2 倍。H2: Redis 持久化开销在 15% 延迟以内。H3: Redis 运维复杂度对团队可承受。三个都成立就选 Redis。
Mei:Who's on the spike?谁参与 spike？
You (TL):Raj owns it. He's our Redis champion. Lin pairs — she has Memcached experience from the old system. James sponsors — he gets the business context. Mei, you shadow — you may own the implementation next quarter.Raj 负责。他最支持 Redis。Lin 配对——她有老系统 Memcached 经验。James 发起人——他懂业务。Mei，你旁观——下季度你可能负责实现。
You (TL):Daily standup at 9:30 AM in the spike room. 15 minutes max. Day 3, 2 PM: spike review — we present findings, decide.每天 9:30 早会，spike 会议室，最多 15 分钟。第 3 天下午 2 点：spike 评审，我们汇报结果，做决定。
Lin:What if the spike doesn't conclude in 3 days?如果 3 天 还没结论呢？
You (TL):Then we stop and present what we have. A spike is a time-box, not a goal. If we don't know by day 3, we de-risk with the safer option — which is Memcached, since it's already in production.那就停，把已知的拿出来。Spike 是时间盒，不是目标。第 3 天还没结论，我们就选更稳的方案——Memcached，因为它已经在生产。
Raj:Sounds good. I'll set up the benchmark harness today.好的。我今天搭好基准测试工具。
You (TL):Perfect. Document the hypotheses in the spike ticket. Add the success criteria. Link to the spike channel. Let's go.好。把假设写进 spike ticket。加成功标准。挂上 spike 频道链接。开干。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Frame I propose a 3-day time-boxed spike.我建议 3 天时间盒的 spike。Always time-box a spike. Open-ended spikes become holidays.
  • Not-this Not 5 days, not 1 week. 3 days max.不要 5 天，不要 1 周，最多 3 天。Pre-empt scope creep by naming what it's NOT.
  • Hypothesis Three hypotheses.三个假设。Spikes test hypotheses, not opinions. If you can't name the hypothesis, you don't have a spike.
  • Owner Raj owns it.Raj 负责。Always name an owner. 'We' is not an owner.
  • Pair Lin pairs.Lin 配对。Pair spreads knowledge. A spike done by one person is a knowledge silo.
  • Sponsor James sponsors.James 发起人。A PO or senior dev sponsors the spike to keep the business lens.
  • Timebox A spike is a time-box, not a goal.Spike 是时间盒，不是目标。Reframe when someone forgets. 'We didn't finish' is fine; 'we need 2 more weeks' is not.
  • De-risk De-risk with the safer option.用更稳的方案降低风险。If the spike is inconclusive, default to the existing/proven option.
  • Deliverable Document the hypotheses in the spike ticket.把假设写进 spike ticket。Spikes need a written deliverable. Slack threads are not deliverables.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Tech spike n.. A short, time-boxed investigation to test a technical hypothesis. 技术 spike
  • POC (Proof of Concept) n.. A small implementation to prove a tech approach works. 概念验证
  • Time-box n.. A fixed amount of time allocated to a task. 时间盒
  • Hypothesis-driven adj.. A spike style where you declare what you're testing upfront. 假设驱动
  • Benchmark harness n.. A repeatable script/test that measures performance. 基准测试工具
  • Operational complexity n.. How hard it is to run, monitor, and debug a system. 运维复杂度
  • Persistence overhead n.. The latency cost of writing data to disk. 持久化开销
  • Single-node throughput n.. How many requests per second one server can handle. 单节点吞吐
  • Spike review n.. A meeting at the end of a spike to present findings. Spike 评审
  • Knowledge silo n.. Information that only one person on the team knows. 知识孤岛

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'Not X, not Y. Z max.' — pre-empting scope creep

Use not X, not Y, Z max when proposing a time-box. The negation pre-empts the inevitable 'can we have more time?'. Not 5 days, not 1 week. 3 days max.

用 not X, not Y, Z max 防止范围蔓延。

② 'X owns / pairs / sponsors' — clear spike roles

Use X owns / X pairs / X sponsors / X shadows to assign roles. Each role has a clear function: owner drives, pair spreads knowledge, sponsor aligns with business, shadow learns.

用 owns / pairs / sponsors / shadows 分配角色。

③ 'A spike is a time-box, not a goal' — reframe the metric

Use X is a Y, not a Z to reframe how success is measured. A spike is a time-box, not a goal / A POC is a learning, not a deliverable.

用 X is a Y, not a Z 重新定义成功的衡量方式。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Spikes test hypotheses, not opinions. If you can't name the hypothesis, you don't have a spike.
  • Always time-box. Always. 3 days is typical. 5 days is the upper bound. 1 week is a small project, not a spike.
  • Name the owner, the pair, the sponsor, the shadow. Unassigned spikes become abandoned tickets.
  • Daily standup during the spike. 15 minutes. Just to keep the time-box honest.
  • Day 3 review is mandatory. 'I need more time' is not acceptable. The spike either concluded or didn't.
  • If inconclusive, default to the existing/proven option. This is the bank's bias for safety.
  • Document the deliverable. Spike ticket with hypotheses, success criteria, results, decision. Slack is not a deliverable.

Exercise 2 — Spot the spike anti-pattern

Which of these spike setups is the most 'spike-as-tool' (not anti-pattern)?

  1. 'Let's spike for 2 weeks to see if Kafka is good.'
  2. '3-day spike, 3 hypotheses, Raj owns, daily 15-min standup, day-3 review.'
  3. 'Lin will investigate MongoDB and report back when ready.'
  4. 'We need to spike this before we commit. Raj, let me know how it goes.'

② is the proper spike. ① is too long. ③ and ④ are open-ended investigations, not time-boxed spikes. A common mistake is calling every investigation a 'spike' when it has no time-box, no owner, no hypothesis, no deliverable.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

Your squad is choosing between gRPC and REST for a new internal service. Propose a 3-day spike: hypotheses, owner, pair, sponsor, success criteria, and what you default to if inconclusive. Write in English.

"I propose a 3-day time-boxed spike. Not 5 days, not 1 week. Three hypotheses. H1: gRPC latency is at least 30% lower than REST on our workload. H2: gRPC schema evolution is manageable for the team. H3: gRPC debugging tools are good enough for our SRE. If all three hold, we go with gRPC. Raj owns it. Lin pairs — she has REST expertise. James sponsors. Daily standup at 9:30. Day 3 review. A spike is a time-box, not a goal. If we don't know by day 3, we de-risk with REST — it's the bank standard."
