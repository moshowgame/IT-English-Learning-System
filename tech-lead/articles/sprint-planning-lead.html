Running a Sprint Planning Meeting

Article 02 · Module 1: Daily Tech-Lead Communication · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Drive the planning: capacity, sequencing, dependencies, commitments

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Monday, 2:00 PM HKT. Sprint Planning for Sprint 24 is starting. You are the Tech Lead of the Payments squad (6 developers). The product owner, James, is online from Singapore. The scrum master, Sarah, facilitates. Priya, a senior developer, is dialing in from London.

周一下午2点香港时间，Sprint 24 的 Sprint Planning 开始。你是支付squad 的 Tech Lead（6 名开发）。PO James 从新加坡上线，SM Sarah 主持，高级开发 Priya 从伦敦接入。

James has brought 28 story points to the sprint. Last sprint, the team delivered 22. You're worried about over-commitment, and there's a hard dependency on the Risk Engine team for the new sanctions screening API.

James 带了 28 个故事点。上个 Sprint 团队交付了 22。你担心过度承诺，并且有个对 Risk Engine 团队新制裁名单筛查 API 的硬依赖。

As TL, you need to: challenge the commitment, raise the dependency, and sequence the work. You are the technical authority in this room — the PO proposes, the team commits, but you make sure the commitments are realistic.

作为 TL，你需要：挑战承诺、提依赖、排优先级。PO 提议，团队承诺，但你确保承诺是现实的。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (PO): OK, sprint goal this time: ship the new sanctions screening integration to UAT. I have 28 points ready. Let's get started.好，本 Sprint 目标：把新制裁名单筛查集成交付到 UAT。我准备好了 28 个点，开始吧。
You (TL): Thanks, James. Before we commit, can we walk through capacity first?谢谢 James。承诺之前，我们先过一下容量？
Sarah (SM): Sure. Wei, go ahead.好，Wei 请。
You (TL): Squad has 6 devs. Subtracting PTO, public holidays, and ceremonies, we have 22 dev-days this sprint. At our average velocity of 22 points, 28 is over-commitment by 27%. I'd cap the sprint at 24 points and carry the remaining 4 into the backlog.Squad 6 个开发。扣除休假、公共假期和会议，本 Sprint 有 22 个开发日。按平均速度 22 个点，28 已经是过度承诺 27%。我建议封顶 24 个点，剩下 4 个进 backlog。
James (PO): Hmm. We promised the regulator a UAT-ready release by end of sprint. If we cut 4 points, we miss that.嗯。我们跟监管承诺了Sprint 末 UAT 准备就绪。砍 4 个点就赶不上了。
You (TL): I hear you. But here's the dependency risk: the new sanctions API from the Risk Engine team isn't signed off yet. If they slip by even 3 days, our 28-point sprint falls apart. I'd rather commit to 24 with a high-confidence delivery than commit to 28 and miss the regulator deadline.我理解。但有个依赖风险：Risk Engine 团队的新制裁名单 API 还没签批。如果他们推迟 3 天，我们 28 点的 Sprint 整个崩掉。我宁愿承诺 24 个点、高信心交付，也不要承诺 28 但错过监管期限。
Priya (Senior Dev): Wei is right. If the API slips, the whole sprint is at risk. Better to sequence the work: let's pull the lowest-priority 4 points and slot them into Sprint 25.Wei 说得对。API 一推迟，整个 Sprint 都风险。不如排个序：把优先级最低的 4 个点拉出来，放到 Sprint 25。
You (TL): OK, agreed commitment is 24 points. Stretch goal is 4 more if the API lands early. James, are you OK with that?好，承诺 24 个点，如果 API 早到就冲刺加 4 个。James，这样可以吗？
James (PO): OK. 24 with a stretch. Let's lock it in.好。24 加冲刺。就这么定。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Capacity Can we walk through capacity first? 我们先过一下容量？ Always start sprint planning with capacity, not stories. Capacity first, commitment second.
  • Velocity At our average velocity of 22 points… 按平均速度 22 个点…… Use 3-sprint average velocity, not last sprint alone. One sprint is a sample, three is a trend.
  • Commit 28 is over-commitment by 27%. 28 是过度承诺 27% Always quantify over-commitment as a percentage. Numbers are harder to argue with than adjectives.
  • Trade-off Commit to 24 with a high-confidence delivery vs. 28 and miss the regulator. 承诺 24 高信心 vs. 承诺 28 错过监管 Frame as a trade-off, not a refusal. The PO can choose if they hear both sides.
  • Dependency If the API slips, the whole sprint is at risk. 如果 API 推迟，整个 Sprint 都风险 Always name the upstream team and the consequence. Vague "dependency risk" is ignored.
  • Stretch Stretch goal is 4 more if the API lands early. 如果 API 早到就冲刺加 4 个 Stretch goals protect the core commitment. If the stretch fails, the sprint is still on track.
  • Sequence Let's sequence the work. 我们排一下顺序 "Sequence" is more professional than "reorder". It implies dependencies and risk management.
  • Lock Let's lock it in. 就这么定 The PO's signal that the commitment is final. Once locked, no more mid-sprint scope changes without re-planning.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Velocity n. 速度 Story points a team delivers per sprint, averaged over 3 sprints.
  • Capacity n. 容量 Total dev-days available in a sprint, after subtracting PTO, holidays, ceremonies.
  • Commitment n. 承诺 The points the team signs up to deliver this sprint.
  • Stretch goal n. 冲刺目标 Additional work the team takes on if the core sprint finishes early.
  • Over-commitment n. 过度承诺 Committing to more than capacity supports, usually >20% over velocity.
  • Sequencing n. 排序 Deciding the order of stories based on dependencies and risk.
  • Risk Engine team n. 风险引擎团队 The team that owns the sanctions screening and AML services.
  • Regulator deadline n. 监管期限 A binding date from HKMA, MAS, FCA, etc.
  • Sprint goal n. Sprint 目标 A one-sentence summary of what the sprint will deliver.
  • Carry (a story) v. 推到下期 Move a story to the next sprint because of capacity or dependency.
  • Re-planning n. 重新规划 The full team meeting called when a mid-sprint scope change requires a new commitment.
  • Sprint 25 n. 下个 Sprint The next sprint after the current one.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I'd cap the sprint at 24 points" — soft authority language

TLs use I'd / I'd rather / I suggest instead of we will / we must in planning. The team commits; the TL recommends. This is a politeness device: it keeps the team in the driver's seat while you steer.

TL 在规划中用 I'd / I'd rather / I suggest 而不是 we will / we must。团队承诺；TL 推荐。

② "If the API slips" — the conditional trap

Use if X slips by N days, then Y falls apart to make risk concrete. The structure: condition + consequence + timeline. Vague risk is ignored; quantified risk gets action.

用 if X slips by N days, then Y falls apart 把风险具体化。结构：条件 + 后果 + 时间。

③ "Stretch goal" — the safety valve

Stretch is a noun that means "additional work beyond commitment". Pair it with if / when to make it conditional: stretch goal is 4 more if the API lands early. The team can deliver the stretch without breaking the core commitment.

Stretch 是"承诺之外的工作"。配 if / when 让它有条件：如果 API 早到，就冲刺加 4 个。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • PO proposes, team commits. Even when you know the commitment is wrong, frame your push-back as a recommendation, not a veto. The team owns the commitment.
  • Capacity before commitment. Insist on this order: capacity → velocity → commitment. Skipping capacity leads to sprint failure.
  • Use 3-sprint average velocity. One good sprint inflates expectations. One bad sprint depresses them. Three is the gold standard for planning.
  • Quantify over-commitment. "We're over-committed" is an opinion. "We're over-committed by 27%" is a fact. The second one wins arguments.
  • Always name the upstream team. "The new sanctions API" is vague. "The Risk Engine team's sanctions API, which is in design phase" is specific. Specific dependencies get chased.
  • Regulator deadlines are sacred. A regulator deadline is not a stretch goal. If you risk it, the SM and PO will remember. If you protect it, you build political capital.
  • End with a clear "lock". Don't leave planning ambiguous. End with "OK, locked at 24 with a stretch of 4". The team needs clarity to commit.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the planning discussion

Translate the following sprint planning snippet into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the TL frames the trade-off and the stretch goal.

"OK, sprint goal this time: ship the new payment validation engine to UAT. I have 32 points ready. Wei, capacity first? Sure. Squad has 7 devs. Subtracting PTO, public holidays, and ceremonies, we have 24 dev-days this sprint. At our average velocity of 24 points, 32 is over-commitment by 33%. I'd cap the sprint at 26 points and carry the remaining 6 into the backlog. I hear you, but here's the dependency risk: the new validation library from the Platform team isn't signed off yet. If they slip by even 3 days, our 32-point sprint falls apart. I'd rather commit to 26 with a high-confidence delivery than commit to 32 and miss the regulator deadline. OK, agreed commitment is 26 points. Stretch goal is 6 more if the library lands early. Let's lock it in."

"好，本 Sprint 目标：把新支付校验引擎交付到 UAT。我准备了 32 个点。Wei，先过容量？好。Squad 7 个开发。扣除休假、公共假期和会议，本 Sprint 有 24 个开发日。按平均速度 24 个点，32 是过度承诺 33%。我建议封顶 26 个点，剩下 6 个进 backlog。我理解，但有个依赖风险：Platform 团队的新校验库还没签批。如果他们推迟 3 天，我们 32 点的 Sprint 整个崩掉。我宁愿承诺 26 高信心交付，也不要承诺 32 但错过监管期限。好，承诺 26 个点。如果库早到就冲刺加 6 个。就这么定。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these planning lines is most "TL-like" and which is most "dev-like"? Why?

  1. "Yesterday I finished the unit tests for the eKYC vendor callback. Coverage is at 91%."
  2. "I'd cap the sprint at 24 points. 28 is over-commitment by 27%."
  3. "Yesterday I fixed the bug in the offline-first KYC capture. The fix is deployed to UAT."
  4. "Stretch goal is 4 more if the API lands early."

② and ④ are TL-like (capacity-based commitment, stretch framing). ① and ③ are dev-like (unit tests, bug fix). A common mistake new TLs make is dropping into implementation-status reports in planning. Reframe yours at the capacity and commitment layer.
