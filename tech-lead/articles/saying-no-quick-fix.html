Saying No to a 'Quick Fix' Request

Article 16 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Senior 🎯 Pushing back on 'just patch it', total cost of ownership, principled refusal

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday, 4:30 PM HKT. James (PO) walks over: "Wei, can we just patch the rate-limiter to whitelist the partner IP for the demo? It's a 5-minute fix." The fix: add the partner's IP to the rate-limiter whitelist, bypassing it for the regulator demo. The risk: that whitelist stays in production long after the demo, silently bypassing the rate-limiter.

周四下午4:30香港时间。James（PO）走过来："Wei，能不能给限流器打个补丁把合作方 IP 加白名单？就 5 分钟的活儿。"方案：把合作方 IP 加进限流器白名单，监管 demo 期间绕过。风险：白名单在 demo 结束后还留在生产里，静默绕过限流器。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (PO):Wei, can we just patch the rate-limiter to whitelist the partner IP for the demo? It's a 5-minute fix.Wei，能不能给限流器打个补丁把合作方 IP 加白名单？5 分钟的活儿。
You (TL):James, I hear the demo pressure. But that 5-minute fix has a 6-month tail of risk. Can I walk you through it?James，我理解 demo 压力。但这 5 分钟的活儿会带来 6 个月的风险尾巴。我跟你过一下？
James:5 minutes vs 6 months — what could go wrong?5 分钟 vs 6 个月——能出啥事？
You (TL):Three things. One: the whitelist stays in prod — we've seen this before, no one removes it. Two: when the partner gets compromised, the rate-limiter doesn't protect us. Three: the regulator asks 'why is this IP whitelisted?' and we don't have a good answer.三件事。一：白名单会留在生产里——我们见过，没有人会去删它。二：合作方被攻破时，限流器保护不了我们。三：监管问"为什么这个 IP 在白名单里"我们答不上来。
James:But the demo is in 2 days. If we don't whitelisting, the rate-limiter will throttle the demo traffic.但 demo 还有 2 天。不加白名单，限流器会限流 demo 流量。
You (TL):Compromise: temporary whitelist, with a hard expiry. Config flag with a TTL of 48 hours — the whitelist auto-removes after the demo. Logged in the audit trail. InfoSec sign-off needed — but it's a 1-hour conversation, not a 5-minute hack.折中：临时白名单，硬过期。配一个 48 小时 TTL 的开关——demo 一结束白名单自动失效。审计日志留痕。需要 InfoSec 签——但这是 1 小时的对话，不是 5 分钟的黑活。
James:Will InfoSec sign off in 2 days?InfoSec 2 天内能签下来吗？
You (TL):Yes, if we frame it as a 'demo-day temporary relaxation with audit-trail logging'. It's a known pattern, not a hack. I'll Slack Margaret now and get it on her desk this afternoon.能，如果我们把它框定为"demo 日临时放宽，带审计日志"。这是已知模式，不是黑活。我现在 Slack Margaret，今天下午就摆到她桌上。
James:OK. Let me know by EOD.好。EOD 前告诉我。
You (TL):Will do. And James — the 5-minute fix would have come back to bite us. Thanks for letting me push back.好。James——5 分钟的活儿会回来咬我们的。谢谢你让我顶回去。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Acknowledge I hear the demo pressure.我理解 demo 压力。Acknowledge first, push back second.
  • Tail risk That 5-minute fix has a 6-month tail of risk.这 5 分钟的活儿会带来 6 个月的风险尾巴。Quantify the tail, not the cost. '6-month tail' beats 'it's risky'.
  • Three things Three things: one, two, three.三件事：一、二、三。Three things is the right number for a structured pushback. Not one (weak), not seven (overwhelming).
  • Stay in prod We've seen this before, no one removes it.我们见过，没有人会去删它。Cite the pattern. 'Whitelists never get removed' is a real-world lesson, not opinion.
  • Compromise Temporary whitelist with a hard expiry.临时白名单，硬过期。Compromise is a TTL, not a removal. The expiry is the safety net.
  • Audit trail Logged in the audit trail.审计日志留痕。Audit-trail logging transforms a hack into a controlled operation. Regulators accept logged exceptions.
  • Pattern, not hack It's a known pattern, not a hack.这是已知模式，不是黑活。Reframe the workaround as a recognized pattern. 'Demo-day temporary relaxation' is a known banking pattern.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Quick fix n.. A small, tactical change to address an immediate problem. 快速修复
  • Whitelist n.. A list of IPs/entities allowed to bypass a security control. 白名单
  • Tail risk n.. The long-term risk that follows a short-term fix. 尾部风险
  • Compromised adj.. A system/account that has been breached by an attacker. 被攻破
  • Hard expiry n.. A TTL that auto-removes the change, with no manual removal needed. 硬过期
  • TTL (Time To Live) n.. The duration a config flag or credential remains active. 生存时间
  • Audit trail n.. An immutable log of who did what and when. 审计日志
  • Demo-day relaxation n.. A temporary control bypass, with audit logging, for a regulated demo. Demo 日临时放宽
  • Total cost of ownership n.. The long-term cost of a 'cheap' fix (ops, audit, security). 总拥有成本
  • Known pattern n.. A workaround recognized by InfoSec and risk teams. 已知模式

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'That X has a Y-month tail of risk' — quantifying the tail

Quantify the tail risk: that 5-minute fix has a 6-month tail of risk / that workaround has a 1-year audit exposure. Numbers beat adjectives.

量化尾部风险。

② 'Three things: one, two, three' — the structured pushback

Use three things / two reasons / one concern to structure a pushback. The number is part of the frame.

用 三件事 / 两条理由 / 一个关切 结构化反对。

③ 'It's a known pattern, not a hack' — the reframe

Reframe a workaround as a recognized pattern: it's a known pattern, not a hack / it's a temporary relaxation with audit logging, not an exception.

把 workaround 重新框定为已知模式。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Acknowledge first, push back second. I hear the demo pressure before but that has a 6-month tail.
  • Quantify the tail, not the cost. 6-month tail beats it is risky.
  • Three things is the right number. Not one (weak), not seven (overwhelming).
  • Cite the pattern, not the principle. Whitelists never get removed is real-world, not opinion.
  • Compromise is a TTL, not a removal. The expiry is the safety net.
  • Audit-trail logging transforms a hack into a controlled operation. Regulators accept logged exceptions.
  • Reframe the workaround as a recognized pattern. Demo-day temporary relaxation is a known banking pattern.

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these is the most TL-as-principled-engineer move?

  1. No. Do not do it. Find another way.
  2. Sure, let me whitelist it now.
  3. I hear the demo pressure. That 5-minute fix has a 6-month tail. Three things. One: whitelist stays in prod. Two: regulator exposure. Three: security incident. Compromise: temporary whitelist with TTL 48h, audit-trail logging, InfoSec sign-off.
  4. I will do it, but I am telling you it is wrong.

③ is the principled TL. ① is gate-keeping. ② is capitulation. ④ is passive-aggressive. A common mistake is either no, I refuse (no compromise) or yes, but it is wrong (capitulation + complaint). The TL job is to give the PO a third option: a controlled workaround with safety nets.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

The PM wants to skip encryption-at-rest for the new analytics database to ship faster. Your concerns: (a) the data is customer PII, (b) HKMA requires encryption at rest, (c) the audit team will flag it. Write the pushback in English. Use I hear the X, quantify the tail, three things, propose a compromise (e.g., column-level encryption for PII fields only). End by reframe as a known pattern.

"I hear the ship-faster pressure. But skipping encryption-at-rest has a regulator tail of risk. Three things. One: it is customer PII — HKMA data classification policy requires encryption at rest. Two: audit will flag it, and remediation is a 3-month project. Three: if we have a breach, encryption-at-rest is the difference between a reportable incident and a near-miss. Compromise: column-level encryption for the PII fields only, 2-week delta. Use the existing Group Cyber KMS — zero new infra. It is a known pattern (PII column encryption), not a hack. Ship in 2 weeks, regulator-safe, audit-clean."
