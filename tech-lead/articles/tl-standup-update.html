Tech Lead Standup Update

Article 01 · Module 1: Daily Tech-Lead Communication · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 TL standup rhythm: capacity, blockers, sprint health, escalations

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 10:00 AM HKT. Your squad's daily standup runs on Microsoft Teams. You are the Tech Lead for the Mobile Onboarding squad, supporting 5 developers across the UK, Hong Kong, and India. Joining the standup are: Sarah, your Scrum Master (London), Raj, a senior developer (Bangalore), and Lin, a backend dev (Hong Kong).

上午10点香港时间，你的 squad 在 Teams 上开每日站会。你是移动开户squad 的 Tech Lead，带领 5 名分布在英国、香港和印度的开发。参会有：Scrum Master Sarah（伦敦）、高级开发 Raj（班加罗尔），以及后端开发 Lin（香港）。

Unlike a developer's standup, your update focuses on squad capacity, sprint burndown, blockers that need escalation, and any cross-team dependencies. You speak in squad-level language, not personal task status.

和开发人员不同，你的更新聚焦squad 容量、Sprint 燃尽、需升级的阻塞，以及跨团队依赖。用 squad 级别语言，而不是个人任务状态。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Sarah (SM): Morning, everyone. Wei, you first as TL.大家早上好，Wei，TL 先来。
You (TL): Morning. Squad update from me.早上好，squad 层面同步一下。
You (TL): Sprint health is amber — we're 3 points behind on the burndown. Two stories: ONB-204 (eKYC vendor callback) is on track, but ONB-218 (offline-first KYC capture) is slipping — the encryption-at-rest NFR is still pending InfoSec sign-off.Sprint 状态琥珀色——燃尽落后 3 个点。两个故事：ONB-204（eKYC 厂商回调）按计划，但 ONB-218（离线优先 KYC 采集）在滑——静态加密 NFR 还在等 InfoSec 签批。
You (TL): Capacity check: Raj is on PTO Thursday and Friday, and Lin is paired with InfoSec on the encryption work — so realistically we have 3.5 dev-days at risk this week.容量检查：Raj 周四周五休假，Lin 在和 InfoSec 配对做加密工作——所以这周实际有 3.5 个开发日风险。
You (TL): No hard blockers, but flagging a soft dependency on the Platform team for the mutual TLS certificate rotation policy — if it doesn't land by Wednesday, I want to pull ONB-218 from this sprint and slot it into next.没有硬阻塞，但提一个软依赖给 Platform 团队——双向 TLS 证书轮换策略。如果周三前不到位，我想把 ONB-218 拉出本 Sprint，放到下一个。
Sarah (SM): Understood. Can you confirm the impact if we have to pull it?明白。拉出去的话，能确认影响吗？
You (TL): Sure. Pulling ONB-218 means the UAT window slips by one week. We still meet the regulator milestone on 31st because we have two weeks of buffer. I'd rather trade sprint scope for a clean NFR sign-off than ship and rework.可以。拉出去意味着 UAT 窗口推迟一周。但 31 号的监管里程碑不受影响，因为有两周缓冲。我宁愿用 Sprint 范围换干净的 NFR 签批，也不要上了再改。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Squad update from me. squad 层面同步一下 Mark yourself as TL. You're not speaking for yourself — you're speaking for the squad.
  • Sprint Sprint health is amber. Sprint 状态是琥珀色（中等风险） Use traffic-light language: green / amber / red. Everyone understands it instantly.
  • Capacity Capacity check: Raj is on PTO Thursday and Friday. 容量检查：Raj 周四五休假 Always surface PTO in your standup. The squad is only as available as its calendar says.
  • Soft Flagging a soft dependency on the Platform team. 提一个软依赖给 Platform 团队 Same politeness device architects use. Reserve "blocker" for things that stop the squad's day.
  • Trade-off Trade sprint scope for a clean NFR sign-off. 用 Sprint 范围换干净的 NFR 签批 This is TL vocabulary. You always offer a trade — never just a complaint.
  • Risk Ship and rework is worse than pull and slot. 上线再改比拉出再排更糟 A TL's job is to protect quality. Always frame in terms of risk, not preference.
  • Impact Can you confirm the impact if we have to pull it? 能确认影响吗 SMs always ask about impact. Have a number ready: stories, points, days, regulator milestones.
  • Buffer We have two weeks of buffer to the regulator milestone. 到监管里程碑有两周缓冲 Buffer is your safety net. Always quantify it in weeks or days.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Sprint health n. Sprint 状态 TL's overall view of the sprint — green / amber / red.
  • Burndown n. 燃尽图 Chart showing remaining story points vs. ideal pace.
  • Capacity n. 容量 How much work the squad can deliver, given PTO, holidays, ceremonies.
  • Squad n. 小队 HSBC's cross-functional delivery team — 6-9 people, 1 TL, 1 SM, devs, QA.
  • Soft dependency n. 软依赖 Not blocking today, but will block in N days if not addressed.
  • Hard blocker n. 硬阻塞 Something that stops the squad from making progress today.
  • PTO (Paid Time Off) n. 休假 Annual leave, sick days, public holidays.
  • Pair programming n. 结对编程 Two devs at one machine, one typing, one reviewing.
  • NFR (Non-Functional Requirement) n. 非功能需求 Performance, security, scalability — must be quantified.
  • Regulator milestone n. 监管里程碑 A binding date set by HKMA, MAS, FCA, or another regulator.
  • Buffer n. 缓冲 Time built into the plan to absorb slips.
  • Pull (a story) v. 拉出 Remove a story from the current sprint and slot it into the next.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Squad update from me" — speaking for the team, not yourself

As a TL, your standup is not about what I did — it's about what we did and what we need. Use squad / we / the team instead of I in 80% of your sentences. Save I for personal actions like "I raised the escalation".

TL 的站会不是说我做了什么——而是我们做了什么，需要什么。80% 句子用 squad/we/team 而不是 I。

② "Is amber" / "is slipping" — naming the risk, not hiding it

TLs don't say "everything is fine" until it's red — they say is amber or is at risk early. This gives the SM time to act. Use present-tense state-of-being: is on track / is slipping / is blocked.

TL 不要等到红了才说"有问题"——早一点用琥珀色 / 有风险。用现在时：is on track / is slipping / is blocked。

③ "Trade X for Y" — the TL's signature frame

Every TL decision is a trade. Use trade X for Y or swap X for Y to frame it: trade sprint scope for a clean NFR sign-off. This makes the trade visible and lets the SM push back if needed.

每个 TL 决策都是 trade。用 trade X for Y 来表达：trade sprint scope for a clean NFR sign-off。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • TL standup ≠ dev standup. Don't report your own stories. Report squad health, capacity, escalations, cross-team dependencies. Your value is at the team level.
  • Always surface PTO. Hidden PTO = sprint surprise. A good TL says "Raj is on PTO Thursday" before anyone asks. Calendar is the source of truth.
  • "Pull" is a polite word. Saying "I want to pull ONB-218" sounds much more professional than "we can't finish it". Both mean the same thing — but one is TL vocabulary.
  • Buffer is political capital. Every buffer day is one day you can afford a slip. Spend it carefully. Don't burn buffer in sprint 1.
  • Trade-off framing wins arguments. "We can't do it" loses. "We can do it if we trade ONB-218 for a clean NFR" wins. Always show the trade, not the complaint.
  • Numbers beat adjectives. "3.5 dev-days at risk" is better than "we're tight on time". Quantify everything: points, days, weeks, NFRs, sign-offs.
  • Regulator milestones are sacred. Never let a soft dependency put a regulator milestone at risk. Pull stories, not deadlines.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the TL standup

Translate the following TL standup into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the TL frames the capacity issue and the trade-off.

"Morning. Squad update. Sprint health is amber — we're 5 points behind on the burndown. Three stories: PAY-301 is on track, but PAY-314 (HSBCnet idempotency layer) is slipping because the Redis cluster upgrade is pending, and PAY-322 (regulator reporting) is blocked on the Model Risk team's sign-off. Capacity: two devs are on a training course Tuesday and Wednesday, so we have 2 dev-days at risk. No hard blockers, but flagging a soft dependency on the Model Risk team — if their sign-off doesn't land by Friday, I'll pull PAY-322 and slot it into next sprint. That trades one regulator-reporting story for a clean Model Risk sign-off. UAT window stays on track."

"早上好，squad 层面同步。Sprint 状态琥珀色——燃尽落后 5 个点。三个故事：PAY-301 按计划，但 PAY-314（HSBCnet 幂等层）在滑，Redis 集群升级还没做；PAY-322（监管报送）被 Model Risk 团队签批阻塞。容量：两个开发周二三在上课，所以有 2 个开发日风险。没有硬阻塞，但提一个软依赖给 Model Risk 团队——如果周五前签批不到位，我把 PAY-322 拉出本 Sprint，放到下一个。用一个监管报送故事换一个干净的 Model Risk 签批。UAT 窗口不变。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these standup lines is most "TL-like" and which is most "dev-like"? Why?

  1. "Yesterday I finished the unit tests for the eKYC vendor callback. Coverage is at 91%."
  2. "Squad update. Sprint health is amber — we're 3 points behind. ONB-218 is slipping because the NFR sign-off is pending."
  3. "Yesterday I fixed the bug in the offline-first KYC capture. The fix is deployed to UAT."
  4. "Capacity check: Lin is on PTO Wednesday, so I want to swap ONB-220 with ONB-225 to balance the load."

② and ④ are TL-like (squad-level status, capacity trade). ① and ③ are dev-like (unit tests, bug fix). A common mistake new TLs make is dropping into personal implementation reports. Reframe yours at the squad and decision layer.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

It's 10:00 AM HKT. You're the TL for a 6-person squad. You have: (a) sprint health amber, 4 points behind, (b) one story slipping because InfoSec NFR sign-off is pending, (c) one dev on PTO tomorrow, (d) a soft dependency on the Platform team for Kafka topic-naming standard. Write your 60-second TL standup in English. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Morning. Squad update. Sprint health is amber — we're 4 points behind. One story is slipping because the InfoSec NFR sign-off is pending; I've booked a 30-min with the InfoSec lead to chase it. Capacity check: Anil is on PTO tomorrow, so we have 1 dev-day at risk. No hard blockers, but flagging a soft dependency on the Platform team for the Kafka topic-naming standard — if it doesn't land by Thursday, I'll pull that story and slot it into next sprint. Trade sprint scope for a clean naming standard. UAT window stays on track. Raj, you're next."
