Pushing Back on Unreasonable Deadline

Article 14 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Senior 🎯 Data-driven pushback, scope cuts, escalation discipline

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Monday, 9:00 AM HKT. James (PO) just told you the regulator demo is moved up 2 weeks — from end of Q2 to mid-Q2. The squad has 4 user stories, 2 of which are still in design. You have 5 working days.

周一上午9点香港时间。James（PO）刚通知监管 demo 提前 2 周——从 Q2 末提前到 Q2 中。Squad 有 4 个用户故事，2 个还在设计。你有 5 个工作日。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (PO):Wei, bad news. The regulator demo moved up 2 weeks — from end of Q2 to mid-Q2. I need all 4 stories live.Wei，坏消息。监管 demo 提前 2 周——从 Q2 末提前到 Q2 中。我需要 4 个故事全部上线。
You (TL):James, I hear the regulator pressure. But 5 working days for 4 stories, 2 still in design, is not achievable. Let's look at the data together.James，我理解监管压力。但 5 个工作日做 4 个故事，其中 2 个还在设计，做不到。我们一起看数据。
You (TL):Our velocity is 18 story points per sprint. The 4 stories are 32 points. 5 working days = 1 sprint = 18 points max. 14 points will not be done.我们 velocity 是每 Sprint 18 story point。4 个故事是 32 point。5 个工作日 = 1 个 Sprint = 最多 18 point。14 point 做不完。
James:Can we add people?能加人吗？
You (TL):No. Adding people to a 5-day deadline makes it slower — Brook's law. The 2 stories in design have a 2-day design-review gate. If we start design today, we lose 2 days before code even begins.不能。5 天 deadline 加人只会更慢——Brook 定律。2 个还在设计的故事要走 2 天设计评审。今天开始设计，开工前就丢了 2 天。
James:What if we cut quality?砍质量呢？
You (TL):No. This is a regulator demo. Cutting test coverage or skipping code review means the demo could fail in front of the regulator — the exact opposite of what we want.不能。这是监管 demo。砍测试覆盖率或跳过 code review 意味着监管面前 demo 可能崩——这恰恰是我们最不想看到的。
You (TL):Compromise: 2 stories live, 2 stories in 'design-complete, code-in-progress' state. We show the regulator 2 working flows and 2 design walkthroughs. This is what 32 points of work actually looks like in 5 days.折中：2 个故事上线，2 个故事是"设计完成、代码进行中"状态。给监管看 2 个可用的流程和 2 个设计演示。这才是 5 天 32 point 工作量的真实样子。
James:The regulator won't accept 'design walkthrough'. They want to click buttons.监管不会接受"设计演示"。他们要点按钮。
You (TL):Then the only way to do 4 stories is 10 days, not 5. Two options: (a) Push back on the demo date — I'll come with you to the regulator track lead to explain the velocity math. (b) Cut scope to 2 stories and demo them polished — better than 4 stories that crash in front of the regulator. My recommendation: (b). A polished 2 beats a broken 4.那 4 个故事只有 10 天能做完，不是 5 天。两个选项：(a) 顶回去 demo 日期——我陪你去见监管 track lead 讲清楚速度数学。(b) 砍范围到 2 个故事，demo 做到精致——比 4 个故事在监管面前崩要好得多。我建议：(b)。精致的 2 胜过崩掉的 4。
James:OK. Let's go with (b). 2 stories, polished. Can you come with me to the regulator track lead and present the trade-off?好。走 (b)。2 个故事，做精致。你能陪我去见监管 track lead 讲取舍吗？
You (TL):Yes. I'll prepare 1 page on velocity, scope, and the demo plan. Send me 30 minutes before the meeting.可以。我会准备 1 页速度、范围、demo 计划。会议前 30 分钟发我。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Acknowledge I hear the regulator pressure.我理解监管压力。Acknowledge first. The PO is also under pressure.
  • Data Our velocity is 18 story points per sprint. 5 days = 18 points max.我们 velocity 是每 Sprint 18 story point。5 天 = 最多 18 point。Data is your shield. Opinions lose; numbers win.
  • Trade-off frame Brook's law: adding people makes a tight deadline slower.Brook 定律：加人只会让紧 deadline 更慢。Cite principles, not preferences. Brook's law, YAGNI, etc.
  • No-go No, this is a regulator demo — we cannot cut quality.不能，这是监管 demo——不能砍质量。Name the red lines. Some cuts are not on the table.
  • Compromise Compromise: 2 live, 2 in design-complete state.折中：2 个上线，2 个设计完成状态。Always have a compromise. The compromise is what saves the conversation.
  • Recommend My recommendation: (b). A polished 2 beats a broken 4.我建议：(b)。精致的 2 胜过崩掉的 4。TLs recommend. The PO chooses. 'My recommendation' is the right register.
  • Joint escalation I'll come with you to the regulator track lead.我陪你去见监管 track lead。Offer to join the escalation. The PO is alone facing the regulator — partnership builds trust.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Velocity n.. The team's average story points completed per sprint. 速度
  • Brook's law n.. 'Adding people to a late project makes it later.' Brook 定律
  • Story point n.. A relative measure of effort for a user story. 故事点
  • Scope cut n.. Reducing the features in scope to meet a deadline. 砍范围
  • Design walkthrough n.. A presentation of a design before it's implemented. 设计演示
  • Polished demo n.. A demo that has been rehearsed and is reliable. 精致 demo
  • Velocity math n.. The arithmetic of what can be shipped in a given time. 速度数学
  • Escalation partnership n.. Joining the PO in a difficult stakeholder conversation. 升级伙伴关系
  • Red line n.. A constraint that cannot be cut no matter what. 红线
  • Trade-off slide n.. A 1-page summary of options + consequences for a decision. 取舍页

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'I hear the X, but the data says Y' — acknowledge then data

Use I hear the X, but / however, the data says Y. Acknowledge first, data second.

用 I hear the X, but the data says Y。先认后数。

② 'No, this is a red line' — naming the no-go

Use no, this is a red line / this is a regulator demo, we cannot cut quality to name what cannot be cut.

用 no, this is a red line 划红线。

③ 'A polished 2 beats a broken 4' — the memorable phrase

Use short, memorable phrases to land the trade-off: a polished 2 beats a broken 4 / done is better than perfect / ship the 80%, not the 100%.

用简短好记的句子表达取舍。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Acknowledge first, data second. The PO is also under pressure.
  • Data is your shield. Opinions lose; numbers win.
  • Cite principles, not preferences. 'Brook's law' is more credible than 'I think adding people is bad'.
  • Name the red lines. Some cuts are not on the table.
  • Always have a compromise ready. The compromise is what saves the conversation.
  • 'A polished 2 beats a broken 4'. Memorable phrases land the trade-off.
  • Offer to join the escalation. The PO is alone facing the regulator — partnership builds trust.

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these is the most 'TL-as-deadline-realist' move?

  1. 'No, I can't do 5 days. Find another TL.'
  2. 'OK, I'll get the team to work weekends. We'll do it.'
  3. 'I hear the pressure. The data: 18 points max in 5 days. 4 stories = 32 points. 14 points don't fit. My recommendation: cut to 2 polished stories. I'll come with you to the regulator.'
  4. 'Maybe if we skip code review…'

③ is the TL-as-realist. ① is gate-keeping. ② is capitulation. ④ is malpractice (cutting code review for a regulator demo). A common mistake is either 'no, I refuse' or 'yes, we'll work weekends'. The TL's job is to give the PO a third option: a realistic plan, with a partner for the escalation.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

Your PO moves a deadline from 4 weeks to 2 weeks. The team has 3 stories, 28 points total, velocity is 16 per sprint. 1 story is in design, 2 are partially coded. There's a regulator demo in 4 weeks (not 2). Write the deadline pushback in English. Use 'I hear the X', 'velocity math', name red lines, propose compromise, recommend, offer to join the escalation.

"I hear the pressure to move faster. But 2 weeks for 3 stories, 28 points total, is not achievable. Velocity math: 16 points per sprint. 2 weeks = 1 sprint = 16 points max. 12 points don't fit. The 1 story in design needs 1-week design review. Cutting quality is a red line — we're shipping to a regulator demo. Compromise: 2 stories live in 2 weeks, 1 story live in 4 weeks (matches the regulator demo). My recommendation: keep the 4-week timeline, ship 2 stories polished in 2 weeks as a 'sneak peek' for stakeholders. A polished 2 in 2 weeks beats a broken 3 in 2 weeks. I'll come with you to the regulator track lead to align on the 4-week plan."
