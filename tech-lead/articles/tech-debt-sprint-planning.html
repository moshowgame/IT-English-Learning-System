Tech Debt Sprint Planning

Article 10 · Module 2: Design & Code Quality · Tech Lead English

⏱ 8 min read 🟠 Advanced 🎯 Negotiating tech-debt capacity with PM, framing ROI, sequencing

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday, 11:00 AM HKT. Sprint planning for the next 2-week sprint. James (PO) wants 100% feature capacity. You, as TL, are pushing for 20% tech-debt capacity. You have a backlog of 8 tech-debt items. The squad has 8 story points per developer.

周五上午11点香港时间。下个 2 周 Sprint 计划会。James（PO）想要 100% 业务功能容量。你作为 TL 争取 20% 技术债容量。你有一个 8 项的技术债 backlog。Squad 每人每 Sprint 8 个 story point。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (PO):Wei, I need 100% feature capacity next sprint. The regulator demo is in 3 weeks. Can we push all tech debt to the sprint after?Wei，下个 Sprint 我需要 100% 业务功能容量。3 周后监管 demo。技术债能不能都推到下下个 Sprint？
You (TL):James, I hear the deadline pressure — but pushing all tech debt is a false economy. Two of the 8 items are blocking the demo — the audit-log retention bug and the rate-limiter reset. The other 6 can wait.James，我理解 deadline 压力——但全部推后是假节约。8 项里有 2 项阻塞 demo——审计日志保留时长 bug 和限流器 reset。另外 6 项可以等。
James:Why are they blocking? The features work, right?为什么阻塞？功能不是跑得起来吗？
You (TL):The features work today, but they won't survive the demo. The audit-log bug means we can't produce the regulator's retention report. The rate-limiter bug means we'll throttle legitimate traffic when the demo goes live. Both are 1-point fixes.今天跑得起来，但撑不过 demo。审计日志 bug 意味着我们交不出监管要的保留时长报告。限流器 bug 意味着demo 上线后正常流量会被限流。都是 1 点的修复。
James:OK, 2 points is fine. What about the other 6?OK，2 点可以。其他 6 项呢？
You (TL):None of them are demo-blocking, but one is a slow-burn risk. The Kafka consumer-group rebalance has been causing 5-minute outages every 2 weeks for 3 months. It's a 3-point fix and it eats one full day of on-call pain per month. I'd like to take it.都不阻塞 demo，但有一项是慢燃风险。Kafka consumer-group rebalance 已经3 个月每 2 周造成 5 分钟故障。3 点修复，每月能省一天的 on-call 痛苦。我想接。
James:5 points out of 48 is a lot.5 点（48 点里）太多了。
You (TL):5 points is 10% capacity. 90% on features. The 2 demo-blockers are non-negotiable. The Kafka fix is a one-time investment that pays back every month. Compromise: take the 2 demo-blockers this sprint, queue the Kafka fix as the first item of next sprint. That keeps us at 4% tech-debt this sprint and 8% next sprint.5 点是 10% 容量。90% 投业务。2 个 demo 阻塞项不可谈。Kafka 修复是一次性投资，每月都有回报。折中：demo 阻塞项本 Sprint 修，Kafka 排下个 Sprint 第一项。本 Sprint 4% 技术债，下个 Sprint 8%。
James:Deal. Can you write up the 2 demo-blockers in the sprint goal doc? I want them visible to the regulator track lead.成交。你能把 2 个 demo 阻塞项写到 Sprint 目标文档里吗？我想让监管 track lead 看到。
You (TL):Done. I'll also add the 6 deferred items to the tech-debt backlog with ROI tags — so when we come back to this in 6 weeks, we have the data.好。我也会把 6 个延后的加进技术债 backlog，带 ROI 标签——6 周后回头看时数据都在。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Acknowledge I hear the deadline pressure.我理解 deadline 压力。Acknowledge first, then push. Otherwise you're just saying 'no'.
  • Frame Pushing all tech debt is a false economy.全部推后是假节约。Reframe the 'all or nothing' to a 'which ones are critical'.
  • Demo-blocker Two of the 8 items are blocking the demo.8 项里有 2 项阻塞 demo。Demo-blockers are non-negotiable. Non-demo-blockers are negotiable.
  • ROI One-time investment that pays back every month.一次性投资，每月都有回报。Frame tech debt as ROI, not as cost. 'Hours saved per month' beats 'I don't like this code'.
  • Compromise Take the 2 demo-blockers this sprint, queue the Kafka fix as the first item of next sprint.demo 阻塞项本 Sprint 修，Kafka 排下个 Sprint 第一项。Always have a compromise ready: this sprint + next sprint split.
  • Visible I want them visible to the regulator track lead.我想让监管 track lead 看到。Make tech debt visible to the stakeholder that cares. They become your champion.
  • Data Add the 6 deferred items to the tech-debt backlog with ROI tags.把 6 个延后的加进技术债 backlog，带 ROI 标签。Don't lose the items you didn't take. Tag them with ROI so the next conversation is data-driven.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Tech debt n.. Suboptimal code/design that slows future development. 技术债
  • Tech-debt capacity n.. The percentage of sprint capacity reserved for tech-debt work. 技术债容量
  • False economy n.. A saving that costs more in the long run. 假节约
  • Demo-blocker n.. A bug or tech-debt item that prevents a planned demo from succeeding. Demo 阻塞项
  • Audit-log retention n.. How long logs are kept, often required by regulators (e.g., 7 years). 审计日志保留
  • Rate limiter n.. A component that caps the number of requests per second. 限流器
  • Slow-burn risk n.. A risk that doesn't block today but accumulates pain over time. 慢燃风险
  • Consumer-group rebalance n.. Kafka's mechanism for redistributing partitions across consumers. 消费者组再平衡
  • On-call pain n.. The operational burden of being paged for production issues. On-call 痛苦
  • ROI tag n.. A label on a tech-debt item quantifying the expected return. ROI 标签

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① 'I hear the X' — acknowledge before push

Use I hear the X / I understand the X / I get the X to acknowledge the other side's pressure. Then push. I hear the deadline pressure, but…

用 I hear the X 先认后推。

② 'That's a false economy' — the cost-frame reframe

Use that's a false economy / that's short-term thinking / that costs us later to reframe cost conversations.

用 that's a false economy 重新框定成本对话。

③ 'Compromise: X this sprint, Y next sprint' — the split

Use compromise: this sprint X, next sprint Y when both sides have valid points. The split gives both sides a win.

用 this sprint X, next sprint Y 分摊。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Acknowledge the pressure first. 'I hear the deadline' before 'but pushing all is a false economy'.
  • Demo-blockers are non-negotiable. Non-demo-blockers are negotiable.
  • Frame tech debt as ROI, not as cost. 'Hours saved per month' beats 'I don't like this code'.
  • Always have a compromise ready. 'This sprint 2, next sprint 1' is a 60% win, not 100%.
  • Make tech debt visible to the stakeholder that cares. They become your champion.
  • Tag the items you didn't take with ROI. The next conversation is data-driven, not opinion-driven.
  • Document the split in the sprint goal doc. If it's not in the doc, it didn't happen.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the tech-debt negotiation

Translate the following negotiation into natural Chinese.

"I hear the deadline pressure — but pushing all tech debt is a false economy. Two of the 8 items are blocking the demo — the audit-log retention bug and the rate-limiter reset. The other 6 can wait. The features work today, but they won't survive the demo. The audit-log bug means we can't produce the regulator's retention report. The rate-limiter bug means we'll throttle legitimate traffic. Both are 1-point fixes. The Kafka consumer-group rebalance has been causing 5-minute outages every 2 weeks for 3 months. It's a 3-point fix and it eats one full day of on-call pain per month. Compromise: take the 2 demo-blockers this sprint, queue the Kafka fix as the first item of next sprint. That keeps us at 4% tech-debt this sprint and 8% next sprint."

"我理解 deadline 压力——但全部推后是假节约。8 项里有 2 项阻塞 demo——审计日志保留时长 bug 和限流器 reset。其他 6 项可以等。今天跑得起来，但撑不过 demo。审计日志 bug 意味着我们交不出监管要的保留时长报告。限流器 bug 意味着 demo 上线后正常流量会被限流。都是 1 点的修复。Kafka consumer-group rebalance 已经 3 个月每 2 周造成 5 分钟故障。3 点修复，每月能省一天的 on-call 痛苦。折中：demo 阻塞项本 Sprint 修，Kafka 排下个 Sprint 第一项。本 Sprint 4% 技术债，下个 Sprint 8%。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the TL mistake

Which of these is the most 'TL-as-tech-debt-champion' move?

  1. 'No, I need 30% tech-debt capacity. Final answer.'
  2. 'Tech debt is important, but I can't fight the PO this sprint. We'll see next sprint.'
  3. 'I hear the deadline. 2 demo-blockers this sprint, Kafka fix first item next sprint. 4% this sprint, 8% next.'
  4. 'You don't understand. The code is bad. We need to refactor.'

③ is the TL-as-champion move. ① is gate-keeping. ② is capitulation. ④ is opinion-driven. A common mistake is either all-or-nothing or zero. The TL's job is to negotiate the 60% win that the squad can actually ship.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

Your PO wants 100% feature capacity. You have 4 tech-debt items: (a) 1-point broken health-check blocking prod deploy, (b) 2-point refactor of legacy auth, (c) 1-point flaky test blocking CI, (d) 3-point monolith-to-microservice split. Negotiate in English. Use 'I hear the X' / 'false economy' / 'compromise'.

"I hear the deadline pressure. But of the 4 items, 2 are deploy-blockers — the health-check and the flaky test. Both are 1-point fixes. The legacy-auth refactor is 2 points and de-risks next quarter's PCI audit. The monolith split is 5 points and not negotiable this quarter. Compromise: take the 2 deploy-blockers this sprint, queue the auth refactor as first item next sprint, defer the monolith split to Q3. That keeps us at 4% tech-debt this sprint and 6% next. Deal?"
