HSBC Systems Architecture & Integration

Article 20 · Module 5: HSBC Banking Context — Systems Landscape & Integration · Architect English

⏱ 11 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎯 HSBCnet, OneWealth, HUB, GLCM, GPB, RBWM — how the bank's core platforms connect

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Monday, 3:00 PM HKT. You are a Solution Architect in the RBWM Digital tribe, and a new Chief Architect — James — just joined from the UK retail bank. He has 20 years of experience, but honestly, he admits he is still learning the HSBC systems map. He has booked a 60-minute whiteboard session on the global RBWM systems landscape: HSBCnet, OneWealth, HUB, GLCM, and how the global businesses (RBWM, CMB, GPB) fit on top.

周一下午 3 点香港时间，你是 RBWM 数字化 tribe 的 Solution 架构师。新来的 Chief Architect James 从英国零售银行加入，有 20 年经验，但坦言 自己还在学汇丰的系统地图。他约了 60 分钟的白板会，主题是 RBWM 的全球 系统全景：HSBCnet、OneWealth、HUB、 GLCM，以及全球业务条线（RBWM、CMB、 GPB）如何落在上面。

You have 60 minutes. The audience is small: James, plus Aisha (peer architect, UAE), Tom (GPB architect, Shanghai), and Lin (CMB architect, HK). You have the HSBC Group Architecture Map v9.2 open in Confluence. The session needs to leave James with three things: a mental model of the customer-facing platforms, a mental model of the integration patterns, and a list of the three integration decisions he should not re-litigate.

60 分钟，观众少：James，加上 Aisha（同侪架构师，阿联酋）、 Tom（GPB 架构师，上海）、Lin（CMB 架构师，香港）。 你打开了 Confluence 上的 汇丰集团架构图 v9.2。会议需要让 James 带 3 样东西走： 面向客户平台的心理模型、集成模式的心理模型、以及 3 个他不该翻案重谈的集成决策。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

James (Chief Architect, UK): Thanks for the slot, Wei. Quick one to start — at a high level, how would you map the HSBC systems landscape for someone who knows retail banking but not HSBC specifically?Wei，谢你给时间。开场问一句——对懂零售银行但不懂汇丰的人，你会怎么画汇丰系统全景？
You (Architect): Sure. Three layers. Layer one — the customer-facing channels. HSBCnet is the global digital banking platform for RBWM and CMB — it's the customer's main entry point. OneWealth is the wealth-management platform — investment, advisory, portfolio rebalancing. OnePay is the card and payments platform for GPB and RBWM.好。三层。第一层——面向客户的渠道。HSBCnet 是 RBWM 和 CMB 的全球数字银行平台——是客户的主要入口。OneWealth 是财富管理平台——投资、顾问、组合再平衡。OnePay 是 GPB 和 RBWM 的卡和支付平台。
James: Right, so HSBCnet is the digital front door, OneWealth sits behind it for the wealth journey, OnePay sits behind it for cards?那 HSBCnet 是数字前门，OneWealth 躲在后面做财富旅程，OnePay 躲在后面做卡？
You (Architect): Exactly. Layer two — the core banking and ledger systems. HUB is the global core banking platform — accounts, balances, transactions, statements. GLCM is the Global Liquidity and Cash Management platform — corporate deposits, payments, liquidity, served to CMB and GPB.对。第二层——核心银行和账本系统。HUB 是全球核心银行平台——账户、余额、交易、流水。GLCM 是全球流动性与现金管理平台——公司存款、支付、流动性，服 CMB 和 GPB。
Tom (Architect, Shanghai): Quick clarification — GLCM is CMB-and-GPB-only, right? RBWM doesn't use it.澄清一下——GLCM 只服务 CMB 和 GPB，RBWM 不用的对吧？
You (Architect): Correct. GLCM is corporate; RBWM is retail wealth. They share the core banking ledger in HUB, but the front-end and product engines are separate.对。GLCM 走公司，RBWM 走零售财富。它们在 HUB 里共享核心银行账本，但前端和产品引擎是分开的。
Aisha (Architect, UAE): Good, that's clearer. What's layer three?清楚了。第三层是什么？
You (Architect): Layer three — the cross-cutting platforms. Group Cyber and Group Resilience for security and DR. Group Data for the customer data platform, the data lake, and the regulatory reporting mart. Group Cloud for the AWS landing zone and the multi-account setup. Every customer-facing platform sits on top of these three.第三层——横向平台。Group Cyber 和 Group Resilience 负责安全和容灾。Group Data 负责客户数据平台、数据湖和监管报送数据集市。Group Cloud 负责 AWS landing zone 和多账号体系。每个面向客户的平台都建在这 3 个之上。
James: And the integration patterns between the layers — what's the default?那层与层之间的集成模式——默认是什么？
You (Architect): Three patterns, in priority order. One, synchronous REST over mTLS for customer-facing real-time — HSBCnet calls OneWealth, OnePay calls HUB. Two, event-driven on Kafka for state changes — OneWealth publishes a portfolio-rebalanced event that HUB consumes to update positions. Three, T+1 batch file over SFTP for regulatory and reconciliation — HUB drops a daily ledger file for the correspondent banks to reconcile against.3 种模式，按优先级排。第一，双向 TLS 上的同步 REST，用于面向客户的实时场景——HSBCnet 调 OneWealth，OnePay 调 HUB。第二，Kafka 上的事件驱动，用于状态变更——OneWealth 发组合再平衡事件，HUB 消费后更新头寸。第三，SFTP 上的 T+1 批量文件，用于监管和对账——HUB 每日落账本文件给代理行对账。
James: OK, and the three decisions I shouldn't re-litigate?那 3 个我不该翻案的决策呢？
You (Architect): Honest answer — three. One, don't relitigate Kafka as the group event bus — that's ADR-0902, signed. Two, don't relitigate HUB as the core ledger — there's no plan B and the cost to migrate is in the billions. Three, don't relitigate AWS as the default cloud for new digital workloads — the Group Cloud landing zone is the path.实话——3 个。一，别翻案Kafka 作为集团事件总线——ADR-0902 已经签了。二，别翻案HUB 作为核心账本——没有 Plan B，迁移成本数十亿美元。三，别翻案AWS 作为新数字化负载的默认云——走 Group Cloud landing zone 是正路。
Lin (Architect, HK): Just to flag — a fourth one, if I may: don't relitigate Group Standard SDL. The SDL gate is the single most-defended control in CAB and eCAB.提一下——如果可以再加第四条：别翻案集团标准 SDL。SDL gate 是 CAB 和 eCAB 上最被严格捍卫的控制项。
You (Architect): Good addition. Right, so — to summarise: HSBCnet, OneWealth, OnePay on layer one; HUB, GLCM on layer two; Group Cyber, Group Resilience, Group Data, Group Cloud on layer three. REST+mTLS, Kafka, T+1 SFTP as the three integration patterns. Kafka, HUB, AWS, SDL as the four no-go zones for re-litigation. Happy to walk through any layer in more detail offline.好补充。总结一下：第一层 HSBCnet、OneWealth、OnePay；第二层 HUB、GLCM；第三层 Group Cyber、Group Resilience、Group Data、Group Cloud。3 个集成模式：REST+mTLS、Kafka、T+1 SFTP。4 个"别翻案"：Kafka、HUB、AWS、SDL。任何一层要细看都可以线下约。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Map Three layers. 三层 A simple count ("three layers") buys the listener 60 seconds of patience while you build the picture. Architects over-explain; count instead.
  • Front door HSBCnet is the digital front door. HSBCnet 是数字前门 "Front door" is the customer-facing metaphor. New joiners picture the system map faster when you use physical metaphors (door, core, basement).
  • Scope GLCM is CMB-and-GPB-only. GLCM 只服务 CMB 和 GPB Always state the business scope. RBWM doesn't use GLCM. CMB doesn't use OnePay. Scope stops people proposing out-of-scope integrations.
  • Cross-cutting Layer three — the cross-cutting platforms. 第三层——横向平台 "Cross-cutting" = used by everything (security, data, cloud). New joiners often miss these because they don't have a "home team" — make them visible.
  • Pattern Three integration patterns, in priority order. 3 种集成模式，按优先级排 "In priority order" tells the listener which pattern to use by default. The first one is the answer 80% of the time.
  • Default Synchronous REST over mTLS for customer-facing real-time. 面向客户实时场景默认双向 TLS 上的同步 REST "Default" is the architect's escape hatch from architecture debates. If someone proposes a new pattern, ask "is the default not working?".
  • State Event-driven on Kafka for state changes. 状态变更用 Kafka 事件驱动 "State changes" is the architect's trigger phrase for events. If the change is a state change, use events; if it's a query, use REST.
  • No re-litigate Three decisions I shouldn't re-litigate. 3 个我不该翻案的决策 "Re-litigate" is the perfect verb for a settled decision. Use it in any "should we revisit X?" meeting to politely shut it down.
  • Plan B There's no plan B and the cost to migrate is in the billions. 没有 Plan B，迁移成本数十亿美元 "Plan B" is the architect's word for the alternative that doesn't exist. Pair it with the cost to make the case land.
  • Gate The SDL gate is the single most-defended control in CAB. SDL gate 是 CAB 上最被严格捍卫的控制项 "Gate" = a mandatory checkpoint. CAB / eCAB / SDL are the three HSBC architecture gates. If you can't pass the gate, you can't ship.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • HSBCnet n. 汇丰网银 The global digital banking platform for RBWM and CMB — the customer's main entry point.
  • OneWealth n. 统一财富平台 The wealth-management platform — investment, advisory, portfolio rebalancing.
  • OnePay n. 统一支付平台 The card and payments platform for GPB and RBWM.
  • HUB n. HUB 核心银行 The global core banking platform — accounts, balances, transactions, statements.
  • GLCM (Global Liquidity and Cash Management) n. 全球流动性与现金管理 The corporate deposits, payments, and liquidity platform for CMB and GPB.
  • RBWM (Retail Banking and Wealth Management) n. 零售银行与财富管理 One of the four global businesses. Retail + wealth customers.
  • CMB (Commercial Banking) n. 商业银行 Mid-market corporate customers.
  • GPB (Global Private Banking) n. 全球私人银行 High-net-worth individuals.
  • Group Cyber n. 集团网络安全 The bank-wide cybersecurity function — owns secure-by-default baseline.
  • Group Resilience n. 集团业务连续性 The bank-wide DR, BCM, and operational resilience function.
  • Group Data n. 集团数据 The bank-wide data platform — CDP, data lake, regulatory reporting mart.
  • Group Cloud n. 集团云 The bank-wide AWS landing zone and multi-account governance.
  • AWS landing zone n. AWS 落地区 A multi-account AWS setup with baseline security, networking, and account vending.
  • CAB / eCAB n. 变更评审委员会 / 紧急变更评审 Change Advisory Board and emergency CAB — the gates an ADR must pass before release.
  • ADR (Architecture Decision Record) n. 架构决策记录 A short, immutable document capturing one decision and its rationale (e.g., ADR-0902 = Kafka as the group event bus).

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Layer one / two / three" — labelled lists for systems

When describing a system landscape, use labelled layers rather than numbered bullet points. Layer one — the customer-facing channels is easier to refer back to than the first category. The label layer one stays in the listener's head; the first category doesn't. Use this pattern in any whiteboarding session — it makes the picture easy to copy off the whiteboard later.

描述系统全景用 "Layer one / two / three" 标签化分层的形式——比"第一类"更易记忆和回指。

② "Don't re-litigate" — the negative imperative for settled decisions

When a decision is already signed (an ADR, a Group Standard, a regulator's circular), use the verb re-litigate with a negative imperative: don't re-litigate Kafka as the group event bus. The verb is borrowed from legal language ("re-open a closed case") and signals politely that the decision is final. Pair it with the ADR number so the listener knows exactly where the decision lives.

用 don't re-litigate + ADR 编号来礼貌地叫停翻案讨论。

③ "The default" — naming the architecture choice

Use the default to name the architecture's standing choice: synchronous REST over mTLS is the default for customer-facing real-time. The word default carries weight: it says "you don't have to choose this; you have to justify not choosing it". When a Tech Lead proposes a new integration pattern, the architect's first question is "what's wrong with the default?".

用 the default 命名架构选择——意味着"你不必选它，但你要解释为什么不选它"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Draw the picture in three layers. Customer-facing channels on top, core banking and ledger in the middle, cross-cutting platforms (Cyber, Resilience, Data, Cloud) on the bottom. New joiners, new architects, new CTOs — everyone gets the same three-layer picture first.
  • Always name the business scope. GLCM is CMB-and-GPB-only. OnePay is GPB-and-RBWM. HSBCnet is RBWM-and-CMB. The moment someone proposes a cross-scope integration, you can point to the scope and ask "is this in scope?".
  • Three integration patterns, in priority order. Synchronous REST over mTLS first (real-time customer-facing). Kafka events second (state changes). T+1 SFTP batch third (regulatory and reconciliation). 80% of new integrations use pattern one. Reach for pattern two only when you have a true state change. Pattern three is for regulators and correspondents.
  • Some decisions are "no re-litigate". Kafka, HUB, AWS, SDL — these are settled. If a Tech Lead proposes replacing any of them, your first move is to cite the ADR, not to debate. Re-litigation costs weeks; the ADR cost the bank months.
  • Cross-region etiquette: the 60-min whiteboard. A new Chief Architect from another region is a 60-min whiteboard session, not a 6-hour deck. Whiteboard beats slides for systems maps. Save the deck for the chapter review.
  • Use physical metaphors. "Digital front door", "core ledger", "cross-cutting platform", "landing zone" — physical metaphors make the picture stick. New joiners remember front door when they forget customer-facing channel.
  • Cite the ADR number, not the prose. "ADR-0902" beats "the Kafka decision" beats "the event-bus standardisation". Numbers are the only thing in a system map that don't move when the prose does.
  • Don't redraw the picture; reuse it. The HSBC Group Architecture Map v9.2 is in Confluence. Reuse the canonical picture, don't redraw it in your slide. The map is the bank's shared language; redrawing it makes yours different and harder to align.

Exercise 2 — Spot the integration-pattern error

A junior architect has proposed the following integrations in ADR-0210. Which integration-pattern choice is wrong? Why?

  1. "HSBCnet will call OneWealth over a synchronous REST API with mTLS to fetch the customer's portfolio."
  2. "OneWealth will publish a 'portfolio-rebalanced' event on Kafka, and HUB will consume it to update positions."
  3. "The GPB squad will call HUB via Kafka for a real-time balance inquiry."
  4. "HUB will drop a T+1 ledger file to the correspondent bank over SFTP for reconciliation."

① is correct — real-time, customer-facing, REST+mTLS. ② is correct — state change, event-driven on Kafka. ③ is wrong — a balance inquiry is a query, not a state change. Real-time queries should be synchronous REST, not Kafka. Kafka is for state changes (events that downstream systems need to react to), not for read-your-own-writes reads. ④ is correct — T+1 regulatory/reconciliation flow over SFTP is the right pattern. The error is ③: the architect has confused "real-time" with "use Kafka" — real-time read traffic still belongs on REST, not on the event bus.

You have completed Module 5: HSBC Banking Context across three articles. The aim of this module is to make the HSBC banking landscape — regulators, terminology, and systems — feel as natural as your code editor. The patterns you have built are the ones you will reach for in your next CAB, your next chapter review, and your next conversation with a regulator's office.

What you should now be able to do

  • Article 18 — Compliance & InfoSec Architecture. Speak the regulator's language. Cite Group Standards by code (GCS-PCI-04, GCS-GDPR-12, GCS-TRM-08). Draw a PCI-DSS scope diagram, and know the SDL gate is non-negotiable at every design review.
  • Article 19 — Banking Architecture Terminology. Define KYC, AML, sanctions screening, real-time, batch, idempotency, and reconciliation. Spot the two terms architects get wrong — idempotency is a guarantee, not a hope; reconciliation needs an external anchor, not just an internal check.
  • Article 20 — HSBC Systems Architecture & Integration. Draw the three-layer picture (channels / core / cross-cutting). Name the three integration patterns in priority order (REST+mTLS, Kafka, T+1 SFTP). Cite the four "no re-litigate" decisions (Kafka, HUB, AWS, SDL) by their ADR numbers.

The three signature phrases to keep using

  • "Honest answer —" — the softener for a sensitive or contrarian point. Use it before pushing back on a CTO, surfacing a regulatory risk, or admitting a design gap.
  • "I recommend…" — the architect's signature verb. Use it in every ADR, every design review, every chapter presentation. It signals an evaluation has been done and a position has been taken.
  • "Don't re-litigate…" — the polite shut-down for a settled decision. Pair it with an ADR number and a one-line rationale. Save it for the four "no-go" zones (Kafka, HUB, AWS, SDL).
