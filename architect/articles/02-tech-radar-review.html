Tech Radar Review

Article 02 · Module 2: Evaluating Emerging Technology · Architect English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Adopt / Trial / Assess / Hold — discussing new tech for the bank

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday afternoon, 14:00 HKT. The quarterly Architecture Tech Radar session is running on Microsoft Teams. Margaret, your Chief Architect, chairs the meeting. Today the radar committee is reviewing six technologies that squads have proposed over the last quarter: OpenTelemetry, Apache Flink, HashiCorp Vault, Service Mesh with Istio Ambient, pgvector, and Databricks Lakehouse for the OneWealth analytics platform.

周二下午 2 点香港时间，季度架构技术雷达评审在 Teams 上进行。Chief Architect Margaret 主持。今天雷达委员会要评审本季度 squad 提的 6 项技术：OpenTelemetry、 Apache Flink、HashiCorp Vault、Istio Ambient 服务网格、 pgvector，以及用于 OneWealth 分析平台的 Databricks Lakehouse。

Each technology gets a verdict — Adopt, Trial, Assess, or Hold. The verdict lands in the public HSBC Tech Radar on Confluence and shapes which technologies the squads can use. Diego joins from the UK to represent the European regulatory view, and Priya, a peer architect from Bangalore, joins to advocate for the Flink stream-processing proposal her RBWM fraud-detection squad wants to trial.

每项技术都有一个判定——Adopt、Trial、 Assess、Hold。判定结果会落到 Confluence 的 HSBC 公共 Tech Radar 上，决定 squad 能用哪些技术。Diego 从英国加入，代表欧洲监管视角；班加罗尔的 同侪架构师 Priya 加入，她要为她的 RBWM 反欺诈 squad 提议试用的 Flink 流处理站台。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Margaret (Chief Architect): Right, everyone — thanks for dialling in. We've got six blips on the radar today. Let's keep it tight: proposer presents for three minutes, then the committee pokes holes. OK, Priya — you're up first with Apache Flink.好的，谢谢大家上线。今天雷达上有 6 项，按节奏来：提议人 3 分钟展示，委员会挑刺。Priya，你先上 Flink。
Priya (Architect, Bangalore): Thanks, Margaret. So, honest answer — we need Flink for the RBWM fraud-detection pipeline. Today we're on batch Spark jobs every 15 minutes, and the business wants sub-second decisioning on card-not-present transactions. Flink gives us true streaming with exactly-once semantics, and it's group-standard aligned because it runs on the Group Kafka platform.谢谢 Margaret。说实话，RBWM 反欺诈需要 Flink。我们现在每 15 分钟跑一次 Spark 批处理，业务要卡不在场交易亚秒级判定。Flink 能给我们真正的流处理和精确一次语义，而且跑在 Group Kafka 平台上是符合集团标准的。
Diego (Architect, UK): Quick one, Priya — what's the operational burden? The UK fraud team burnt a quarter standing up Flink last year and rolled back to Spark Structured Streaming. Are we doubling down on someone else's mistake?问一句，Priya——运维成本怎么样？英国反欺诈团队去年花了 1 个季度搭 Flink，又回滚到了 Spark Structured Streaming。我们要重复别人的坑吗？
Priya (Architect, Bangalore): Good pushback. Honest answer — we learnt from the UK post-mortem. The rollback was caused by state-backend tuning on RocksDB, not Flink itself. We've already engaged the SRE chapter in Bangalore and they've given us a reference runbook. We're proposing Trial, not Adopt — one squad, one use case, three months, with a clear go/no-go gate at the end of Q1.好问题。说实话，我们吸取了英国复盘的教训。回滚是 RocksDB state 后端调参 引起的，不是 Flink 本身的问题。我们已经请了班加罗尔的 SRE 章节，他们给了我们一份参考 runbook。我们提议 Trial，不是 Adopt——一个 squad、一个用例、3 个月，Q1 末有明确的 go/no-go 关卡。
Margaret (Chief Architect): OK, I'm sympathetic to Trial — but the Group Cyber caveat is non-negotiable. Flink jobs process card data, so we need an InfoSec design review before the trial spins up. Wei, can you own that handoff?我倾向 Trial，但 Group Cyber 的附加条件 不能省。Flink 作业处理卡数据，所以试用启动前要过 InfoSec 设计评审。Wei，这个交接你负责一下？
You (Architect): Yep, happy to. I'll book the InfoSec design review for week 2 of the trial and add it as a precondition to the Trial blip on the radar. Priya, can you drop me a one-pager on the data flows by Friday?没问题，我来。我会把 InfoSec 设计评审安排在试用第 2 周，并把它作为雷达 Trial 项的前提条件。Priya，周五前能给我一份数据流一页纸吗？
Priya (Architect, Bangalore): Will do. And just to flag — the Group Resilience team has already signed off on the DR posture for the trial cluster, so that part is done.没问题，提一句——Group Resilience 团队已经签了试用集群的 DR 姿态，这部分已经完成。
Margaret (Chief Architect): Lovely. Right, next on the list is pgvector — Wei, you wanted to pitch this for the OneWealth semantic-search use case, right?好的。下一项是 pgvector——Wei，OneWealth 语义搜索的用例是你要提的对吧？
You (Architect): That's right. Honest answer — pgvector is convenient, but for OneWealth we should Hold on pgvector and Assess on a dedicated vector database. The semantic-search workload will outgrow pgvector's recall and operational maturity by mid-2027, and we'd be doing a re-platform in 18 months. My recommendation is to Assess on Pinecone or Weaviate, and revisit in two quarters.对。说实话，pgvector 用起来很方便，但 OneWealth 场景我建议 Hold pgvector，Assess 专用向量数据库。语义搜索的负载到 2027 年中会超过 pgvector 的召回和运维成熟度，等于 18 个月后要重新选型。我建议 Assess Pinecone 或 Weaviate，两个季度后再评审。
Margaret (Chief Architect): Agreed, that's a clean Hold. Let's move on. Diego, are you good to write up the verdicts on Confluence by end of week?同意，这是一个干净的 Hold。下一项。Diego，这周内能把判定结果写到 Confluence 上吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Honest answer — we need Flink for the fraud pipeline. 说实话，反欺诈管线需要 Flink "Honest answer" = a softener that signals "I'm about to make a strong claim". Architects use it before stating a real need.
  • Open What six blips are on the radar today? 今天雷达上有哪 6 项？ "Blip" is the canonical Tech Radar term for one technology entry. A blip moves between quadrants (Adopt / Trial / Assess / Hold).
  • Pushback Good pushback — we learnt from the UK post-mortem. 好问题——我们吸取了英国复盘的教训 "Good pushback" is the politeness move that turns a critique into a compliment. Always acknowledge the challenge before answering it.
  • Verdict We're proposing Trial, not Adopt — one squad, one use case. 我们提议 Trial，不是 Adopt——一个 squad、一个用例 Always scope your verdict: who, what, how long, and the go/no-go gate. An unscoped verdict is a recipe for scope creep.
  • Caveat The Group Cyber caveat is non-negotiable. Group Cyber 的附加条件不能省 "Non-negotiable" = hard, no discussion. Group Cyber, Group Resilience, SDL — these are the pillars you don't push back on.
  • Handoff Can you own that handoff? 这个交接你负责一下？ A "handoff" is an explicit transfer of an action from one person to another. Naming it avoids the "I thought you were doing it" trap.
  • Precondition Add it as a precondition to the Trial blip. 把它作为 Trial 项的前提条件 "Precondition" = "this must happen before the blip is consumed". Use it to attach InfoSec / Group Cyber gates to a Trial.
  • Soft Just to flag — the Group Resilience team has signed off. 提一句——Group Resilience 团队已经签了 "Just to flag" = the politeness device for raising a side issue without derailing the main discussion. Same as "flagging" in Article 01.
  • Verdict That's a clean Hold. 这是一个干净的 Hold "Clean" = unambiguous, no caveats. Holds are usually clean; Trials and Adopts often come with preconditions.
  • Recommend My recommendation is to Assess on Pinecone or Weaviate. 我建议 Assess Pinecone 或 Weaviate Always name 2-3 options. "Assess on X or Y" is better than "Assess on X" because it shows you considered the market.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Tech Radar n. 技术雷达 A public, time-boxed list of recommended technologies organised into four quadrants.
  • Adopt v. 采用 The technology is recommended for new projects. Group-supported, with runbooks and reference implementations.
  • Trial v. 试用 The technology is approved for a bounded use case. Requires a go/no-go gate.
  • Assess v. 评估 The technology is worth a small proof-of-concept. No production use yet.
  • Hold v. 暂缓 The technology is not recommended. Existing use is tolerated, but new use requires an exception.
  • Blip n. 雷达点 A single technology entry on the radar, with a name, quadrant, and a one-line rationale.
  • Exactly-once semantics n. 精确一次语义 A streaming guarantee that each event is processed exactly once, even on failure.
  • State backend n. 状态后端 The storage layer that holds a stream processor's in-flight state (e.g., RocksDB).
  • Post-mortem n. 复盘 A blameless write-up of an incident, focusing on root cause and learning.
  • Go/no-go gate n. go/no-go 关卡 A formal decision point at the end of a Trial where the committee decides whether to move the blip to Adopt.
  • Reference runbook n. 参考 runbook A documented operating procedure that has been used successfully in another team.
  • Re-platform v. 重新选型 Migrating a workload to a different technology, usually because the original choice no longer scales.
  • Quadrant n. 象限 One of the four radar categories (Adopt, Trial, Assess, Hold).

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Honest answer" / "Honest take" — the soft strong claim

Honest answer — we need Flink is a way of saying "I'm about to state a need that might be controversial, but I want you to hear it straight". It's softer than we clearly need Flink and softer than we must have Flink. The format is Honest [noun] — [claim]. Use it before stating a position you've already taken internally.

"Honest answer" 是"我要说一个可能有点争议的需求，但我想直说"的礼貌化表达，比"we clearly need"或"we must have"都更柔和。

② "Proposing Trial, not Adopt" — the architect's scope setter

When you state a verdict, always include the scope: one squad, one use case, three months. The pattern is proposing [verdict], not [broader verdict] — [scope]. This signals that you've thought about risk and you've bounded the blast radius. The committee can then accept the verdict without worrying about cross-tribe spread.

陈述判定时一定要带范围："proposing Trial, not Adopt — [scope]"。这表示你已经评估过风险并限制了爆炸半径。

③ "The X caveat is non-negotiable" — the Group Standards shield

The Group Cyber caveat is non-negotiable is a grammatical way to insulate a decision from being revisited. The structure is the [Group Standard] [noun] is non-negotiable. Reserve "non-negotiable" for things that are hard, mandatory, regulator-driven. Don't use it for preferences — that dilutes it.

"The [集团标准] caveat is non-negotiable" 是把一个决定锁起来不被反复挑战的语法结构。只对监管驱动的硬性条款用"non-negotiable"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Tech Radar verdicts are public and durable. A "Hold" lands in Confluence and stays there. If you put a Hold on something a squad is using, expect a 1:1 explaining the migration path. Don't drop Holds without a conversation.
  • Always include a go/no-go gate for Trial. An unscoped Trial is a slow Adopt by stealth. A go/no-go gate is the committee's lever: it forces a decision at the end of the bounded period.
  • Group Standards (Cyber, Resilience, SDL) override local preferences. If Flink processes cardholder data, the InfoSec design review is a precondition, not a "nice to have". Cite the Group Standard by name when you set a precondition.
  • "Honest answer" is a politeness device, not an apology. Native speakers use it to introduce a strong claim. It signals "I respect the committee enough to be direct". Don't be afraid to use it.
  • Cross-region learn-first, copy-second. When Diego flags that the UK team "burnt a quarter" on Flink, Priya's answer must be "we learnt from the post-mortem" — not "yes but we're different". Always reference the post-mortem explicitly.
  • Bangalore, Shanghai, HK, UK voice etiquette. In Tech Radar meetings, everyone gets a clean turn. Don't talk over Priya. Wait for Margaret to call on you. And if you're pitching something, the first 30 seconds of your pitch is the "claim" — state it cleanly.
  • "Recommend" still wins. "I'm Assess on Pinecone" is weaker than "my recommendation is to Assess on Pinecone or Weaviate". The recommendation verb forces you to take a position. Don't hide behind "we could look at".
  • Write up the verdicts the same day. If Diego says "I'll write up the verdicts by end of week", that's a soft commitment. Verbal verdicts don't exist in HSBC's governance — the Confluence page is the record.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the Tech Radar pitch

Translate the following Tech Radar proposal into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the architect scopes the verdict and the precondition.

"Honest answer — we need to Trial Istio Ambient for the GPB API mesh. The classic sidecar pattern is eating our CPU budget; Ambient cuts sidecar overhead by about 60%. We're proposing Trial, not Adopt — one squad (GPB Markets), one cluster, two months, with a go/no-go gate at the end of Q2. Precondition: Group Cyber sign-off on the mTLS posture before we even spin up the trial. We learnt from the Wealth squad's earlier Consul attempt — they skipped the security review and rolled back after six weeks."

"说实话，我们要在 GPB API mesh 上 Trial Istio Ambient。传统 sidecar 模式吃 CPU 太多，Ambient 能把 sidecar 开销砍掉大约 60%。我们提议 Trial，不是 Adopt——一个 squad（GPB Markets）、一个集群、2 个月，Q2 末有 go/no-go 关卡。前提条件：试用启动前要有 Group Cyber 对 mTLS 姿态的签批。我们吸取了 Wealth squad 之前 Consul 试点的教训——他们当时跳过了安全评审，6 周后回滚了。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the radar mistake

Which of these Tech Radar proposals is most aligned with HSBC architecture culture? Why?

  1. "We should adopt Apache Flink because it's fast and modern."
  2. "Honest answer — we need Flink for sub-second card-not-present decisioning. Proposing Trial, not Adopt — one squad, one use case, three months, with a go/no-go gate. Precondition: InfoSec design review."
  3. "Flink is the future of streaming. Everyone should use it. The UK team is wrong about RocksDB."
  4. "My recommendation is to Assess on Apache Flink and to revisit Spark Structured Streaming as the fallback. We learnt from the UK post-mortem that state-backend tuning is the real risk."

② and ④ are HSBC-aligned: scoped verdict (Trial/Assess with squad + duration + gate), precondition, and a cross-region learning reference. ① is a "we should" (architect anti-pattern). ③ dismisses the UK post-mortem, which violates cross-region etiquette. Always scope your verdict and always cite the relevant Group Standard or post-mortem.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're a Solution Architect in the CMB Digital tribe. The Onboarding squad has proposed adopting HashiCorp Vault for secrets management, replacing the legacy CyberArk integration. The squad's argument: Vault has dynamic secrets, supports AWS KMS auto-unseal, and reduces secret-rotation toil by 80%. Concerns from InfoSec: the on-call rotation runbook isn't written yet, and CyberArk is the current Group Standard for tier-1 systems. Write your 60-second Tech Radar pitch in English. Choose a verdict (Adopt / Trial / Assess / Hold), scope it, and cite at least one Group Standard or post-mortem. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Honest answer — the Onboarding squad's case for Vault is strong, but I'm proposing Trial, not Adopt. The dynamic-secrets and auto-unseal story is real, and the 80% toil reduction is hard to argue with. However, the on-call runbook isn't written yet, and InfoSec has flagged that CyberArk is the current Group Standard for tier-1 systems. We're proposing Trial, not Adopt — one squad, one workload (the Onboarding KYC API), three months, with a go/no-go gate at the end of Q3. Precondition: an InfoSec design review and a published runbook before the trial spins up. We learnt from the RBWM CyberArk migration in 2024 — they skipped the runbook phase and paid for it during a 3 AM rotation. Margaret, can the chapter agree to Trial with those preconditions?"
