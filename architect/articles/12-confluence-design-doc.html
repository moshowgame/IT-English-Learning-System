Confluence Design Doc Review

Article 12 · Module 4: Async Architecture Communication · Architect English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Async review etiquette: structured comments, decisions captured in line, polite disagreement

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday morning, 9:00 AM HKT. A Tech Lead in the OnePay squad has just published a 14-page design document on Confluence — "OnePay Idempotency & Retry Strategy v3" — linked from ADR-0158. You are the Solution Architect covering OnePay, and your review is one of three peer reviews before the document goes to chapter sign-off on Friday.

周二上午 9 点香港时间，OnePay squad 的 Tech Lead 在 Confluence 上发布了一份 14 页 的设计文档 "OnePay 幂等与重试策略 v3"，挂在 ADR-0158 下面。 你是覆盖 OnePay 的 Solution 架构师，评审是文档送到 chapter 签批前的 3 个同侪评审之一。

The Tech Lead, Rohit (Bangalore), has explicitly asked for review by Thursday EOD IST. Because Margaret, your Chief Architect, is at a Group Architects' offsite in London this week, she has asked you to consolidate the comments and surface the key trade-offs to her in a single Slack DM on Thursday evening. Two other reviewers — Sofia (peer architect, HK) and Daniel (InfoSec, UK) — will also leave inline comments on the same page.

Tech Lead Rohit（班加罗尔）明确要求 周四 EOD 印度时间前完成评审。 Chief Architect Margaret 本周在伦敦参加集团架构师 offsite，所以她请你把评审意见 汇总，周四晚上通过一条 Slack DM 把关键 trade-off 给她。两位同侪评审—— Sofia（同侪架构师，香港）和 Daniel（InfoSec，英国）—— 也会在同一个页面留下行内评论。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (Architect): Thanks, Rohit — picked it up. I'll read end-to-end and leave inline comments on the page rather than a separate doc — that way Sofia and Daniel can see my thinking when they review.Rohit 收到。我会通读一遍，直接在页面行内评论，不开新文档——这样 Sofia 和 Daniel 评审时能看到我的想法。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Async Could you take a look by Thursday EOD? 能在周四 EOD 前看一下吗？ Always set a deadline in async reviews. "Take a look" is vague; "by Thursday EOD" is a commitment.
  • Inline I'll leave inline comments on the page rather than a separate doc. 我直接在页面行内评论，不开新文档 Inline comments are visible to all reviewers. Separate docs create review islands — avoid them.
  • Deviate Was that considered, or is there a reason we are deliberately deviating? 这个选项考虑过吗？还是有什么原因要主动偏离？ "Deliberately deviating" is a politeness pattern. It signals "you may have a reason" instead of "you broke the rule".
  • Blocker I'm not saying no — if the trade-off holds, that's a valid call. 我不是说不行——如果 trade-off 站得住，这也是合理选择 Separate the decision from the process. You can approve the choice but require the deviation paperwork.
  • Quantify The DLQ design is not quantified. What is the retention and replay policy? DLQ 设计没量化。保留和重放策略是什么？ "Not quantified" = no numbers. Always push for retention, replay window, ownership, alert thresholds.
  • Hard The 72-hour TTL is a hard requirement from Group Cyber. 72 小时 TTL 是 Group Cyber 的硬性要求 Use "hard requirement" for Group Standards, MAS TRM, HKMA, PCI-DSS. "Soft" for things you can debate.
  • Reference Add the SDL threat model reference inline, not in a footnote. 把 SDL 威胁模型引用放在正文里，不要放脚注 Footnotes are skipped. Inline references force the reader to confront the constraint.
  • Consolidate I'll consolidate all the open items in a comment at the top. 我会把所有待办汇总到顶部的一条评论 The "consolidation comment" is an architect's signature move. It transforms scattered threads into a single sign-off list.
  • Honest Honest answer — we benchmarked both and Redis came in 30% cheaper. 坦白说——我们两个都做了基准测试，Redis 便宜 30% "Honest answer" invites candour. It signals "I want the truth, not the polished version".
  • Loop Quick Slack ping once you've consolidated — I want to make sure nothing falls through the cracks. 你汇总完之后 Slack 戳我一下——我想确保没遗漏 "Quick Slack ping" closes the loop. Async reviewers must explicitly hand back to the author before sign-off.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Design document (design doc) n. 设计文档 A long-form technical document, usually 5–20 pages, capturing a proposed design end-to-end.
  • Inline comment n. 行内评论 A comment anchored to a specific paragraph, table, or diagram in a document — not a separate email.
  • Peer review n. 同侪评审 Review by a person of equal seniority (vs. sign-off, which is a hierarchical approval).
  • Chapter sign-off n. Chapter 签批 Formal approval by the Architecture chapter after peer review passes.
  • Deviation request n. 偏离申请 A formal document requesting to depart from a Group Standard. Always needs InfoSec + chapter approval.
  • Dead-letter queue (DLQ) n. 死信队列 A holding area for messages that failed processing — needs explicit retention and replay policy.
  • At-least-once delivery n. 至少一次投递 A delivery guarantee where a message may be delivered more than once — requires idempotency on the consumer side.
  • Idempotency key n. 幂等键 A unique key (often UUID v7) attached to a request to ensure duplicate processing produces the same result.
  • Dedup store n. 去重存储 A persistence layer (Redis, DynamoDB) that remembers seen idempotency keys within a TTL window.
  • SDL (Secure Development Lifecycle) threat model n. SDL 威胁模型 A structured analysis of security threats, required for any change touching customer data.
  • Group Resilience n. 集团韧性 HSBC's function that defines recovery time objectives (RTO), replay windows, and DR drill cadence.
  • Group Cyber n. 集团网络安全 HSBC's function that owns InfoSec standards — including TTL, encryption, key rotation.
  • Sanctions screening n. 制裁名单筛查 Real-time check against OFAC, UN, EU, HMT sanctions lists — TTL must cover retry window.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Was that considered, or is there a reason we are deliberately deviating?"

The deliberately adverb + deviating present participle is the architect's politeness pattern when challenging a choice. Compare: "Why didn't you use X?" (accusatory) vs. "Was that considered, or is there a reason we are deliberately deviating?" (collaborative). The first assumes negligence; the second assumes intentionality and invites the rationale.

Deliberately + -ing 是架构师提出质疑的礼貌句式。先假设对方有意，再请其说明理由。

② "I'm not saying no — if the trade-off holds, that's a valid call."

The pattern I'm not saying no / I'm not saying X separates the decision from the process. It signals: I am not blocking the technical choice; I am requiring the deviation paperwork, sign-off, or quantification. Use it whenever a Tech Lead hears "no" when you actually mean "yes, with conditions".

"I'm not saying no" 把"技术选择"和"流程要求"分开。你不是拒绝方案，而是要流程/签批/量化。

③ The consolidation comment — third-person summary in the present tense

When you consolidate review comments, write in the present tense and third person so the author can copy-paste the summary into the ADR: "Section 3.4 deviates from G-SDL-2024-09. Deviation request required before sign-off." Avoid "I think you should…" — it reads as opinion. The present-tense third-person summary reads as fact and is harder to argue with.

汇总评审意见时用现在时 + 第三人称，方便作者直接 copy 到 ADR 里。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Async review is the default at HSBC, not a fallback. Margaret and Daniel being in different time zones (HK / UK) makes async the only practical mode. Default to Confluence + Slack. Use live meetings only for unresolved disagreement.
  • Inline comments, not email threads. Email is a private review island. Inline comments let Sofia and Daniel see the thread and avoid the "Daniel disagreed in email but Wei didn't know" failure mode.
  • Quantify or it didn't happen. "Robust", "scalable", "secure" are not NFRs. Numbers are NFRs. Always push: retention, replay window, TTL, RPS, p99 latency, error budget.
  • Deviation ≠ exception. Deviation = documented decision. When a Tech Lead proposes something off the Group Standard path, do not block — require the deviation paperwork. The bank expects deviations; it does not expect undocumented ones.
  • Cross-region etiquette — IST vs HKT vs GMT. Rohit thinks in IST, you in HKT, Daniel in GMT. Always quote the deadline in two time zones: "Thursday EOD IST / 9 PM HKT". Avoid abbreviations that mean different things (HKT vs HKC).
  • "Honest answer" creates psychological safety. It signals you want the truth, not the polished answer. Rohit admitted the deviation wasn't formally raised — that admission only came because you invited candour.
  • The consolidation comment is a deliverable. Pin it at the top of the doc. Tag each item with the owner. Treat it as a living checklist, not a one-off email.
  • Loop the author back in. Async reviews fail when the reviewer disappears. End every thread with "Quick Slack ping once you've updated" — close the loop explicitly.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the async review

Translate the following async review message into natural Chinese. Notice how the architect challenges a deviation without sounding accusatory.

"Hi Priya — picked up the v2 design doc for the HSBCnet payment callback flow. Section 4.1 proposes storing the callback payload in PostgreSQL for 7 days. Was that considered against Group Standard G-DR-2023-11, which recommends S3 with 30-day retention for audit replay? I'm not saying no to PostgreSQL — if the squad has a concrete reason (e.g. cost, query patterns), we can raise a deviation. But I want it documented before the chapter review. Could you either align with the Group Standard or submit the deviation request by Wednesday EOD HKT?"

"Priya 你好——刚看到 HSBCnet 支付回调流程的 v2 设计文档。4.1 节提了把回调 payload 存到 PostgreSQL 保留 7 天。和集团标准 G-DR-2023-11 比对过吗？那份标准建议用 S3 保留 30 天用于审计重放。我不是说不能用 PostgreSQL——如果 squad 有具体理由（比如成本、查询模式），我们可以提偏离申请。但我希望在 chapter 评审前把它落到纸面上。麻烦在香港时间周三 EOD前，要么对齐集团标准，要么把偏离申请提交上来。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the difference: async vs live review

Which of these is the right pattern for an async Confluence review, and which would only work in a live meeting? Why?

  1. "This is wrong. Use Redis, not DynamoDB."
  2. "Section 3.4 deviates from G-SDL-2024-09. Could the squad share the benchmark that drove the choice, or raise a formal deviation request before chapter sign-off?"
  3. "We need to talk about this. Let's book 30 min."
  4. "Daniel raised a hard requirement (sanctions-screening TTL = 72h). Section 2.1 currently says 24h. Suggest updating to 72h and citing the OnePay InfoSec standard inline."

② and ④ are right for async. ② cites the Group Standard by name, names the section, and proposes a concrete next step. ④ attributes the source, quotes the requirement, and suggests an edit. ① is too blunt and offers no rationale. ③ is fine in a live meeting but useless in async — it demands a calendar action without context.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You are the Solution Architect reviewing the GPB Onboarding squad's v1 design doc for an event-driven customer-duplicate-check flow. The doc proposes: (a) Kafka for the event bus, (b) a DynamoDB dedup table, (c) a 7-day retention on the dedup table. You noticed Group Standard G-DR-2024-02 requires 30-day retention on dedup tables for cross-region replay, and InfoSec requires the SDL threat model to be referenced inline in section 5, not as a footnote. Write a single async review comment that: (1) thanks the Tech Lead, (2) raises the retention deviation, (3) raises the SDL reference placement, and (4) sets a deadline. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Hi Anil — thanks for getting the v1 design doc out. Two comments from my side, both leave the technical choice open but require the process to be followed. First, section 4.3 proposes 7-day retention on the dedup table. Group Standard G-DR-2024-02 requires 30-day retention for cross-region replay. Was that benchmarked, or is there a reason we are deliberately deviating? I'm not saying no to 7 days — if there's a concrete cost or query-pattern reason, raise a deviation request and we'll take it to InfoSec. Second, section 5.1 references the SDL threat model only in a footnote. InfoSec has asked us to move that reference inline — footnotes get skipped, inline references force the reader to confront the threat surface. Could you update the doc and Slack me by Thursday EOD HKT? I'll consolidate any other open items in a comment at the top of the doc by Friday noon HKT so we can chase chapter sign-off Monday."
