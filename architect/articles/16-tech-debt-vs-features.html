Negotiating Tech Debt vs New Features

Article 16 · Module 5: Difficult Conversations · Architect English

⏱ 10 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎯 Convince PM and business to fund a tech-debt sprint — make the cost of inaction visible

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday, 11:00 AM HKT. Quarterly planning is next week, and the RBWM Digital tribe is allocating ~85% of next quarter's squad capacity to new features (KYC refresh, spending insights, OnePay fraud signals) and ~15% to tech debt. The 15% bucket is not ringfenced — the tribe lead has informally said that any tech-debt item that "doesn't tie to a customer outcome" will get deprioritised.

周二上午 11 点香港时间，季度规划下周进行。RBWM 数字化 tribe 把下季度 ~85% 的 squad 容量分给新功能（KYC 刷新、消费洞察、OnePay 欺诈信号）， ~15% 给技术债。这 15% 没有专门圈出来——tribe lead 非正式地说过， 任何"不挂钩客户产出"的技术债项会被降级。

You are the Solution Architect covering RBWM. The technical reality is ugly: the OneWealth portfolio-rebalancing service is on a Java 8 monolith, deploys take 45 minutes, p99 latency is ~3.2 seconds (SLA is 800ms), and the last 3 production incidents in 6 months all traced back to that service. If the team takes no tech-debt sprint, the RBWM tribe will miss its Q4 customer-experience targets — not because the features aren't shipping, but because the platform underneath them is collapsing.

你是覆盖 RBWM 的 Solution 架构师。技术现实很糟糕：OneWealth 投资组合再平衡服务 跑在 Java 8 monolith 上，部署要 45 分钟，p99 延迟 ~3.2 秒（SLA 是 800ms）， 过去 6 个月的 3 起生产事件全部溯源到这个服务。如果团队不做一个技术债 Sprint， RBWM tribe 一定完不成 Q4 客户体验目标——不是因为功能没上线，而是因为底座在塌。

I know that doesn't sound like a customer outcome. 3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open I want to be upfront — it's a tech-debt sprint. 先说清楚——是一个技术债 Sprint "Be upfront" is the politeness technology for naming the elephant in the room. Don't pretend it's a feature; name the ask, then make the case.
  • Outcome Stay with me for 2 minutes and I'll show you the customer outcome. 给我 2 分钟，我给你看客户产出 PMs and tribe leads live in "customer outcome". Re-route your tech-debt argument through that lens, not the "clean code" lens.
  • Quant Three numbers I want to put on the table. 三个数字先放桌上 Always lead with numbers. PMs will listen to numbers; they will not listen to "the code is bad".
  • SLA p99 latency of 3.2 seconds — SLA is 800ms. 4× over SLA. p99 延迟 3.2 秒——SLA 是 800ms。超 SLA 4 倍 The "X× over SLA" framing is the strongest tech-debt argument. SLA is a contract; being over SLA is a contract violation.
  • Blocker We ship features 3× slower than the rest of the tribe. 我们交付功能比 tribe 其它 squad 慢 3 倍 "Velocity drag" is the PM's word. Tech debt is not abstract — it shows up as features-not-shipped.
  • Reframe Let me reframe. The customer sees a 3-second spinner 3 times out of 10. 我重新组织一下。客户看到的是投资组合页面上 3 秒的转圈，10 次里有 3 次 Translate the p99 number into a customer-visible behaviour. "3-second spinner, 3 in 10" lands. "p99 = 3.2s" doesn't.
  • Caution That's a ribbon-cutting risk for HKMA's next tech-risk review. 那是 HKMA 下次技术风险审查的切开风险 "Ribbon-cutting risk" = a regulator formally opening an issue. Mentioning HKMA / MAS / FCA immediately elevates the conversation above "tech debt".
  • Scope I'm not asking for the full rewrite. I'm asking for a 2-sprint, 1-squad tech-debt sprint. 我不要完全重写。我要的是 2 个 Sprint、1 个 squad 的技术债 Sprint Always scope your ask tightly. "Full rewrite" loses; "1 squad × 2 sprints" is something a PM can defend in planning.
  • Deal Here's the deal I'd offer the tribe — Q4 investment that buys Q1 velocity. 我可以和 tribe 谈的条件——Q4 的投资买 Q1 的速度 Frame the tech-debt sprint as an investment with measurable return. "Velocity", "features", "shipped" are PM words.
  • Re-evaluate If p99 doesn't drop below 1s by end of sprint 2, we stop and re-evaluate. 如果 Sprint 2 结束时 p99 没降到 1 秒以下，我们就停并重新评估 A clear go/no-go gate protects both sides. The PM gets a defined exit; the architect gets the runway to deliver.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Tech debt (technical debt) n. 技术债 The accumulated cost of shortcuts in the codebase that slow future delivery.
  • Tech-debt sprint n. 技术债 Sprint A dedicated sprint (or set of sprints) where a squad pays down technical debt instead of shipping features.
  • Ringfenced capacity n. 圈定容量 Capacity that is explicitly reserved for a purpose (e.g., tech debt) and cannot be reallocated to features mid-quarter.
  • Customer outcome n. 客户产出 A measurable change in customer behaviour, satisfaction, or value — the lens PMs and tribe leads use to evaluate all work.
  • p99 latency n. p99 延迟 The 99th-percentile latency — the slowest 1% of requests. The standard measure for customer-visible slowness.
  • SLA (Service Level Agreement) n. 服务等级协议 The contracted maximum latency / availability. p99 over SLA is a contract violation.
  • Velocity drag n. 速度拖累 The phenomenon where tech debt slows feature delivery — the PM's reason to care about tech debt.
  • Feature velocity n. 功能交付速度 The rate at which a squad ships features. A successful tech-debt sprint increases future feature velocity.
  • Ribbon-cutting risk n. 切开风险 The risk that a regulator formally opens (or "ribbon-cuts") an issue at a review — usually triggered by a measurable breach + repeat incidents.
  • HKMA TM-G-1 n. 港金局 TM-G-1 Hong Kong Monetary Authority's technology risk management circular — sets expectations on customer-facing latency remediation.
  • Go/no-go gate n. 继续 / 停止关卡 A pre-agreed point where a tech-debt sprint is evaluated against metrics; the squad either continues, re-scopes, or stops.
  • Production incident n. 生产事件 A customer-facing outage or degradation. Repeat incidents on the same service are a tech-debt signal.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Stay with me for 2 minutes" — the time-boxed request

The pattern Stay with me for X minutes and I'll show you Y is the politeness-technology for asking a PM to give you their most scarce resource: attention. The grammar: imperative + time duration + promise of value. The PM grants you 2 minutes because the alternative ("let me explain…") sounds open-ended. Use this when you have a counter-intuitive ask (tech debt, no customer feature).

Stay with me for X minutes and I'll show you Y——时间盒 + 价值承诺。

② "Let me reframe" — moving from your frame to theirs

PMs have a frame: customer outcome, velocity, ship date. Architects have a frame: clean code, observability, debt. The reframe pattern explicitly acknowledges the gap and translates: "Let me reframe. The customer sees a 3-second spinner 3 times out of 10. That's the customer outcome." The grammar: imperative + translate their number into their frame. Without the reframe, the PM hears "tech debt = slow code = not my problem".

Let me reframe 显式承认双方视角不同，再把数字翻译成 PM 的语言。

③ "Q4 investment that buys Q1 velocity" — the trade-off inversion

The strongest tech-debt argument inverts the trade-off. Frame it as: spend X now, get Y back next quarter, with Y > X. The grammar: "It's a [time-period] investment that buys [future-time-period] [gain]." This is the pattern PMs understand — it's how they think about every other investment. Apply the same lens to tech debt.

"Q4 的投资买 Q1 的速度"——把技术债 Sprint 框成投资回报，PM 听得懂。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Translate tech debt into customer language. PMs don't care about clean code; they care about shipped features and customer satisfaction. The architect's first job is to translate the technical reality into PM language: customer-visible behaviour, velocity, regulatory exposure.
  • Lead with three numbers, not adjectives. "3.2s p99", "45 min deploys", "3 incidents in 6 months" — that's a story. "The service is in bad shape" is not.
  • p99 is the PM's word, not the architect's. Architects default to average latency, error rate, MTTR. PMs default to p99 because it matches the customer experience. When talking to PMs, lead with p99.
  • Mention the regulator once. Once is enough. "HKMA TM-G-1 expects remediation of customer-facing latency materially above SLA" is a single sentence. It changes the conversation from "tech debt" to "regulatory risk" without you needing to escalate.
  • Scope the ask tightly. "Full rewrite" loses. PMs can defend "1 squad × 2 sprints = 12% capacity" in planning. They cannot defend "stop the world, rewrite the platform". Always come with a scoped, time-boxed ask.
  • Offer a go/no-go gate. "If p99 doesn't drop below 1s by sprint 2, we stop" gives the PM a defined exit. They support tech debt when there's a measurable deliverable and a defined off-ramp.
  • Use the dashboard as the artefact. A single dashboard with p99, deploy time, incident count — updated weekly — is the artefact that keeps the tech-debt sprint alive. Without it, the conversation dies in week 2.
  • Frame it as investment, not cost. "Q4 investment that buys Q1 velocity" is a sentence PMs understand. "We need to fix the Java 8 monolith" is a sentence they don't. The word "investment" reframes the budget line.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the tech-debt pitch

Translate the following tech-debt pitch into natural Chinese. Notice how the architect translates technical numbers into customer language.

"Thanks, Jonathan — quick one. I want to be upfront: I'm asking for a 2-sprint tech-debt sprint on the HSBCnet payment callback service. Three numbers: p99 latency is 4.1 seconds — SLA is 1 second. 4× over SLA. Deploys take 60 minutes, which means we ship features 2× slower than the rest of the tribe. The last 4 production incidents in 8 months all traced back to this service. Let me reframe — the customer sees a 'payment submitted, please wait' spinner for 4 seconds, 1 time in 100. That's 1% of payments, which at HSBCnet volume is thousands of customers per day. My ask: 1 squad × 2 sprints. The deal: we recover ~25% feature velocity in Q1 because deploys drop to 12 min and p99 drops below 1s. It's a Q4 investment that buys Q1 velocity."

"Jonathan，谢谢——插一下会外的。我想说清楚：我要的是一个 2 Sprint 的 HSBCnet 支付回调服务技术债 Sprint。3 个数字：p99 延迟 4.1 秒——SLA 是 1 秒。超 SLA 4 倍。部署要 60 分钟，意味着我们交付功能比 tribe 其它 squad 慢 2 倍。过去 8 个月 4 起生产事件全部溯源到这个服务。让我重新组织一下——客户看到的是"支付已提交，请等待"的转圈 4 秒，100 次里有 1 次。按 HSBCnet 的量级，这是每天几千个客户。我的请求：1 个 squad × 2 个 Sprint。条件：Q1 能回收约 25% 的功能速度，因为部署降到 12 分钟、p99 降到 1 秒以下。这是一笔 Q4 的投资，买 Q1 的速度。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the difference: winning vs losing the pitch

Which of these pitches would a HSBC PM likely approve, and which would be cut? Why?

  1. "We need a tech-debt sprint to clean up the code."
  2. "Three numbers: p99 latency 3.2s vs SLA 800ms — 4× over. Deploys 45 min — 3× slower than the rest of the tribe. 3 production incidents in 6 months, all on this service. The customer sees a 3-second spinner 3 times in 10. My ask: 1 squad × 4 sprints, ~12% of Q4 capacity. The deal: Q1 we recover 30% feature velocity. Q4 investment, Q1 velocity. Go/no-go gate: if p99 doesn't drop below 1s by sprint 2, we stop."
  3. "The Java 8 monolith is hard to maintain. We should refactor it."
  4. "The OnePay fraud-signal service is hitting HKMA TM-G-1 ribbon-cutting risk. p99 2.8s vs SLA 600ms, 4 incidents in 6 months, customer-visible as 'transaction under review' spinner. Ask: 1 squad × 2 sprints, 10% of Q4 capacity. Deal: Q1 velocity +20% and HKMA risk closes. Gate: p99 < 800ms by sprint 2 or stop."

② and ④ would be approved. ② leads with three numbers, reframes in customer language, scopes the ask tightly (1 squad × 4 sprints = 12%), names the deal (Q1 +30% velocity), and offers a go/no-go gate. ④ adds the HKMA TM-G-1 ribbon-cutting risk, which elevates it above pure tech debt. ① and ③ are cut — they speak only architect language ("clean up", "refactor") with no numbers, no customer translation, no scoped ask, and no deal. PMs cannot defend those in planning.
