Architecture Office Hours

Article 04 · Module 4: Unblocking the Squads · Architect English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 Drop-in sessions, answering dev questions, unblocking squads

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Wednesday, 16:00 HKT. You are running the weekly Architecture Office Hours for the RBWM Digital tribe. This is an open, drop-in Microsoft Teams session that the squads know about: no agenda, no minutes, no slides. Anyone can join — Tech Leads, BAs, devs, QAs, Product Owners — to ask architecture questions or get an unblock on a design decision.

周三下午 4 点香港时间，你在主持 RBWM 数字化 tribe 的每周架构师 Office Hours。 这是一个开放的、可随时加入的 Teams 会议：没有议程、没有会议纪要、没有 PPT。 任何人都可以加入——Tech Lead、BA、开发、QA、Product Owner——来问架构问题或解除设计决策上的阻塞。

Today's queue has three drop-ins: Sam (Wealth TL) wants a quick read on his caching strategy, Linh (Mobile BA) needs to understand the data classification for an offline KYC feature, and Carlos (a junior dev on Onboarding) has a question about why the squad was told to use the Group Kafka platform instead of a managed AWS offering. The tone of office hours is informal — but the answers go into Confluence afterwards.

今天的排队有 3 个 drop-in：Sam（Wealth TL）想要一个关于缓存策略的快速意见； Linh（Mobile BA）需要理解离线 KYC 功能的数据分级；Carlos （Onboarding 的初级开发）想问为什么 squad 被要求用 Group Kafka 平台，而不是 AWS 的托管 Kafka 服务。Office Hours 的氛围是随意的——但答案事后会写到 Confluence。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (Architect): OK, hi everyone, office hours is open. Quick reminder — no slides, no minutes, but I will write up the answers on the chapter Confluence page afterwards. First in the queue is Sam — fire away.好，大家好，Office Hours 开始。提醒一下——没 PPT、没纪要，但我会把答案写到 chapter 的 Confluence 页面上。队列里第一个是 Sam——请讲。
Sam (Tech Lead, Wealth): Thanks, Wei. Quick one — for the OneWealth rebalancing flow, we're considering Redis read-through cache for the position data. Honest answer — the data is reconciliation-sensitive, and I'm worried about cache drift between Redis and the source-of-truth DB. Should we cache at all?谢谢 Wei。简短一个问题——OneWealth 再平衡流程，我们考虑给头寸数据加 Redis read-through 缓存。说实话，这些数据对账敏感，我担心 Redis 和源数据库之间的缓存漂移。到底该不该加缓存？
You (Architect): Good question. Honest answer — yes, cache, but with a different pattern. For reconciliation-sensitive data, I recommend cache-aside with a short TTL and a Kafka change-data-feed that invalidates the cache on every source-of-truth write. The pattern is in our chapter ADR-0089 — let me drop the link in the chat. You should not use read-through for this data class — read-through hides the staleness.好问题。说实话——加缓存，但用别的模式。对对账敏感的数据，我推荐 cache-aside + 短 TTL，外加一个 Kafka 变更数据流在源库写入时让缓存失效。这个模式在 chapter 的 ADR-0089 里，我把链接发到聊天里。这种数据类不要用 read-through——read-through 掩盖了陈旧性。
Sam (Tech Lead, Wealth): Got it. Quick follow-up — what's a sensible TTL?收到。追问一下——TTL 多大合适？
You (Architect): For position data, I'd start at 30 seconds and tune from there. The Kafka invalidation handles the writes; the TTL is your belt-and-braces. Document the TTL and invalidation strategy in the ADR-0203 update — Diego will ask about it.头寸数据，我建议先设 30 秒，再根据实际情况调。Kafka 失效处理写，TTL 是双保险。把 TTL 和失效策略写进 ADR-0203 更新里——Diego 会问的。
Linh (BA, Mobile): Hi Wei, thanks for slotting me in. Quick context — the Mobile squad is designing an offline KYC capture feature for customers with poor connectivity. What data classification applies to the captured ID images on the device?Wei 你好，谢谢你给我加塞。简短背景——Mobile squad 在设计一个离线 KYC 采集功能，给网络不好的客户用。设备上采集的身份证图片适用什么数据分级？
You (Architect): Good question, and this is a Group Cyber policy question, so the answer is fixed. Captured ID images are customer PII at "Restricted" classification. That means encryption at rest on the device is mandatory — AES-256 with a key in the platform keystore, not in app code. You'll need an InfoSec design review before the feature ships. I've dropped the link to the Group Cyber data-classification standard in the chat.好问题，这是 Group Cyber 政策问题，答案是固定的。采集的身份证图片属于"Restricted"级别的客户 PII。这意味着设备静态加密是强制的——AES-256，密钥放在平台 keystore 里，不能放在 app 代码里。这个功能上线前要过 InfoSec 设计评审。我把 Group Cyber 数据分级标准的链接发到聊天里了。
Linh (BA, Mobile): That's super clear. One more — do we need consent capture for the offline mode?很清晰。再问一个——离线模式需要采集同意吗？
You (Architect): Yes, before any capture. The consent must be explicit, granular, and recorded in the audit log with a timestamp and a device fingerprint. That's also a Group Standard. If you need a consent template, ping me after the session — I have one from the RBWM onboarding project.是的，任何采集之前都需要。同意书必须明确、颗粒度细，并记录到审计日志里，带时间戳和设备指纹。这也是集团标准。如果你需要同意书模板，会后 ping 我——我手上有 RBWM 开户项目的模板。
Carlos (Junior Dev, Onboarding): Hi Wei, thanks for the slot. Quick one — my squad was told to use the Group Kafka platform for the Onboarding event flow, not the AWS Managed Kafka service. Honest answer — I don't really get why. Is it a cost thing? A security thing?Wei 你好，谢谢你加塞。简短问题——我们 squad 被要求 Onboarding 事件流用 Group Kafka 平台，不是 AWS Managed Kafka。说实话，我不太明白为什么。是成本考虑？安全考虑？
You (Architect): Good question, and it's worth understanding the "why". It's not primarily a cost decision. Honest answer — three reasons. One, the Group Kafka platform is the Group Standard for event streaming — so alignment with InfoSec and Group Cyber is already done. Two, cross-tribe consumers (CMB, GPB, RBWM) all subscribe to the same platform, so we get network-of-networks reuse. Three, the Group platform has built-in lineage and auditability for MAS TRM and HKMA reviews. AWS Managed Kafka would mean re-doing all of that.好问题，理解"为什么"很重要。这不是主要出于成本考虑。说实话——3 个原因。一、Group Kafka 平台是事件流的集团标准——与 InfoSec 和 Group Cyber 的对齐已经做好了。二、跨 tribe 的消费者（CMB、GPB、RBWM）都订阅同一个平台，网络协同复用。三、Group 平台内置了 lineage 和可审计性，满足 MAS TRM 和 HKMA 评审。用 AWS Managed Kafka 等于把这一切重做一遍。
Carlos (Junior Dev, Onboarding): Got it, that makes sense. One more — is there a "why" page on Confluence I can read?明白了，有道理。再问一个——Confluence 上有讲"为什么"的页面可以读吗？
You (Architect): Yes, ADR-0052 — "Group Standard for event-streaming platforms". I'll drop the link in the chat. And honestly — questions like this are exactly what office hours is for. If you're ever unsure why we do something a certain way, just ask.有的，ADR-0052——"事件流平台集团标准"。我把链接发到聊天里。说实话——这种问题就是 Office Hours 存在的意义。如果你不确定我们为什么这样做，直接问就行。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Office hours is open — quick reminder, no slides, no minutes. Office Hours 开始——提醒一下，没 PPT、没纪要 Open with the format reminder. Office hours are informal, but the answers are not — they go to Confluence.
  • Handoff First in the queue is Sam — fire away. 队列里第一个是 Sam——请讲 "Fire away" = the queue-management verb. It tells the next person to start talking.
  • Open Quick one — should we cache at all? 简短一个问题——到底该不该加缓存？ "Quick one" signals the question is bounded. Use it for the simple stuff. "Genuine concern" is for the harder stuff (see Article 03).
  • Recommend I recommend cache-aside with a short TTL. 我推荐 cache-aside + 短 TTL Always name the specific pattern, not just the goal. "Cache-aside" beats "use a cache".
  • Cite The pattern is in our chapter ADR-0089 — let me drop the link. 这个模式在 chapter 的 ADR-0089 里——我把链接发出来 Always cite the ADR. "Drop the link in the chat" turns the answer from opinion into reference.
  • Recommend You should not use read-through for this data class. 这种数据类不要用 read-through Architects give negative recommendations too. "You should not X because Y" is just as architectural as "I recommend X".
  • Reasoning Honest answer — three reasons. 说实话——3 个原因 "Three reasons" is the politeness device for explaining a Group Standard. It signals "I've thought about this and it's not arbitrary".
  • Standard This is a Group Cyber policy question, so the answer is fixed. 这是 Group Cyber 政策问题，答案是固定的 Use "the answer is fixed" when the question is policy-driven, not preference-driven. It saves a 20-minute debate.
  • Handoff If you need a template, ping me after the session. 如果你需要模板，会后 ping 我 Office hours are a triage. Move detailed follow-ups to 1:1s — don't expand the meeting.
  • Invite Questions like this are exactly what office hours is for. 这种问题就是 Office Hours 存在的意义 Closing the loop with "this is what the session is for" invites the next junior dev to ask. It builds the office-hours culture.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Office hours n. 答疑时间 A regular, informal drop-in session where an architect answers squad questions.
  • Drop-in adj. 随时加入的 A session that doesn't require a calendar invite or pre-registration. People join as needed.
  • Read-through cache n. 读穿透缓存 A caching pattern where the cache itself loads missing data from the source on a miss.
  • Cache-aside n. 旁路缓存 A pattern where the application code explicitly reads from the cache and falls back to the source.
  • Cache drift n. 缓存漂移 A divergence between the cached value and the source-of-truth value, usually caused by missed invalidations.
  • TTL (time-to-live) n. 生存时间 The duration a cached value is considered valid before it must be refreshed.
  • Change-data-feed (CDF) n. 变更数据流 A stream of database write events used to invalidate downstream caches.
  • Belt-and-braces phr. 双保险 Using two independent mechanisms to guard against the same failure mode.
  • Data classification n. 数据分级 The categorisation of data by sensitivity (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted).
  • Group Standard n. 集团标准 A bank-wide mandatory standard that overrides local preferences.
  • Lineage n. 数据血缘 The trail that shows where data came from, how it was transformed, and where it went.
  • Network of networks n. 网络协同 HSBC's pattern of having one platform (e.g., Kafka) shared across tribes for cross-tribe reuse.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Quick one" / "Quick follow-up" — the queue-management phrases

Office hours are queue-based, so the language is short. Quick one opens a bounded question. Quick follow-up opens a second bounded question. Quick context opens a short background. The format is always Quick [noun] — [content]. Use these to signal "I'm not opening a 30-minute debate".

"Quick one / Quick follow-up / Quick context" 是 Office Hours 的排队语言。结构是"Quick [名词] — [内容]"。

② "It's not primarily a cost decision" — the architect's "why" framing

When a junior dev asks is it a cost thing?, the architect's answer is rarely "yes". The pattern is It's not primarily a [X] decision. [Number] reasons. — followed by an enumerated list. The structure: name the misconception, then list the real reasons. This is a politeness device that respects the question without agreeing with the framing.

当 junior dev 问"是不是 X 考虑？"，架构师一般回答"这不是主要出于 X 考虑，N 个原因……"——这是一种礼貌化结构。

③ "The answer is fixed" — the policy shield

This is a Group Cyber policy question, so the answer is fixed uses a cause-and-effect structure: [authority] + [policy question], so the answer is [adjective]. Fixed is the strongest word — it means "no debate, no alternative, no trade-off". Reserve it for things driven by Group Standards, regulators, or law. Using it for preferences is a credibility hit.

"The answer is fixed" 是最强的政策表达——只在集团标准、监管、法律驱动的场景使用。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Office hours are not design reviews. No agenda, no minutes, no slides. But the answers are durable — they go on the chapter Confluence page. If a junior dev asks a question, write up the answer once, link the ADR, and the next person with the same question self-serves.
  • Always cite the ADR. "The pattern is in ADR-0089 — let me drop the link" turns the answer from opinion into reference. Junior devs in particular need citations, not just confident answers.
  • Junior devs ask the best "why" questions. When Carlos asks "is it a cost thing?", that's not a stupid question — it's the question. The "why" of a Group Standard is the most important thing you can teach. Take 60 seconds to explain three reasons.
  • Group Cyber / Group Resilience answers are fixed. When a question hits a Group Standard, say so explicitly: "this is a Group Cyber policy question, so the answer is fixed." Don't open a debate on a closed policy. Cite the standard by name.
  • Negative recommendations are valid architecture. "You should not use read-through for this data class" is a real architectural statement. Saying "no" is harder than saying "yes", but it's a service to the squad.
  • Triage to 1:1s. Office hours are 30-60 minutes for 3-6 questions. If a question is going long, say "let's take this offline — book a 1:1 with me". Don't let one drop-in eat the queue.
  • Encourage the next question. End with "questions like this are exactly what office hours is for". A junior dev who felt heard today will ask the next hard question tomorrow. That's how the architecture culture scales.
  • Cross-region voice etiquette. Office hours in RBWM Digital tend to be HK + Bangalore heavy, with UK joining for the harder questions. If the question is regulator-driven (HKMA, FCA, MAS TRM), let the relevant region's voice answer first.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the office-hours answer

Translate the following office-hours answer into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the architect cites the ADR and gives a negative recommendation.

"Quick one — should we use AWS Lambda for the OneWealth nightly batch, or stick with the on-prem job scheduler? Honest answer — stay on the on-prem job scheduler for now. Three reasons. One, the on-prem scheduler is the Group Standard for batch workloads above 30 minutes — alignment with Group Resilience is already done. Two, AWS Lambda has a 15-minute execution cap, and the OneWealth batch runs for 45 minutes — we'd need to break it up and stitch it back together, which is operational complexity we don't need. Three, the on-prem scheduler integrates with the Group Cyber audit log out of the box; AWS Lambda would need a custom integration. So my recommendation is to keep the on-prem scheduler, and revisit if we ever break the batch into smaller pieces."

"简短一个问题——OneWealth 的夜间批处理用 AWS Lambda 还是继续用本地的 job scheduler？说实话——暂时继续用本地的 job scheduler。3 个原因。一、本地 scheduler 是 30 分钟以上批处理负载的集团标准——和 Group Resilience 的对齐已经做好了。二、AWS Lambda 有 15 分钟执行上限，而 OneWealth 批处理要跑 45 分钟——我们就得拆开再拼回去，这是我们不需要的运维复杂度。三、本地 scheduler 开箱即用就能对接 Group Cyber 审计日志；AWS Lambda 需要做自定义对接。所以我建议继续用本地 scheduler，除非将来我们把批处理拆成小块再说。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the office-hours mistake

Which of these office-hours answers is most aligned with HSBC architecture culture? Why?

  1. "You should just use Redis. It's faster and everyone uses it."
  2. "I recommend cache-aside with a short TTL and a Kafka change-data-feed for invalidation. The pattern is in chapter ADR-0089 — I'll drop the link. You should not use read-through for reconciliation-sensitive data."
  3. "That's a stupid question. Read the Group Standard and stop asking me."
  4. "Whatever, pick what you want. It's not a big deal."

② is HSBC-aligned: specific pattern, ADR citation, negative recommendation with a reason. ① is a "just use X" (architect anti-pattern). ③ is rude and breaks the office-hours culture. ④ abdicates the architect's role. Always name the pattern, cite the ADR, and say yes or no with a reason.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're running office hours for the GPB Digital tribe. A junior dev (Ravi, Bangalore) drops in with: "Quick one — for our GPB Markets pricing API, the squad is proposing to use a self-managed PostgreSQL cluster on EC2 instead of Amazon RDS. Is that OK?" Write your 60-second answer in English. Cite at least one Group Standard or ADR, give a clear yes/no recommendation, and end with an invitation. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Good question, Ravi. Honest answer — recommend Amazon RDS, not self-managed PostgreSQL on EC2. Three reasons. One, RDS is the Group Standard for managed relational databases — the operational runbook, the backup posture, and the Group Cyber audit hooks are already aligned. Two, self-managing PostgreSQL on EC2 means your squad owns the failover, the patching, and the backup verification — that's a real toil burden for a squad that should be focused on pricing logic. Three, the Group Resilience DR test rig only covers RDS, not self-managed EC2 databases — so you'd be carving out an exception. The pattern is in chapter ADR-0117 — I'll drop the link in the chat. So my recommendation is to use Amazon RDS, multi-AZ, with the standard backup window. And honestly — questions like this are exactly what office hours is for. If you're ever unsure why we do something a certain way, just ask."
