Banking Architecture Terminology

Article 19 · Module 5: HSBC Banking Context — Banking Architecture Vocabulary · Architect English

⏱ 10 min read 🟠 Intermediate 🎯 KYC, AML, sanctions, real-time vs batch, idempotency, reconciliation — speak the bank's language

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday, 11:00 AM HKT. You are a Solution Architect in the GPB Payments tribe, supporting the Cross-Border Payments and Sanctions Screening squads. A new Tech Lead, Priya, just joined from HSBC's Bangalore hub. She has 8 years of payments experience but admits she is "still learning the HSBC banking vocabulary" — terms like SWIFT MT103, OFAC SDN list, idempotency key, T+1 reconciliation keep coming up in the daily standup, and she wants a half-hour walkthrough.

周四上午 11 点香港时间，你是 GPB 支付 tribe 的 Solution 架构师，支持 跨境支付 和 制裁名单筛查 两个 squad。一位新来的 Tech Lead Priya 从汇丰班加罗尔中心加入，有 8 年支付经验，但坦言自己 "还在学汇丰的银行业术语"——SWIFT MT103、OFAC SDN 名单、 幂等键、T+1 对账 这些词每天站会都冒出来，她想要半小时的串讲。

You book a 30-min Microsoft Teams session. The session is informal — Priya will pick the terms, you'll explain them, and you'll wrap with the two terms that architects most often get wrong: idempotency and reconciliation. These are the words that separate a real banking architect from a generic payments engineer.

你约了 30 分钟的 Teams 会议。形式非正式——Priya 选词、你解释，最后你用架构师最容易 用错的两个词收尾：幂等性和对账。这两个词是 区分真正的银行架构师和普通支付工程师的分水岭。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Priya (Tech Lead, Bangalore): Quick one before we start — can we go through the terms in the order I keep hearing them in standup? I want to build the mental map first, the trade-offs second.开始前问一句——能不能按我在站会里听到的顺序讲？先建心理模型，再讲 trade-off。
You (Architect): Sure. Quick banking-vocab walkthrough. I'll group them: regulatory screening first, then payment mechanics, then data correctness — that's the order you actually need them in production.好。快速过一下银行术语。我会分组：先监管筛查，再支付机制，最后数据正确性——这是你生产环境真正需要的顺序。
You (Architect): Group one — KYC, AML, sanctions screening. KYC is Know Your Customer — the bank's process for verifying the customer's identity at onboarding and refresh. AML is Anti-Money Laundering — the broader set of controls to detect money-laundering patterns across accounts. Sanctions screening is the act of checking every customer and every payment against the OFAC SDN list, the EU consolidated list, and the UN sanctions list.第一组——KYC、AML、制裁名单筛查。KYC 是了解你的客户——银行在开户和信息更新时核验客户身份的过程。AML 是反洗钱——跨账户检测洗钱模式的更广泛控制集。制裁名单筛查是把每个客户和每笔交易对照 OFAC SDN 名单、EU 合并名单 和 UN 制裁名单 的动作。
Priya (Tech Lead): Right, so KYC is at onboarding, AML is ongoing, sanctions screening is per-transaction?那 KYC 在开户，AML 是持续的，制裁名单筛查是每笔交易？
You (Architect): Exactly. And just to flag — sanctions screening has to happen before the payment is released, not after. Screening at settlement is a regulatory finding waiting to happen.对。提一下——制裁名单筛查必须在支付放行前做，不能在之后。结算时才筛查是等着被监管处罚。
Priya (Tech Lead): Got it. Next — payment mechanics. What's the difference between SWIFT MT103, CHAPS, and FPS?收到。下一组——支付机制。SWIFT MT103、CHAPS 和 FPS 有什么区别？
You (Architect): Good question. SWIFT MT103 is the cross-border message format — it's a single message, not a transfer. CHAPS is a UK real-time gross settlement system — pound-denominated, settled in seconds. FPS is the UK's Faster Payments Service — also real-time, but for smaller-value retail. All three are governed by the FCA.好问题。SWIFT MT103 是跨境报文格式——它是一条消息，不是转账。CHAPS 是英国实时全额结算系统——英镑计价，秒级结算。FPS 是英国快速支付服务——也是实时，但面向小额零售。三者都受 FCA 监管。
Priya (Tech Lead): And what does real-time actually mean here? Honest answer — in my old shop "real-time" was anything under 5 minutes.那"实时"到底指什么？实话——在老东家"实时"是 5 分钟以内。
You (Architect): In HSBC-land, real-time means sub-second at the edge and end-to-end under 5 seconds, with the customer seeing the payment in their account before the next UI refresh. Batch means anything that runs on a schedule — typically T+0 end-of-day or T+1 morning, for things like SWIFT MT103 confirmation files and statement rendering.在汇丰，"实时"指边缘亚秒级，端到端 5 秒以内，客户在 UI 下一次刷新前看到钱到账。批量指按计划跑的东西——通常是 T+0 日终或 T+1 上午，跑 SWIFT MT103 确认文件和账单出账这类。
Priya (Tech Lead): OK, so the last group is what I'm most worried about — idempotency and reconciliation.好，最后一组也是我最担心的——幂等性和对账。
You (Architect): These are the two that architects most often get wrong. Idempotency means: if the same payment request is sent twice, the system processes it once. It's not "we try not to duplicate" — it's a guarantee, enforced by an idempotency key (usually a UUID v7 generated at the API edge) and a dedup window (typically 24 hours in Redis).这两个是架构师最容易用错的。幂等性指：同一笔支付请求发两次，系统只处理一次。不是"我们尽量不重复"——这是一个保证，靠幂等键（通常是 API 入口生成的 UUID v7）和去重窗口（一般 Redis 24 小时）来强制执行。
You (Architect): Reconciliation is different — it's the act of matching our internal ledger to the correspondent bank's ledger at end of day, to prove we sent the same value they received. T+1 reconciliation means we run the match at 9 AM the next business day. Any unmatched item is a break, and breaks go to the ops team with a 2-day SLA to resolve.对账不一样——它是把我们内部账本和代理行的账本核对的动作，日终跑，证明我们发出的金额和他们收到的对得上。T+1 对账是下一个工作日上午 9 点跑核对。任何未匹配项就是差异，交给 ops 团队，SLA 2 天 内解决。
Priya (Tech Lead): Good, that's the picture. One last thing — when a payment fails at the correspondent, is that an idempotency issue or a reconciliation issue?清晰了。最后一问——当支付在代理行失败，是幂等问题还是对账问题？
You (Architect): Good question. Honest answer — both, in different places. Idempotency prevents the re-send. Reconciliation catches the unmatched funds. If the correspondent rejects the payment after we've debited the customer, we reverse the debit and the reversal itself has to be idempotent. Both guarantees have to hold, end-to-end.好问题。实话——在不同环节，两个都涉及。幂等性防止重复发送。对账捕获未匹配的资金。如果代理行拒付了但我们已经从客户账上扣款，我们就要冲正，而冲正本身也要幂等。两个保证都必须端到端成立。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Quick banking-vocab walkthrough. 快速过一下银行术语 Frames a vocabulary session as a walkthrough, not a lecture. Sets the casual, learn-as-we-go tone.
  • Order I'll go in the order you actually need them in production. 按你在生产中真正需要的顺序讲 Architects teach in dependency order, not alphabetical order. Screening → mechanics → correctness is the order a real payment actually uses them.
  • Scope KYC at onboarding, AML ongoing, sanctions screening per-transaction. KYC 在开户，AML 持续，制裁名单筛查每笔交易 Scope each control by when it fires. Junior engineers confuse KYC and AML because both "screen" — but they fire at different points in the lifecycle.
  • Order Sanctions screening has to happen before the payment is released, not after. 制裁名单筛查必须在支付放行前，不能在之后 The verb "release" is the architect's word. The mistake juniors make is "screen at settlement" — that's a regulatory finding.
  • Definition Real-time means sub-second at the edge, end-to-end under 5 seconds. 实时指边缘亚秒级，端到端 5 秒以内 Always define "real-time" with two numbers: edge latency and end-to-end latency. "Real-time" alone is meaningless.
  • Definition Idempotency means the same request processed twice is processed once. 幂等性指同一请求发两次只处理一次 The "twice → once" definition is the cleanest. Anything else is wishful thinking.
  • Definition Reconciliation is matching our ledger to the correspondent's ledger. 对账是把我们的账本和代理行的账本核对 The "correspondent's ledger" is the external anchor. Without an external anchor, you have an internal check, not a reconciliation.
  • SLA Breaks go to ops with a 2-day SLA to resolve. 差异交给 ops，SLA 2 天内解决 Every break has an SLA. If you don't have an SLA, you don't have a process — you have a wish.
  • Reverse If the correspondent rejects the payment, we reverse the debit. 如果代理行拒付，我们就冲正扣款 "Reverse" is the architect's word for an undo. The reversal itself must be idempotent — that's the trap most juniors miss.
  • End-to-end Both guarantees have to hold, end-to-end. 两个保证都必须端到端成立 "End-to-end" is the architect's signature phrase. A guarantee that only holds in the middle is not a guarantee.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • KYC (Know Your Customer) n. 了解你的客户 The bank's process for verifying customer identity at onboarding and refresh.
  • AML (Anti-Money Laundering) n. 反洗钱 Controls to detect money-laundering patterns across accounts, transactions, and time.
  • Sanctions screening n. 制裁名单筛查 Checking customers and payments against OFAC SDN, EU consolidated, and UN sanctions lists.
  • OFAC SDN list n. OFAC SDN 名单 The US Office of Foreign Assets Control's Specially Designated Nationals list — a global sanctions watchlist.
  • SWIFT MT103 n. SWIFT MT103 报文 The standard SWIFT message format for single customer credit transfer — a message, not a transfer.
  • CHAPS n. CHAPS 英镑实时清算 UK real-time gross settlement system for high-value pound payments.
  • FPS (Faster Payments Service) n. 英国快速支付 UK real-time retail payment system, sub-value threshold.
  • Real-time n. 实时 Sub-second at the edge, end-to-end under 5 seconds (HSBC definition).
  • Batch n. 批量 Scheduled processing — T+0 end-of-day or T+1 morning — for non-urgent workloads.
  • Idempotency n. 幂等性 A guarantee: the same request processed twice is processed once, enforced by an idempotency key.
  • Idempotency key n. 幂等键 A unique identifier (typically UUID v7) attached to a request, used to dedup at the API edge.
  • Reconciliation n. 对账 Matching our internal ledger to the correspondent bank's ledger at end of day to prove value parity.
  • Break n. 差异 An unmatched item in reconciliation; routed to ops with a defined SLA.
  • Correspondent bank n. 代理行 The intermediary bank that holds nostro accounts and settles cross-border payments on our behalf.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Means" as a definition verb

When teaching banking terms, the verb means is your workhorse. Use the pattern X means Y where Y is a definition the listener can verify: real-time means sub-second at the edge, idempotency means the same request processed twice is processed once. Avoid X is when you do Y — that pattern defines activities, not properties, and it breaks for terms like idempotency that aren't activities.

教银行术语用 "X means Y" 句式。Y 是一句可验证的定义。

② "Has to" vs "should" vs "must" — regulatory obligation

Three near-synonyms with very different weight. Should is advice. Has to is operational obligation. Must is regulatory obligation. Notice sanctions screening has to happen before the payment is released — not should happen. When the obligation comes from a regulator (OFAC, MAS, HKMA), the architect uses has to / must. When it's an internal preference, use should.

Should 建议 / has to 操作义务 / must 监管义务——根据义务来源选词。

③ "End-to-end" — the architect's signature adverb

A guarantee that only holds in the middle of the system is not a guarantee — it's a partial guarantee. Use end-to-end as the adverb to extend a property to the full path: both guarantees have to hold, end-to-end. The phrase is almost a tic in banking architects — if you hear yourself say "end-to-end" three times in a meeting, you're probably architecting correctly.

End-to-end 是架构师的口头禅——把一个属性推到整条链路上。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Banking vocabulary is an inclusion lever. New joiners — especially from non-banking backgrounds — get excluded not by being unfriendly, but by people using 12 acronyms in one sentence. If you spot a new joiner going quiet, slow down and define the term out loud.
  • Always screen before release, not after. Sanctions screening at settlement is the single most common finding in cross-border payments. The verb to remember is release — if it isn't screened before release, you have a finding.
  • SWIFT MT103 is a message, not a transfer. Juniors often confuse the SWIFT message with the underlying funds movement. The message is the instruction; the funds settle on the correspondent's books. Architects distinguish these explicitly in ADRs.
  • Real-time needs two numbers. "Real-time" is meaningless without (1) edge latency and (2) end-to-end latency. HSBC architects say sub-second at the edge, end-to-end under 5 seconds. If a Tech Lead says "real-time" without numbers, ask for the two numbers.
  • Idempotency is a guarantee, not a hope. "We'll try not to duplicate" is not idempotency. Idempotency is enforced by a UUID v7 idempotency key + a Redis-backed dedup window + a tested retry path. The ADR must show all three.
  • Reconciliation always has an external anchor. An internal "check" between two of your own systems is not reconciliation. Reconciliation matches your ledger to an external party's ledger (correspondent bank, custodian, card scheme). No external anchor = no reconciliation.
  • Reversals are still payments. When a payment fails at the correspondent, the reversal is itself a payment — and it must be idempotent, screened, and reconciled. Most double-posting bugs come from reversals that bypass the idempotency check.
  • Breaks have SLAs, not vibes. A reconciliation break that "should be looked at" is not a process. Every break needs an owner, an SLA, and a runbook. If the SLA is missing, raise it as an ADR item.

Exercise 2 — Spot the definition error

A junior architect has drafted the following definitions for a new joiner deck. Which definitions are wrong or misleading? Why?

  1. "KYC is when you check the customer's ID at onboarding."
  2. "AML is when you run sanctions screening on every payment."
  3. "Real-time means it happens fast, like under a minute."
  4. "Idempotency means we try not to send the same payment twice."
  5. "Reconciliation is checking that our two internal systems agree on balances."

① is roughly OK but uses "is when you do" — fine for an activity (KYC), but keep it simple. ② is wrong — AML is anti-money-laundering (transaction monitoring, pattern detection), not sanctions screening (which is a separate per-transaction control). ③ is wrong — "real-time" in HSBC is sub-second at the edge, end-to-end under 5 seconds, not "under a minute". ④ is wrong — "we try not to" is not idempotency; idempotency is a guarantee, not an intent. ⑤ is wrong — checking two internal systems is a consistency check, not reconciliation; reconciliation needs an external anchor (correspondent bank, custodian). Definitions ②, ③, ④, ⑤ must be rewritten.

You are a Solution Architect in the RBWM Payments tribe. "Quick walkthrough, in the order you need them in production. First — sanctions screening: every payment is checked against the OFAC SDN list, the EU consolidated list, and the UN sanctions list.
