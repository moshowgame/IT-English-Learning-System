Cross-Team Architecture Sync

Article 05 · Module 5: Aligning Across Regions and Tribes · Architect English

⏱ 9 min read 🟠 Upper-intermediate 🎯 Aligning with other architects across regions and tribes

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday, 09:00 HKT. The monthly Cross-Tribe Architecture Sync is starting — a 60-minute Microsoft Teams meeting chaired by Margaret, your Chief Architect. The attendees are the solution architects from five tribes: RBWM Digital (you, Hong Kong), CMB Digital (Wei, Shanghai), GPB Digital (Hugo, London), Group Platform (Rashida, Bangalore), and Group Cyber (Anika, Hong Kong, joining for the last 20 minutes).

周二上午 9 点香港时间，每月一次的跨 Tribe 架构同步会开始——这是 Chief Architect Margaret 主持的 60 分钟 Teams 会议。参会者来自 5 个 tribe的 solution 架构师：RBWM Digital（你，香港）、CMB Digital（Wei，上海）、GPB Digital （Hugo，伦敦）、Group Platform（Rashida，班加罗尔），以及Group Cyber （Anika，香港，最后 20 分钟加入）。

The agenda: OneWealth API gateway strategy alignment (your ADR-0142), Group Kafka topic-naming standard (Rashida's draft), and cross-tribe observability with OpenTelemetry (Wei from CMB wants to trial it). The room dynamic is delicate — five tribes, four time zones, one shared platform. The goal is to leave the meeting with a clean alignment, not a fistfight.

议程：OneWealth API gateway 策略对齐（你的 ADR-0142）、Group Kafka topic 命名标准 （Rashida 的草稿）、以及OpenTelemetry 跨 tribe 可观测性（CMB 的 Wei 想试用）。房间里的 氛围比较微妙——5 个 tribe、4 个时区、1 个共享平台。目标是带着干净的对齐离场，而不是不欢而散。

You (Architect, RBWM): Will do — I'll circulate ADR-0142 with the reference diagram by EOD today. Rashida (Architect, Group Platform, Bangalore): Quick walkthrough of the draft — the proposed convention is [region].[tribe].[domain].[event-type].[version], e.g. apac.rbwm.onewealth.rebalancerequested.v1. 3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Right, let's get started — three items and 60 minutes. 好，开始——3 项议题，60 分钟 Open with a timebox and item count. A cross-tribe sync without a timebox is a 90-minute talkathon.
  • Align We want to align before we sign off our own ADR. 我们想在签自己的 ADR 之前对齐一下 "Align" is the politeness word for "let's not have two tribes doing the same thing differently". Use it liberally.
  • Steer Honest answer — in front of, not replacing. 说实话——放在前面，不是替换 "In front of, not replacing" — the negative-part construction clarifies scope. Always name what you are NOT doing.
  • Commit I'm happy to share the reference architecture diagram offline. 我乐意线下分享参考架构图 "I'm happy to" = a soft commitment. "Offline" = move the detail work out of the cross-tribe meeting.
  • Flag Just to flag — can we get the ADR circulated today? 提一句——ADR 今天能发出来吗？ "Just to flag" introduces a side issue without derailing the main discussion. Same device as Article 02.
  • Steer Let's do that now while we're all in the room. 趁大家都在，现在过一下 "While we're all in the room" = the cross-tribe politeness device. It's expensive to get five tribes on a call — use the moment.
  • Trade-off Honest answer — we can live with the dot if the chapter is aligned. 说实话——如果 chapter 对齐了，用点我们也能接受 "We can live with X" is a politeness concession. It signals "I have a preference, but tribe alignment matters more".
  • Constraint The trace-data residency question is non-trivial. trace 数据驻留的问题不简单 "Non-trivial" = a soft "this is hard". Architects use it for things that aren't blocking but need real work.
  • Commit Before I commit Group Cyber to a sign-off, I need to see the data flow. 在我让 Group Cyber 承诺签批之前，我需要看数据流 "Before I commit [Group] to [verb]" = the architect's polite refusal. It signals "I want to say yes, but I need evidence".
  • Close Summary of decisions: one, two, three, four. 决策总结：一、二、三、四 End with a numbered summary. The cross-tribe meeting's only durable output is the decision list. Always verbalise it.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Cross-tribe sync n. 跨 tribe 同步 A regular meeting where architects from multiple tribes align on shared decisions.
  • Tribe n. 部落 A collection of squads aligned to a business outcome (RBWM, CMB, GPB, Group Platform, etc.).
  • Chapter n. 职能社区 The functional community of practice (e.g., the Architecture chapter).
  • Central tenant n. 中心租户 A shared cloud landing zone owned by a central team and consumed by multiple tribes.
  • Landing zone n. 落地区 A pre-provisioned cloud account/environment with baseline controls, networking, and security.
  • Shared platform n. 共享平台 A platform (e.g., Kafka, API Gateway) that multiple tribes consume from a single owner.
  • Network of networks n. 网络协同 The pattern of one shared platform with multiple consumers across tribes.
  • Lineage n. 数据血缘 The trail that shows where data came from, how it was transformed, and where it went.
  • Data residency n. 数据驻留 The requirement that data stays within a specific geographic region for regulatory reasons.
  • Trace data n. trace 数据 The data emitted by distributed tracing systems (e.g., OpenTelemetry spans).
  • Vendor-locked adj. 厂商锁定的 A state where the architecture is dependent on a single vendor's proprietary product.
  • APM (Application Performance Monitoring) n. 应用性能监控 Vendor tools for monitoring application performance (e.g., Dynatrace, AppDynamics).
  • Quota policy n. 配额策略 A documented rule for how shared resources are allocated across consumers.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "We can live with X" — the cross-tribe concession

Honest answer — we can live with the dot if the chapter is aligned uses can live with to express a soft concession. The pattern is we can live with [option] if [condition]. It signals "I have a preference, but alignment matters more". Use it when you've stated a preference, the room is leaning the other way, and you want to land the decision without a fight.

"We can live with X if Y" 是跨 tribe 会议的礼貌让步——表示"我有偏好，但对齐更重要"。

② "Before I commit [Group] to [verb]" — the architect's polite refusal

Anika's Before I commit Group Cyber to a sign-off, I need to see the data flow is a soft refusal. The pattern is Before I commit [organisation] to [commitment], I need [evidence]. It's softer than I cannot sign off and softer than Group Cyber will not approve. It leaves the door open while demanding evidence. Use it whenever you speak on behalf of a Group function.

"Before I commit [组织] to [承诺], I need [证据]" 是代 Group function 发言时的礼貌拒绝，比直接拒绝更柔和。

③ "In front of, not replacing" — the negative-part clarification

Honest answer — in front of, not replacing uses two parallel noun phrases separated by a comma and "not" to clarify scope. The pattern is [positive] [noun phrase], not [negative] [noun phrase]: in front of, not replacing; internal, not external; squad-level, not chapter-level. Always name what you are NOT doing. Architects over-specify the negative because the positive is easy to misread.

"[正向] [名词]，not [负向] [名词]" 是架构师用来明确范围的标准结构。永远要说明你"不做什么"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Cross-tribe time is precious. Five tribes, four time zones, one shared platform. The cross-tribe sync is the only forum where all the architects are in the room. Use the slot for alignment, not for status updates. Status belongs in the standup.
  • "Align" is the politeness word for "stop duplicating work". When Hugo says "we want to align before we sign off our own ADR", he's not asking for help — he's saying "we have a similar pattern, let's not do it twice". Honour the move by sharing the reference architecture.
  • Cross-region voice etiquette. If a question is regulator-driven, let the relevant region's voice answer first. Anika (Group Cyber, HK) speaks for HKMA and MAS TRM. Hugo (GPB, London) speaks for FCA. Rashida (Group Platform, Bangalore) speaks for central platform decisions. Don't talk over them.
  • "We can live with X" is a landing move, not a surrender. When Hugo concedes on the dot separator, he's not losing — he's landing the decision. A cross-tribe meeting that lands 4 decisions in 60 minutes is a success, even if every decision is a compromise.
  • Group Cyber answers are fixed when the regulator is named. When Anika says "MAS TRM and HKMA both expect trace data to stay in-region", the answer is fixed. Don't relitigate. The compromise lives in the architecture (collector-to-backend data flow), not in the policy.
  • End with a numbered decision list. "Summary of decisions: one, two, three, four." The cross-tribe meeting's only durable output is the decision list — it goes into the chapter meeting minutes. Always verbalise it before closing.
  • Central tenants beat per-tribe reinvention. When Rashida offers the API Gateway central tenant for OneWealth and future CMB Mobile, take it. Five tribes each spinning up their own API Gateway is a Group Cyber, Group Resilience, and operational nightmare. Shared platform, shared controls, shared lineage.
  • Move the detail work offline. The cross-tribe meeting is for alignment, not for deep design. If a question goes long, say "happy to share the reference diagram offline — let's grab 30 min". Don't let one question eat 20 minutes of cross-tribe time.

Exercise 2 — Spot the cross-tribe mistake

Which of these cross-tribe moves is most aligned with HSBC architecture culture? Why?

  1. "We're going to do this our way. Other tribes can copy or not."
  2. "We can live with the dot if the chapter is aligned. Let me share the reference architecture offline. Let's grab 30 min after this meeting to walk through the trade-off."
  3. "Group Cyber will never approve this. Forget it."
  4. "Whatever, just decide. I have another meeting."

② is HSBC-aligned: a soft concession ("we can live with"), a willingness to share, and a move to offline detail work. ① is unilateral — it kills cross-tribe alignment. ③ speaks for Group Cyber without authority. ④ abdicates the architect's role. Cross-tribe work is "align, concede gracefully, move detail offline".

Write your 60-second intervention in English. Use a concession, name a regulator, and propose a concrete change. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks, Margaret. Quick one on the Group observability standard.
