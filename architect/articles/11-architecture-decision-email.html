Architecture Decision Email

Article 11 · Module 6: Communicating Decisions · Architect English

⏱ 9 min read 🟠 Upper-intermediate 🎯 Communicate a final architecture decision to dev, PM, BA, and business stakeholders — clearly and without re-opening the debate

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday, 5:30 PM HKT. You are a Solution Architect in the GPB Digital tribe. The chapter review of ADR-0191 finished an hour ago with a sign-off. The decision: the GPB onboarding API surface will use REST for partner-facing, GraphQL behind the BFF for the mobile app, and gRPC for east-west service-to-service. The Idempotency-Key header is mandated on all state-changing endpoints. The pagination is cursor for partner GET, offset for internal.

周五下午 5:30 香港时间，你是 GPB 数字化 tribe 的 Solution 架构师。ADR-0191 的 chapter 评审一小时前结束，已经签批。结论是：GPB 开户 API 接口合作方走 REST、手机 BFF 走 GraphQL、 东西向服务间走 gRPC。所有有状态变更的接口强制 Idempotency-Key 请求头。分页方面，合作方 GET 走 cursor，内部走 offset。

You need to email the decision to Mei (Tech Lead, Shanghai), Jonathan (BA), Rohit (QA Lead, Bangalore), Sara (Product Owner, RBWM Wealth), and CC Margaret (Chief Architect), Diego (peer architect, UK), and the Group Cyber distribution list. The email must: (1) state the decision in 3 sentences, (2) summarise the trade-offs in a table, (3) list the next actions with owners and dates, (4) close the debate without sounding dismissive.

你需要给 Mei（Tech Lead，上海）、Jonathan（BA）、 Rohit（QA 负责人，班加罗尔）、Sara（产品负责人，RBWM 财富）发邮件， 并抄送 Margaret（首席架构师）、Diego（同侪架构师，英国）和 集团网络邮件组。邮件要做到：(1) 3 句话讲清决策，(2) 用表格总结 trade-off， (3) 列出下一步行动和 owner、日期，(4) 关掉讨论但语气不拒人千里。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Subject [DECISION] ADR-0191 — GPB Onboarding API Surface — Protocol Trifecta, Idempotency, Pagination [决策] ADR-0191——GPB 开户 API 接口——协议三件套、幂等、分页 Lead with [DECISION] in the subject. Without it, the email gets deprioritised to the "next week" pile.
  • Close This email is the final decision; please use it as the source of truth going forward. 这封邮件是最终决策；后续请以此为准 "Source of truth" + "going forward" = the sentence that stops the debate. State it in the second paragraph.
  • Recommend We are recommending REST for partner-facing, GraphQL behind the BFF, gRPC for east-west. 我们建议合作方走 REST、BFF 后 GraphQL、东西向 gRPC Always use "recommend" in a decision email. "We have decided" is too authoritative; "we recommend" carries the rationale.
  • Mandate The Idempotency-Key header is mandated on every state-changing endpoint. 所有有状态变更的接口强制 Idempotency-Key 请求头 "Mandated" = non-optional. Use it for hard rules (Group Standards, Idempotency-Key, HSM).
  • Trade-off Here's the short version: REST-only, GraphQL-everywhere, and the trifecta. 简化版：纯 REST、GraphQL 全用、三件套 Always give the alternatives — even if you've already chosen. People accept decisions they've seen considered.
  • Owner Mei: update the OpenAPI spec by EOD Wednesday. Jonathan: walk through the partner contract next week. Mei：周三 EOD 前更新 OpenAPI 规约。Jonathan：下周走查合作方契约 "Name: action by date." This is the architect's action template. Repeat for every action; never leave a date or an owner blank.
  • Soft Diego has offered to chase it — Diego, please confirm by Tuesday. Diego 主动要跟——Diego，麻烦周二前确认 "Has offered to" is a politeness device. You're assigning the action, but you're framing it as their offer, not your demand.
  • Fallback If the carve-out is denied, we fall back to REST with sparse fieldsets. 如果豁免被拒，我们就退回 REST + sparse fieldsets Always name the plan B in a decision email. The plan B is what makes the decision survivable.
  • Channel If you have concerns, please raise them in the chapter sync — not on the Slack thread, which I'm closing EOD today. 如果有不同意见，请在 chapter 例会上提——不要在 Slack 线程里，那条讨论我今天 EOD 关掉 Naming the channel where debate is allowed is the politeness way to close the discussion. "I'm closing the thread" is the boundary.
  • Sign-off — Wei, Solution Architect, GPB Digital · ADR-0191 · Confluence link 落款：Wei，Solution 架构师，GPB 数字化·ADR-0191·Confluence 链接 Always end with: name, role, tribe, ADR number, Confluence link. Five things — missing any one loses traceability.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Source of truth n. 唯一权威 The single document / email that everyone agrees to refer to. Stops re-debate.
  • Sign-off n. 签批 The formal approval that closes a decision. ADR sign-off is the gate for squad coding.
  • Carve-out n. 豁免 An exception to a Group Standard — must be approved in writing by the standard owner.
  • Trade-off matrix n. 取舍矩阵 A small table comparing 2-3 options across 3-4 criteria. Always include the chosen one.
  • Action item n. 行动项 A named task with an owner and a date. The output of every decision email.
  • Plan B / Fallback n. 备选方案 The pre-agreed alternative if the chosen path is blocked. Reduces re-litigation.
  • Channel discipline n. 渠道纪律 The rule that certain conversations happen in certain places (e.g., design debates in chapter, not Slack).
  • CC etiquette n. 抄送礼仪 The HSBC norm of CC'ing the Chief Architect, the platform team, and InfoSec on every decision email.
  • Closing the loop n. 闭环 Sending the final email / update that tells the team "we are done debating this".
  • Open question n. 悬而未决项 A known unknown that the team will resolve later. Always named in the decision email.
  • Stakeholder n. 利益相关方 Anyone affected by the decision: dev, PM, BA, QA, business, Group Cyber.
  • Distribution list (DL) n. 邮件组 An email alias that reaches a whole team (e.g., group-cyber@hsbc.com).

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "We are recommending X" vs "We have decided X" — the verb of authority

In a decision email, we are recommending carries the rationale; we have decided carries the authority. Use "we are recommending" when the decision is fresh and the room may still want to discuss. Use "we have decided" only after the chapter has signed off. After sign-off, the email subject leads with [DECISION] and the body uses we are recommending + this email is the final decision. The two phrases together signal "this is closed, but with the humility of a rationale".

We are recommending 带 rationale，we have decided 带 authority。决策邮件里两个都用。

② "Name: action by date." — the action-item template

Every action in a decision email follows the "Name: action by date." template. Mei: update the OpenAPI spec by EOD Wednesday. The colon after the name is a hard separator — it tells the reader "this is now an action, not a sentence about an action". The trailing by date is non-negotiable: an action without a date is a wish. Use this template for every action item; the rhythm makes the email scannable.

Name: action by date. 是决策邮件的行动项模板——冒号、动作、日期，缺一不可。

③ "If you have any concerns, please raise them in X — not in Y" — the channel-redirect

If you have any concerns, please raise them in the chapter sync — not on the Slack thread, which I'm closing EOD today. The channel-redirect is the politeness way to close a debate. Please raise them in X gives people a legitimate place to object. The — not in Y, which I'm closing is the boundary. The em-dash + not construction is more graceful than do not post on Slack. Use it once per decision email; over-use sounds controlling.

If you have concerns, raise them in X — not in Y 是关闭讨论的得体句式。给一个合法渠道、给一个边界。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Lead the subject with [DECISION]. An email about an architecture decision without [DECISION] in the subject gets deprioritised to "next week". HSBC inboxes are noisy; the tag is the only thing that makes Margaret read it on Friday night.
  • CC the Chief Architect, the platform team, and Group Cyber. This is the HSBC CC norm on every architecture decision email. Skipping the CC looks like you're hiding the decision. Over-CC'ing (e.g., the whole department) looks like CYA. The right CC is: chief architect, peer architect, the relevant group function (Cyber / Resilience / InfoSec).
  • State the decision in 3 sentences, then the trade-offs. Decision emails that lead with the trade-off matrix are PM-style; they read like a "let me explain my thinking" memo. Architects lead with the decision, then the trade-offs. Recipients want the conclusion in the first paragraph.
  • Always name the action, owner, and date. "Mei: update the OpenAPI spec by EOD Wednesday." An action without an owner is a wish; an action without a date is a wish with a name. Architects write all three.
  • Always name the open question and the fallback. "Group Standard carve-out pending — Diego chasing by Tuesday. If denied, REST + sparse fieldsets fallback." Open questions are not failures; they are honest disclosures. Fallbacks are not pessimism; they are risk-reduction.
  • Channel discipline closes the debate gracefully. "Please raise concerns in the chapter sync — not on the Slack thread, which I'm closing EOD." This sentence turns a debate-closer into a respectful one. People feel heard (the chapter sync is real) and the boundary is clear (Slack is closed).
  • End with name, role, tribe, ADR number, Confluence link. Five things, every time. The signature block is the audit trail. Two years from now, when the squad asks "why did we choose REST over GraphQL?", the email is the only artifact that has all five — name, role, tribe, ADR, link.

Exercise 2 — Spot the architect mistake

Which of these decision email lines is most "architect-like" and which is most "PM-like" or "developer-like"? Why?

  1. Subject: "Quick question on the fraud-screening service" — body: "Hey, just wanted to float an idea…"
  2. Subject: "[DECISION] ADR-0194 — OnePay Fraud-Screening — NFRs, Cold-Start, Model Risk" — body: "Following today's chapter review, ADR-0194 is signed off. This email is the final decision…"
  3. Subject: "Update on the fraud-screening service" — body: "I've been thinking about the design and I think we should…"
  4. Subject: "[DECISION] ADR-0194" — body: "We recommend X. Mei: action 1 by Wed. Rohit: action 2 by Fri. — Wei, Solution Architect, GPB Digital."

② is the gold standard — [DECISION] tag, ADR number, final-decision sentence, source-of-truth phrase. ④ is the architect's terse close (correct elements, minimal prose). ① is dev-style ("Quick question" + "float an idea" is a Slack post, not a decision email). ③ is PM-style ("Update" + "I've been thinking" is mid-debate, not closed). A common mistake new architects make is writing ① or ③ and wondering why the decision gets re-litigated two weeks later.
