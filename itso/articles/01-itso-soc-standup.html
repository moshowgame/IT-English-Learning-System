SOC Daily Standup — Speak Alerts, Not Feelings

Article 01 · Module 1: Daily Security Operations · ITSO English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 Alert counts, P1/P2, on-call, IOC sweep, escalations, asks

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 09:00 HKT. You are the ITSO SOC Lead for the bank's 24/7 Security Operations Center, supporting 6 analysts across Hong Kong, Shanghai, and Manila. The SOC standup runs on Microsoft Teams at the top of every shift change. Joining: Ravi, the Manila shift lead handing over, Emily, the Hong Kong day-shift lead, and Mark, the Group CISO observer.

上午 9 点香港时间，你是银行 24/7 安全运营中心（SOC）的 ITSO 团队负责人，带领 6 名分布在香港、上海、马尼拉的分析师。SOC 站在每次轮班交接时通过 Teams 召开。参会有：马尼拉交班负责人 Ravi、香港日班负责人 Emily，以及集团 CISO 观察员 Mark。

Unlike a developer's standup, the SOC standup is measured in alerts, incidents, IOC sweeps, and on-call status. You don't say "I worked on JIRA-1234" — you say "3 P1s open, 1 confirmed breach under containment, IOC sweep clean, on-call is Sarah".

和开发人员不同，SOC 站会用告警、事件、IOC 扫描、值班状态说话。你不说"我做了 JIRA-1234"——你说"3 个 P1 待处理，1 个确认泄露在遏制，IOC 扫描干净，值班是 Sarah"。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Ravi (Manila shift lead): Morning, team. Handing over. SOC update from me.大家早上好，交班。SOC 层面同步一下。
Ravi (Manila shift lead): Alert volume overnight: 1,420 SIEM events, 38 opened as tickets, 4 escalated to P1, 1 confirmed incident — a phishing campaign targeting treasury staff.夜间告警量：1420 条 SIEM 事件，38 个开为工单，4 个升级为 P1，1 个确认事件——针对财务部门的钓鱼攻击。
Ravi (Manila shift lead): Phishing campaign status: 1 mailbox compromised, 1 wire transfer attempt blocked at MFA, credentials revoked, session invalidated, endpoint isolated. Containment complete at 04:12 HKT. Investigation continues on day shift.钓鱼状态：1 个邮箱失陷，1 次电汇尝试在 MFA 处被拦截，凭据吊销，会话作废，端点隔离。04:12 HKT 完成遏制。调查交日班继续。
You (SOC Lead): Thanks, Ravi. Three follow-ups. One: is the phishing IOC sweep clean across the rest of the estate? You (SOC Lead): Confirmed. 1-page summary, in the inbox by 17:00.确认。1 页摘要，17:00 前入邮箱。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • SOC update SOC update from me. SOC 层面同步一下 Mark yourself as the SOC lead. You speak for the SOC, not for yourself.
  • Alert volume Alert volume overnight: 1,420 SIEM events, 38 tickets, 4 P1s, 1 confirmed incident. 夜间告警量：1420 条 SIEM 事件，38 个工单，4 个 P1，1 个确认事件 Always quote the chain: events → tickets → P1s → incidents. The CISO remembers the incident count.
  • Containment complete Containment complete at 04:12 HKT. 04:12 HKT 完成遏制 Name the time of containment. The CISO will ask. Always have the timestamp ready.
  • IOC sweep IOC sweep at 04:30 and 06:00 — both clean. 04:30 和 06:00 IOC 扫描——都干净 Quote the time of the sweep. "Clean" alone is weak. "Clean at 04:30 and 06:00" is professional.
  • SLA Fraud team acknowledged at 04:35, 23 minutes from detection, inside the 1-hour SLA. 反欺诈 04:35 确认，从检测起 23 分钟，1 小时 SLA 内 Always quote the actual minutes against the SLA. "Inside SLA" or "outside SLA" is the binary the CISO wants.
  • Regulator exposure Does this trigger regulator notification under PDPO? 这是否触发 PDPO 监管通报？ Always ask the regulator-exposure question early. Privacy team confirms, but the SOC flags first.
  • Workstream Three workstreams: forensic, mitigation, lessons learned. 三个工作流：取证、缓解、复盘 A confirmed incident always has three workstreams: forensic (what happened), mitigation (stop the bleeding), lessons (close the loop).
  • 1-page summary By 17:00, a 1-page summary: what happened, scope, containment, residual risk, regulator exposure. 17:00 前 1 页摘要：发生了什么、范围、遏制、残留风险、监管暴露 CISO always asks for 1 page, 5 sections. The structure is fixed; the content varies.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • SOC (Security Operations Center) n. 安全运营中心 24/7 team that triages alerts and coordinates incident response.
  • SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) n. 安全信息与事件管理 Platform that aggregates logs and generates alerts.
  • Alert n. 告警 A SIEM-generated event that needs analyst review.
  • P1 / P2 / P3 n. 优先级 P1 = critical, business impact now; P2 = high; P3 = medium.
  • IOC (Indicator of Compromise) n. 失陷指标 An artefact (IP, domain, hash) that signals a compromise.
  • IOC sweep v. IOC 扫描 Query the SIEM/EDR for known IOCs across the estate.
  • TTP (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures) n. 战术/技术/过程 How an attacker operates; used in threat intel.
  • Containment n. 遏制 Stopping an incident from spreading — isolate endpoint, revoke credentials, block domain.
  • Eradication n. 根除 Removing the attacker's foothold — reimage, reset, harden.
  • Recovery n. 恢复 Restoring normal service with monitoring.
  • Lessons learned n. 经验教训 Post-incident review — what worked, what didn't, what we change.
  • PDPO (Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance) n. 个人资料（私隐）条例 Hong Kong's data protection law.
  • MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication) n. 多因素认证 Login requiring 2+ factors — password + token / biometric.
  • On-call n. 值班 The named analyst who takes the phone outside business hours.
  • CISO (Chief Information Security Officer) n. 首席信息安全官 The senior executive accountable for security.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "X events, Y tickets, Z P1s" — quote the chain, in that order

The SOC standup has a fixed chain: events → tickets → P1s → confirmed incidents. Always quote in that order. The CISO reads the chain top-down; the analyst reads it bottom-up.

SOC 站会有固定链：事件 → 工单 → P1 → 确认事件。总是按顺序报。CISO 自上而下读，分析师自下而上读。

② "Containment complete at HH:MM" — name the time, always

Containment without a time is half a report. Containment complete at 04:12 HKT. The time anchors the incident timeline. The auditor will read the time, not the verb.

遏制没有时间等于半份报告。04:12 HKT 完成遏制。时间锚定事件时间线。审计员读的是时间，不是动词。

③ "Inside the 1-hour SLA" — quote the actual minutes

"We responded quickly" is not enough. Fraud team acknowledged at 04:35, 23 minutes from detection, inside the 1-hour SLA. The minutes are the proof. The SLA is the binary.

"我们响应很快"不够。反欺诈 04:35 确认，从检测起 23 分钟，1 小时 SLA 内。分钟数是证据，SLA 是判定。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • SOC standup is measured in alerts, not feelings. "I had a busy night" is weak. "1,420 events, 38 tickets, 4 P1s, 1 confirmed incident" is professional.
  • Always name the containment time. The CISO will ask. The auditor will read. Time is the anchor of every incident.
  • Quote the SLA, in minutes. "Inside SLA" or "outside SLA" is the binary. The minutes are the proof.
  • Flag the regulator-exposure question early. Privacy team confirms, but the SOC flags first. Ask "PDPO / GDPR / MAS" early — not at the end.
  • Three workstreams for every confirmed incident: forensic, mitigation, lessons learned. The CISO expects this trio; missing one signals an immature SOC.
  • 1-page summary is the CISO's standard ask. Five sections, fixed: what happened, scope, containment, residual risk, regulator exposure. Memorise the structure.
  • Handovers are the SOC's most political moment. Manila → HK → Shanghai. Each shift writes the same chain: events, tickets, P1s, incidents, on-call. Consistency is credibility.
  • Never leave a P1 without a named owner and a containment time. The CISO's first two questions are always: who owns it, and when was it contained.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the SOC standup

Translate the discussion excerpt into natural Chinese.

"Handing over. SOC update from me. Alert volume overnight: 980 SIEM events, 22 opened as tickets, 2 escalated to P1, 1 confirmed incident — a credential stuffing attack against the corporate VPN. Containment status: rate-limiting applied at 02:15 HKT, attacker IPs blocked at 02:40, no accounts compromised, MFA held on all attempts. IOC sweep at 03:00 and 05:00 — both clean. Fraud team acknowledged at 02:50, 35 minutes from detection, inside the 30-minute SLA. Privacy team's view: no personal data exfiltrated, no PDPO notification triggered. Day-shift plan: three workstreams. Workstream 1: forensic analysis of the attack pattern, root cause, lateral movement check. Workstream 2: update the VPN rate-limit policy, push the attacker IP list to the edge firewall, refresh the user-awareness training for IT admins. Workstream 3: lessons learned for the next Cyber Risk Forum. By 17:00, a 1-page summary: what happened, scope, containment, residual risk, regulator exposure."

"交班。SOC 层面同步。夜间告警量：980 条 SIEM 事件，22 个开为工单，2 个升级 P1，1 个确认事件——针对公司 VPN 的撞库攻击。遏制状态：02:15 HKT 启用限速，02:40 拉黑攻击者 IP，无账号失陷，所有尝试都被 MFA 拦下。03:00 和 05:00 IOC 扫描——都干净。反欺诈 02:50 确认，从检测起 35 分钟，30 分钟 SLA 内。隐私团队意见：无个人数据外泄，不触发 PDPO 通报。日班计划：三个工作流。工作流 1：攻击模式取证分析、根因、横向移动检查。工作流 2：更新 VPN 限速策略、把攻击者 IP 推到边缘防火墙、给 IT 管理员刷新安全意识培训。工作流 3：为下次 Cyber Risk Forum 起草经验教训。17:00 前 1 页摘要：发生了什么、范围、遏制、残留风险、监管暴露。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the SOC standup mistake

Which line would damage credibility with the CISO the most? Why?

  1. "Alert volume overnight: 1,420 events, 38 tickets, 4 P1s, 1 confirmed incident."
  2. "Containment complete at 04:12 HKT."
  3. "We had a really busy night and worked really hard on the phishing case."
  4. "By 17:00, a 1-page summary: what happened, scope, containment, residual risk, regulator exposure."

③ is the credibility-damager. "We had a really busy night and worked really hard" is a feelings update, not a SOC update. The CISO wants the chain (events, tickets, P1s, incidents), not feelings. ① quotes the chain. ② names the containment time. ④ confirms the 1-page summary. ③ replaces numbers with adjectives; it signals an immature SOC.

Exercise 3 — Run a 15-minute SOC standup

You are the SOC lead. Overnight, there were 2,100 SIEM events, 45 tickets, 6 P1s, 2 confirmed incidents (one ransomware attempt blocked at EDR, one BEC fraud attempt blocked at MFA). Containment: 04:30 for ransomware, 05:15 for BEC. IOC sweep: 05:00 and 06:30, both clean. Build a 15-minute script: (1) hand over, (2) alert chain, (3) two incidents with containment times, (4) IOC sweep, (5) regulator exposure, (6) day-shift plan with 3 workstreams, (7) CISO 1-page summary by 17:00.

"Handing over. SOC update from me. Alert volume overnight: 2,100 SIEM events, 45 tickets, 6 P1s, 2 confirmed incidents. Incident 1: ransomware attempt blocked at EDR, containment complete at 04:30 HKT, no encryption, no lateral movement, endpoint isolated and reimaged. Incident 2: BEC fraud attempt blocked at MFA, containment complete at 05:15 HKT, no wire transfer, credentials rotated, session invalidated. IOC sweep at 05:00 and 06:30 — both clean. Fraud team acknowledged both at 04:55 and 05:35, inside the 30-minute SLA. Privacy team's view: no personal data exfiltrated on either, no PDPO notification triggered. Day-shift plan, three workstreams per incident. Workstream 1: forensic analysis, root cause, lateral movement check. Workstream 2: update EDR rule, push IOC to the gateway, refresh user-awareness training for finance. Workstream 3: lessons learned for the next Cyber Risk Forum. By 17:00, a 1-page summary: what happened, scope, containment, residual risk, regulator exposure, in the Group Cyber inbox."
