Banking Regulator & Standards Map — Who Expects What

Article 20 · Module 5: Banking CyberSecurity & IAM Context · ITSO English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 HKMA, MAS, FCA, PRA, APRA, OSFI, RBI, CBIRC, PCI-DSS, GDPR, SOX, ISO 27001, NIST

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday 11:00 HKT. You are a senior in the bank's Group Cyber Risk team. The new CISO has asked for a one-page map of the banking regulators and standards the bank operates under, with the 3-5 cyber-relevant expectations of each. The map will be the front page of the Group Cyber Risk Framework. You have 60 minutes to draft it. The bank's footprint is HK, SG, UK, US, India, mainland China, Canada, Australia — eight regulators, plus the global standards. This article is the map.

周五 11:00 香港时间，你是集团网络风险团队的高级成员。新 CISO 要求一张银行监管和标准的一页地图，列出每个的3-5 个网络相关期望。地图将是集团网络风险框架的首页。你有 60 分钟起草。银行足迹覆盖香港、新加坡、英国、美国、印度、中国大陆、加拿大、澳大利亚——八个监管，加上全球标准。本节是地图。

The regulator map is the front page of a CISO's mental model. Get it right, and every audit, every waiver, every incident maps to a named expectation. Get it wrong, and the bank is "compliant" with no definition of compliance. Speak the regulators' language — supervisory letter, attestation, evidence pack, iCAST, cyber resilience — and the controls follow.

监管地图是 CISO 心理模型的第一页。做对了，每次审计、每次豁免、每次事件都映射到具名期望。做错了，银行"合规"但没有合规的定义。说监管的话——监管函、合规声明、证据包、iCAST、网络韧性——控制自然跟上。

2. The Map — 8 Regulators, 5 Global Standards

① Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA)

  • Regulator: HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority) — the banking regulator of Hong Kong.
  • Key framework: SPM (Supervisory Policy Manual) — module SA-2 on outsourcing, module TM-G-1 on technology risk management, and the Cyber Resilience Assessment Framework (C-RAF).
  • iCAST: Intelligence-led Cyber Attack Simulation Testing — the regulator's red-team exercise. The bank participates annually.
  • Cyber Resilience: the higher-level framework covering 7 domains: governance, identification, protection, detection, response, recovery, learning. Tier-1 findings are reserved for the most serious gaps.
  • Expectations (3-5 cyber-relevant):
    1. Phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access (Article 18).
    2. Independent assurance over critical systems (annual external pen test, annual external vulnerability assessment).
    3. Cyber incident notification within 1 hour of detection for P1, with a full report within 72 hours.
    4. Third-party risk management for all material outsourcing, with annual review.
    5. Board-level cyber risk oversight, with the Board Risk Committee receiving a cyber update at least quarterly.

② Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)

  • Regulator: MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) — the banking regulator of Singapore.
  • Key framework: Technology Risk Management Guidelines (TRM) — 14 cyber hygiene domains, with annual attestation by the bank's senior management.
  • Notice on Cyber Hygiene: the 14 cyber hygiene domains include MFA, patch management, backup, network segmentation, and incident response.
  • Expectations (3-5 cyber-relevant):
    1. 14 cyber hygiene domains attested annually (Article 8).
    2. Phishing-resistant MFA for all customer and operator authentication.
    3. Independent assurance over critical systems (annual external audit, annual external pen test).
    4. Cyber incident notification within 1 hour, with a full report within 14 days.
    5. Third-party risk management for all material outsourcing, with the MAS notification for any new material outsourcing.

③ UK Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA) and Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)

  • PRA: the prudential regulator of UK banks, focused on safety and soundness.
  • FCA: the conduct regulator of UK banks, focused on market conduct and consumer protection.
  • Key frameworks: PRA SS2/21 on operational resilience, FCA Handbook SYSC on senior management accountability, PRA SS1/18 on cyber resilience.
  • Operational resilience: the framework that requires banks to identify important business services, set impact tolerances, and remain within them through disruption. Cyber is one of the sources of disruption.
  • Expectations (3-5 cyber-relevant):
    1. Operational resilience: impact tolerances set for important business services, with self-attestation by the Board.
    2. Phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access.
    3. Independent assurance over critical systems (annual external audit, annual external pen test).
    4. Cyber incident notification to FCA and PRA within 72 hours, with a full report within 14 days.
    5. Third-party risk management for all material outsourcing, with the FCA / PRA notification for any new material outsourcing.

④ US Federal Regulators (OCC / Fed / FDIC) and NYDFS

  • Federal banking regulators: the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), the Federal Reserve (Fed), the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) — collectively, the FFIEC (Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council).
  • FFIEC CAT: the Cybersecurity Assessment Tool, used in the annual examination.
  • NYDFS: the New York State Department of Financial Services, with its 23 NYCRR 500 regulation — the most prescriptive US cyber regulation.
  • Expectations (3-5 cyber-relevant):
    1. 23 NYCRR 500: annual certification of compliance, with a CISO named, a cyber programme documented, MFA, training, incident response, and 72-hour notification to NYDFS.
    2. FFIEC CAT: maturity assessment across 5 domains, with the bank's inherent risk profile and maturity level documented.
    3. Safeguarding customer PII per GLBA, with incident notification to the federal regulator as required.
    4. Phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access.
    5. Independent assurance over critical systems (annual external audit, annual external pen test).

⑤ Other Asia-Pacific Regulators (APRA, OSFI, RBI, CBIRC, HKMA-mainland)

  • APRA (Australia): CPS 234 on information security, with annual attestation to APRA.
  • OSFI (Canada): B-13 on technology and cyber risk management.
  • RBI (India): master direction on outsourcing, IT services, and cyber resilience.
  • CBIRC (mainland China): regulations on commercial bank information technology risk management.
  • Expectations (common pattern):
    1. Phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access.
    2. Independent assurance over critical systems.
    3. Cyber incident notification within 24-72 hours, depending on the jurisdiction.
    4. Third-party risk management for all material outsourcing, with prior approval for new material outsourcing.
    5. Board-level cyber risk oversight, with quarterly Board updates.

⑥ Global Standards — PCI-DSS, GDPR, SOX, ISO 27001, NIST

  • PCI-DSS v4.0: the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard. Applies to all systems that store, process, or transmit cardholder data. 12 requirements, with quarterly external scan by an ASV (Approved Scanning Vendor) and annual ROC (Report on Compliance) by a QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) for Level 1 merchants.
  • GDPR (EU) / UK GDPR / PDPO (HK) / PDPA (SG): data protection regulations. 72-hour breach notification, lawful basis for processing, Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) within 30 days, Data Protection Officer (DPO) for high-risk processing.
  • SOX (US): the Sarbanes-Oxley Act, with Section 404 on internal controls over financial reporting (ITGC). IT general controls — access security, change management, computer operations — are tested annually by the external auditor.
  • ISO 27001:2022: the international information security management system standard. Annex A controls (93 controls in 4 themes: organisational, people, physical, technological), with annual surveillance audit and re-certification every 3 years.
  • NIST CSF 2.0: the US National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework. 6 functions (Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover), used as the bank's internal control framework.

4. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Common pattern is the 80% case The common pattern is the 80% case. The 20% case is regulator-specific. 共同模式是 80% 情况，20% 情况是监管特定的 The framing that prevents the bank from over-engineering for any one regulator. The 80% is the foundation; the 20% is the regulator's voice.
  • Attestation, signed by the CISO 5 attestations the CISO signs every year. CISO 每年签署的 5 个合规声明 An attestation is the CISO's signature on a document that says "we are compliant". A false attestation is a personal liability for the CISO. The 5 are the calendar.
  • Supervisory letter The regulator's voice comes through a supervisory letter. 监管的声音通过监管函发出 A supervisory letter is the regulator's written expectation, addressed to a specific bank or to all banks. The bank tracks every supervisory letter it has received.
  • Evidence pack The evidence pack is what the auditor and the regulator read. 证据包是审计和监管阅读的内容 The evidence pack is the collection of documents (policies, procedures, configurations, logs, screenshots) that proves the controls work. Without the evidence pack, the controls are "trust me".
  • 72-hour notification 72-hour notification to the regulator for any personal data breach. 72 小时内通知监管任何个人数据泄露 72 hours is the GDPR / NYDFS / PDPO standard. The bank's incident response playbook must achieve the 72-hour notification from detection, not from confirmation.
  • Board-level oversight Board-level cyber risk oversight, with the Board Risk Committee receiving a cyber update at least quarterly. 董事会级别网络风险监督，董事会风险委员会至少季度接受网络更新 "Board-level oversight" is the regulator's way of saying the Board is on the hook, not the CISO alone. The quarterly update is the cadence that proves the Board is engaged.

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • The map is the front page of the CISO's mental model. 8 regulators, 5 global standards, 5 common expectations, 5 specific attestations. Memorise the structure; the rest is lookup.
  • Common pattern is the 80% case. The 5 common expectations (MFA, independent assurance, incident notification, third-party risk, board oversight) are the foundation. The 20% is regulator-specific.
  • Attestation is the CISO's signature. A false attestation is a personal liability. The 5 attestations are the calendar; the CISO signs them every year.
  • Supervisory letter is the regulator's voice. The bank tracks every supervisory letter it has received. A supervisory letter is the regulator's written expectation; the bank's response is the file.
  • Evidence pack is what the regulator reads. Without the evidence pack, the controls are "trust me". The pack is the proof; the regulator reads the pack, not the policy.
  • 72-hour notification is the global standard. GDPR, NYDFS, PDPO, PDPA all use 72 hours. The incident response playbook must achieve 72 hours from detection, not from confirmation.
  • Board-level oversight is the regulator's backstop. The Board is on the hook, not the CISO alone. The quarterly update is the cadence that proves the Board is engaged.

Exercise 2 — Spot the regulator-map mistake

Which line would damage the regulator map the most? Why?

  1. "8 regulators: HKMA, MAS, PRA, FCA, OCC, RBI, APRA, CBIRC. 5 global standards: PCI-DSS, GDPR, SOX, ISO 27001, NIST CSF. 5 common expectations. 5 specific attestations. The CISO signs 5 attestations every year."
  2. "We are compliant with everything because we have a cyber programme and a CISO and we do training. The regulators should be happy."
  3. "5 attestations the CISO signs every year: HKMA C-RAF Tier-1, MAS 14 cyber hygiene attestation, PRA SS2/21 impact tolerance self-attestation, NYDFS 23 NYCRR 500 annual certification, APRA CPS 234 annual attestation."
  4. "Evidence pack is what the regulator reads. Without the evidence pack, the controls are 'trust me'. 72-hour notification is the global standard. Board-level oversight is the regulator's backstop."

② is the credibility-damager. "We are compliant with everything because we have a cyber programme and a CISO" replaces the 8 regulators, the 5 global standards, the 5 common expectations, and the 5 specific attestations with generalities. The auditor and the regulator will read ② as evidence that the bank does not know what compliance means. ① names the 8 regulators, the 5 standards, the 5 expectations, the 5 attestations, and the CISO's signature. ③ names the 5 attestations with the regulator and the framework. ④ names the evidence pack, the 72-hour notification, and the board oversight. ② turns a 60-minute draft into a regulatory finding.

Exercise 3 — Build a regulator map for a smaller bank

You are the CISO of a smaller bank with footprint in only 2 jurisdictions: HK and SG. The bank's cyber programme must be compliant with HKMA and MAS, plus PCI-DSS for card data and GDPR for any EU customer. Build the map: (1) regulators, (2) common pattern (3-5 expectations), (3) specific attestations (2), (4) global standards (2), (5) CISO's annual calendar.

"Section 1 — Regulators. HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority) and MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore). 2 regulators, 2 global standards (PCI-DSS, GDPR). Section 2 — Common pattern. (1) Phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access. (2) Independent assurance over critical systems (annual external pen test, annual external vulnerability assessment). (3) Cyber incident notification within 1 hour for P1, with a full report within 72 hours. (4) Third-party risk management for all material outsourcing, with annual review. (5) Board-level cyber risk oversight, with the Board Risk Committee receiving a cyber update at least quarterly. Section 3 — Specific attestations. (a) HKMA Cyber Resilience Assessment (C-RAF) Tier-1 attestation, signed annually. (b) MAS 14 cyber hygiene attestation, signed annually. Section 4 — Global standards. PCI-DSS v4.0 for card data (annual ROC by QSA, quarterly ASV scan). GDPR for any EU customer (72-hour breach notification, DSAR within 30 days, DPO named). Section 5 — CISO's annual calendar. Q1: HKMA C-RAF attestation, MAS 14 cyber hygiene attestation. Q2: PCI-DSS ROC, GDPR DSAR review. Q3: HKMA iCAST participation, MAS TRM review. Q4: Board cyber update, annual cyber training, incident response plan refresh. Every month: cyber dashboard to the Board Risk Committee. The 5 expectations are the foundation; the 2 attestations are the CISO's signature; the 2 standards are the control framework; the 12-month calendar is the discipline."
