Convincing MD to Invest in MFA / PAM — Risk, Cost, Regulator, Peer, ROI

Article 18 · Module 4: Difficult Security Conversations · ITSO English

⏱ 8 min read 🟠 Advanced 🎯 MD, sponsor, board — risk, cost, regulator, peer benchmark, ROI, named ask

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Monday 10:00 HKT. You are the Group Cyber Risk Lead. You have a 30-minute slot with Steven, MD of Group Technology, to ask for $4.2M over 18 months to roll out phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2) for 8,500 employees and Privileged Access Management (PAM) for 1,200 privileged accounts. The previous ask (12 months ago) was deferred — Steven said "show me the regulator expectation, the peer benchmark, and the ROI". You now have all three. Steven is fiscal; he will want the number, the comparators, and the consequence of saying no.

周一 10:00 香港时间，你是集团网络风险负责人。你与集团技术 MD Steven 有 30 分钟档期，申请420 万美元、18 个月内为 8,500 名员工推广抗钓鱼 MFA（FIDO2），为 1,200 个特权账户推广特权访问管理（PAM）。12 个月前的申请被推迟——Steven 说"给我看监管期望、同业基准和 ROI"。你现在有了这三项。Steven 重视财务；他会要数字、对比、不做的后果。

Convincing an MD to invest in security is a numbers conversation dressed as a risk conversation. The instinct is to lead with the breach story. The professional pattern is: lead with the regulator expectation, anchor with peer benchmark, walk the ROI, name the consequence of saying no, end with the named ask. The MD remembers the number; the CISO remembers the discipline.

说服 MD 投资安全是穿着风险外衣的数字对话。本能是以泄露故事开头。专业模式是：以监管期望开头，用同业基准锚定，过 ROI，具名不做的后果，以具名申请结尾。MD 记住数字；CISO 记住纪律。

2. The Investment Ask — 6 Sections, 30 Minutes

You (Cyber Risk): Steven, thanks for the slot. I'm back on the MFA / PAM investment — last time you asked for the regulator expectation, the peer benchmark, and the ROI. I have all three today. The ask: $4.2M, 18 months, FIDO2 for 8,500 employees and PAM for 1,200 privileged accounts.Steven，感谢档期。我回来谈 MFA / PAM 投资——上次你要监管期望、同业基准和 ROI。今天我都有。申请：420 万美元，18 个月，为 8,500 员工部署 FIDO2，为 1,200 特权账户部署 PAM。
You (Cyber Risk): Regulator expectation. HKMA Cyber Resilience Assessment 2025 (iCAST): "phishing-resistant MFA for all privileged access" is a Tier-1 finding for any bank that has not deployed it. MAS TRM 2024: "strong customer and operator authentication" is in the 14 cyber hygiene domains. PRA SS2/21 (UK): "multi-factor authentication for all privileged access" is in the top 3 expectations. FCA: "operational resilience includes identity controls" is the latest supervisory letter. Bottom line: every major regulator we operate under now expects phishing-resistant MFA and PAM. A bank without them is in the regulator's sights.监管期望。HKMA 网络韧性评估 2025（iCAST）："所有特权访问的抗钓鱼 MFA"是任何未部署的银行的 Tier-1 发现。MAS TRM 2024："强客户和操作员认证"在 14 个网络卫生域中。PRA SS2/21（UK）："所有特权访问的多因素认证"是前三期望。FCA："运营韧性包括身份控制"是最新监管函。底线：我们运营所在的每个主要监管都期望抗钓鱼 MFA 和 PAM。没有它们的银行在监管视线内。
You (Cyber Risk): Peer benchmark. The 5 peer banks (HSBC, Standard Chartered, Citi, JPM, Deutsche) all have phishing-resistant MFA for privileged access in production. 3 of the 5 have FIDO2 rolled out to all employees; 2 are in rollout. PAM is universal in the peer set. We are the only one of the 5 with password + SMS as the default for privileged access. The peer gap is visible to the regulator — the iCAST team benchmarks us against the peer set every year.同业基准。5 家同业银行（汇丰、渣打、花旗、JPM、德意志）都已在生产中为特权访问部署抗钓鱼 MFA。5 家中 3 家已将 FIDO2 推广到所有员工；2 家在推广中。PAM 在同业中是普遍的。我们是 5 家中唯一将密码 + SMS 作为特权访问默认的银行。同业差距对监管可见——iCAST 团队每年按同业基准评估我们。
You (Cyber Risk): ROI. Three numbers. Number 1 — cost of doing nothing. Industry data (IBM Cost of a Data Breach 2025): the average cost of a credential-based breach is $4.8M per incident. In the last 3 years, we have had 2 credential-based incidents (phishing, credential stuffing), average cost $2.1M each. Probability of a third incident in the next 24 months without MFA / PAM: high, given the threat landscape. Expected loss: $4.8M × 0.6 probability = $2.9M over 24 months.ROI。三个数字。数字 1——不做的成本。行业数据（IBM 数据泄露成本 2025）：基于凭证的泄露平均成本为每事件 480 万美元。过去 3 年，我们有 2 起基于凭证的事件（钓鱼、撞库），平均成本 210 万美元。未来 24 个月内无 MFA / PAM 情况下第三起事件的概率：高，考虑到威胁态势。预计损失：480 万 × 0.6 概率 = 24 个月内 290 万美元。
You (Cyber Risk): Number 2 — investment. $4.2M over 18 months: $2.4M FIDO2 (hardware keys + platform integration), $1.5M PAM (vendor + integration), $0.3M change management and training. Payback period: 18 months against the expected loss.数字 2——投资。18 个月 420 万：FIDO2 240 万（硬件密钥 + 平台集成），PAM 150 万（厂商 + 集成），变更管理和培训 30 万。回收期：相对预计损失 18 个月。
You (Cyber Risk): Number 3 — cost of a regulator-driven remediation. If the regulator issues a Tier-1 finding and a remediation order, we will be required to deploy MFA / PAM within 12 months, with no say in vendor or phasing, and the cost is typically 30-40% higher than a planned rollout. Estimated cost in a regulator-driven scenario: $5.5-5.9M, with the same 12-month deadline.数字 3——监管驱动的整改成本。如果监管发出 Tier-1 发现和整改令，我们将被要求在 12 个月内部署 MFA / PAM，无厂商或分阶段发言权，成本通常比计划推广高 30-40%。监管驱动场景的预计成本：550-590 万，同样的 12 个月期限。
Steven: Steven: Bottom line.Steven：底线。
You (Cyber Risk): Bottom line: $4.2M now buys MFA / PAM in 18 months, on our phasing, with our vendors. Saying no: 70% probability of a $4.8M breach in 24 months, and 30% probability of a regulator-driven $5.5-5.9M deployment in 12 months. The plan-positive scenario is cheaper than both risk scenarios. Three asks: sign off the $4.2M today, name me the executive sponsor for the steering committee, and back the case at the next Capex Committee.底线：现在 420 万，18 个月内按我们的分阶段、我们的厂商部署 MFA / PAM。说不：24 个月内 70% 概率发生 480 万泄露，30% 概率发生监管驱动的 12 个月内 550-590 万部署。计划正向场景比两个风险场景都便宜。三个请求：今天签批 420 万，为 steering committee 具名执行 sponsor，在下次 Capex 委员会背书该案。
Steven: Steven: Approved $4.2M. I'll sponsor the steering committee. I'll back the case at Capex. Send me the executive summary by 17:00 HKT today.Steven：批准 420 万。我担任 steering committee 的 sponsor。我会在 Capex 背书。今天 17:00 HKT 前发我执行摘要。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Regulator expectation first Every major regulator we operate under now expects phishing-resistant MFA and PAM. 我们运营所在的每个主要监管都期望抗钓鱼 MFA 和 PAM Always lead with the regulator. The MD will not argue with HKMA, MAS, PRA, and FCA in the same paragraph. The regulator expectation is the foundation; the rest of the case is built on it.
  • Peer benchmark We are the only one of the 5 peer banks with password + SMS as the default for privileged access. 我们是 5 家同业银行中唯一将密码 + SMS 作为特权访问默认的 "Only one of 5" is the line that ends the debate. The MD is being benchmarked by the regulator against the peer set. Being the only laggard is a career risk for the MD.
  • Cost of doing nothing Cost of doing nothing: $4.8M expected loss over 24 months. 不做的成本：24 个月内预计损失 480 万 "Cost of doing nothing" is the line that frames the ask. The MD is not being asked to spend money; the MD is being asked to avoid a bigger loss.
  • Payback period Payback period: 18 months against the expected loss. 回收期：相对预计损失 18 个月 "Payback period" is the financial language that earns the Capex Committee's attention. Always name the payback period in months or years.
  • Regulator-driven remediation Regulator-driven scenario: $5.5-5.9M with a 12-month deadline. 监管驱动场景：550-590 万，12 个月期限 The regulator-driven scenario is the "say no" cost. It is always more expensive than the planned scenario, and on a tighter deadline. The MD sees this number and remembers it.
  • Plan-positive cheaper than risk scenarios The plan-positive scenario is cheaper than both risk scenarios. 计划正向场景比两个风险场景都便宜 This is the line that closes the deal. The MD is being shown that spending $4.2M is cheaper than both doing nothing and waiting for the regulator. The math is the answer.
  • Three asks Three asks: sign off the budget, name the executive sponsor, back the case at Capex. 三个请求：签批预算、具名执行 sponsor、Capex 背书 Three asks, paired with the three sections (regulator, peer, ROI). Each ask requires the MD's authority — not the CISO's. The MD is being asked to use the authority only the MD has.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • FIDO2 n. FIDO2 标准 Phishing-resistant authentication standard (passkeys, hardware keys).
  • PAM (Privileged Access Management) n. 特权访问管理 The system that controls and audits privileged accounts.
  • Phishing-resistant MFA n. 抗钓鱼 MFA MFA that cannot be bypassed by a phishing site (FIDO2, certificate-based).
  • Tier-1 finding n. Tier-1 发现 A regulator's most serious finding category.
  • iCAST n. iCAST HKMA's Intelligence-led Cyber Attack Simulation Testing.
  • MAS TRM n. MAS TRM The Monetary Authority of Singapore's Technology Risk Management Guidelines.
  • PRA SS2/21 n. PRA SS2/21 UK Prudential Regulation Authority's supervisory statement on operational resilience.
  • Peer benchmark n. 同业基准 The comparison of the bank's posture against a named peer set.
  • Cost of doing nothing n. 不做的成本 The expected loss from inaction, expressed in dollars.
  • Payback period n. 回收期 The time for the investment to recover its cost against the expected loss.
  • Regulator-driven remediation n. 监管驱动整改 A remediation ordered by the regulator, on the regulator's timeline.
  • Capex Committee n. 资本支出委员会 The committee that approves capital expenditure above a threshold.

5. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • 6 sections, fixed order. Regulator expectation, peer benchmark, ROI (cost of doing nothing, investment, regulator-driven), bottom line, three asks. The MD reads the structure, not the prose.
  • Lead with the regulator. HKMA, MAS, PRA, FCA in the same paragraph is the foundation. The MD will not argue with the four largest regulators the bank operates under.
  • Anchor with peer benchmark. "Only one of 5" is the line that ends the debate. The MD is being benchmarked by the regulator against the peer set every year.
  • Three numbers in the ROI. Cost of doing nothing, investment, regulator-driven remediation. The first frames the ask; the second is the ask; the third is the "say no" cost.
  • Payback period in months or years. The financial language that earns the Capex Committee's attention. Always name the period.
  • Bottom line: plan-positive cheaper than risk scenarios. The line that closes the deal. The math is the answer; the regulator is the justification; the peer is the benchmark.
  • Three asks, paired with the three sections. Each ask requires the MD's authority — not the CISO's. The MD is being asked to use the authority only the MD has.

6. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the bottom-line ask

Translate the bottom-line ask into natural Chinese.

"Bottom line: $4.2M now buys MFA / PAM in 18 months, on our phasing, with our vendors. Saying no: 70% probability of a $4.8M breach in 24 months, and 30% probability of a regulator-driven $5.5-5.9M deployment in 12 months. The plan-positive scenario is cheaper than both risk scenarios. Three asks: sign off the $4.2M today, name me the executive sponsor for the steering committee, and back the case at the next Capex Committee. Approved $4.2M. I'll sponsor the steering committee. I'll back the case at Capex. Send me the executive summary by 17:00 HKT today."

"底线：现在 420 万，18 个月内按我们的分阶段、我们的厂商部署 MFA / PAM。说不：24 个月内 70% 概率发生 480 万泄露，30% 概率发生监管驱动的 12 个月内 550-590 万部署。计划正向场景比两个风险场景都便宜。三个请求：今天签批 420 万，为 steering committee 具名执行 sponsor，在下次 Capex 委员会背书该案。批准 420 万。我担任 steering committee 的 sponsor。我会在 Capex 背书。今天 17:00 HKT 前发我执行摘要。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the investment ask mistake

Which line would damage the investment ask the most? Why?

  1. "Regulator expectation: HKMA, MAS, PRA, FCA all expect phishing-resistant MFA and PAM. Peer: we are the only one of 5 with password + SMS. ROI: $4.2M investment, 18-month payback, regulator-driven scenario is $5.5-5.9M."
  2. "We need to invest in MFA because cyber attacks are scary and the press is full of breach stories. Please approve $4.2M."
  3. "Three asks: sign off the $4.2M today, name the executive sponsor, back the case at Capex. Plan-positive cheaper than both risk scenarios."
  4. "Payback period: 18 months against the expected loss of $2.9M. Cost of doing nothing: $4.8M expected loss over 24 months."

② is the credibility-damager. "Cyber attacks are scary and the press is full of breach stories" replaces the regulator expectation, the peer benchmark, and the ROI with fear. The MD will respond with "give me a number" and the conversation has to restart. ① cites four regulators, names the peer gap, and walks the ROI. ③ names the three asks and the bottom line. ④ names the payback period and the cost of doing nothing. ② turns a 30-minute slot into a 3-hour argument, with no decision.

Exercise 3 — Build an investment ask

You are the Cyber Risk Lead. The bank's Data Loss Prevention (DLP) platform is 7 years old, end-of-vendor-support in 18 months. Replacement cost: $6.8M over 24 months. The MD has asked for the regulator expectation, the peer benchmark, and the ROI. Build the 6 sections: (1) regulator expectation, (2) peer benchmark, (3) cost of doing nothing, (4) investment, (5) regulator-driven scenario, (6) three asks.

"Section 1 — Regulator expectation. HKMA Cyber Resilience Assessment 2025: 'data loss prevention for customer data in motion and at rest' is a Tier-1 expectation. MAS TRM 2024: 'data loss prevention' is in the 14 cyber hygiene domains, with annual attestation. PRA SS2/21: 'data protection controls including DLP' is in the top 5 expectations. FCA: 'operational resilience includes data protection controls' is the latest supervisory letter. Section 2 — Peer benchmark. The 5 peer banks all have DLP platforms that are ≤ 4 years old, with vendor support ongoing. We are the only one of the 5 with a DLP platform that is end-of-support in 18 months. The peer gap is visible to the iCAST team. Section 3 — Cost of doing nothing. Industry data: the average cost of a data exfiltration incident involving customer PII is $5.2M per incident. In the last 5 years, we have had 3 DLP-relevant incidents (2 unintended data sharing, 1 insider exfiltration attempt), average cost $1.8M each. Probability of a DLP-relevant incident in the next 24 months without a supported platform: high, given the threat landscape. Expected loss: $5.2M × 0.5 probability = $2.6M over 24 months. Section 4 — Investment. $6.8M over 24 months: $4.5M platform (vendor licence + integration), $1.5M data classification engine, $0.8M change management and training. Payback period: 30 months against the expected loss. Section 5 — Regulator-driven scenario. If the regulator issues a Tier-1 finding and a remediation order, we will be required to replace the DLP platform within 18 months, with no say in vendor or phasing, and the cost is typically 30-40% higher than a planned replacement. Estimated cost: $8.8-9.5M, with an 18-month deadline. Section 6 — Three asks. Ask 1: sign off the $6.8M today. Ask 2: name me the executive sponsor for the steering committee. Ask 3: back the case at the next Capex Committee. Bottom line: $6.8M now buys a supported DLP platform in 24 months, on our phasing, with our vendor. Saying no: 50% probability of a $5.2M incident in 24 months, and 30% probability of a regulator-driven $8.8-9.5M replacement in 18 months. The plan-positive scenario is cheaper than both risk scenarios."
