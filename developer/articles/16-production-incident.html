Production Incident Communication

Article 16 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Developer English

⏱ 9 min read 🔴 Advanced 🚨 Communicate clearly under pressure: status, impact, ETA, mitigation

1. Scenario 场景背景

It's 2:47 AM HKT. A P1 incident has been declared: the HSBCnet payment callback service is throwing 500 errors, and ~30% of inbound cross-border payments are failing. You are the on-call developer this week. You need to communicate clearly in the #incident-war-room Slack channel.

香港时间凌晨 2:47。发生 P1 事故：HSBCnet 支付回调服务 抛 500 错误， 约 30% 的跨境支付失败。你是本周 on-call 开发。需要在 #incident-war-room Slack 频道清晰沟通。

4. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Severity 🚨 INCIDENT DECLARED — P1 事故声明 — P1 Use severity prefixes. P1 = critical, P2 = high, P3 = medium, P4 = low.
  • Structure Service: / Symptom: / Impact: / Mitigation: 服务 / 症状 / 影响 / 缓解 Use the same structure for every incident update. Scannable in seconds.
  • Time Started investigating… 开始调查 Always state what you're doing right now. "Investigating… / Rolling back… / Monitoring…"
  • Tag Tagging @Priya, @Sarah, @SRE. @Priya @Sarah @SRE Tag the right people. TL + PM + SRE for P1.
  • ETA Rollback ETA 5 min (3:00 AM HKT). 回滚 ETA 5 分钟（3:00 AM HKT） Always give an ETA for the next action. Even if rough.
  • Workaround Workaround: None yet. 临时方案：暂无 If no workaround exists, say so explicitly. Don't leave the team wondering.
  • Update Next update: 3:00 AM HKT or sooner. 下次更新：3:00 AM HKT 或更早 Always commit to a next update time. Stops the team from pinging you.
  • Resolve RESOLVED. Service fully restored. 已解决。服务完全恢复。 Always end the incident with a clear RESOLVED statement.

5. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • P1 / P2 / P3 / P4 n. 事故优先级 (1-4) P1 = critical (full outage / major customer impact). P4 = minor.
  • on-call adj. on-call 值班 The person responsible for handling incidents during a specific shift.
  • rollback n. 回滚 Reverting a deployment to the previous version. Common mitigation for P1s.
  • war room n. 作战室 (事故沟通频道) A dedicated Slack channel / bridge for an active incident. All comms go here.
  • root cause n. 根本原因 The fundamental reason for the incident. Identified in the post-mortem.
  • post-mortem n. 事后分析 / 复盘会 A meeting after the incident to review what happened, root cause, and improvements.
  • mitigation n. 缓解措施 (临时方案) The immediate action to reduce impact. Different from resolution (full fix).
  • backlog / retry queue 积压 / 重试队列 Items that failed and are waiting to be retried. Drains over time after the fix.

6. Grammar Notes 语法点

① Time Anchoring in Incident Updates

Always anchor updates in time:

  • UPDATE 1 — 8 min in (relative to incident start)
  • 2:55 AM HKT (absolute, with timezone)
  • Detected: 2:46 AM HKT (incident start time)

② Passive vs Active Voice in Status

In incident communication, both are used, but differently:

  • Active (what you're doing): "Rolling back now." / "Investigating."
  • Passive (what's happening): "Rollback complete at 3:02." / "Service restored."

③ "ETA" vs "Status" vs "Plan"

Three pieces of information every update needs:

  • ETA: Rollback ETA 5 min (3:00 AM HKT).
  • Status: Error rate dropping — 5xx at 3%.
  • Plan: Monitoring drain, next update at 3:20.

7. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🚨 The 5 Golden Rules of Incident Communication
  1. Update early, update often. Silence is the worst enemy. No update for 20 min? Ping "Still investigating, no change."
  2. Stick to facts, not speculation. Say "Investigating", not "It's the new code" — until you know.
  3. Always commit to a next update time. Stops people from flooding you with "any update?"
  4. Use the same structure for every update. Service / Symptom / Impact / Mitigation / ETA. Scannable in 5 seconds.
  5. End with a clear RESOLVED. People need to know when to stop worrying.
⚠️ HSBC Incident Culture
  • P1 incidents must have a war room within 15 minutes of detection. No exceptions.
  • Customer-facing comms are owned by the Corporate Communications team. Do not communicate directly with customers.
  • Regulator notification: for P1s impacting regulated services, InfoSec + Legal must be looped in within 30 min.
  • Post-mortem is mandatory for every P1/P2, due within 5 business days.
  • Blame-free culture — focus on systems and processes, not individuals.
