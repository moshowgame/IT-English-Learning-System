Sprint Retrospective

Article 07 · Module 2: Meeting Workshops · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🔄 Reflect on what worked, what didn't, what to improve

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is the end of the sprint. Your team is doing a Sprint Retrospective on Teams. David (SM) uses the "Start / Stop / Continue" format. You need to give honest, constructive feedback — not just complain, not just praise.

冲刺结束了，团队在 Teams 上开 Sprint 回顾会。David（SM）用 Start / Stop / Continue 格式。你需要给出诚实、有建设性的反馈——不只是抱怨，也不只是表扬。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

David (SM): OK, last item on the agenda — sprint retrospective. You know the format — Start, Stop, Continue. Wei, why don't you kick us off?好，今天议程最后一项——冲刺回顾会。大家都知道格式——Start、Stop、Continue。Wei，你先开始？
You: Sure. Quick reflections from me.好，我简单说一下反思。
You: Start — I'd like us to start doing mid-sprint check-ins with InfoSec on long-pending tickets. We had the DBA blocker and the KMS key both sit for 3+ days this sprint. A 15-min sync on Wednesday would have caught it earlier.Start——希望我们开始做冲刺中期对 InfoSec 长 pending ticket 的同步。本冲刺DBA 阻塞和KMS key都卡了3天以上，周三一次15分钟同步就能早点发现。
You: Stop — I'd like us to stop taking 8-pointers in the same sprint as 5-pointers, unless we have clear capacity. This sprint we had 21 points of work and the team was stretched thin.Stop——希望我们停止在同一冲刺里同时接 8 点和 5 点的卡，除非有明确容量。本冲刺 21 点的活，团队压力很大。
You: Continue — the pair programming on the sanctions screening integration worked really well. Sam and I caught a lot of edge cases faster together. I'd like us to keep doing that.Continue——制裁名单筛查的结对编程效果很好。我和 Sam 一起更快发现了不少边界场景，希望继续保持。
David (SM): Thanks, Wei. Really constructive. Quick one on the mid-sprint InfoSec sync — who would you want in the room?谢谢 Wei，很有建设性。问一下冲刺中期 InfoSec 同步——你觉得谁应该参加？
You: My suggestion — the SM (you), one tech lead, and the InfoSec partner for our portfolio. 15 minutes max, just to walk through the open tickets. No deep dives.我的建议是——SM（你）、一个技术负责人，加我们组合的InfoSec 接口人。最多 15 分钟，过一下开放的 ticket，不深挖。
Priya (TL): I second that. One thing to add — the start / stop / continue format is good, but for the next sprint I'd love to try the 4Ls — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For — to mix it up. Any objections?我附议。补充一点——Start/Stop/Continue 格式挺好，但下个冲刺我想试一下 4Ls——Liked、Learned、Lacked、Longed For，换换口味。有反对意见吗？
You: Sounds good to me. Worth trying at least once.我觉得可以。值得试一次。
Sam: Quick concern — I find 4Ls harder to fill in on the spot. Can we prep them in the channel the day before?一个关注点——我觉得 4Ls 现场填比较费劲。能不能在频道里提前一天预填？
Priya (TL): Good idea, Sam. Let's do that — David can post a template on the day before retro.好主意 Sam。就这么办——David 在回顾会前一天发个模板到频道。
David (SM): Lovely. Action item — we'll try the 4Ls format next sprint and pre-fill in the channel. Wei, can you own the mid-sprint InfoSec sync? Just to set it up, no need to run it.好。行动项——下个冲刺试 4Ls 格式，并在频道预填。Wei，冲刺中期 InfoSec 同步你能负责吗？只要搭起来，不用亲自主持。
You: Sure, happy to. I'll set up a recurring meeting and post the invite in the channel by end of week.好的，乐意接。我会建一个周期性会议，把邀请发到频道里，周末前搞定。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Reflect Quick reflections from me. 我说说我的反思 Standard retro opening. Reflections = looking back. Thoughts is also fine.
  • SSC Start / Stop / Continue 开始做 / 停止做 / 继续做 Classic retrospective format. Each bucket is a behavior, not a person.
  • Suggest I'd like us to start doing X. 我希望我们开始做 X Frame suggestions as "I'd like us to…" — inclusive, non-prescriptive.
  • Unless Stop doing X unless we have Y. 停止做 X，除非有 Y Unless is great for making nuanced suggestions that acknowledge exceptions.
  • Praise Worked really well. 效果很好 Don't skip the positive — Continue is as important as Stop.
  • Action Action item — Wei, can you own the X? 行动项——Wei，你能负责 X 吗？ Every retro should end with at least one action item and an owner.
  • Mix Worth trying at least once. 值得试一下 Openness to experiments. Worth a try / let's see how it goes.
  • Object Any objections? 有反对意见吗？ Use to invite dissent politely. Any concerns? / any pushback?.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • retrospective (retro) n. 回顾会 End-of-sprint meeting to reflect on the process. NOT a status meeting.
  • Start / Stop / Continue n. 三栏式回顾格式 A widely-used retro format. Focus on behaviors, not people.
  • 4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For) n. 四L回顾格式 A more reflective retro format. Good for mature teams.
  • action item n. 行动项 A concrete task that comes out of a meeting. Always has an owner.
  • stretched thin adj. 捉襟见肘 / 压力很大 A team or person over-allocated. Better than "overworked" which sounds dramatic.
  • edge case n. 边界场景 / 极端情况 Unusual scenarios that might break the normal flow.
  • pushback n. 反对 / 异议 Direct disagreement on a proposal. "Any pushback?" = any disagreements?

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I'd like us to…" — Inclusive Suggestions

Frame team-level suggestions inclusively, not as commands:

  • ❌ "We should start doing X." (sounds commanding)
  • ✅ "I'd like us to start doing X." (suggests, invites discussion)
  • ✅ "I think we should start doing X." (slightly more direct)

② "Worth doing" — 低成本的建议表达

"Worth trying" / "worth a shot" softens experimentation suggestions:

  • Worth trying at least once.
  • Worth a shot — we can always revert if it doesn't work.
  • Worth exploring in the next sprint.

③ "Unless" — 留出例外空间

Using unless shows nuanced thinking:

  • ❌ "Stop taking 8-pointers." (absolute)
  • ✅ "Stop taking 8-pointers unless we have clear capacity." (nuanced)

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🔄 Retrospective Culture at HSBC
  • Critique the work, not the people. "The PR review was slow" ✅ vs. "Sam reviews PRs too slowly" ❌.
  • Always have an action item. A retro without action items is just complaining. Insist on a concrete next step with an owner.
  • Mix up formats. Repeating the same format gets stale. Try Mad / Sad / Glad, 4Ls, Sailboat.
  • Time-box the "Stop" bucket. Negativity spirals. If the team spends 25 minutes on Stop, move on.
  • Action items are reviewed next retro. If you said "we'll do X", you better do X or explain why not next time.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ❌ "I have no feedback, the sprint was fine." (passive-aggressive or disengaged)
  • ✅ "It was a solid sprint. One thing to flag is…" (engaged)
  • ❌ Naming and blaming individuals.
  • ❌ Suggesting things with no owner — they will never happen.

7. Practice 练习题

✍️ Practice Drills
Q1. Translate to natural English:

"开始：希望我们开始做代码审查的轮值制度，本冲刺大家都很忙。停止：希望我们停止在站会里深挖技术细节，应该线下讨论。继续：双周一次的 Tech Talk 做得很好。"

"Start — I'd like us to start doing a rotating PR review schedule — this sprint everyone was stretched thin. Stop — I'd like us to stop diving into deep technical details in standup — those should be taken offline. Continue — the bi-weekly Tech Talk has been working really well."
Q2. Frame this as a "Start" item (not "Stop"):

"Stop with the late-night deployments. They cause too many issues."

"Start doing deployments only between 9am-5pm HK time, unless it's a P1 hotfix. The late-night deployments last sprint caused a lot of fatigue and we had two rollbacks."
Q3. Fill in the right retro phrases:

"I'd like us to _____ (start) doing cross-team demos. / Worth _____ (try) the 4Ls next sprint. / Any _____ (object)? / Action item: Wei to own the InfoSec _____ (sync)."

start (after to, use base form) / trying (after worth, use gerund) / objections / sync
Q4. Role-play:

Write your own Start / Stop / Continue for last sprint, with one item in each bucket. Pick from these scenarios: (a) lots of last-minute scope changes, (b) team did great on production incident response, (c) cross-timezone handoffs are still unclear, (d) post-mortem documentation was excellent.

"Quick reflections.

Start — I'd like us to start doing a mid-sprint scope freeze. We had 3 mid-sprint changes this sprint, which derailed our burndown.

Stop — I'd like us to stop assuming UK and HK are 8 hours apart. With daylight saving time, it's actually 7 hours in winter. We had two meetings missed because of this.

Continue — the incident response was excellent. We had a P1 outage on Wednesday, and the team resolved it in 30 minutes. Keep it up."
