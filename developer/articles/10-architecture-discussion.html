Architecture Discussion

Article 10 · Module 2: Meeting Workshops · Developer English

⏱ 9 min read 🔴 Advanced 🏗️ Contribute to design discussions, trade-offs, and decisions

1. Scenario 场景背景

The platform architect Rajesh (based in Pune, India) is presenting the target architecture for the customer onboarding service migration from on-premise WebSphere to AWS EKS. The team is discussing whether to use the Strangler Fig pattern or the big-bang cutover. You are a backend developer and want to contribute meaningfully.

平台架构师 Rajesh（印度浦那）正在介绍 客户开户服务 从本地 WebSphere 迁移到 AWS EKS 的目标架构。团队在讨论用 Strangler Fig 模式 还是 大爆炸切换。你是一名后端开发，想有意义地参与。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Rajesh (Architect): OK, so two options on the table for the migration: Strangler Fig vs. big-bang cutover. Quick recap — Strangler means we run both old and new in parallel and gradually shift traffic over weeks. Big-bang is a single cutover weekend. Thoughts?好的，迁移有两个方案：Strangler Fig 模式 vs. 大爆炸切换。简单回顾——Strangler 是新旧并行运行、几周内逐步切流量；大爆炸是周末一次性切换。怎么看？
You: Quick thought from the dev side — I think the Strangler pattern makes more sense for us, for a few reasons.从开发角度快速说下——我觉得对我们来说 Strangler 模式更合适，理由有几点。
You: First, risk. With a big-bang, if anything goes wrong on cutover weekend, we have a P1 incident with no easy rollback. With Strangler, if the new service has a bug, we can route 100% of traffic back to the old service in minutes.第一是风险。大爆炸切换周末出问题，就是 P1 事故，没有简单回滚。Strangler 模式如果新服务有 bug，几分钟内就能把 100% 流量切回老服务。
You: Second, data consistency. Big-bang means we have to migrate the entire customer DB in one shot — high risk of data corruption. With Strangler, we can migrate customer-by-customer or account-by-account, and validate as we go.第二是数据一致性。大爆炸要求一次性迁移整个客户数据库——数据损坏风险高。Strangler 模式可以逐客户或逐账户迁移，边走边验证。
You: Third, observability. Running both in parallel gives us real production data to compare — we can see if the new service is behaving the same as the old one before fully cutting over. With big-bang, we find out in production.第三是可观测性。并行运行能让我们对比真实生产数据——完全切换前能看到新旧服务行为是否一致。大爆炸就只能在生产环境里发现了。
Rajesh (Architect): Those are good points, Wei. One concern with Strangler is the operational complexity — running two stacks for 2-3 months is non-trivial. Have you thought about the cost overhead of running both?说得不错，Wei。Strangler 的一个顾虑是运维复杂度——同时维护两套系统 2-3 个月不简单。你考虑过并行运行的成本开销吗？
You: Yeah, I have. From what I understand, the AWS cost for the new EKS cluster is roughly 30% of what we pay for the WebSphere on-prem — so we're effectively saving even while running both. But more importantly, a P1 rollback in big-bang would cost us way more in reputational damage and incident response than the 2-3 months of dual-running cost.是的，我考虑过。据我了解，新 EKS 集群的 AWS 成本大约是本地 WebSphere 的 30%——并行运行实际上也是省钱的。但更重要的是，大爆炸里一次 P1 回滚造成的声誉损失和事故响应成本，远超 2-3 个月双轨运行的成本。
Priya (TL): I'd second the Strangler approach, but add one more thing — we should set explicit cutover milestones: 1% canary at week 1, 10% at week 3, 50% at week 5, 100% at week 8. Hard stop if error rate goes above 0.5% at any milestone.我支持 Strangler 方案，但要加一点——应该设定明确的切换里程碑：第 1 周 1% 灰度，第 3 周 10%，第 5 周 50%，第 8 周 100%。任何里程碑错误率超过 0.5% 就硬性停止。
Rajesh (Architect): Solid proposal. Let me capture this in the ADR — Architecture Decision Record. Wei, can you draft the dev-side rationale section? I'll add the cost analysis and operational complexity, and we'll circulate it for review before the next architecture board.方案很扎实。我把这个写进 ADR（架构决策记录）。Wei，你来起草开发侧的论证部分吗？我补充成本分析和运维复杂度，下次架构委员会前发出来审阅。
You: Sure, happy to. I'll have a draft by Friday and post it in the channel for review.没问题，很乐意。周五前出初稿，发到频道里供大家审阅。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Quick thought from the dev side. 从开发角度说一个想法 Identify your angle (dev / QA / SRE / PM) to frame your contribution.
  • Structure First / Second / Third — [reason]. 第一 / 第二 / 第三 When contributing to architecture discussions, always structure your points.
  • Trade-off The trade-off is operational complexity vs. risk. 权衡是运维复杂度 vs. 风险 Trade-off is the key word in architecture discussions. Always frame as trade-offs.
  • Risk High risk of data corruption. 数据损坏的高风险 Name the specific risk. "High risk of X" / "Non-trivial chance of Y".
  • Mitigate Running both gives us real production data to compare. 并行运行给我们真实生产数据做对比 Show how the option mitigates a specific risk.
  • Push back One concern with X is… 对 X 有一个关注点 A graceful way to challenge the architect. Never "X is wrong".
  • Data From what I understand, the AWS cost is ~30% of WebSphere. 据我了解，AWS 成本大约是 WebSphere 的 30% Always back up with data when challenging. "From what I understand" hedges uncertainty.
  • Counter But more importantly, the reputational damage would cost way more. 更重要的是，声誉损失成本会高得多 Use but more importantly / but the bigger concern is to pivot the argument.
  • Decision Let me capture this in the ADR. 我把这个记到 ADR 里 ADR = Architecture Decision Record. Standard for documenting design decisions.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Strangler Fig pattern n. 绞杀者无花果模式 (渐进式迁移) Named after the strangler fig tree. Gradually replaces old system with new, one piece at a time.
  • big-bang cutover n. 大爆炸切换 (一次性切换) Cut everything over in a single event. High risk, hard to rollback.
  • canary deployment n. 灰度发布 / 金丝雀部署 Roll out to a small % of users first, monitor, then expand.
  • cutover milestone n. 切换里程碑 Defined checkpoints (e.g., 1%, 10%, 50%, 100%) during a phased migration.
  • ADR (Architecture Decision Record) n. 架构决策记录 A document that captures an important architectural decision: context, options, decision, consequences.
  • AWS EKS AWS AWS 弹性 Kubernetes 服务 Managed Kubernetes service. Common target for containerization.
  • rollback n. 回滚 Reverting to a previous version after a failed deployment.
  • data consistency / corruption 数据一致性 / 数据损坏 Ensuring data is correct and uncorrupted. Critical during migration.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① Structuring Arguments: "First / Second / Third"

When contributing to architecture discussions, always structure. Use signposting words:

  • First, [argument 1].
  • Second, [argument 2].
  • Third, [argument 3].
  • Another point is…
  • On top of that…

② "From what I understand…" — Hedging Data Points

When citing data you're not 100% sure about:

  • From what I understand, the AWS cost is ~30%.
  • Based on the analysis I did last quarter…
  • According to the SRE team…

③ Conditional Structures for Scenarios

When describing a risk scenario, use conditional structures:

  • If anything goes wrong, we have a P1.
  • With Strangler, if the new service has a bug, we can route 100% back.
  • Without parallel running, we'd find out in production.

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🏗️ Contributing to Architecture Discussions

Junior developers often stay silent in architecture discussions. Don't. Your perspective as the person who will actually build and operate the system is invaluable. Tips:

  • Bring data, not just opinions. "The latency is 600ms" beats "It's slow."
  • Frame as trade-offs. Architects love trade-off discussions. Avoid "X is right, Y is wrong."
  • Speak from your angle. "From the dev side…" / "From an operational perspective…" — naming your angle is more credible.
  • Push back with questions, not statements. "Have you considered the cost of dual-running?" invites discussion; "That's too expensive" shuts it down.
  • Use the ADR pattern. If a decision is being made, ask "Can we document this in an ADR?" Shows maturity.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ❌ "I think big-bang is wrong." (judgmental, no reasoning)
  • ✅ "From the dev side, I see three concerns with big-bang: first, rollback risk… second, data consistency… third, observability…"
  • ❌ "This is too complex." (vague, dismissive)
  • ✅ "The operational complexity of running two stacks for 3 months is roughly 30% cost overhead. Have we benchmarked this against the rollback cost of a failed big-bang?"

7. Practice 练习题

✍️ Practice Drills
Q1. Translate to native-sounding architecture English:

"从开发的角度看，我建议用消息队列（Kafka）解耦。不是直接同步调用。原因是：1）解耦后两个服务可以独立扩展；2）失败重试更稳；3）可以记录审计日志。"

"From the dev side, I'd suggest decoupling the two services with Kafka rather than synchronous calls. Three reasons: first, decoupled services can scale independently; second, failure retry is much more robust with a message queue; third, we get built-in audit logging from the Kafka events."
Q2. Push back politely on an architect's proposal:

The architect suggests storing all customer PII in a single PostgreSQL database for simplicity. What's a careful, structured pushback?

"One concern with a single DB: regulatory separation. From what I understand, InfoSec at HSBC requires data residency separation for customer PII — HK customers in HK, mainland in mainland, EU in EU. A single DB complicates this significantly. Have you considered a sharded approach by region, or a per-region DB with a federated query layer on top? Happy to dig into the trade-offs if useful."
Q3. Fill in the right architecture phrases:

"The trade-off is _____ (X) vs. _____ (Y). / That'll _____ (affect) the latency budget. / Have you _____ (consider) the cost? / From the dev _____ (side), I see three concerns."

operational complexity rollback risk / affect / considered / side
Q4. Role-play:

The architect proposes using a single AWS RDS instance for all three microservices (auth, payment, statement) to reduce cost. Write 3 structured concerns and propose an alternative. (Hint: think about blast radius, scaling, compliance.)

"From the dev side, three concerns with a single RDS:
  1. Blast radius — if the RDS goes down, all three services go down. A DB crash in production means customers can't log in, pay, or view statements simultaneously.
  2. Scaling — auth is read-heavy, payment is write-heavy with low latency requirements, statement is batch-heavy. One RDS instance means they fight for the same resources.
  3. Compliance — InfoSec usually requires payment data to be in a separate, more restricted DB with stricter access controls. Co-locating with auth and statement weakens the security boundary.
Alternative: one RDS per service (auth, payment, statement), or AWS Aurora Serverless for the variable-load ones. Slightly higher cost, but much better isolation and resilience."
