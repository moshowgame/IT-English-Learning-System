Politely Pushing Back on Decisions

Article 13 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Developer English

⏱ 9 min read 🔴 Advanced 🤝 Disagree without damaging the relationship

1. Scenario 场景背景

Your PM Sarah (London) has just announced that the team must deliver the OneWealth trade order service one week earlier than originally planned, to align with a HKMA regulatory submission deadline. You strongly believe this is unrealistic — the team is already at 95% capacity and the UAT environment is not yet ready. You need to push back without damaging the relationship.

你的 PM Sarah（伦敦）刚宣布团队必须把 OneWealth 交易订单服务 比原计划 提前一周交付， 以配合 HKMA 监管申报截止日期。你强烈认为不现实——团队已经在 95% 容量，UAT 环境还没准备好。 你需要 不伤关系地 push back。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Sarah (PM): OK team, one thing to flag — HKMA just confirmed the regulatory submission date. We need to pull in the OneWealth delivery by one week. New target: 23rd of next month, not 30th.团队注意——HKMA（金管局）刚确认监管提交日期。OneWealth 交付要提前一周，新目标是下月 23 号，不是 30 号。
David (SM): Ouch. That's tight given where we are. Wei, what do you think?哎呀。按当前进度看很紧。Wei，你怎么想？
You: Sarah, thanks for the heads-up — totally understand the regulatory driver. I'd like to push back gently on the timeline, but not on the regulatory commitment. Three things make me think 23rd is high risk:Sarah，谢谢提前告知——完全理解监管要求。我想温和地就时间表提点意见，但绝不是要反对监管承诺。三点让我觉得 23 号风险很高：
You: First, capacity. The team is already at ~95% allocated this sprint. Pulling in by a week means ~25% additional effort in the same time, with no room for the production bug we know is pending.第一是容量。团队本 sprint 已经 95% 满负荷，提前一周意味着同样时间内多干 25%，而且没有空间处理那个已知要来的生产 bug。
You: Second, UAT. The UAT environment is still being provisioned by SRE — target ready date is the 18th, which only gives us 3 working days for UAT instead of the planned 10. That's not enough for the BA and Compliance to do a proper sign-off.第二是 UAT。UAT 环境 SRE 还在搭建，目标就绪日期是 18 号——只给我们 3 个工作日做 UAT，而不是计划的 10 天。BA 和合规部没有足够时间做正式 sign-off。
You: Third, quality risk. Rushing this is very likely to introduce defects in the trade order flow, which is a revenue-critical path. One bug in production could cost more than a one-week delay.第三是质量风险。赶工很可能在交易订单流程里引入缺陷，而这是营收关键路径。生产环境一个 bug 造成的损失可能比延期一周更大。
You: I want to be clear — I'm not saying no. I think we have three options to explore:强调一下——我不是说不。我觉得有三种方案可以探讨：
You: Option A — descope. We cut the secondary features (e.g., the multi-leg order) and ship the core on the 23rd. Secondary features slip to the following sprint.方案 A——缩减范围。砍掉次要功能（比如多腿订单），23 号交付核心功能，次要功能顺延到下一 sprint。
You: Option B — bring in more people. We borrow two devs from the GLCM team for two weeks. Risky because they need ramp-up, but could work for the simpler stories.方案 B——增加人手。从 GLCM 团队借调 2 个开发借两周。有风险因为他们要熟悉项目，但简单的故事可以交给他们。
You: Option C — phased delivery. We ship the core on 23rd and flag to HKMA that the secondary features will follow in a maintenance release 2 weeks later. Less ideal but possible.方案 C——分阶段交付。23 号交付核心功能，并向 HKMA 说明次要功能将在 2 周后的维护版本中提供。不太理想但可行。
You: My recommendation is Option A — descope — because it preserves quality and avoids the cross-team coordination of Option B. But this is your call, Sarah. Happy to sync with HKMA directly if useful to discuss which features are must-have vs nice-to-have for the regulatory submission.我推荐方案 A——缩减范围——因为它能保证质量，避免方案 B 的跨团队协调。但这你说了算，Sarah。如果需要直接和 HKMA 沟通哪些功能是必须、哪些是 nice-to-have，我很乐意配合。
Sarah (PM): Wei, this is really helpful — thank you. The three reasons are valid, and I didn't realize the UAT environment was that tight. Let me take this back to HKMA and see if we can negotiate descoping the secondary features. I'll come back with a revised plan by EOD tomorrow.Wei，这真的很有帮助——谢谢。三个理由都成立，我之前不知道 UAT 环境这么紧张。我拿回去和 HKMA 谈一下，看能否协商砍掉次要功能。明天下班前给你修订后的计划。
You: Thanks, Sarah. Just to support, I can put together a one-pager on what's in scope vs out of scope, so you have concrete options for the HKMA conversation. By tomorrow morning, your time.谢谢 Sarah。为支持你，我可以整理一份一页纸的范围清单，列出包含什么、不包含什么，这样你和 HKMA 谈的时候有具体选项。明早给你，按你的时区。
Sarah (PM): That would be great, thanks. Much appreciated.太好了，谢谢。非常感谢。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Soft I'd like to push back gently… not on the regulatory commitment. 我想温和地 push back——但不是反对监管承诺 Distinguish what you're pushing back on from what you support. Removes the "you're against us" framing.
  • Acknowledge Totally understand the regulatory driver. 完全理解监管驱动 Always start by acknowledging the other side's constraint. Shows you listened.
  • Structure First / Second / Third — [reason]. 第一 / 第二 / 第三 Structure your pushback. Never list just one reason — looks like opinion.
  • Data Team is at ~95% allocated this sprint. 团队本冲刺已经分配约 95% Always back up pushback with data. "It's tight" is opinion; "95% allocated" is fact.
  • Risk Very likely to introduce defects. 很可能引入缺陷 Frame as risk, not certainty. "Likely to" is more persuasive than "will".
  • Clarity I'm not saying no. 我不是说不 Pre-empt the "you're being negative" interpretation. Make clear you're problem-solving, not blocking.
  • Options We have three options to explore. 有三个选项可以探索 Always come with options. A pushback without alternatives is just a complaint.
  • Recommend My recommendation is X — but this is your call. 我建议 X——但你决定 Make a recommendation, but explicitly defer authority. Avoids "you're telling me what to do".
  • Support Happy to sync with HKMA directly if useful. 如果需要，可以直接和 HKMA 同步 Offer to do the work to make your pushback actionable. Removes the "easy for you to say" perception.
  • Receive This is really helpful — thank you. 非常有帮助——谢谢 The other side's acknowledgment is a sign your pushback was received well.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • push back v. 反对 / 表达异议 Polite disagreement. Often softened with "gently / softly / respectfully".
  • pull in (a deadline) v. 提前（截止日期） Opposite of push out. "Pull in by one week" = bring forward by one week.
  • descope / scope down v. 缩减范围 Reduce the project scope to meet the deadline. Common in fixed-deadline projects.
  • HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority) banking 香港金融管理局 Hong Kong's central banking institution. Sets regulatory standards for HK banks.
  • revenue-critical path n. 收入关键路径 A system or flow where any failure directly causes revenue loss.
  • must-have vs nice-to-have 必须有 vs. 锦上添花 Standard prioritization framework. MoSCoW: Must / Should / Could / Won't.
  • phased delivery n. 分阶段交付 Ship core features first, secondary features in a follow-up release.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I'd like to push back gently on X, but not on Y" — Scope限定

Always scope what you're pushing back on. This pattern is gold:

  • I'd like to push back on the timeline, but not on the regulatory commitment.
  • I'd push back on the scope, but not on the deadline.
  • I disagree with the approach, but I support the goal.

② Hedging with "could / might / likely to"

When pushing back, hedge predictions to avoid sounding like a fortune-teller:

  • ❌ "Rushing will introduce bugs." (over-confident)
  • ✅ "Rushing is very likely to introduce bugs."
  • ✅ "Rushing could introduce bugs."

③ "I'm not saying no" — Pre-empting Negativity

Pre-empt the "you're being negative" perception:

  • I'm not saying no — I want to find a way to say yes.
  • I'm not against the goal — I want to find a safer path to it.
  • I'm not blocking — I'm flagging the risks so we can decide with full info.

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🤝 The "Push Back Sandwich" Framework

Pushing back to a senior (especially in cross-region HSBC teams) requires skill. Use the push-back sandwich:

  1. Top bun — Acknowledge the constraint. "I understand HKMA is the driver."
  2. Filling — Structure your concerns with data. 2-3 reasons, each with evidence.
  3. Bottom bun — Offer 2-3 alternatives + a recommendation. Never just complain — propose solutions.
  4. Side dish — Offer to do the work. "I can put together a one-pager by tomorrow."

Skipping any layer makes your pushback ineffective or confrontational.

⚠️ Cross-Cultural Pushback Tips
  • UK colleagues tend to appreciate direct, structured pushback with data. Be clear about the issue.
  • HK colleagues may prefer softer framing with face-saving. Lead with the relationship.
  • Indian colleagues often appreciate explicit alternatives — they love problem-solving modes.
  • Never push back in writing first. Always have a quick call or face-to-face, then document the outcome.
  • Be careful with "no". Replace with "I see significant risk in X — here's my concern and a proposed path forward."

  1. Compliance risk — InfoSec at HSBC requires two-person review for any production change. Bypassing review would be an audit finding in the next HKMA inspection.

I'm not saying no — two alternatives to consider:
  • Option A — Expedited review: I do a 5-minute self-review, then Priya does a fast-track review (10 minutes). Total: 15 min, still compliant.

My recommendation is A — keeps us audit-compliant with minimal delay. But this is your call."
