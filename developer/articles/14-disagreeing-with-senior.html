Disagreeing with a Senior

Article 14 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎓 Challenge your tech lead or architect with respect

1. Scenario 场景背景

In a design review, your tech lead Priya (UK) has proposed using PostgreSQL for the trade order audit log. You have a different view — you think append-only Cassandra would be a better fit because of the write-heavy, never-updated nature of audit logs. You are a mid-level developer, she's a senior tech lead with 12 years of experience. You need to challenge her with evidence, not ego.

设计评审中，技术负责人 Priya（英国）提议用 PostgreSQL 做交易订单审计日志。 你有不同的看法——你认为 append-only Cassandra 更合适，因为审计日志是 写多读少、从不更新。 你是中级开发，她是12年经验的资深技术负责人。你需要 用证据、而不是自尊 挑战她。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Priya (TL): OK, proposal: we'll use PostgreSQL for the trade order audit log. We already have it in our tech stack, the team knows it, and it supports JSON columns for the flexible audit payload. Any objections?好的，方案：用 PostgreSQL 做交易订单审计日志。技术栈里已有，团队熟悉，而且支持 JSON 列存灵活的审计数据。有人反对吗？
You: Priya, thanks for laying out the rationale — the JSON column idea is nice for the flexible payload. I'd like to challenge this with an alternative, but not at all suggesting we change direction lightly. Could I share a different perspective?Priya，谢谢说明理由——JSON 列的想法不错，灵活的数据结构很好。我想挑战一下，提出另一种方案，但绝不是要轻率地改变方向。我可以说说不同看法吗？
Priya (TL): Sure, go ahead. I'm open to alternatives if there's a strong case.好的，请讲。如果有强有力的情况，我愿意接受其他方案。
You: Three things make me think Cassandra might be a better fit for this specific workload:三点让我觉得 Cassandra 可能更合适这个特定工作负载：
You: One — write pattern. Audit logs are append-only by definition — we never update or delete rows. PostgreSQL, while flexible, is optimized for read-write mixed workloads. Cassandra is purpose-built for append-heavy, time-series-like data and would scale horizontally without sharding pain.第一——写入模式。审计日志本质上是只能追加的——从不更新或删除。PostgreSQL 虽然灵活，但针对读写混合负载做了优化。Cassandra 专门为追加密集型、时序类数据设计，能水平扩展而不需要分片。
You: Two — volume projection. From what I've modelled with the BA, we expect ~5 million audit events per day at peak. With PostgreSQL, this would require aggressive partitioning and archiving after 90 days. Cassandra's TTL feature would auto-expire old data without manual intervention.第二——容量预测。根据我和 BA 的建模，峰值时每天约 500 万条审计事件。用 PostgreSQL 需要激进分区和 90 天后归档。Cassandra 的 TTL 功能可以自动过期旧数据，无需人工干预。
You: Three — query pattern. Our primary query is "give me all audit events for trade order X in the last 30 days" — a point-in-time lookup by primary key. Cassandra is designed exactly for this. PostgreSQL would need a heavily indexed time + order_id column to do the same efficiently.第三——查询模式。我们的主要查询是“给我某交易订单最近 30 天的所有审计事件”——按主键做时间点查询。这正是 Cassandra 的设计目标。PostgreSQL 需要在时间 + order_id 上建大量索引才能高效完成同样查询。
You: That said, I completely see the merit of your PostgreSQL choice — less operational overhead, team familiarity, no new tech to learn. Those are legitimate trade-offs.不过我完全理解你选 PostgreSQL 的优点——运维开销小、团队熟悉、无需学新技术。这些都是合理的取舍。
You: My suggestion is that we don't decide in this meeting. How about I put together a 1-page comparison — PostgreSQL vs Cassandra for this workload — with rough cost and effort estimates? We can revisit at the next design review. That way we're deciding with data, not opinions.我的建议是这次会议先不定。我做一份 1 页的对比——PostgreSQL vs Cassandra 在这个工作负载下的对比，附粗略的成本和工作量估算。下次设计评审再讨论。这样我们用数据决策，而不是凭观点。
Priya (TL): Wei, that's a really mature take. The three points are well-argued, and the volume projection is something I didn't think through carefully. Yes, please do the comparison — by next Tuesday would be great. And please loop in Rajesh (architect) — he'd have input on the operational side.Wei，这个想法很成熟。三个论点很有力，容量预测我之前没仔细考虑。好，请做对比——下周二前可以吧。也让 Rajesh（架构师）参与一下——他对运维方面会有建议。
You: Will do. I'll have the comparison ready by Tuesday EOD HKT and share it in the channel. Thanks for being open to it, Priya.好的。我会在下周二香港时间下班前出对比，发到频道里。谢谢你愿意听不同意见，Priya。
Priya (TL): Always open to a good argument with data. 👍一直欢迎有数据支撑的讨论 👍

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Could I share a different perspective? 能分享一个不同视角吗？ Always ask permission before challenging a senior. Shows respect.
  • Acknowledge Thanks for laying out the rationale — the JSON column idea is nice. 谢谢阐述理由——JSON 字段的想法不错 Open with what's good about the senior's proposal. Even if you disagree, find something to agree with.
  • Humble I'd like to challenge this, but not at all suggesting we change direction lightly. 我想挑战，但不是说轻易改变方向 Pre-empt the "you're being difficult" perception. Show you understand the cost of changing course.
  • Credit Completely see the merit of your choice — less operational overhead, team familiarity. 完全理解你的选择优点——运维低、团队熟悉 Always acknowledge the trade-offs the senior made. Shows you understand the bigger picture.
  • Data From what I've modelled, we expect ~5 million events per day. 据我建模，峰值每天约500万事件 Data > opinion. Always do the math before challenging.
  • Defer How about I put together a 1-page comparison? 要不我做一份1页对比？ Don't try to decide in the meeting. Take it offline for proper analysis.
  • Process That way we're deciding with data, not opinions. 这样用数据决策，而不是观点 Reframe the disagreement as data-gathering, not you're wrong.
  • Receive That's a really mature take. 这是非常成熟的看法 When a senior praises your pushback, it builds your credibility for the future.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • append-only adj. 仅追加 (不能修改/删除) A data pattern where records are only added, never modified. Audit logs are typically append-only.
  • scale horizontally v. 水平扩展 (增加节点) Add more servers to handle load. Opposite of scale vertically (bigger server).
  • sharding n. 分片 Splitting data across multiple databases. Painful in PostgreSQL, native in Cassandra.
  • TTL (Time To Live) n. 生存时间 (自动过期) A setting that auto-expires data after N seconds. Native in Cassandra, requires jobs in PostgreSQL.
  • point-in-time lookup n. 时间点查找 A query that fetches records for a specific entity at a specific time range.
  • trade-off n. 权衡 A balance between two competing concerns. Every design decision is a trade-off.
  • JSON column n. JSON 字段 (关系数据库中的灵活字段) A column that stores JSON data. PostgreSQL's jsonb type is famous for this.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I'd like to challenge this, but not at all suggesting…" — 加缓冲的反对

The but not at all suggesting… pattern removes the confrontational edge:

  • I'd like to challenge this, but not at all suggesting we change direction lightly.
  • I'd push back, but not on the overall goal.
  • I'd disagree, but not without understanding the constraints first.

② "That said" / "With that said" — Pivot Pattern

When you've made your point and want to acknowledge the other side:

  • That said, I completely see the merit of your choice.
  • With that said, we should still consider X.
  • That being said, the time pressure is real.

③ "How about I…" — Soft Proposal

"How about I…?" is much softer than "I'll do…":

  • ❌ "I'll do the comparison." (sounds presumptuous)
  • ✅ "How about I put together a comparison?" (asks permission)

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🎓 The Art of "Upward Pushback"

Disagreeing with a senior is one of the most important — and delicate — skills in a developer's career. Done well, it builds your credibility. Done poorly, it ends it. Five rules:

  1. Always ask permission first. "Could I share a different perspective?"
  2. Open with what's good about their idea. Find something genuine to agree with.
  3. Bring data, not opinions. Numbers, benchmarks, projections. Never "I feel…"
  4. Acknowledge the trade-offs they made. Show you understand the bigger picture.
  5. Don't decide in the meeting. Propose taking it offline for proper analysis.
⚠️ Cross-Cultural Senior Pushback
  • UK seniors often appreciate structured, data-led pushback. Be direct, but professional.
  • HK seniors may read pushback as "losing face". Use softer framing, lead with the relationship.
  • Indian seniors often welcome constructive debate and explicit alternatives.
  • Never challenge in front of a wider audience without first raising it privately.
  • If you lose the argument, lose gracefully. "Fair point, let's go with your call."

7. Practice 练习题

✍️ Practice Drills
Q1. Soften this pushback to a senior:

"Priya, I think we should use Redis, not Memcached. Memcached doesn't support persistence."

"Priya, thanks for laying out the rationale — Memcached's simplicity is appealing. Could I share a different perspective? Not at all suggesting we change direction lightly. One thing gives me pause on Memcached: persistence. In our setup, the cache needs to survive a pod restart — otherwise we get a cold-cache storm on every deployment. From what I understand, Memcached is pure in-memory with no native persistence, while Redis offers RDB + AOF persistence. How about I put together a 1-page comparison on this specific point? We can revisit next design review."
Q2. Translate to native-sounding English:

"我想挑战一下用PostgreSQL的想法。不是说轻易改变，但Cassandra对append-only日志更合适。让我做个对比页，下次会议讨论。"

"Could I share a different perspective on the PostgreSQL choice? Not at all suggesting we change direction lightly — but for an append-only, write-heavy workload like audit logs, Cassandra is purpose-built for this. How about I put together a 1-page comparison with rough cost and operational estimates? We can revisit at the next design review and decide with data, not opinions."
Q3. Fill in the right phrases:

"Could I share a _____ (different)? / Not at all _____ (suggest) we change direction lightly. / That said, I completely see the _____ (merit). / How about I put together a 1-page _____ (compare)? / That way we're _____ (decide) with data, not opinions."

perspective / suggesting / merit / comparison / deciding
Q4. Role-play:

Your tech lead Priya wants to use synchronous HTTP calls between 3 new microservices for simplicity. You think async messaging (Kafka) is more appropriate because of: (1) the request-response pattern can fail at multiple points, (2) audit logging is easier with events, (3) services can scale independently. Write a polite, structured pushback with 3 reasons and a proposal to take it offline.

"Priya, totally see the appeal of sync HTTP — simpler, less infra to manage, team knows it well. Could I share a different perspective? Not at all suggesting we change direction lightly, but three things give me pause:
  1. Failure modes — sync HTTP means every call can fail independently, and we'd need distributed tracing + circuit breakers for every interaction. With async messaging, the broker handles retry and persistence.
  2. Audit logging — events going through Kafka are natively logged. With sync HTTP, we'd need to manually instrument every call. Compliance will appreciate the event log.
  3. Independent scaling — sync means cascading load — when Service A spikes, it directly hammers B and C. Async decouples them, so each can scale on its own.
That said, the operational overhead of Kafka is real — new infra to learn, Zookeeper / KRaft, monitoring. Not a free win.

How about I put together a 1-page comparison — sync HTTP vs Kafka for this 3-service flow — with effort and risk estimates? Re-evaluate at the next design review with Rajesh in the room."
