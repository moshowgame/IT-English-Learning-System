Vendor / Contractor Calls

Article 17 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 📞 Professional, firm, and clear calls with offshore vendors

1. Scenario 场景背景

You are on a Microsoft Teams call with Rahul from Infosys — the offshore vendor team that supports the OneWealth platform. They've been working on a SWIFT MT202 message adapter for 6 weeks and the deliverable is 2 weeks late. You need to be firm but professional — push them on a delivery date, but maintain the working relationship (they'll be a long-term partner).

你在 Microsoft Teams 上和 Infosys 的 Rahul 通话——支持 OneWealth 平台的外包团队。 他们做 SWIFT MT202 报文适配器 已经6周，交付晚了2周。你需要 坚定但专业—— 推一个交付日期，但维护长期合作关系。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You: Hi Rahul, thanks for joining on short notice. I wanted to check in on the MT202 adapter delivery. The latest status I had was that you'd commit to the 15th of last month, but we're now 2 weeks past that. Can you give me a candid update?嗨 Rahul，感谢临时抽空参加。我想跟进 MT202 适配器的交付。我之前收到的最新状态是你承诺上个月 15 号交付，但现在已经超期 2 周。能给个坦率的情况吗？
Rahul (Vendor): Yes Wei, my apologies for the delay. We've had some resource constraints — two of our developers were on leave. We're now planning to deliver by the 30th of this month.是的 Wei，抱歉延迟了。我们有一些资源限制——两个开发在休假。现在计划本月 30 号交付。
You: I appreciate the update, Rahul, but I have to be straight with you — the 30th is also not going to work for us. We have a regulatory commitment on the 28th for the OneWealth trade order reporting. The MT202 adapter is a hard dependency for that.谢谢 Rahul 的更新，但我必须直说——30 号对我们也不可行。28 号我们有 OneWealth 交易订单报告的监管承诺，MT202 适配器是硬依赖。
You: What I need from you today is a concrete plan that gets us to a working adapter by the 25th at the latest. What would it take to make that happen?我今天需要的是一个具体方案，能让我们最迟 25 号拿到能用的适配器。怎么做才能实现？
Rahul (Vendor): The 25th is very tight, Wei. We still have the integration test and the SWIFT certification pending. Typically that takes 2-3 weeks.25 号太紧了，Wei。集成测试和 SWIFT 认证还没完成，通常需要 2-3 周。
You: I understand the certification timeline is non-negotiable, but I have to be clear: missing the 28th is not an option for us — it's a regulatory deadline, not a business deadline. What can we do together to compress the timeline?我理解认证时间不可谈判，但说清楚：28 号是死线，是监管要求不是业务要求。我们一起能做什么来压缩时间？
You: For example: can your team hand off the integration test to us earlier, and we run it in parallel with your certification? Or can you add one more developer for the next 2 weeks? I'm open to options, but I need a commitment by end of this call.比如：你们的团队能否提前把集成测试交给我们，让我们在你们做认证时并行跑？或者再加一个开发做 2 周？我接受各种方案，但今天会议结束前我需要一个承诺。
Rahul (Vendor): Let me think… Yes, I think we can do this: option 1 — we can add a third developer starting tomorrow for 2 weeks, focused on integration test. Option 2 — we can hand off the SWIFT environment credentials to your team so you can run integration in parallel. Both together should compress the timeline by about a week.让我想想……可以，我想到两个方案：方案 1——从明天起加第三个开发，做 2 周，专注集成测试；方案 2——把 SWIFT 环境凭证交给我们团队，让你们认证时我们并行跑集成测试。两者一起应该能压缩约一周。
You: Both together? Great. So the plan is: (1) third developer starting tomorrow; (2) we get SWIFT credentials by EOD tomorrow; (3) you commit to delivery by the 25th EOD IST.两个一起？太好了。计划是：(1) 明天上第三个开发；(2) 明天 EOD 前给我们 SWIFT 凭证；(3) 你承诺 25 号 EOD（IST）交付。
You: Can you confirm that on the call so I have a written commitment? I'll follow up with an email capturing this conversation and the new commitments.能在电话里确认一下吗？这样我有书面承诺。我会发跟进邮件，记录本次对话和新的承诺。
Rahul (Vendor): Yes, confirmed. Third developer tomorrow, SWIFT credentials by EOD tomorrow, delivery by 25th EOD IST.好的，确认。明天上第三个开发，明天 EOD 给 SWIFT 凭证，25 号 EOD（IST）交付。
You: Perfect, thanks Rahul. One more thing — to de-risk the 25th, I'd like daily check-ins for the next 2 weeks, 15 minutes each, to track progress against the new plan. Is that workable on your side?好的，谢谢 Rahul。还有一件——为 25 号降低风险，接下来 2 周我想每天开会 15 分钟，跟进新计划的进度。你那边可行吗？
Rahul (Vendor): Yes, we can do that. 15 min daily, same time, starting tomorrow at 10:00 IST / 12:30 HKT.好的，可以。15 分钟每天，同样时间，从明天 10:00 IST / 12:30 HKT 开始。
You: Great, that's locked in. Thanks for working with us on this, Rahul. Appreciate it. I'll send the follow-up email with the new commitments and the daily check-in invite by EOD today.好的，这就定了。谢谢和我们一起推进，Rahul。非常感谢。我今天 EOD 前会发跟进邮件，包含新的承诺和每日会议邀请。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Can you give me a candid update? 能给我一个坦诚的更新吗？ Candid = honest, even if uncomfortable. Signals you want the truth, not spin.
  • Firm I have to be straight with you — the 30th is also not going to work. 我得直说——30号也不行 Use "I have to be straight / honest / candid" before a firm statement. Softens the firmness.
  • Hard It's a hard dependency / hard deadline. 硬依赖 / 硬截止 Hard = cannot be moved. Soft = nice to have but movable.
  • Ask What would it take to make that happen? 需要什么才能做到？ The classic vendor pushback. Puts the burden of problem-solving on them.
  • Non-negotiable Non-negotiable deadline. 不可谈判的截止日期 Strong phrase. Use for regulatory / contractual deadlines.
  • Collaborate What can we do together to compress the timeline? 我们能一起做什么来压缩时间？ Frames the problem as joint, not adversarial. "We together" not "you need to".
  • Confirm Can you confirm that on the call? 能在电话里确认吗？ Verbal commitment on a recorded call is much stronger than "we'll try".
  • De-risk To de-risk the 25th, I'd like daily check-ins. 为了降低 25 号风险，需要每日 check-in Reframe oversight as de-risking, not micromanaging.
  • Close That's locked in. 定了 / 锁定 Use at the end of a commitment. "Locked in" / "Confirmed" / "Done deal".

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • vendor / contractor n. 供应商 / 承包商 An external company providing services. Common partners: Infosys, TCS, Wipro, Accenture.
  • commitment n. 承诺 A firm promise. "We commit to delivery by X" is much stronger than "we'll try".
  • SWIFT certification banking SWIFT 认证 Mandatory certification by SWIFT for any system sending/receiving SWIFT messages.
  • hand off v. 移交 Transfer ownership of work to another team. "Hand off the integration test to us".
  • compress the timeline v. 压缩时间表 Make the schedule shorter without reducing scope. Often requires more resources or parallel work.
  • IST (Indian Standard Time) n. 印度标准时间 (UTC+5:30) The time zone for most Indian vendors. 2.5 hours behind HKT.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I have to be straight with you" — Softening Firmness

When you need to be firm, soften the firmness with these lead-ins:

  • I have to be straight with you — the 30th doesn't work.
  • To be honest, we're concerned about the timeline.
  • I have to be candid — missing the 28th is a regulatory issue.

② "What would it take to…" — Vendor Pushback Pattern

When you want commitment without dictating the solution:

  • What would it take to make the 25th?
  • What would it take to get there by Q4?
  • What would it take to deliver this scope?

③ "Can you confirm that on the call?" — 录音确认

Always ask for verbal confirmation on recorded calls. It converts "we'll try" to a binding commitment.

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🤝 Vendor Management Principles at HSBC
  1. Firm but professional. You are the customer, but they're a long-term partner. Don't burn the relationship.
  2. Always end a call with a written commitment. Send a follow-up email same day with bullet points: what was agreed, by when, by whom.
  3. Use "we" not "you". "What can we do together" not "What will you do". HSBC values partnership.
  4. Escalate early. If a vendor is consistently late, escalate to vendor management — don't try to fix it alone.
  5. Document everything. Vendor delays, missed commitments, quality issues — all in the vendor scorecard.
⚠️ Cross-Cultural Vendor Tips
  • Indian vendors often appreciate structured agendas and clear outcomes. Send the agenda 24h before.
  • Never accept "we'll try". Always push for "we commit to" with a specific date.
  • Watch for "almost done" — usually means 50% done. Ask for % completion.
  • Use the SOW (Statement of Work) as your leverage. If a deliverable is in the SOW, you have a contractual right to demand it.
  • Build personal rapport. Ask about their team, working hours, even cricket 🏏. Long-term partnerships matter.

Q4. Role-play:

Vendor (TCS) is 3 weeks late on delivering the KYC document upload feature. They want another 2 weeks. You have a hard regulatory deadline in 4 weeks. Write a 1-page email/IM that (a) acknowledges the delay, (b) is firm on the new date, (c) proposes 2-3 ways to de-risk, (d) requests a commitment.

To: Rajiv (TCS Delivery Manager)
Cc: Sarah (PM), Rajesh (Architect)
Subject: [KYC-UPLOAD] Action needed — Commitment by EOD Friday

Hi Rajiv,

Thanks for the status update on the KYC document upload feature. I appreciate the team's effort, and I understand the scope was larger than initially estimated.

I have to be straight with you, though — the proposed 2-week extension isn't workable for us. We have a hard MAS TRM regulatory deadline in 4 weeks, and the KYC upload is a critical-path deliverable for it.

What I need from you by EOD Friday is a concrete commitment to deliver a working feature by 3 weeks from today, with 1 week buffer for our internal UAT. To de-risk this, I'd like to discuss:

  1. Can your team add 1 more developer for the next 3 weeks?
  2. Can we hand off the integration testing to our team so it runs in parallel with your dev work?
  3. Can we descope the secondary feature (e.g., multi-language support) and ship it in a follow-up release?

I'm open to all three options — whatever gets us to delivery. Can we set up a 30-min call tomorrow to lock in the plan?

Best,
Wei
