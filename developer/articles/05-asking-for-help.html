Asking for Help Gracefully

Article 05 · Module 1: Daily Standup · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🤲 How to ask without sounding weak or unprepared

1. Scenario 场景背景

You have been stuck for two hours on a tricky Spring Boot issue: a transaction rollback exception that only happens in the UAT environment but not locally. You've already done your homework — read logs, checked the DataSource config, reproduced it once. You need help from your senior colleague Tom.

你在 Spring Boot 上卡了两个小时——一个 事务回滚异常，只在 UAT 环境 出现，本地复现不出来。 你已经做了功课——查了日志、检查了 DataSource 配置、复现了一次。需要找资深同事 Tom 帮忙。

The key is: show what you've tried first, then ask. Never ask blind.

关键：先展示你已经尝试过的，再问。永远不要盲目求助。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (IM): Hey Tom, do you have 10 minutes? I could use a second pair of eyes on a Spring Boot issue. Not urgent — I can wait until after your meeting if needed.嘿 Tom，有空 10 分钟吗？有个 Spring Boot 的问题想请你帮我看两眼。不急，你开完会也行。
Tom: Sure, what's up?没问题，怎么了？
You: I'm seeing a TransactionSystemException in the UAT logs for the customer onboarding service. Locally it works fine — only fails in UAT. I've been digging for about two hours.UAT 日志里客户开户服务报TransactionSystemException，本地跑没问题，只在 UAT 报。我查了大约两个小时。
You: Here's what I've tried so far: checked the application-uat.yml — datasource config looks the same as prod. Looked at the HikariCP pool settings — they're identical. Pulled the UAT logs and saw the rollback is happening at commit time, not at the SQL level. I also tried reproducing it locally with the UAT config and couldn't trigger it.我已经试过的：查了application-uat.yml，datasource 配置和 prod 看起来一样。看了HikariCP 连接池配置也一样。拉了 UAT 日志，回滚发生在 commit 时，不是 SQL 层。还试过用 UAT 配置在本地复现，复现不出来。
You: My hunch is that it's connection-pool related — maybe UAT has a smaller pool and we're hitting a timeout on commit. But I'm not 100% sure. Could you take a quick look with me?我猜测是连接池相关——可能 UAT 池小，commit 超时了。但我不太确定。能一起快速看一下吗？
Tom: Yeah, let's pull it up. Good thinking on the connection pool — actually, that reminds me, there was a similar issue last quarter on the GLCM team. UAT was running on a smaller container with a default HikariCP size of 10. The fix was to bump it to 30 for UAT.行，打开看看。连接池思路不错。其实提醒我了，上季度 GLCM 团队也遇到过类似问题。UAT 跑在小容器上，HikariCP 默认大小是 10。后来调到 30 修好的。
You: Oh, that's exactly it. Let me check the container spec — you're right, UAT is on a 2-CPU 2GB container. Let me try the config bump and see if it resolves.哦，就是这个。我看下容器规格——你说得对，UAT 是2 核 2G。我先调一下配置看能不能解决。
Tom: Sounds good. One thing though — make sure you check with the SRE team before bumping the pool size, in case there's a downstream impact on the shared UAT DB.好。不过注意——先和 SRE 团队确认下，共享的 UAT DB可能受下游影响。
You: Will do. Thanks a lot, Tom — I was going in circles before. I'll let you know if the bump fixes it.好的。非常感谢 Tom，之前一直在原地打转。调好了告诉你。
Tom: Anytime. Happy to help.随时，乐意帮忙。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Soft Ask Do you have 10 minutes? 你有10分钟吗？ Always state the time ask upfront. "Got a sec?" is even softer for quick questions.
  • Frame I could use a second pair of eyes. 我想请你帮我看下 A second pair of eyes is a humble, idiomatic way to ask for review. Never say "please help me".
  • Show work Here's what I've tried so far. 这是我目前试过的 The single most important sentence. Always show your homework before asking.
  • Hypothesis My hunch is that… 我猜测是… Hunch is more humble than "I think". Other options: "My guess is…" / "It looks like…".
  • Confidence I'm not 100% sure. 我不百分百确定 Showing uncertainty is professional, not weak. "I'm pretty sure but not certain" works too.
  • Request Could you take a quick look with me? 能一起快速看一下吗？ With me (not "for me") signals collaboration, not delegation.
  • Feedback Good thinking. 思路不错 When seniors acknowledge your work, it builds trust. "That's a fair point" is also common.
  • Process Check with the SRE team before… 先和 SRE 团队确认下… Seniors often add process reminders — listen carefully, these are HSBC compliance hints.
  • Closing I'll let you know if it fixes it. 修好了告诉你 Always close the loop. "I'll circle back" / "I'll update you" are alternatives.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • second pair of eyes n. 另一双眼睛（指协助审查） Idiomatic. Use it to ask for review without sounding incompetent.
  • TransactionSystemException n. Spring 事务系统异常 Thrown by Spring when a transaction cannot be committed or rolled back properly.
  • HikariCP n. HikariCP (Java 高性能JDBC连接池) The default JDBC connection pool in Spring Boot. Pool size matters for concurrency.
  • DataSource n. 数据源 (Java EE 概念) A factory for database connections. Configured in application.yml.
  • SRE (Site Reliability Engineering) n. 站点可靠性工程团队 Owns production / UAT infrastructure. Always consult before infra changes.
  • downstream impact n. 下游影响 A change in one place that affects other systems / teams / users.
  • going in circles adj. 原地打转 / 没有进展 A casual way to admit you're stuck. Better than "I don't know".

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I could use…" — Polite Self-Reference

"I could use a hand" / "I could use a second pair of eyes" is a humble, native pattern for asking help. Compare:

  • ❌ "Please help me." (too direct, sounds junior)
  • ❌ "I need help." (urgent, sometimes hostile)
  • ✅ "I could use a second pair of eyes." (collaborative, professional)
  • ✅ "I could use a hand on this." (slightly more casual)

② "Hunch" vs. "I think" — Expressing Hypothesis with Humility

When you have a guess but not certainty:

  • My hunch is that… (informal, humble)
  • My best guess is that… (slightly stronger)
  • It looks like… (based on evidence)
  • From the logs, it seems… (evidence-led)
  • ❌ Avoid "I think it's definitely…" when you don't have proof.

③ Past Tense in Recap — 复盘时使用过去式

When describing what you've tried, always use past tense:

  • I checked the config.
  • I tried reproducing locally.
  • I pulled the logs.
  • I couldn't reproduce it. ← Use couldn't, not "can't", because it's a past attempt.

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

💡 The "Show Your Work" Rule

At HSBC (and most banks), asking for help without showing your work is seen as a red flag. The rule of thumb: 30 minutes of self-debugging minimum before asking. Come with:

  • 1. What you observed (the symptom)
  • 2. What you tried (your investigation steps)
  • 3. What you think (your hypothesis)
  • 4. What you need (the specific ask)

This pattern shows you can solve problems independently — you only escalate when you genuinely need help.

⚠️ HSBC-Specific Tips
  • Don't say "I don't know" alone. Always pair it with "…but here's my next step".
  • Use Confluence / Jira to log the issue. When asking for help, link the ticket — it makes the conversation auditable.
  • Respect seniors' time. Always state the time ask: "Do you have 10 minutes?" not "Can I ask you something?".
  • Close the loop. After they help, follow up with the result. "Just to close the loop — the fix worked."
  • Avoid "打扰一下" / "Sorry to bother you". Excessive apology in English sounds weak. "Got a sec?" is enough.

7. Practice 练习题

✍️ Practice Drills
Q1. Translate to natural English (口语化):

"打扰一下，你能帮我看一下吗？我在 Spring Cloud Gateway 上配 JWT 鉴权，本地 OK，但 UAT 上 401。已经试了2个小时。"

"Hey, do you have a few minutes? I could use a second pair of eyes on a Spring Cloud Gateway issue — JWT auth config. Works locally, but UAT is throwing 401s. I've been digging for about two hours."
Q2. Rewrite this "ask for help" message in a more professional way:

"Help me, the Jenkins pipeline is broken, I don't know what to do."

"Hey, I'm running into a Jenkins pipeline issue. The build is failing at the Docker push step with a 403 Forbidden. Here's what I've tried so far: (1) checked the credentials in Jenkins — they look correct; (2) tried re-pushing manually with the same creds — works fine; (3) pulled the Jenkins logs and saw the error is from the docker-credential-ecr-login plugin. My hunch is the IAM role trust policy needs an update. Not urgent — but if you have 15 mins today, would appreciate a second pair of eyes."
Q3. Reply when a senior acknowledges your finding:

Tom says: "Good catch on the connection pool. That was my hunch too."

"Thanks, Tom. Will run with that. Will let you know if the pool size bump fixes it. Appreciate the help."
Q4. Role-play:

You are stuck on a Kafka consumer lag issue in production. You've checked the consumer group, the topic partition count, the broker health, and the JVM heap. You suspect the deserialization logic is slow. Write a 5-line Slack message to your senior asking for help, following the 4-step pattern (observation / tried / hypothesis / ask).

"Hey Priya, got a sec? I'm seeing consumer lag spike to 50k+ on the trade-events topic in prod since this morning. Things I've checked: (1) consumer group is healthy, no rebalances; (2) all 12 partitions are assigned; (3) brokers are green, no under-replicated partitions; (4) JVM heap on the consumer pod is at 60%. My hunch is the JSON deserialization for the new MT202 message type is slower than expected. Any chance you can take a quick look with me later today? Not blocking the release, but I want to close it out before the weekend."
