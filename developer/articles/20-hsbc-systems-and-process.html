HSBC Systems & Process

Article 20 · Module 5: HSBC Banking Context · Developer English

⏱ 10 min read 🔴 Advanced 🏦 HSBCnet, OneWealth, HUB, GLCM, and internal workflow terms

1. Scenario 场景背景

It's your third week at HSBC. Your Tech Lead (David) is giving you a system overview on a whiteboard — HSBC's internal landscape can feel like alphabet soup: HSBCnet, OneWealth, HUB, GLCM, GPB, CMB, RBWM, GPS, HOST, GLBa. You also hear process terms: SDL, CAB, RIB, Operational Risk, Risk and Control Assessment (RCA), Three Lines of Defence (3LoD), SOX controls.

第三周，你的 Tech Lead (David) 给你做系统 overview。汇丰内部系统像字母汤： HSBCnet, OneWealth, HUB, GLCM, GPB, CMB, RBWM, GPS, HOST, GLBa。还有流程术语： SDL, CAB, RIB, 操作风险, 风险与控制评估 (RCA), 三道防线 (3LoD), SOX 控制。

This article is a spoken English tour of the core HSBC systems, the business lines, the change-management process, and the control framework. Mastering these lets you follow meetings, write Jiras, and contribute in design reviews with confidence.

2. Dialogue Part 1: Business Lines & Internal Systems

David (Tech Lead): Wei, let me give you the lay of the land. HSBC is split into four main business lines: RBWM — Retail Banking and Wealth Management, CMB — Commercial Banking, GPB — Global Private Banking, and GB&M — Global Banking and Markets. Our service sits under CMB.Wei，给你介绍下整体情况。HSBC 分为四大业务线：RBWM（零售银行与财富管理）、CMB（工商金融）、GPB（全球私人银行）、GB&M（全球银行与市场）。我们的服务在 CMB 下。
You: Got it. And the system landscape — what are the core platforms I need to know about?明白。系统层面——我需要了解哪些核心平台？
David (Tech Lead): Top of the list is HSBCnet — that's the customer-facing online banking platform for corporate and institutional clients. Anything customer-visible on the corporate side goes through HSBCnet.排在首位的是 HSBCnet——这是面向公司和机构客户的网银平台。企业端所有客户可见的功能都通过 HSBCnet。
You: OK. And on the wealth side?好的。财富管理方面呢？
David (Tech Lead): OneWealth is the global wealth platform for RBWM and GPB. It handles investment products, portfolio management, and advisory. If you're touching anything wealth-related, you'll go through OneWealth APIs.OneWealth 是 RBWM 和 GPB 的全球财富管理平台，处理投资产品、投资组合管理和顾问服务。任何财富相关的东西都会走 OneWealth API。
You: Understood. What about HUB and GLCM — I keep seeing those in Jira tickets.明白。HUB 和 GLCM 呢——我经常在 Jira 单子里看到。
David (Tech Lead): Right. HUB is the Hong Kong Universal Banking platform — a legacy core banking system for HK retail and commercial. GLCM is Global Liquidity and Cash Management — the platform for corporate cash management, payments, and FX. Both are critical-path systems.对。HUB 是香港 Universal Banking 平台——HK 零售和工商的遗留核心银行系统。GLCM 是 Global Liquidity and Cash Management（全球流动性与现金管理）——企业现金管理、支付和外汇的平台。两个都是关键路径系统。
You: Got it — and the others I keep hearing: GPS, HOST, GLBa?明白——还有几个我经常听到的：GPS、HOST、GLBa？
David (Tech Lead): GPS is Global Payments Solutions — the cross-border payments engine. HOST is the Hong Kong Operations and Systems Technology — internal IT for HK. GLBa is Group Legal and Board Affairs — not a tech system, but you'll hear it in compliance discussions.GPS 是 Global Payments Solutions（全球支付解决方案）——跨境支付引擎。HOST 是 Hong Kong Operations and Systems Technology（香港运营与系统技术）——HK 的内部 IT。GLBa 是 Group Legal and Board Affairs（集团法务与董事会事务）——不是技术系统，但合规讨论中会听到。

2. Dialogue Part 2: Change Management Process

David (Tech Lead): Wei, just to walk you through the change process. Every production change goes through CAB — Change Advisory Board — which meets twice a week. Standard changes can be pre-approved, but anything touching a critical-path system — like GLCM or HUB — needs full CAB approval.Wei，给你讲讲变更流程。每个生产变更都要走 CAB（Change Advisory Board，变更咨询委员会），每周开两次。标准变更可以预先批准，但涉及关键路径系统（比如 GLCM 或 HUB）需要完整 CAB 审批。
You: OK. What does a CAB submission need to include?好的。CAB 提交需要包含什么？
David (Tech Lead): Five things, at minimum: (1) change description — what and why; (2) risk assessment — low / medium / high; (3) implementation plan — including rollback plan; (4) test evidence — UAT sign-off, non-functional testing results; (5) approval chain — Tech Lead, BA, Product, and for some changes, InfoSec and Compliance.至少 5 项：(1) 变更描述——变更什么和为什么；(2) 风险评估——低/中/高；(3) 实施计划——含回滚计划；(4) 测试证据——UAT sign-off、非功能测试结果；(5) 审批链——Tech Lead、BA、Product，某些变更还需要 InfoSec 和 Compliance。
You: Got it. And what about emergency changes — like a P1 incident fix?明白。紧急变更呢——比如 P1 事故修复？
David (Tech Lead): Emergency changes follow the eCAB — emergency CAB — process. The Incident Commander fast-tracks the approval, usually within 30 minutes. But the change still needs to be retroactively documented and go through a post-implementation review (PIR) within 5 business days.紧急变更走 eCAB（紧急 CAB）流程。事故指挥官快速审批，通常 30 分钟内完成。但变更仍需要事后补文档，并在 5 个工作日内进行 PIR（实施后回顾）。
You: Understood. And SDL — what is that exactly?明白。SDL 具体是什么？
David (Tech Lead): SDL stands for Secure Development Lifecycle. It's the Group Standard for how we build software — covers threat modelling, secure coding, code review, security testing. Any production system has to comply with SDL. Auditors check it.SDL 是 Secure Development Lifecycle（安全开发生命周期）。这是集团标准，规定我们如何构建软件——涵盖威胁建模、安全编码、代码审查、安全测试。任何生产系统都必须符合 SDL，审计员会检查。

2. Dialogue Part 3: Risk, Controls & Three Lines of Defence

Priya (BA): Wei, just to make sure you understand the governance side: at HSBC, every system has an RCA — Risk and Control Assessment. It's a document that lists the risks of the system and the controls we have in place to mitigate them.Wei，确保你理解治理方面：HSBC 每个系统都有一个 RCA（Risk and Control Assessment，风险与控制评估）。这是列出系统风险和缓解控制措施的文档。
You: OK. And who owns the RCA — the dev team, or the business?好的。RCA 谁负责——开发团队还是业务？
Priya (BA): The first line of defence is us — the business and IT teams who own and operate the system. We're responsible for identifying risks, implementing controls, and running them effectively.第一道防线是我们——拥有和运营系统的业务和 IT 团队。我们负责识别风险、实施控制、并有效运行。
You: Right. And the second and third lines?好的。第二道和第三道防线呢？
Priya (BA): Second line is Risk and Compliance — they oversee and challenge the first line. Third line is Internal Audit — they provide independent assurance to the Board. That's the Three Lines of Defence model.第二道防线是风险与合规部门——他们监督并挑战第一道防线。第三道防线是内部审计——他们为董事会提供独立保证。这就是“三道防线”模型。
You: Understood. In practical terms, if I'm building a new feature, do I need to update the RCA?明白。实际工作中，如果我在做新功能，需要更新 RCA 吗？
Priya (BA): Yes — every change that introduces a new risk or modifies an existing control needs an RCA update. The change ticket won't be approved without it. And for SOX-scoped systems — anything impacting financial reporting — the SOX controls also need to be re-tested and signed off.是的——任何引入新风险或修改现有控制的变更都需要更新 RCA。变更单没有 RCA 更新不会被批准。对于 SOX 范围内的系统——任何影响财务报告的——SOX 控制也需要重新测试并签字。
You: Got it. And RIB — what is that?明白。RIB 是什么？
Priya (BA): RIB stands for Risk Issues Book. It's where we log and track all known risks and issues within a system. Anything in the RIB needs a mitigation plan and a target close date. It's reviewed monthly by the system owner.RIB 是 Risk Issues Book（风险问题册）。我们在这里记录和跟踪系统内所有已知风险和问题。RIB 里的每项都需要缓解计划和目标关闭日期，每月由系统负责人审查。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Overview Let me give you the lay of the land. 我给你介绍一下整体情况 Standard opener for system / org overview.
  • Confirm Our service sits under CMB. 我们的服务属于 CMB "Sits under" = 业务上属于哪个条线。Always confirm this first.
  • Path Anything customer-visible goes through HSBCnet. 任何面向客户的功能都走 HSBCnet "Goes through" = 流量/逻辑上经过。常用来指代 critical path。
  • Approval Anything touching a critical-path system needs full CAB approval. 任何涉及 critical-path 系统的变更都需要完整 CAB 审批 "Touching" / "impacts" = 影响。HSBC 高度关注 "what does this change touch?"
  • Fast-track The Incident Commander fast-tracks the approval, usually within 30 minutes. 事件指挥官快速通道审批，通常 30 分钟内 "Fast-track" = 紧急审批通道。区别于"skip approval"——仍然需要审批，只是更快。
  • Compliance Any production system has to comply with SDL. 任何生产系统都必须符合 SDL "Comply with" = 符合/遵守。"Has to" = 必须（强调）。
  • Own The first line of defence is us — we're responsible for identifying risks. 第一道防线是我们——我们负责识别风险 "Own" = 拥有/负责。在风险和合规语境中非常重要。
  • Block The change ticket won't be approved without an RCA update. 没有 RCA 更新，变更单不会被批准 "Won't be approved without…" = 缺少某项就无法通过。HSBC 治理的硬规则。

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

① Business Lines 业务条线

  • RBWM (Retail Banking and Wealth Management) n. 零售银行与财富管理 Mass-affluent and retail customers.
  • CMB (Commercial Banking) n. 工商金融 Mid-market and SME commercial clients. Our service sits here.
  • GPB (Global Private Banking) n. 环球私人银行 High-net-worth individuals.
  • GB&M (Global Banking and Markets) n. 环球银行与资本市场 Investment banking, markets, securities services.

② Core Systems 核心系统

  • HSBCnet n. HSBC 企业网银 Customer-facing online banking for corporate and institutional clients.
  • OneWealth n. 全球财富管理平台 Global wealth platform for RBWM and GPB.
  • HUB (Hong Kong Universal Banking) n. 香港 Universal Banking 核心系统 Legacy core banking platform for HK. Critical-path.
  • GLCM (Global Liquidity and Cash Management) n. 全球流动性与现金管理 Corporate cash management, payments, FX. Critical-path.
  • GPS (Global Payments Solutions) n. 全球支付解决方案 Cross-border payments engine.
  • HOST n. 香港 IT 与运营 Hong Kong IT and operations. Internal team / function.

③ Change & Risk Process 变更与风险流程

  • CAB (Change Advisory Board) n. 变更咨询委员会 Approval body for production changes. Meets 2x/week.
  • eCAB (Emergency CAB) n. 紧急变更审批 Fast-track approval for P1 incident fixes. Usually < 30 mins.
  • PIR (Post-Implementation Review) n. 实施后回顾 Required after eCAB changes, within 5 business days.
  • SDL (Secure Development Lifecycle) n. 安全开发生命周期 Group Standard for secure coding. Auditors check it.
  • RCA (Risk and Control Assessment) n. 风险与控制评估 Document listing risks and controls for a system.
  • RIB (Risk Issues Book) n. 风险与问题登记簿 Log of all known risks / issues for a system. Reviewed monthly.
  • 3LoD (Three Lines of Defence) n. 三道防线 1st = business/IT, 2nd = Risk/Compliance, 3rd = Internal Audit.
  • SOX controls n. SOX 控制（萨班斯-奥克斯利法案） Sarbanes-Oxley controls for financial reporting. Quarterly re-test.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Sits under" / "Goes through" / "Touches" — System Topology Verbs

Three core verbs for talking about how systems connect:

  • Sits under CMB — business-line ownership
  • Goes through HSBCnet — customer-facing flow
  • Touches GLCM — impacts / integrates with

② "Has to comply" / "Has to go through" — Mandatory Compliance

HSBC governance language uses have to / need to / won't be approved without:

  • ❌ "We should probably do CAB."
  • ✅ "We have to go through CAB."
  • ✅ "The change won't be approved without an RCA update."

③ "Just to walk you through / make sure" — Framing Phrases

Useful for transitions in spoken English:

  • Just to walk you through the change process…
  • Just to make sure you understand the governance side…
  • In practical terms, if I'm building a new feature…

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🏦 HSBC Internal Navigation
  1. "Critical-path" is sacred. If a system is critical-path, changes take longer, need more approvals, and have stricter change windows. Always ask: "Is this system critical-path?"
  2. Three Lines of Defence is not a slogan. When a BA or PM says "we are the first line", they mean "we own the risk, not just the code."
  3. Acronyms are dense. Use the "ABC" rule. When you hear a new acronym, ask: "Sorry, what does [X] stand for?" Nobody will mind — everyone does it.
  4. "Per Group Standard" wins all arguments. When in doubt, refer to the Group Standard / Group Policy / SDL. It's the single source of truth.
  5. Region matters even for systems. HUB is HK-only. OneWealth is global. GPS is global. Always confirm which region the system serves.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ❌ "I'll just push the change directly to production." — at HSBC, this is a dismissible offence.
  • ✅ "I'll raise a CAB ticket and loop in the Tech Lead and BA for approval."
  • ❌ "We don't need an RCA for this small change." — RCA updates are mandatory for any new risk.
  • ❌ "I think this is a low-risk change." — let Risk and Compliance decide, not the dev team.
  • ❌ Treating "fast-track" as "skip approval" — fast-track still requires approver sign-off.

Q2. Fill in the HSBC system / process terms:

Q3. Role-play: Cab approval walk-through

You need to deploy a hotfix to GLCM (a critical-path system) for a P1 incident. Explain to a junior dev: (a) which approval process, (b) what documentation is required, (c) what happens after the fix.

"OK, here's how this fast-track works for the P1.

(1) Approval process: We go through eCAB — emergency CAB. The Incident Commander fast-tracks the approval, usually within 30 minutes. Critical-path systems like GLCM still need approval — we just skip the standard CAB queue.

(2) Documentation: Before the deploy, we need: change description, risk assessment, rollback plan, test evidence, and approval chain (Tech Lead, BA, Product, InfoSec). The CAB ticket has to be raised — won't be approved without it.

(3) After the fix: The change still needs to be retroactively documented, and we have to run a PIR (Post-Implementation Review) within 5 business days. The RCA has to be updated for any new risk the change introduces. And the SDL controls — threat modelling, code review, security testing — still apply, even on an emergency change."
Q4. Explain the Three Lines of Defence to a new joiner

You are onboarding a new developer. Explain the 3LoD model in spoken English. Cover: (a) what the three lines are, (b) who does what, (c) why a developer should care.

"OK, just to walk you through the Three Lines of Defence model. It's how HSBC thinks about risk.

The first line is us — the business and IT teams who own and operate the system. We're responsible for identifying risks, implementing controls, and running them effectively. In practice, that means updating the RCA, running SDL controls, and owning the Risk Issues Book (RIB).

The second line is Risk and Compliance. They oversee and challenge the first line — so they review the RCA, they sign off on changes, they set the policies we follow. They're our partners, not blockers.

The third line is Internal Audit. They provide independent assurance to the Board — so they audit our systems, they check our controls, they report findings directly to senior management.

Why should you care? As a developer, you're first line — you own the risk, not just the code. That means when you build a new feature, you have to think about the risks and the controls. It's not just the BA's job."
