Email & IM — Concise Tech Communication

Article 11 · Module 3: Written Communication · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate ✉️ Write to-the-point emails and chat messages to overseas teams

1. Scenario 场景背景

At HSBC, you write many short technical messages every day — Slack/Teams chat to teammates, formal emails to UK colleagues, Jira comments on tickets. The challenge: be concise without being rude, professional without being verbose. This article covers the three most common formats.

在汇丰，你每天写 大量简短的技术消息——给队友的 Slack/Teams 聊天、给英国同事的正式邮件、 工单上的 Jira 评论。挑战：简洁而不失礼貌，专业而不冗长。本文覆盖三种最常见格式。

4. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Subject [TICKET-ID] Action — Description [工单号] 动作 - 描述 Use square brackets for ticket ID, dash for action verb. Scannable in inbox.
  • Open Quick update on… 快速同步… The cleanest email opener for status updates. FYI for information only.
  • Format Status: / UAT: / Blockers: / Ask: 状态 / UAT / 阻塞 / 请求 Use bolded key + colon + content. Scannable in 5 seconds.
  • Ask Could you confirm the UAT window? 能确认一下 UAT 窗口吗？ Always end emails with a concrete ask, not just "let me know".
  • Soften No rush — your morning is fine. 不急——你早上有空就行 Asynchronous communication killer phrase. Always add when the request isn't urgent.
  • Appreciate Lifesaver. Thanks! 救了我 / 帮大忙 Quick thanks closes the loop. "Appreciate it" is more formal.
  • Document Documented in Confluence — TICKET-ID Test Plan. 文档见 Confluence — 工单号 Test Plan Always link to the source of truth rather than re-explain in the message.
  • Tag Tagging @Priya for review. @Priya 请 review Use @mention to direct the request. Avoid "Please review" with no owner.

5. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • FYI n. 仅供参考 (For Your Information) Used in subject lines to indicate the email is informational, no action needed.
  • ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival) n. 预计完成时间 When will X be done? Used for deliveries, deployments, ticket completion.
  • source of truth (SoT) n. 唯一事实来源 The authoritative source for a piece of information (usually Confluence, not chat).
  • code-complete n. 代码完成 (开发完成, 待测试) All code written and merged. Next step: testing.
  • build green adj. 构建通过 All tests passed, no compilation errors. Red build = failing.
  • Jira / Confluence Jira 工单 / Confluence 文档 HSBC's standard tools. Jira for tickets, Confluence for documentation.

6. Grammar Notes 语法点

① Email Opener Patterns

Three standard openers, in order of formality:

  • Hi Priya, (standard, neutral — use this by default)
  • Hi team, (group email)
  • Dear Mr. Smith, (very formal, only for first contact)

② Concise Bullet Structure

The "Status: / Blockers: / Ask:" pattern is the gold standard for technical status emails:

  • Status: Code-complete, PR merged
  • Deployed: UAT — 10:00 HKT
  • Blockers: None
  • Ask: Confirm UAT window

③ "Could you…?" vs "Please…" in Writing

In writing, "Please…" can sound curt or transactional. "Could you…?" sounds more professional:

  • ❌ "Please review the PR."
  • ✅ "Could you review the PR when you have a moment?"
  • ❌ "Please send the file."
  • ✅ "Could you send the file when you get a chance?"

7. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

✉️ The HSBC Written Communication Principles
  1. Lead with the action. Subject line should say what you want, not just describe the topic. "[TICKET] Update — Code Complete" beats "Update on payment retry".
  2. One email = one ask. Multiple asks? Send multiple emails or clearly numbered points.
  3. Be scannable. Use bolded labels (Status:, Blockers:, Ask:), short paragraphs, bullet points.
  4. Always link the source. Don't paste full context — link to Confluence, Jira, or the PR. Reduces email length.
  5. End with an ask or "no action needed". Otherwise the reader is left wondering "What do you want me to do?"
  6. Time-zone aware. When saying "tomorrow" — clarify "tomorrow HKT" or "tomorrow GMT".
⚠️ Common Mistakes in Written English
  • ❌ "Please kindly revert back to me." (Indian-English influence; awkward in HSBC)
  • ✅ "Could you let me know when you have a moment?"
  • ❌ "Sorry for the inconvenience." at the top of every email (over-apologizing)
  • ✅ Only apologize when genuinely at fault: "Apologies for the late reply — was on annual leave."
  • ❌ "Dear Sir/Madam" in 2025 (sounds stilted, impersonal)
  • ✅ "Hi Priya," or "Hi team,"

"Hey @priya, can you pls review my PR asap, I need to deploy today. Thanks!!!"

"Hi @Priya 👋 — could you take a look at PR #1421 when you have a moment? Aiming to deploy today if the review goes smoothly, but happy to push to tomorrow if you need more time. No rush if you've got other priorities. Thanks!"

Deployed (past tense in subject) / update (noun) / None / confirm (base form after could you) / rush
