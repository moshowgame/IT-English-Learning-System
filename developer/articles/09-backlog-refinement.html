Backlog Refinement / Grooming

Article 09 · Module 2: Meeting Workshops · Developer English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🔍 Ask sharp questions, clarify AC, raise technical concerns

1. Scenario 场景背景

The team is doing Backlog Refinement for the next sprint. Jonathan (BA) walks through a story: PAY-2210 — "Allow customer service reps (CSRs) to view recent transaction disputes for a customer account." The story is in the next sprint, and you need to help clarify the requirements, identify technical risks, and refine the AC.

团队在为下个冲刺做 需求澄清。Jonathan（BA）介绍一张卡：PAY-2210—— 允许客服代表（CSR）查看客户账户最近的交易争议记录。这张卡在下个冲刺， 你需要 澄清需求、识别技术风险、完善 AC。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Jonathan (BA): OK, story PAY-2210. The high-level AC: CSRs should be able to view a list of recent disputes for a customer, filtered by account. Initial estimate — 5 points. Thoughts?好的，故事 PAY-2210：高级验收标准——客服坐席能查看某客户最近的争议列表，并按账户筛选。初步估算 5 个故事点。有什么想法？
You: Quick clarifying question — by "recent", do we mean last 30 days, last 90 days, or configurable? And is this read-only, or do CSRs also need to take actions (e.g., add notes, escalate)?快速澄清一下——“最近”是指最近 30 天、90 天，还是可配置的？另外这是只读，还是坐席也要能操作（比如添加备注、升级）？
Jonathan (BA): Good question. Read-only for now, but we want to leave room for actions in the next release. "Recent" — let's say last 90 days by default, but with a date range filter in the UI.好问题。暂时只读，但下一版要预留操作空间。“最近”默认按最近 90 天算，UI 上提供日期范围筛选。
You: Got it. Two technical concerns I'd like to flag:明白。我想提两个技术上的顾虑：
You: One — data source. Where does the dispute data live? Is it in the same DB as the transaction, or a separate disputes service? That'll affect the query approach.第一——数据源。争议数据存在哪里？和交易同库，还是独立的争议服务？这会影响查询方案。
Jonathan (BA): Separate service — the Disputes microservice owns it. There's a REST API we can call.独立服务——Disputes 微服务拥有数据，提供 REST API 可调用。
You: OK, that's manageable. Two — performance. If a CSR is viewing disputes for a high-net-worth client with hundreds of accounts, the N+1 problem could be an issue. Have you thought about whether we need pagination and sorting from day one?好的，这好办。第二——性能。如果坐席查看高净值客户（几百个账户）的争议，N+1 问题会很严重。你们有没有考虑过从第一天起就加分页和排序？
Jonathan (BA): Hmm, good point. Let's add pagination — default 20 per page, with the option to filter by status (open, closed, under review).嗯，说得对。加分页——默认每页 20 条，可以按状态筛选（待处理、已关闭、审核中）。
Priya (TL): I'd add access control to the AC. CSRs should only see disputes for customers in their portfolio, not all customers. RBAC check at the API gateway.我建议在验收标准里加权限控制。坐席只能看自己名下的客户争议，不能看所有客户。在 API 网关层做 RBAC 校验。
You: Second that — and we should log every dispute view in the audit log. Compliance will definitely ask for this.同意——而且我们应该把每次争议查看都记到审计日志里，合规部门一定会要求这点。
Jonathan (BA): Good catch — I'll add audit logging as a non-functional requirement. OK, revised estimate?提得好——我把审计日志加为非功能性需求。好的，修正后的估算是多少？
You: With pagination, RBAC, and audit logging, I'd revise up to 8 points. The audit logging alone is a 2-pointer — we need to design the schema with InfoSec first.加上分页、RBAC 和审计日志，我修正为 8 个故事点。光审计日志就值 2 点——需要先和 InfoSec 一起设计 schema。
Jonathan (BA): Fair enough — 8 points, revised AC as discussed. Anything else before we move on?没问题——8 个点，验收标准按讨论的修改。还有其他要补充的吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Clarify Quick clarifying question — by "recent", do we mean…? 澄清一下——"最近"是指…？ Vague terms (recent, fast, secure) must be quantified in refinement.
  • Flag Two technical concerns I'd like to flag. 两个技术关注点想提一下 Use flag for risks. Softens the delivery.
  • Affect That'll affect the query approach. 这会影响查询方式 Always link your question to a concrete technical consequence.
  • Risk The N+1 problem could be an issue. N+1 问题可能是个风险 Use could be / might be / could become to soften risk statements.
  • Suggestion Have you thought about pagination from day one? 考虑过从一开始就分页吗？ A polite way to suggest adding a feature to the AC.
  • Agree Second that. 同意 Quick agreement. "I second that" is a formal version.
  • Predict Compliance will definitely ask for this. Compliance 一定会问这个 When flagging a regulatory concern, name the team that will enforce it.
  • Revise I'd revise up to 8 points. 我会调整到 8 个点 Always give a revised size when new AC is added. Revise up / down.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • backlog refinement / grooming n. 需求澄清会 Ongoing meeting to break down, estimate, and prioritize backlog items.
  • acceptance criteria (AC) n. 验收标准 Conditions that must be met for a story to be considered done.
  • CSR (Customer Service Representative) n. 客服代表 Frontline staff handling customer queries, disputes, and account changes.
  • RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) n. 基于角色的访问控制 Access control based on user's role. CSR can only view their portfolio.
  • audit log banking 审计日志 Immutable log of who did what and when. Required by banking regulators.
  • non-functional requirement (NFR) n. 非功能需求 Requirements about how the system behaves (performance, security, audit) rather than what it does.
  • pagination n. 分页 Splitting large result sets into pages (e.g., 20 per page).
  • high-net-worth (HNW) banking 高净值客户 HSBC's Premier / Jade / Private Bank segments serve HNW clients.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① Quantifying Vague Terms

Always push for specific numbers in refinement:

  • "Recent" → last 30 days? 90 days? Configurable?
  • "Fast" → p95 latency < 200ms?
  • "All users" → which roles? Any exclusions?
  • "Soon" → by when? What's the deadline?

② "Could be / might be" for Risk

When raising a technical concern, hedge:

  • ❌ "This will fail in production." (over-confident)
  • ✅ "This could be an issue if we have high-net-worth clients."

③ "Revise up / down"

When new info changes the estimate:

  • I'd revise up to 8 points. (estimate increased)
  • I'd revise down to 3 points. (estimate decreased)
  • I'd stick with 5 points. (no change)

6. Cultural Tips 文化/职场 Tips

🔍 The Developer's Role in Refinement

Many developers think refinement is the BA's job. It's not. At HSBC, developers are expected to:

  • Quantify vague requirements — "fast" must become a number.
  • Flag technical risks early — N+1, RBAC, audit logging, KMS keys.
  • Push back on under-sized stories — better now than in the sprint.
  • Name compliance concerns — InfoSec, Audit, Data Privacy teams.

Silent developers in refinement lead to under-sized stories, mid-sprint surprises, and missed regulatory requirements.

⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ❌ "Looks fine, let's go." (passive)
  • ✅ "Looks good overall. One thing to clarify — the data source is the Disputes service, right?"
  • ❌ Nitpicking on minor wording instead of raising real technical risks.
  • ❌ Saying "this will take 8 points" without explaining why — always justify the size.

7. Practice 练习题

✍️ Practice Drills
Q1. Translate to native-sounding refinement English:

"这个能说具体点吗？'最近'是几天？还有，CSR 能修改争议状态吗，还是只能看？"

"Quick clarifying question — by "recent", do we mean last 30 days, 90 days, or configurable? And is this read-only, or can the CSR also update the dispute status?"
Q2. Flag a technical concern during refinement (sample):

The story: "Allow users to download their transaction history as CSV, last 12 months, max 100k rows." What technical concern would you raise?

"One technical concern — 100k rows as a synchronous CSV download could timeout the request and block the worker thread. Have you considered an async generation approach where the user requests the export, we generate in the background, and email them the download link when ready? It's a more robust pattern for large exports."
Q3. Fill in the right refinement phrases:

"Quick _____ (clarify) question — by 'soon', do we mean this sprint or next? / I'd _____ (revise) up to 8 points with the audit logging added. / That'll _____ (affect) the API design. / Compliance will definitely _____ (ask) for this."

clarifying (gerund) / revise (base form after I'd) / affect (base form) / ask (base form)
Q4. Role-play:

The BA presents: "TRD-3300: Allow Relationship Managers (RMs) to assign a customer to a different RM, with email notification to both RMs. Initial estimate: 2 points." Write 3 questions / concerns you would raise, then give a revised estimate.

"Quick questions / concerns:
  1. RBAC — can any RM reassign any customer, or only their own portfolio? I'd guess the latter, but want to confirm with InfoSec.
  2. Audit logging — reassigning a customer is a sensitive action. We should log the old RM, new RM, timestamp, and reason. Compliance will want this.
  3. Email — is the email transactional (real-time) or batched? If real-time, we need to design the email template with the comms team and confirm the SMTP / SES sender is approved.
With audit logging and email integration, I'd revise up to 5 points. The base reassignment logic is straightforward, but the audit + email + RBAC is the real cost."
