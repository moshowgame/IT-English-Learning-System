Welcome, Project Manager / 项目经理你好

As a Project Manager (PM) in a foreign bank IT organisation, you sit at the intersection of delivery, governance, risk, and stakeholder communication. You run SteerCos, own the RAID log, manage vendors, chase regulatory milestones (HKMA, MAS, FCA), and translate between engineers and senior business sponsors across the UK, Hong Kong, mainland China, India, and the US. This section focuses on the spoken English scenarios you actually face — from a 60-second standup to telling a sponsor the project will slip a quarter.

面向外企银行 IT 项目经理，本节聚焦你日常真正遇到的口语场景——从 60 秒站会到 告诉 Sponsor 项目要推迟一个季度。每篇文章包含真实场景、英文对话、中文解释、关键句型与练习。

What you'll learn / 你将学到

  • ✅ Run a project standup, weekly status, and SteerCo with confidence: RAG status / on track / at risk / slipped / blocked
  • ✅ Own the RAID log — speak the language of risk, assumption, issue, dependency
  • ✅ Manage budget, resources, vendors, and phase-gate governance in spoken English
  • ✅ Escalate politely but firmly to senior stakeholders, MDs, and Group functions
  • ✅ Communicate regulatory projects (HKMA, MAS TRM, FCA, GDPR) with the right tone and vocabulary
  • ✅ Use banking PM terminology correctly: SteerCo, Gating, RAG, RAID, NPV, BAU, MOC, P50/P80

How to Use This Section / 学习建议

💡 Daily Practice Tip

Pick one article per day. PMs live in status updates, escalations, and trade-off conversations. After reading each dialogue, try to paraphrase it from memory — that is the muscle you actually use in SteerCo.

⚠️ For Real Foreign-Bank Work

Always validate project governance terms (SteerCo, Gating, RAG, MOC) with your local Group PMO and Risk teams before circulating. Group Standards (Project Lifecycle, Group Cyber, Third-Party Risk) override local practice. Regulator milestones (HKMA, MAS, FCA) are sacred — never trade them for scope.
