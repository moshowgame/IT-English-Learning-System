Telling the Sponsor the Project Will Slip

Article 15 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · PM English

⏱ 9 min read 🟠 Advanced 🎯 Bad news early, root cause, options, recommendation, no surprise, no blame

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday 15:00 HKT. You have a 30-minute 1:1 with the Sponsor, Margaret, MD of Risk & Compliance Technology. You need to tell her that the Regulatory Reporting Modernisation project will slip the HKMA Q3 milestone by 4 weeks. The cause is a combination of the vendor (AcmeTech) missing M3 by 2 weeks and a Group Cyber resourcing gap that delayed M4 SIT by 2 weeks. You have the conversation face-to-face; you followed it with a written escalation email yesterday.

周二 15:00 香港时间，你和 Sponsor Margaret（风险与合规技术部 MD）有 30 分钟 1:1。你需要告诉她监管报送现代化项目将推迟 HKMA Q3 里程碑 4 周。原因有两个：厂商（AcmeTech）M3 延期 2 周，以及集团 Cyber 资源缺口推迟 M4 SIT 2 周。你面对面谈；昨天你已经发过书面升级邮件。

Telling the Sponsor the project will slip is the PM's hardest conversation. The instinct is to delay, soften, or hide. Don't. The professional pattern is: headline the slip, own the cause, present options, recommend, ask. The Sponsor respects the PM who tells the truth early — and learns to distrust the one who doesn't.

告诉 Sponsor 项目要延期是 PM 最难的对话。本能是推迟、软化或隐藏。不要。专业模式是：开门见山说延期、承担原因、给出方案、推荐、ask。Sponsor 尊重早说实话的 PM——并学会不信任不早说的 PM。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Margaret: Wei, let's start with where we are. Anything I need to know?Wei，先说现状。有什么我需要知道的吗？
You (PM): Yes. I have to tell you the project will slip the HKMA Q3 milestone by 4 weeks. No surprise — you have the escalation email from yesterday. Headline: HKMA slips from 15 September to 13 October.有。我必须告诉你项目将延期 HKMA Q3 里程碑 4 周。没有意外——你昨天有升级邮件。一句话：HKMA 从 9 月 15 日推迟到 10 月 13 日。
Margaret: What's the root cause? Is it the vendor or us?根因是什么？是厂商还是我们？
You (PM): Both, in roughly equal measure. Vendor: AcmeTech missed M3 build by 2 weeks. Root cause was a key engineer who left and a schema change that took longer than estimated. On our side: the Group Cyber resourcing gap delayed M4 SIT by 2 weeks — we knew about the gap from M2 but couldn't close it until the SteerCo approval 3 weeks ago.两者各占一半。厂商：AcmeTech M3 构建延期 2 周。根因是核心工程师离职和schema 变更比预期长。我们这边：集团 Cyber 资源缺口推迟 M4 SIT 2 周——我们 M2 就知道缺口，但直到 3 周前 SteerCo 批准后才关闭。
Margaret: 4 weeks is a lot. What are our options?4 周有点多。我们有什么方案？
You (PM): Three options, with cost and risk. Option A: compress M4 SIT from 6 to 4 weeks, recovers 2 weeks. Probability medium, impact medium. Cost $0, but test coverage drops by 15%. Option B: add 2 offshore QA contractors for 6 weeks, recovers 2 weeks. Probability high, impact low. Cost $120K, recoverable from the offshore saving. Option C: request a 4-week HKMA extension. Probability high, impact high — regulator relationship risk.三个方案，有成本和风险。方案 A：M4 SIT 从 6 周压缩到 4 周，恢复 2 周。概率中，影响中。成本 0 美元，但测试覆盖率降 15%。方案 B：增加 2 名离岸 QA 外包 6 周，恢复 2 周。概率高，影响低。成本 12 万美元，可从离岸节省中回收。方案 C：申请 HKMA 延期 4 周。概率高，影响高——监管关系风险。
Margaret: Why is Option C high impact?为什么方案 C 影响高？
You (PM): Two reasons. First, we've committed to the HKMA in writing on 15 September. A public extension is visible in the supervisory dialogue. Second, we've burned buffer on a prior extension 18 months ago; a second one tests the regulator's patience. Not recommended unless we have no other choice.两个原因。一，我们已书面承诺 HKMA 9 月 15 日。公开延期在监管对话中可见。二，我们18 个月前已用了一次延期；再延期会测试监管耐心。除非没选择否则不推荐。
Margaret: What's your recommendation?你推荐哪个？
You (PM): Strong recommendation: Option A + Option B in parallel. This recovers 3 weeks. Combined slip: 1 week, not 4. Total cost $120K, recoverable. Test coverage drops 15%, but I have a workaround — the offshore QA team does regression tests on the cutover weekend, recovering coverage.强烈建议：方案 A + 方案 B 并行执行。恢复 3 周。合并延期 1 周，而非 4 周。总成本 12 万美元，可回收。测试覆盖率降 15%，但我有 workaround——离岸 QA 团队在切换周末做回归测试，恢复覆盖率。
Margaret: Option A + B is approved. What's the impact on the MAS TRM date?方案 A + B 批准。MAS TRM 日期影响是什么？
You (PM): No impact. MAS TRM is 28 September, and the workstream sequencing means MAS TRM does not depend on the M4 SIT recovery. MAS TRM stays on plan.无影响。MAS TRM 是 9 月 28 日，工作流排序意味着 MAS TRM 不依赖 M4 SIT 恢复。MAS TRM 仍按计划。
Margaret: Good. What do you need from me?好。你需要我做什么？
You (PM): Three things. One: sign off the $120K contractor cost. Two: endorse the test coverage workaround with Group Cyber. Three: notify the HKMA in writing that we've shifted the submission window by 1 week, not 4. I'd like to send the note by Friday, after I've walked Group Cyber through the workaround.三件事。一：签批 12 万美元外包成本。二：背书和集团 Cyber 的测试覆盖率 workaround。三：书面通知 HKMA 我们已把提交窗口调整 1 周，而非 4 周。我希望在周五前发出通知，先和集团 Cyber 过 workaround。
Margaret: All three approved. Thank you for telling me early, Wei. Anything else?三件都批准。Wei，谢谢你早告诉我。还有别的吗？
You (PM): No from me. I'll send the HKMA note draft to you by EOD for sign-off.我这边没有。HKMA 通知草稿今日 EOD 前发你签批。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Headline I have to tell you the project will slip the HKMA Q3 milestone by 4 weeks. 我必须告诉你项目将延期 HKMA Q3 里程碑 4 周 Open with the slip itself. No throat-clearing. The Sponsor needs the headline in 5 seconds.
  • No surprise No surprise — you have the escalation email from yesterday. 没有意外——你昨天有升级邮件 "No surprise" signals: I told you in writing first. The Sponsor can't claim they didn't know.
  • Root cause Both, in roughly equal measure. Vendor: … On our side: … 两者各占一半。厂商：……我们这边：…… Own the part you control. Don't blame the vendor exclusively. Sponsor respects PMs who own their half.
  • Options Three options, with cost and risk for each. 三个方案，每个有成本和风险 Always present 2-3 options. The Sponsor chooses. PMs recommend, not decide.
  • Recommendation Strong recommendation: Option A + B in parallel. 强烈建议：方案 A + B 并行执行 "Strong recommendation" + name the option + explain the trade-off. Defend it.
  • Impact What's the impact on the MAS TRM date? MAS TRM 日期影响是什么？ The Sponsor will ask the impact on the OTHER regulator date. Always be ready with the second answer.
  • Ask What do you need from me? 你需要我做什么？ Always end with the Sponsor's question, not yours. "What do you need from me?" gives them control.
  • Early Thank you for telling me early, Wei. Wei，谢谢你早告诉我 The Sponsor's gratitude for early bad news is the PM's reward. Build the habit. Trust compounds.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Slip n. 延期 A delay in a milestone or deliverable.
  • Root cause n. 根因 The underlying reason for the slip, not the symptom.
  • Own v. 承担 Take responsibility for the part you control.
  • No surprise n. 不出意外 The PM's commitment: bad news first, in writing.
  • Strong recommendation n. 强烈建议 The PM's preferred option, defended with evidence.
  • Workaround n. 临时方案 A short-term fix used while a permanent solution is being built.
  • Combined slip n. 合并延期 The total slip after recovery options are applied.
  • Test coverage n. 测试覆盖率 The percentage of code or requirements covered by tests.
  • Regression test n. 回归测试 Tests run to ensure existing functionality still works after changes.
  • Supervisory dialogue n. 监管对话 Formal communication between the bank and its regulators.
  • Workstream sequencing n. 工作流排序 The order in which workstreams deliver, dictating dependencies.
  • Notify in writing n. 书面通知 A formal written communication, usually by the Sponsor or PM.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I have to tell you the project will slip" — modal + future

The hardest sentence in PM English. I have to tell you the project will slip. The modal have to signals obligation; will slip is the future fact. Don't soften it: "might slip" loses urgency; "may not make it" loses clarity. Will slip is the professional verb.

PM 英语中最难的一句话。I have to tell you the project will slip. have to 表义务；will slip 是未来事实。

② "Both, in roughly equal measure" — own your half

When the cause is shared, name both halves. Both, in roughly equal measure. Vendor: … On our side: … Sponsors respect PMs who own their half more than PMs who blame the vendor.

当原因分担时，命名两半。两者各占一半。厂商：……我们这边：……

③ "Strong recommendation: Option A + B in parallel" — combine options

Sometimes the recommendation is a combination. Strong recommendation: Option A + B in parallel. This shows you've thought about the trade-offs and combined the best of each. Don't be afraid to recommend a combination.

有时建议是组合方案。强烈建议：方案 A + B 并行执行。不要害怕推荐组合。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Tell the truth early, in writing. Bad news early is forgiven. Bad news late is not. The escalation email yesterday set the stage; the 1:1 today is the close.
  • Own your half. When the cause is shared, name both halves. Sponsors respect PMs who own more than they blame.
  • Headline the slip in 10 seconds. "HKMA slips from 15 Sep to 13 Oct" is the first sentence. No throat-clearing. The Sponsor needs the headline.
  • Root cause next, options after. Don't dump options before the Sponsor knows why. Cause → impact → options → recommendation → ask.
  • Present 2-3 options, never one. The Sponsor chooses. PMs recommend, not decide. "What do you need from me?" gives them control.
  • Be ready for the second regulator question. The Sponsor will ask the impact on the OTHER regulator date. Have the answer before they ask.
  • End with the Sponsor's question. "What do you need from me?" — let the Sponsor close the agenda, not you.
  • "Thank you for telling me early" is the reward. Trust compounds. Every early bad-news conversation is a deposit in the trust account.
  • Don't argue the cause in the room. If the Sponsor challenges the root cause, acknowledge and take it away. Argue in writing, not in the 1:1.

Exercise 1 — Translate the slip conversation

Translate the slip conversation excerpt into natural Chinese.

Exercise 2 — Spot the slip-conversation mistake

Which opening line is most professional? Why?

  1. "I have to tell you the project will slip the HKMA Q3 milestone by 4 weeks."
  2. "There's a small problem with the timeline, but we have it under control."
  3. "Margaret, do you have a few minutes? I have some bad news."
  4. "We may or may not slip, but it's probably fine."

① is the most professional. It headlines the slip, names the milestone, quantifies the delay. ② hides the slip ("small problem"). ③ buries the headline. ④ is non-committal. The Sponsor needs the slip in the first sentence. Anything else loses trust.
