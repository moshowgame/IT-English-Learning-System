Meeting Minutes for PM — Decisions, Owners, Due Dates

Article 12 · Module 3: Written Communication · PM English

⏱ 7 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 Standard template, decisions log, actions log, distribution discipline, 24-hour rule

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday 11:00 HKT. You have just finished a 60-minute SteerCo pre-meeting with the workstream leads. The Sponsor (MD) joins SteerCo proper in 30 minutes, and the PMO needs the minutes for the SteerCo pack. You have 20 minutes to draft, send, and have Karen (PMO) reflect them. Speed and accuracy both matter.

周二 11:00 香港时间，你刚结束和工作流负责人的 60 分钟SteerCo 预备会。Sponsor（MD）30 分钟后参加正式 SteerCo，PMO 需要会议纪要放入 SteerCo 资料。你有 20 分钟起草、发送、并让 Karen（PMO）反映出来。速度与准确都重要。

Meeting minutes are the PM's audit trail. Every decision, every action, every owner, every due date. Send them within 24 hours. The minutes that arrive late are the minutes nobody reads.

会议纪要是 PM 的审计追踪。每个决策、每个 action、每个 owner、每个 due date。24 小时内发出。迟到的纪要是没人读的纪要。

2. Sample Minutes 会议纪要样例

The following is a complete minutes email for a SteerCo pre-meeting. Read the structure — it's the standard PM template that gets sent within 24 hours of any governance meeting.

以下是一封完整的 SteerCo 预备会纪要邮件。读结构——这是任何治理会议后 24 小时内发出的标准 PM 模板。

From: Wei.Zhang@hsbc.com.hk
To: Daniel.Chen@hsbc.co.uk (TL); Mark.Li@hsbc.com.hk (Data Quality); Mei.Wong@hsbc.com.hk (Compliance); Steve.Kumar@hsbc.co.uk (Cyber); Yuki.Tanaka@hsbc.co.jp (Architect); Priya.Patel@hsbc.com (PMO); Karen.Walsh@hsbc.co.uk (Group PMO)
Cc: Margaret.Lee@hsbc.com.hk (Sponsor, MD)
Date: Tue 8 Jul 2026 11:25 HKT
Subject: [RRM] SteerCo Pre-Meeting Minutes — 8 Jul — 4 actions, 2 decisions for SteerCo

All —

Please find the minutes from this morning's SteerCo pre-meeting. 4 actions, 2 decisions for SteerCo. Please review and flag any corrections by EOD today. Final minutes will be in the SteerCo pack.

Meeting details

  • Meeting: SteerCo pre-meeting, RRM programme
  • Date / time: Tue 8 Jul 2026, 10:00–11:00 HKT
  • Chair: Wei Zhang (PM)
  • Attendees: Daniel Chen, Mark Li, Mei Wong, Steve Kumar, Yuki Tanaka, Priya Patel, Karen Walsh
  • Apologies: None

Decisions for SteerCo (decisions needed today)

  1. D-2026-07-08-01 — Approve the 2-FTE Group Cyber contract uplift, cost $320K for 9 months, fully recoverable from the central programme budget. Recommended for approval. Owner: Steve Kumar. Due: SteerCo today.
  2. D-2026-07-08-02 — Approve the $200K FX contingency drawdown to close the M3 cost forecast gap. Recommended for approval. Owner: Grace Liu (Finance). Due: SteerCo today.

Decisions made in the meeting (no SteerCo required)

  1. D-2026-07-08-03 — Endorse the Data Quality recovery plan: 1 internal move (Lin from KYC squad) starting 1 Aug. Owner: Mark Li. Status: endorsed.
  2. D-2026-07-08-04 — Endorse the cutover planning workshop on Monday 15 Jul. Owner: Wei Zhang. Status: endorsed.

Actions

  1. A-2026-07-08-01 — Steve to engage Cyber panel for 2 contractor FTEs. Due: Fri 12 Jul EOD.
  2. A-2026-07-08-02 — Mark to formalise Lin's internal move with HR. Due: Wed 10 Jul EOD.
  3. A-2026-07-08-03 — Yuki to circulate the API rate-limiting decision one-pager. Due: Wed 10 Jul EOD.
  4. A-2026-07-08-04 — Wei to circulate the cutover workshop agenda. Due: Fri 12 Jul EOD.

Discussion summary

  • Cyber resourcing: Steve confirmed Option A (contract uplift) is realistic; Option B (defer pen tests) was rejected due to MAS milestone risk.
  • FX exposure: Grace confirmed the forward contract is locked through month 16; no further exposure after that.
  • Data Quality: Mark confirmed Lin's internal move has been agreed in principle; HR formalisation this week.
  • Cutover planning: All workstream leads agreed to attend the Monday workshop. Agenda to follow.

Risks raised

  • R-2026-07-08-01 — Vendor cutover window still not confirmed by AcmeTech. Probability medium, impact high. Owner: Wei. Due: 18 Jul.

Next meeting: SteerCo Tue 8 Jul 14:00 HKT.

Best,
Wei
PM, Regulatory Reporting Modernisation

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Subject [RRM] SteerCo Pre-Meeting Minutes — 8 Jul — 4 actions, 2 decisions for SteerCo [RRM] SteerCo 预备会纪要——7 月 8 日——4 个 action，2 个 SteerCo 决策 Subject line carries: project tag, meeting type, date, action count, decision count. Scan in 5 seconds.
  • Decisions for SteerCo Decisions for SteerCo (decisions needed today) SteerCo 决策（今日需决策） Always separate "decisions for SteerCo" from "decisions made in the meeting". Different routing.
  • Decision ID D-2026-07-08-01 决策编号 Number every decision. The ID is the audit trail. Auditors will read it.
  • Action Steve to engage Cyber panel. Due: Fri 12 Jul EOD. Steve 启动 Cyber panel。Due: 7 月 12 日周五 EOD Every action: name, what, by when. Three attributes. No more, no less.
  • Status Status: endorsed / approved / in progress / closed 状态：背书 / 批准 / 进行中 / 已关闭 Status flag at the end of each item. One word. The reader scans the statuses.
  • Owner Owner: Steve Kumar. Due: Fri 12 Jul EOD. Owner: Steve Kumar。Due: 7 月 12 日周五 EOD Single owner per action. Shared ownership is no ownership.
  • Recommended Recommended for approval. 建议批准 Tag decisions as "recommended for approval" so the chair knows the PM's position.
  • 24-hour rule Please flag any corrections by EOD today. 请今日 EOD 前指出任何更正 Send minutes within 24 hours. Ask for corrections by EOD. Late minutes don't get read.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Meeting minutes n. 会议纪要 The written record of a meeting: attendees, decisions, actions.
  • Decisions log n. 决策日志 The list of decisions made in the meeting, each with an ID.
  • Actions log n. Action 日志 The list of actions from the meeting, each with owner and due date.
  • Decision ID n. 决策编号 A unique identifier for each decision (e.g., D-2026-07-08-01).
  • Endorsed n. 已背书 The decision has been agreed in the meeting and needs no further approval.
  • Apologies n. 缺席致歉 People who were invited but could not attend.
  • Discussion summary n. 讨论摘要 A short narrative of what was discussed, not a transcript.
  • Audit trail n. 审计追踪 The written record that allows auditors to reconstruct what happened.
  • Distribution list n. 分发名单 The people who should receive the minutes, in To and Cc.
  • 24-hour rule n. 24 小时规则 Minutes must be sent within 24 hours of the meeting.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Decisions for SteerCo" vs "Decisions made in the meeting" — separate the buckets

Always separate decisions that need SteerCo approval from decisions that were made in the meeting. Different routing, different timelines, different consequences. The SteerCo chair reads the first list; the workstream leads read the second.

始终分开需 SteerCo 批准的决策和会议中已做的决策。不同的流转路径。

② "D-2026-07-08-01" — number every decision

Every decision gets a unique ID: D-YYYY-MM-DD-NN. The ID is the audit trail. Auditors and SteerCo packs reference decisions by ID, not by name.

每个决策分配唯一编号：D-YYYY-MM-DD-NN。编号就是审计追踪。

③ "Owner + Due" — the action pair

Every action gets exactly two attributes: owner + due. Three-attribute is even better: owner, what, due. But minimum two. "We're working on it" is not an action.

每个 action 恰好两个属性：owner + due。"我们正在处理"不是 action。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • 24-hour rule is non-negotiable. Minutes sent after 24 hours are minutes nobody reads. Send within the day, ideally within the hour for governance meetings.
  • Decisions and actions are different. Decisions change project state. Actions are tasks. Separate them. Number them. Owner them.
  • Single owner per action. Shared ownership is no ownership. "Mark and Steve" means nobody owns it.
  • Always include a discussion summary. Auditors and absentees need to know what was discussed, not just the decisions.
  • Cc the Sponsor. Even if the Sponsor wasn't in the meeting, Cc them. They need to know what was said before SteerCo.
  • End with the next meeting. "Next meeting: SteerCo Tue 8 Jul 14:00 HKT" — readers know the cadence and the next touchpoint.
  • Flag corrections by EOD. "Please flag any corrections by EOD today" — gives people a deadline to dispute, not drift.
  • Use the same template every time. Meeting details → decisions for SteerCo → decisions made → actions → discussion → risks → next meeting. Same order, every meeting.

Exercise 2 — Spot the minutes mistake

Which minutes line is most professional? Why?

  1. "Steve to engage the panel. Mark to formalise the move. Yuki to send the one-pager."
  2. "A-2026-07-08-01 — Steve to engage Cyber panel for 2 contractor FTEs. Due: Fri 12 Jul EOD."
  3. "The team agreed to engage the panel, formalise the move, and send the one-pager."
  4. "We're all working on these things, more details to follow."

② is the most professional. It has an action ID, an owner, what, and a due date. ① has owners but no IDs or dates. ③ is collective — no owner. ④ is hand-waving. Minutes are an audit trail. Every action needs an ID, owner, what, due.

Exercise 3 — Write a minutes email

You are the PM. Write a 6-section minutes email for a 30-min RAID review meeting: 2 decisions made (endorse risk scoring, close issue I-003), 3 actions (Mei by Fri, James by Wed, you by Mon), 1 risk raised. Use the standard template.
