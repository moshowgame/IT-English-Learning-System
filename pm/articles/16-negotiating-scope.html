Negotiating Scope with a Senior Stakeholder — MoSCoW and Trade-offs

Article 16 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · PM English

⏱ 8 min read 🟠 Advanced 🎯 MoSCoW, business value, protected list, scope cut, phase 2, win-win

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday 14:00 HKT. You have a 60-minute scope negotiation with Richard, the Head of Wholesale Banking (MD-level stakeholder, sits on the Group ExCo). Richard has just sent an email asking for 4 new features in the Real-Time Cross-Border Payments project — 3 of which are not in the current scope. The HKMA milestone is in 4 months. You have 60 minutes to negotiate without damaging the relationship.

周四 14:00 香港时间，你和 Richard（批发银行部负责人，MD 级 stakeholder，集团 ExCo 成员）有 60 分钟范围谈判。Richard 刚发邮件要求在实时跨境支付项目中增加 4 项新功能——其中 3 项不在当前范围内。HKMA 里程碑还有 4 个月。你有 60 分钟谈判，不损害关系。

Scope negotiation is the PM's core commercial skill. Saying "yes" to everything burns the team. Saying "no" without alternatives loses the relationship. The professional pattern is: acknowledge, MoSCoW, propose, phase 2, ask. The stakeholder leaves with a yes; the project keeps its scope.

范围谈判是 PM 的核心商业技能。对所有事说 yes 会烧掉团队。没有替代方案说 no 会失去关系。专业模式是：acknowledge, MoSCoW, propose, phase 2, ask。Stakeholder 带着 yes 离开；项目保留范围。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Richard: Wei, thanks for making time. I've asked for 4 features in the email. Can we fit them in?Wei，感谢抽时间。我在邮件里要求了 4 项功能。能加进去吗？
You (PM): Richard, thank you for the email. I've walked the 4 features with the Tech Lead and the BA. I'd like to walk you through what we can do — not just a yes or no, but options.Richard，感谢你的邮件。我和 Tech Lead、BA 过过这 4 项功能。我想给你过一下我们能做的——不只是 yes 或 no，而是方案。
You (PM): Of the 4 features: Feature A (FX hedging on the corridor) — MoSCoW Must Have, fits the HKMA milestone, we can include it. Feature B (multi-currency wallet for the customer) — MoSCoW Should Have, doesn't fit HKMA but fits the FCA Q3 milestone. Feature C (sanctions screening rework) — MoSCoW Won't Have for this phase. It's out of scope per the charter and needs a separate programme.这 4 项功能中：功能 A（corridor 上 FX 对冲）——MoSCoW 必须有，符合 HKMA 里程碑，我们可以纳入。功能 B（客户多币种钱包）——MoSCoW 应该有，不符合 HKMA，但符合 FCA Q3 里程碑。功能 C（制裁筛查重构）——本阶段不做。章程中已排除，需要独立项目群。
You (PM): Feature D (instant FX rate display) — MoSCoW Could Have, low effort (about 2 dev-weeks), low business value on the wholesale side. I'd recommend deferring — but if you can name the specific business value, we can swap it in for a lower-priority Could Have in the backlog.功能 D（即时 FX 汇率显示）——MoSCoW 可以有，工作量小（约 2 个开发周），批发侧业务价值低。我建议推迟——但如果你能说出具体业务价值，我们可以在 backlog 中替换一个低优先级的 Could Have。
Richard: Feature B is important to me. Wholesale clients have been asking for the multi-currency wallet for 18 months. Can we push it into Phase 1?功能 B 对我很重要。批发客户18 个月来一直在要求多币种钱包。能推到 Phase 1 吗？
You (PM): I hear that. Counter-proposal: we pull Feature B into the HKMA milestone provided we drop one Could Have — the instant FX rate display — and recover 2 dev-weeks. Net scope impact: 0. Risk: the FCA Q3 milestone loses 1 Could Have, but we have 2 more in the backlog we can promote.我听到了。反建议：我们把功能 B 拉进 HKMA 里程碑，前提是我们砍掉一个 Could Have——即时 FX 汇率显示——回收 2 个开发周。净范围影响：0。风险：FCA Q3 里程碑失去 1 个 Could Have，但我们backlog 中还有 2 个可以提。
Richard: That works. What about Feature C?可以。功能 C 呢？
You (PM): Feature C is a separate programme. The scope charter explicitly excludes it, and the sanctions platform is owned by a different programme (the Group-wide Sanctions Screening Platform). I'd like to introduce you to that PM so we can sequence the two programmes. Best outcome: Feature C lands in Phase 2 of the sanctions platform, and we align the API contracts now so the integration is clean.功能 C 是独立项目群。章程明确排除，制裁平台由不同项目群拥有（集团级制裁名单筛查平台）。我想介绍那个 PM 给你认识，我们可以排序两个项目群。最好结果：功能 C 落地制裁平台 Phase 2，我们现在对齐 API 契约，集成更干净。
Richard: I appreciate the introduction. What do you need from me?感谢介绍。你需要我做什么？
You (PM): Two things. One: sign off the MoSCoW change (Feature B in, Feature D out) at SteerCo next week. Two: endorse the introduction to the sanctions platform PM so we can start the API contract conversation this week.两件事。一：下周 SteerCo 签批 MoSCoW 变更（功能 B 进，功能 D 出）。二：背书与制裁平台 PM 的介绍，本周开始 API 契约对话。
Richard: Both approved. Thank you, Wei. I appreciate the options rather than a flat no.两件都批准。谢谢你，Wei。感谢你给方案而不是直接拒绝。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Options I'd like to walk you through what we can do — not just a yes or no, but options. 我想给你过一下我们能做的——不只是 yes 或 no，而是方案 Open with options, not a yes/no. Sponsors respect PMs who bring choices, not refusals.
  • MoSCoW MoSCoW Must Have / Should Have / Could Have / Won't Have. MoSCoW 必须有 / 应该有 / 可以有 / 不做 MoSCoW is the universal scope framework. Use it to put each feature in a bucket.
  • Fits / Doesn't fit Fits the HKMA milestone. Doesn't fit HKMA but fits FCA Q3. 符合 HKMA 里程碑。不符合 HKMA，但符合 FCA Q3 Anchor every feature to a milestone. "Fits X" is the test for MoSCoW.
  • Out of scope Out of scope per the charter, needs a separate programme. 章程中已排除，需要独立项目群 "Out of scope per the charter" is the polite way to say "we already agreed this". The charter is your friend.
  • Business value If you can name the specific business value, we can swap it in. 如果你能说出具体业务价值，我们可以替换进来 Business value is the gate. PMs ask for value, not preference. Stakeholders respond to the question.
  • Counter-proposal Counter-proposal: we pull Feature B in provided we drop one Could Have. 反建议：把功能 B 拉进，前提是砍掉一个 Could Have Counter-proposal is the PM's secret weapon. Always pair the yes with a trade.
  • Net scope impact Net scope impact: 0. 净范围影响：0 "Net scope impact" is the Sponsors' favourite number. It says: you got what you asked for, the team didn't take on more.
  • Appreciate I appreciate the options rather than a flat no. 感谢你给方案而不是直接拒绝 The stakeholder's gratitude for options is the PM's reward. Build the habit. Sponsors remember who brought choices.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • MoSCoW n. MoSCoW 优先级 Must Have / Should Have / Could Have / Won't Have — the universal scope framework.
  • Business value n. 业务价值 The measurable benefit a feature brings to the business.
  • Counter-proposal n. 反建议 An alternative offer made in response to a request that can't be met as asked.
  • Swap v. 替换 Replace a lower-priority item with a higher-priority one at the same effort.
  • Drop v. 砍掉 Remove a Could Have from the current scope to make room for something else.
  • Promote v. 提升 Move a Could Have up to a Should or Must Have based on new value.
  • Net scope impact n. 净范围影响 The total scope change after adds and drops are netted.
  • Scope charter n. 项目章程 The boundary document defining in-scope and out-of-scope.
  • API contract n. API 契约 The formal interface agreement between two systems.
  • Sequencing n. 排序 Deciding the order of related programmes or workstreams.
  • Win-win n. 双赢 An outcome where both sides get value and neither loses.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "MoSCoW Must Have" — categorise, don't describe

When the stakeholder asks for a feature, categorise it. MoSCoW Must Have / Should Have / Could Have / Won't Have. The category drives the next sentence: fit, don't fit, swap, drop, phase 2.

当 stakeholder 要求功能时，分类它。MoSCoW 必须有 / 应该有 / 可以有 / 不做。

② "Provided we drop X" — conditional counter-proposal

The counter-proposal is conditional. Provided we drop one Could Have, we can pull Feature B in. The word provided signals the trade. Without it, the counter-proposal looks like a gift; with it, the trade is visible.

反建议是有条件的。前提是砍掉一个 Could Have，我们可以拉功能 B 进来。 provided 一词信号着 trade。

③ "Net scope impact: 0" — the headline number

Sponsors love the net number. Net scope impact: 0. It says: you got what you asked for, the team didn't take on more work. One number, ten seconds, the deal closes.

Sponsor 喜欢净数字。净范围影响：0。 一个数字，10 秒钟，成交。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Bring options, not yes/no. Sponsors respect PMs who bring choices, not refusals. The MoSCoW framework gives you four options per feature.
  • Anchor to milestones. "Fits HKMA, doesn't fit FCA" is the test. Every feature gets a milestone anchor.
  • Out of scope is your friend. When a feature is out of scope, say so — and cite the charter. The charter is the document the sponsor signed.
  • Ask for business value, not preference. "Why is this important?" beats "I want it". Sponsors respond to value questions.
  • Counter-proposal is the secret weapon. Pair every yes with a trade. "Yes, if we drop X". The trade is what makes the yes sustainable.
  • Net scope impact is the headline. "Net scope impact: 0" closes the deal. Sponsors love the net number.
  • Phase 2 is a valid answer. "We'll do it in phase 2" is a yes that protects phase 1. Always have a phase 2 envelope.
  • End with the stakeholder's question. "What do you need from me?" — let the stakeholder close the agenda, not you.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the scope negotiation

Translate the scope negotiation excerpt into natural Chinese.

"Of the 4 features: Feature A (single-customer view across corridors) — MoSCoW Must Have, fits the HKMA milestone, we can include it. Feature B (instant payment notifications) — MoSCoW Should Have, doesn't fit HKMA but fits the FCA Q3 milestone. Feature C (blockchain-based reconciliation) — MoSCoW Won't Have for this phase. It's out of scope per the charter and needs a separate programme. Feature D (in-app customer chat support) — MoSCoW Could Have, low effort (about 1 dev-week), low business value on the wholesale side. I'd recommend deferring — but if you can name the specific business value, we can swap it in for a lower-priority Could Have in the backlog. Counter-proposal: we pull Feature B into the HKMA milestone provided we drop one Could Have — the in-app chat support — and recover 1 dev-week. Net scope impact: 0. Two things I need. One: sign off the MoSCoW change at SteerCo next week. Two: endorse the introduction to the reconciliation platform PM so we can start the API contract conversation this week."

"这 4 项功能中：功能 A（跨 corridor 单一客户视图）——MoSCoW 必须有，符合 HKMA 里程碑，我们可以纳入。功能 B（即时支付通知）——MoSCoW 应该有，不符合 HKMA，但符合 FCA Q3 里程碑。功能 C（基于区块链的对账）——本阶段不做。章程中已排除，需要独立项目群。功能 D（应用内客户聊天支持）——MoSCoW 可以有，工作量小（约 1 个开发周），批发侧业务价值低。我建议推迟——但如果你能说出具体业务价值，我们可以替换 backlog 中一个低优先级的 Could Have。反建议：把功能 B 拉进 HKMA 里程碑，前提是砍掉一个 Could Have——应用内聊天——回收 1 个开发周。净范围影响：0。我需要两件事。一：下周 SteerCo 签批 MoSCoW 变更。二：背书与对账平台 PM 的介绍，本周开始 API 契约对话。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the negotiation mistake

Which line would damage the negotiation most? Why?

  1. "I'd like to walk you through what we can do — options, not just a yes or no."
  2. "We can't do any of that, the project is locked."
  3. "Feature B is MoSCoW Should Have. Counter-proposal: pull B in provided we drop one Could Have."
  4. "What do you need from me?"

② is the negotiation-damager. "We can't do any of that" is a flat no with no options. The stakeholder leaves angry. ① brings options. ③ categorises and counters. ④ gives the stakeholder control. PMs bring options, not refusals.

Exercise 3 — Run a 30-minute scope negotiation

You are the PM. A senior stakeholder asks for 3 new features in the next sprint. Build a 30-minute script: (1) acknowledge, (2) MoSCoW each, (3) recommend 1 in / 1 out via swap, (4) push 1 to phase 2, (5) end with 2 asks.

"Thank you for the email. I've walked the 3 features with the Tech Lead. Options, not a yes or no. Feature A (regulator dashboard) — MoSCoW Must Have, fits HKMA, we can include it. Feature B (real-time alerts) — MoSCoW Should Have, doesn't fit HKMA but fits FCA Q3. Feature C (AI-based anomaly detection) — MoSCoW Won't Have for this phase, out of scope per the charter, separate programme. Counter-proposal: pull Feature A in, drop one Could Have (the in-app chat support) to recover 1 dev-week. Net scope impact: 0. Feature B goes to FCA Q3, we plan it now. Feature C goes to phase 2, I'll introduce you to the AI platform PM. Two asks. One: sign off the MoSCoW swap at SteerCo next week. Two: endorse the introduction to the AI platform PM. What do you need from me?"
