Project Status Email — The Weekly Sponsorship Update

Article 11 · Module 3: Written Communication · PM English

⏱ 8 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Subject line, RAG headline, milestones, top risks, asks, sign-off — sponsor-friendly format

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday afternoon HKT. You are the PM for the Regulatory Reporting Modernisation programme. Every Friday at 16:00 you send a weekly status email to the Sponsor (MD), the workstream leads, the PMO, and key Group functions. The email must be scannable in 30 seconds, with the RAG colour, the top 3 items, and any asks. The MD reads the email on her phone.

周五下午香港时间，你是监管报送现代化项目群的 PM。每个周五 16:00 你向 Sponsor（MD）、工作流负责人、PMO 以及关键集团职能发一封周状态邮件。邮件必须能在 30 秒内扫读，包含 RAG 颜色、前 3 项、以及任何 ask。MD 在手机上读邮件。

Status emails are the PM's asynchronous signature. If the email is sloppy, the project looks sloppy. If the email is sharp, the project looks under control — even when it isn't.

状态邮件是 PM 的异步签名。邮件潦草，项目看起来潦草。邮件利落，项目看起来在控——即使不在控。

2. Sample Email 邮件样例

The following is a complete status email sent to Margaret (Sponsor, MD), the four workstream leads, the PMO, and key Group functions. Read it carefully — it follows the standard PM template.

以下是一封发给 Margaret（Sponsor，MD）、四个工作流负责人、PMO 和关键集团职能的完整状态邮件。仔细读——它遵循标准 PM 模板。

From: Wei.Zhang@hsbc.com.hk
To: Margaret.Lee@hsbc.com.hk (Sponsor, MD); Daniel.Chen@hsbc.co.uk (TL); Priya.Patel@hsbc.com (PMO); Mei.Wong@hsbc.com.hk (Compliance); Steve.Kumar@hsbc.co.uk (Group Cyber); Yuki.Tanaka@hsbc.co.jp (Architect); Mark.Li@hsbc.com.hk (Data Quality)
Cc: Karen.Walsh@hsbc.co.uk (Group PMO); David.OConnor@hsbc.com.hk (Group COO Office)
Date: Fri 12 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT
Subject: [RRM] Weekly Status — W28 — RAG Amber — 1 ask for Sponsor sign-off by Mon EOD

Margaret, all —

Please find this week's status for the Regulatory Reporting Modernisation programme. RAG is amber.

1. Headline

On track for the HKMA Q3 milestone (15 Sep) and MAS TRM submission (28 Sep). One ask from the programme: Sponsor sign-off on the FX contingency drawdown of $200K by Mon EOD. Detail in section 5.

2. Milestone view

  • M2 design sign-off — closed on time, 8 Jul. Closed.
  • M3 build — 62% complete, on plan. Reporting Engine and Integration on plan, Data Quality 3 days behind (root cause: vendor API schema change).
  • M4 UAT — starts 18 Aug, on plan.
  • Regulator milestones — no change. HKMA 15 Sep, MAS TRM 28 Sep.

3. Top 3 risks

  1. Group Cyber resourcing — 2 FTE short, probability high, impact high. Mitigation: 2 contract uplifts approved at SteerCo last week, panel engagement in flight. Owner: Steve. Due: 25 Jul.
  2. Vendor cutover window — AcmeTech has not confirmed December slot. Probability medium, impact high. Mitigation: cutover planning workshop Monday. Owner: me. Due: 18 Jul.
  3. FX exposure — $180K over budget on offshore contractor, probability medium, impact medium. Mitigation: forward contract locked through month 16. Owner: Grace (Finance). Due: closed.

4. Decisions made this week

  • SteerCo approved the 2-FTE Cyber contract uplift ($320K, recoverable from central budget).
  • SteerCo approved the $200K FX contingency drawdown (see ask below).
  • Data Quality recovery plan endorsed: 1 internal move (Lin from KYC squad) starting 1 Aug.

5. Asks

  • Margaret — sign-off on the $200K FX contingency drawdown by Mon 15 Jul EOD. One-pager attached. Without sign-off, we cannot close the M3 cost forecast and may delay M4 UAT entry by 2 days.
  • Steve — confirm Cyber panel engagement by Fri 19 Jul.
  • Mei — confirm Group Compliance sign-off on the regulator engagement plan by Wed 17 Jul.

6. Calendar

  • Mon 15 Jul, 10:00 HKT — Cross-team sync (Core Banking + Group Platform).
  • Mon 15 Jul, 14:00 HKT — RAID review.
  • Fri 19 Jul, 10:00 HKT — Weekly status forum.

Happy to take questions on the call or offline.

Best,
Wei
PM, Regulatory Reporting Modernisation

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Subject line [RRM] Weekly Status — W28 — RAG Amber — 1 ask for Sponsor sign-off by Mon EOD [RRM] 周报——W28——RAG 琥珀——1 个 Sponsor 签字 ask 周一 EOD 前 Subject line carries the project tag, week, RAG, and the most important ask. MDs filter by subject.
  • RAG RAG is amber. RAG 是琥珀色 First sentence after the greeting. RAG always comes first.
  • Headline On track for HKMA Q3 milestone, one ask for Sponsor sign-off by Mon EOD. HKMA Q3 里程碑按计划，1 个 Sponsor 签字 ask 周一 EOD 前 The headline answers: where are we, and what do we need. Two sentences maximum.
  • Milestone view M3 build: 62% complete, on plan. M3 构建：完成 62%，按计划 One bullet per milestone. Status word first (closed / on plan / behind / at risk), then the number.
  • Top 3 risks Probability high, impact high. Mitigation: … 概率高，影响高。缓解：…… Three risks max. Each gets: name + P×I + mitigation + owner + due date.
  • Ask Margaret — sign-off on the $200K FX drawdown by Mon EOD. Margaret——周一 EOD 前签批 20 万美元 FX 动用。 Each ask starts with the name. The ask is: who, what, by when. One sentence.
  • One-pager One-pager attached. 附一页摘要 Always attach a one-pager for an ask. The email is the cover; the one-pager is the evidence.
  • Sign-off Wei, PM, Regulatory Reporting Modernisation Wei，PM，监管报送现代化 Sign off with name, role, programme. Same every week. Consistency is a PM's signature.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Status email n. 状态邮件 A weekly (or periodic) written update to sponsors and stakeholders.
  • Subject line n. 邮件标题 The first thing the reader sees. Carries RAG and key ask.
  • Headline n. 一句话总结 The opening sentence(s) that summarise the week's status.
  • One-pager n. 一页摘要 A single-page attachment with detail supporting an ask.
  • Ask n. 请求 A specific decision or action needed from a named person.
  • Sign-off n. 签字 Formal approval, usually in reply to the status email.
  • Recoverable n. 可回收 Cost that lands in another budget or is offset by a saving.
  • On plan / Behind plan / At risk n. 按计划 / 落后 / 有风险 The three-state milestone status.
  • Root cause n. 根因 The underlying reason for a slip, not the symptom.
  • Closed n. 已关闭 Status flag: the item is complete and archived.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "RAG is amber" — colour first, narrative after

The RAG colour must be in the first sentence, ideally in the first line of the email. The narrative — why it's amber, what it means — comes after. MDs read colour first.

RAG 颜色必须出现在第一句，最好是邮件第一行。叙述——为什么是琥珀色、意味着什么——在后。MD 先读颜色。

② "On plan / Behind / At risk" — three-state milestone status

Each milestone gets one of three states: on plan / behind / at risk. Never "going well" or "almost done". The state is the headline; the number is the evidence.

每个里程碑给出三个状态之一：on plan / behind / at risk。

③ "By Mon EOD" — every ask needs a date

Every ask needs three attributes: who, what, by when. "Margaret, sign off the $200K drawdown by Mon EOD" has all three. "Sign off the drawdown" has only one. Asks without dates drift.

每个 ask 需要三个属性：who, what, by when。"Margaret, sign off the $200K drawdown by Mon EOD" 都有。"Sign off the drawdown" 只有一个。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Subject line is sacred. MDs filter by subject. Always include the project tag, week, RAG, and the most important ask.
  • 30-second rule. The email must be scannable in 30 seconds. If the MD has to scroll to find the ask, the email failed.
  • Three risks max. Fewer feels thin. More is too many to remember. Three is the magic number.
  • Each ask is a single sentence. "Margaret, sign off the $200K drawdown by Mon EOD." That's it. The why is in the one-pager.
  • Always attach a one-pager for an ask. The email is the cover; the one-pager is the evidence. No one-pager = no ask.
  • Reply-to-confirm is the standard sign-off. "Wei, approved" in reply is a sign-off. You don't need a meeting.
  • Same template every week. MDs read templates. Section 1 Headline, 2 Milestones, 3 Risks, 4 Decisions, 5 Asks, 6 Calendar. Same order, every week.
  • Sign off with name + role + programme. Consistency is a PM's signature. The same signature every week builds trust.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the status email

Translate the subject line and the "Asks" section of the status email into natural Chinese.

"Subject: [RRM] Weekly Status — W28 — RAG Amber — 1 ask for Sponsor sign-off by Mon EOD

Asks:
- Margaret — sign-off on the $200K FX contingency drawdown by Mon 15 Jul EOD. One-pager attached. Without sign-off, we cannot close the M3 cost forecast and may delay M4 UAT entry by 2 days.
- Steve — confirm Cyber panel engagement by Fri 19 Jul.
- Mei — confirm Group Compliance sign-off on the regulator engagement plan by Wed 17 Jul."

"标题：[RRM] 周报——W28——RAG 琥珀——1 个 Sponsor 签字 ask 周一 EOD 前

Asks:
- Margaret——周一 7 月 15 日 EOD 前签批 20 万美元 FX contingency 动用。附一页摘要。不签批则无法关闭 M3 成本预测，可能推迟 M4 UAT 进入 2 天。
- Steve——7 月 19 日周五前确认 Cyber panel 启动。
- Mei——7 月 17 日周三前确认集团合规签批监管沟通计划。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the email mistake

Which status email subject line is most professional? Why?

  1. "Weekly update"
  2. "[RRM] W28 — RAG Amber — 1 ask for Sponsor sign-off by Mon EOD"
  3. "Status — please read"
  4. "Update on the regulatory reporting thing"

② is the most professional. It has the project tag, week, RAG, and the most important ask in 12 words. ① has no info. ③ is begging to be read. ④ is vague and informal. Subject line is the PM's first impression — make it count.
