RAID Log Sync — Speaking Risk, Assumption, Issue, Dependency

Article 03 · Module 1: Daily PM Communication · PM English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 R-A-I-D vocabulary, scoring, owner-by-due-date discipline, escalation language

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday 14:00 HKT. You are running a 30-minute RAID review for the Group-wide Trade Surveillance programme. The goal is to walk every open RAID item, confirm owners, refresh scores, and decide which items to escalate to the Programme Steering Committee. On the call: Linda (PMO, London), Ahmed (Risk Lead, Dubai), Mei (Compliance Lead, Hong Kong), and James (Tech Lead, London).

周二 14:00 香港时间，你在主持集团级交易监控项目群的 30 分钟 RAID review。目标是过每个开放 RAID 项、确认 owner、刷新评分、决定哪些项升级到项目群 SteerCo。参会有：Linda（PMO，伦敦）、Ahmed（风险负责人，迪拜）、Mei（合规负责人，香港）、James（Tech Lead，伦敦）。

Unlike a standup, a RAID review is a working session: you walk the log line by line, confirm owner, refresh probability and impact, and close items that are no longer relevant. You speak in the language of Risk, Assumption, Issue, Dependency — not personal tasks.

和站会不同，RAID review 是一个工作会议：逐行过日志、确认 owner、刷新概率和影响、关闭不相关的项。用风险、假设、问题、依赖语言，不是个人任务。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (PM): OK, RAID review. We have 14 open items: 7 risks, 2 assumptions, 3 issues, 2 dependencies. Let's start with the amber and red ones.好，RAID review。当前 14 个开放项：7 个风险，2 个假设，3 个问题，2 个依赖。先看琥珀和红色的。
You (PM): R-007 — Group Compliance review of the MAS TRM control mapping. Owner: Mei. Last week it was probability medium, impact high. Mei, any update?R-007——MAS TRM 控制映射的集团合规评审。Owner: Mei。上周是概率中、影响高。Mei，有更新吗？
Mei: Score unchanged. I'm in a workshop with Group Compliance on Thursday. I'd like to keep the score and review again next week.评分不变。周四和集团合规开工作坊。建议保留评分，下周再 review 一次。
You (PM): Fine. Action: Mei to update by EOD Friday. If it's not closed by next Tuesday, we escalate to SteerCo as a red risk. Next — R-012.好。Action: Mei 周五 EOD 前更新。下周二前没关闭，升级到 SteerCo 作为红色风险。下一个——R-012。
You (PM): R-012 — cutover weekend slot. The vendor can't confirm a December slot. Owner: me, due this Friday. I'm running a workshop with the vendor on Monday — should resolve it. Recommend we keep the score.R-012——切换周末时段。厂商无法确认 12 月时段。Owner: 我，due 本周五。周一和厂商开工作坊——应该能解决。建议保持评分。
Linda (PMO): Flagging for SteerCo? It's a regulator-tied date.要在 SteerCo 提一下吗？这个日期和监管相关。
You (PM): Yes — I'll add it to the SteerCo decisions page as 'for awareness'. Not a decision ask yet. Next — I-003.对——我会加到 SteerCo 决策页作为'知情'。还不是决策 ask。下一个——I-003。
You (PM): I-003 — test environment was down for 4 days. We've closed it with a workaround. Recommendation: close and log as a lesson learned. Ahmed, agree?I-003——测试环境停机 4 天。我们用临时方案已关闭。建议关闭并记入经验教训。Ahmed，同意吗？
Ahmed: Agreed. Let's close it and tag the root cause in the lessons learned log.同意。关闭并在经验教训中标记根因。
You (PM): Done. Two actions out of this meeting: Mei by Friday, me on R-012 by Friday. Anything else before we close?好。本次会议两个 action：Mei 周五前，我 R-012 周五前。还有别的吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open We have 14 open items: 7 risks, 2 assumptions, 3 issues, 2 dependencies. 当前 14 个开放项：7 个风险，2 个假设，3 个问题，2 个依赖 Open the RAID review with a count by category. Sets the agenda in 5 seconds.
  • Score Last week it was probability medium, impact high. 上周是概率中、影响高 Always anchor back to the last score. RAID reviews are about change, not just status.
  • Owner Owner: Mei, due this Friday. Owner: Mei，本周五到期 Every RAID item has one owner, one due date. "We're working on it" is not enough.
  • Escalate We escalate to SteerCo as a red risk. 升级到 SteerCo 作为红色风险 Escalation has a destination (SteerCo / CISO / Sponsor) and a state (red risk / blocker / decision ask).
  • For awareness Add it to the SteerCo decisions page as 'for awareness'. 加到 SteerCo 决策页作为"知情" Distinguishes "for decision" (needs a yes/no) from "for awareness" (FYI only). PMs use this constantly.
  • Close Close and log as a lesson learned. 关闭并记入经验教训 Closed issues don't disappear — they become lessons learned. That's the loop.
  • Root cause Tag the root cause in the lessons learned log. 在经验教训中标记根因 Root cause without a fix is just history. The lesson learned is the fix.
  • Close-out Two actions out of this meeting: Mei by Friday, me on R-012 by Friday. 本次会议两个 action：Mei 周五前，我 R-012 周五前 Every RAID review closes with action count. No exceptions.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • RAID n. 风险/假设/问题/依赖 Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies — the PM's core log.
  • Risk n. 风险 A potential future event that could impact objectives. Has probability × impact.
  • Assumption n. 假设 Something taken to be true today. If proven false, becomes a risk or issue.
  • Issue n. 问题 A current event that is impacting the project. Needs action now.
  • Dependency n. 依赖 Something the project needs from another team or party to deliver.
  • Score (P×I) n. 评分 概率×影响 Probability multiplied by impact — usually Low/Medium/High on each axis.
  • Mitigation n. 缓解措施 Action taken to reduce probability or impact of a risk.
  • Workaround n. 临时方案 A short-term fix used while a permanent solution is being built.
  • Lessons learned n. 经验教训 Documented findings from a closed issue, kept for future projects.
  • For awareness n. 知情项 An item shared for information only, not requiring a decision.
  • For decision n. 决策项 An item requiring a yes/no from a named authority at SteerCo.
  • Programme n. 项目群 A group of related projects managed together for coordinated benefits.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Owner + due" — the two-attribute rule

Every RAID item, when spoken aloud, must include one owner and one due date. "We're working on it" has neither. "Owner: Mei, due Friday" has both. Train yourself to attach these attributes as a reflex.

每个 RAID 项口头表述时必须包含一个 owner和一个 due date。"我们正在处理"两者都没有。"Owner: Mei，due Friday"两者都有。

② "Probability × Impact" — the universal scoring format

Always state both attributes, in that order, every time. Probability medium, impact high. Some banks use P×I to compute a numeric score (e.g., 3×3=9). The spoken format is the same regardless.

始终按顺序说出两个属性。Probability medium, impact high. 一些银行用 P×I 计算数值分数。

③ "For awareness" vs "For decision" — the SteerCo distinction

Every SteerCo item is either for decision (needs a yes/no from a named authority) or for awareness (FYI). Mixing them confuses the meeting. PMs tag items in advance so SteerCo runs cleanly.

每个 SteerCo 项要么是for decision（需要具名权威给 yes/no），要么是for awareness（仅供参考）。提前标记好让 SteerCo 顺利进行。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • RAID is the PM's core artefact. If your RAID log is messy, your project looks messy. Keep it current, owned, and reviewed weekly.
  • One owner per item. Shared ownership is no ownership. Assign a single name; the owner can delegate work, but accountability stays.
  • Score every risk, every review. Don't skip scoring because "nothing changed". Confirming no-change is itself valuable signal.
  • Closed ≠ forgotten. A closed issue that doesn't become a lesson learned is a wasted project. The lesson log is the project's institutional memory.
  • Escalation is not failure. Escalating on time is good PM. Escalating late, or never, is bad PM. Pick the right destination: SteerCo, CISO, Sponsor, Group PMO.
  • "For awareness" saves SteerCo time. Not every item needs a decision. Use "for awareness" to keep the SteerCo agenda clean.
  • Regulator-tied items are red until proven green. Anything touching HKMA, MAS, FCA, GDPR dates defaults to red. Demote only with evidence.
  • The RAID review is a working session, not a status meeting. You are deciding things, not just reporting. Decisions should be visible at the end.

Translate the following RAID review into natural Chinese.

Flagging for SteerCo as 'for awareness' because the date is regulator-tied. I-007 — sandbox environment was unavailable for 3 days. We've closed it with a workaround. Exercise 2 — Spot the PM mistake

Which RAID review line is most professional? Why?

  1. "We're looking at R-007, hopefully we'll have an answer soon."
  2. "R-007, owner Mei, due Friday, score unchanged, recommend keep."
  3. "R-007 is concerning. We need to think about it more."
  4. "I'm worried about R-007, it's been red for a while."

② is the cleanest — single owner, due date, score, recommendation. ① has no owner or date. ③ is emotional, not actionable. ④ is a feeling, not a fact. RAID reviews are about facts and decisions, not feelings.

Write a 10-minute RAID review transcript. Include: opening count, two risk walks, one issue close, one dependency update, close-out with actions.
