PM Daily Standup Update

Article 01 · Module 1: Daily PM Communication · PM English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 RAG status, milestones, blockers, asks — the PM standup rhythm

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 9:30 AM HKT. The Regulatory Reporting Modernisation project runs a 15-minute daily project standup on Microsoft Teams. You are the Project Manager. On the call: Daniel, the Tech Lead (London), Priya, the BA (Bangalore), Mark, the Workstream Lead for Data Quality (Hong Kong), and Sarah, the Project Sponsor's chief of staff (London).

上午 9:30 香港时间，监管报送现代化项目在 Teams 上开 15 分钟的项目站会。你是 项目经理。参会有：Tech Lead Daniel（伦敦）、BA Priya（班加罗尔）、数据质量工作流负责人 Mark（香港），以及 Sponsor 办公室主任 Sarah（伦敦）。

Unlike a developer's standup, your update focuses on overall RAG status, milestone movement, RAID items that need attention, and any asks from leadership. You speak in project-level language, not personal task status.

和开发人员不同，你的更新聚焦整体 RAG 状态、里程碑变化、需要关注的 RAID 项、以及来自领导层的 asks。用项目级别语言，不是个人任务状态。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Sarah (Sponsor Office): Morning all. PM, RAG and headline, please.大家早上好。PM，RAG 和一句话总结。
You (PM): Morning. Project RAG is amber. Headline: on track for the HKMA Q3 milestone, but we have a regulator clarification pending that needs a decision from the Sponsor by Friday.早上好。项目 RAG 琥珀色。一句话：按计划交付 HKMA Q3 里程碑，但有一个监管澄清需要Sponsor 周五前给决定。
You (PM): Milestone view: M2 design sign-off closed on Monday. M3 build is 62% complete — Daniel's team is 2 days ahead of plan. M4 UAT is on track to start 18th, no change since last week.里程碑视角：M2 设计签批周一关闭。M3 构建完成 62%——Daniel 团队比计划提前 2 天。M4 UAT计划 18 日启动，无变化。
You (PM): RAID: one new risk logged — the MAS TRM control mapping is pending Group Compliance review. Probability low, impact high. Mitigation: I'm walking it through with Group Compliance tomorrow. No new issues, no escalations.RAID：新登记一个风险——MAS TRM 控制映射等集团合规评审。概率低，影响高。缓解措施：明天和集团合规过一遍。无新问题，无升级。
You (PM): One ask from the project: we need Sponsor sign-off on the regulator clarification response by Friday EOD, otherwise we cannot hit the HKMA submission window. I've sent the deck separately — flagging here so it's not a surprise.项目的一个 ask：需要Sponsor 周五 EOD 前对监管澄清回复进行签批，否则赶不上 HKMA 提交窗口。幻灯片已单独发——这里提前打个招呼。
Sarah: Got it. I'll chase the Sponsor this morning. Anything else before we move on?收到，我上午追一下 Sponsor。还有其他事吗？
You (PM): No from me. Daniel, M3 status?我这边没有。Daniel，M3 状态？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Project RAG is amber. 项目 RAG 是琥珀色 Open with the colour. No throat-clearing. Senior stakeholders read top-down.
  • Headline Headline: on track for the HKMA Q3 milestone, but… 一句话：HKMA Q3 里程碑按计划，但是…… One sentence, two halves: the good and the watch-out. Never just the good.
  • Milestone M3 build is 62% complete, 2 days ahead of plan. M3 构建完成 62%，比计划提前 2 天 Always quantify movement: % complete, days ahead/behind, on plan.
  • RAID One new risk logged, no new issues, no escalations. 新登记一个风险，无新问题，无升级 The PM's three-bullet rhythm: risks, issues, escalations. Memorise it.
  • Risk Probability low, impact high. 概率低，影响高 Standard risk vocabulary. Always pair probability with impact.
  • Ask One ask from the project: Sponsor sign-off by Friday EOD. 项目的一个 ask：Sponsor 周五 EOD 前签批 "Ask" is PM vocabulary. Always state who, what, by when.
  • No surprise Flagging here so it's not a surprise. 这里先打个招呼，避免意外 Use this to signal "I've told you now, you can't say I didn't warn you."
  • Hand-off Daniel, M3 status? Daniel，M3 状态？ Run the standup. Don't talk for 14 minutes then ask others.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • RAG status n. RAG 状态 Red / Amber / Green — the universal project health indicator.
  • Milestone n. 里程碑 A significant checkpoint in the project plan, often tied to a deliverable or gate.
  • RAID n. 风险/假设/问题/依赖 Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies — the PM's core log.
  • Sponsor n. 项目发起人 The senior executive who funds the project and owns the business case.
  • SteerCo n. Steering Committee 高层指导委员会 The governance body that approves scope, budget, and major changes.
  • Workstream n. 工作流 A sub-track within a project (e.g., Data Quality, Reporting, Integration).
  • Regulator milestone n. 监管里程碑 A binding date set by HKMA, MAS, FCA, or another regulator.
  • Escalation n. 升级 Raising an issue to a higher authority for decision or unblocking.
  • Probability × Impact n. 概率×影响 Standard risk scoring: how likely × how bad.
  • Mitigation n. 缓解措施 An action taken to reduce probability or impact of a risk.
  • Ask n. 请求 A specific decision or resource the project needs from leadership.
  • Headline n. 一句话总结 A single-sentence summary a senior stakeholder can read in 5 seconds.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Project RAG is amber" — state the colour, then explain

PMs never open with "everything is going well" or "we have some concerns". They open with the colour (green / amber / red) and then justify it. This trains stakeholders to trust your signal. The colour comes first; the explanation comes second.

PM 不要用"一切都好"或"我们有些担心"开头。要先说颜色，再解释。这让 stakeholder 信任你的信号。

② "On track / ahead of plan / behind plan / slipped" — five-state milestone vocabulary

Never use "almost done" or "going slowly". Use one of the five project states: on track / ahead of plan / behind plan / slipped / blocked. Pair with a number: "2 days ahead", "3 days behind", "M3 slipped by 1 week".

不要用"差不多好了"或"有点慢"。用五个项目状态之一：on track / ahead / behind / slipped / blocked，并配上数字。

③ "One ask from the project" — every standup ends with an ask (or "no asks")

The PM's standup always closes with either a specific ask (who, what, by when) or the explicit statement "no asks this morning". Saying nothing is risky — leadership can't help if they don't know you need them.

PM 站会永远以具体 ask或"无 ask"收尾。沉默很危险——领导帮不了你如果他们不知道你需要。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Lead with RAG, always. In foreign banks, the RAG colour is sacred. Don't bury it. The first word out of your mouth should be "green / amber / red" or the project status.
  • Headline goes before detail. MDs read the first sentence and the colours. Everything else is "evidence". Respect their time.
  • Quantify everything. "62% complete" is better than "going well". "2 days ahead" is better than "a bit fast". Numbers are PM vocabulary.
  • RAID is your friend, not your burden. A clean "no new issues, no escalations" is a sign of control. Don't invent drama. Calm = professional.
  • Always end with an ask — or "no asks". The standup's job is to surface needs. If you have none, say so. Silence is ambiguous.
  • Run the meeting. Don't monologue. After your update, hand off: "Daniel, M3 status?" Use names, not "anyone else?"
  • Flag surprises early, not late. "Flagging here so it's not a surprise" is PM gold. Surprises are punished; early warnings are rewarded.
  • Regulator milestones are sacred. Anything that puts HKMA / MAS / FCA dates at risk gets escalated the same day. Not tomorrow.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the PM standup

Translate the following PM standup into natural Chinese. Pay attention to the RAG-first rhythm and the ask at the end.

"Morning. Project RAG is amber. Headline: on track for MAS TRM milestone on 28th, but the third-party risk assessment is blocking the architecture sign-off. Milestone view: M2 closed Friday, M3 build is 70% complete and on plan, M4 UAT starts 22nd. RAID: one new issue — the offshore QA team is short by 1 FTE and we're 1.5 weeks behind on test case prep. Mitigation: I've approved a contractor to backfill from Monday. One ask: the Sponsor needs to sign the architecture exception this week so we don't miss MAS TRM. Flagging early so it's not a surprise."

"早上好，项目 RAG 琥珀色。一句话：28 号 MAS TRM 里程碑按计划，但第三方风险评估阻塞了架构签批。里程碑：M2 周五关闭，M3 构建完成 70%，按计划；M4 UAT 22 日启动。RAID：新登记一个问题——离岸 QA 团队缺 1 个 FTE，测试用例准备落后 1.5 周。缓解：已批准周一外包补充。一个 ask：Sponsor 本周需签批架构例外，避免错过 MAS TRM。提前告知，避免意外。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the PM mistake

Which of these standup lines is most "PM-like" and which is most "dev-like"? Why?

  1. "Yesterday I finished drafting the status report and sent it to the Sponsor."
  2. "Project RAG is amber. Headline: on track for HKMA milestone, but the Group Cyber review is pending."
  3. "I'm blocked on getting the firewall rule approved by the network team."
  4. "M3 build is 70% complete, 1 day behind plan. Mitigation: Priya is pairing with Daniel to recover by Friday."

② and ④ are PM-like (RAG-first, milestone-level, mitigation, no personal status). ① is admin-style, ③ is dev-style. A common new-PM mistake is dropping into personal task reports. Reframe everything at the project and risk layer.

It's 9:30 AM HKT. You're the PM for a 12-month cross-border payments platform. You have: (a) RAG is amber, (b) M3 is on track, M4 is 3 days behind, (c) one new risk — InfoSec source-code review is pending, (d) one ask — Sponsor sign-off on a $200K vendor change. Write your 60-second PM standup in English. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Morning. Project RAG is amber. Headline: M3 on track, but M4 has slipped 3 days due to a sandbox provisioning delay. Milestone view: M3 build is 65% complete, M4 SIT is 3 days behind, M5 UAT starts 25th with no change. RAID: one new risk — InfoSec source-code review is pending, probability medium, impact high. Mitigation: I'm chasing Group Cyber today; if no answer by Wednesday, I'll escalate to the CISO office. One ask: Sponsor needs to sign off on a $200K vendor change by Friday EOD so we can lock the December go-live. Flagging here so it's not a surprise. Daniel, M3 detail?"
