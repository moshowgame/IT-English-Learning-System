Welcome, Business Sponsor / 业务发起方你好

As a Business Sponsor, Business Lead, or Stakeholder in a foreign bank, you sit on the other side of the IT conversation. You own the business case, the budget, the prioritisation, the UAT sign-off, and the customer impact. You sit in SteerCo, ask IT for status, push back on timelines, accept scope cuts, and translate regulator and customer needs into IT language. This section focuses on the spoken English scenarios you actually face — from a 60-second standup with your IT PM to telling the IT Director the timeline is not acceptable.

面向外企银行 IT 业务发起方、业务负责人、业务方，本节聚焦你日常真正遇到的口语场景——从 60 秒与 IT PM 站会到告诉 IT 总监时间线不可接受。每篇文章包含真实场景、英文对话、中文解释、关键句型与练习。

How to Use This Section / 学习建议

💡 Daily Practice Tip

Business sponsors live in SteerCos, status asks, sign-offs, and difficult conversations with IT. Pick one article per day and try to paraphrase the dialogue from memory — that is the muscle you use when the IT PM calls with a delay and you have 5 minutes to respond to the Group CEO.

⚠️ For Real Foreign-Bank Work

Business sponsor vocabulary (MoSCoW, MVP, UAT, NPV, KPI, RAG, regulator milestone, customer impact) carries cross-function weight. Always validate with Group Finance, Group Risk, and Group Compliance before any commitment that touches capex, headcount, or regulator milestones. Group Standards override local practice. Regulator milestones (HKMA, MAS, FCA) are sacred — never trade them for revenue or schedule.
