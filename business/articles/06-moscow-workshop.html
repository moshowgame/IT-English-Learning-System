MoSCoW Prioritisation Workshop — Must, Should, Could, Won't

Article 06 · Module 2: Requirements & Prioritisation · Business English

⏱ 7 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 MoSCoW, regulator vs revenue, trade, named decisions

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday 09:00 HKT. You are the Head of Customer Onboarding. You are chairing a 90-minute MoSCoW prioritisation workshop for the Digital Onboarding 2.0 programme. Present: Karen (your MD, remote), Sara (IT PM), Priya (BA), Daniel (Tech Lead), and Steve (Group Cyber). 14 functional requirements from the BRD must be sorted into Must / Should / Could / Won't for Phase 1. Capacity is 7.5 FTE for 9 months. Two items are regulator-tied. Two are revenue-tied. You have to make the trade-offs.

周四 09:00 HKT，你是客户开户业务负责人。你在主持数字开户 2.0项目群 90 分钟MoSCoW 优先级工作坊。参会有：Karen（你的 MD，远程）、Sara（IT PM）、Priya（BA）、Daniel（Tech Lead）、Steve（集团 Cyber）。BRD 的 14 个功能需求必须按 Must / Should / Could / Won't 分类用于第 1 阶段。容量是 7.5 FTE 9 个月。2 项和监管绑定，2 项和收入绑定。你必须做权衡。

MoSCoW is the prioritisation language of every bank IT programme. Must = regulator or contract, no Phase 1 without it. Should = high value, fit if capacity allows. Could = nice to have, Phase 2 candidate. Won't = explicit defer. The workshop's job is to fight over the M/S boundary — that is where the trade lives.

MoSCoW 是每个银行 IT 项目群的优先级语言。Must=监管或合同，第 1 阶段不可少。Should=高价值，容量允许时做。Could=锦上添花，第 2 阶段候选。Won't=明确推迟。工作坊的工作就是争 M/S 边界——权衡在那里。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Regulator-tied = Must, no debate Regulator-tied = Must, no debate. 和监管绑定=Must，不讨论 State the rule at the top of the workshop. Anything regulator-tied goes straight to Must; the room does not relitigate the regulator. This saves 30 minutes.
  • Revenue-tied = Should if capacity Revenue-tied = Should if capacity allows. 和收入绑定=容量允许时 Should Revenue is a Should, not a Must, unless it's contractually committed. The workshop fights over the M/S boundary for revenue items.
  • If I have to choose, X wins If I have to choose, FR-04 audit log retention wins over FR-13 voice biometrics. 如果必须二选一，FR-04 审计日志保留赢 The trade-off sentence. Always name both options and the winner. "We have to choose" loses; "X wins because of regulator" wins.
  • Final MoSCoW: M / S / C / W with counts Final MoSCoW: Must 6, Should 4, Could 2, Won't 2. 最终 MoSCoW：Must 6、Should 4、Could 2、Won't 2 Always close with the final counts. The MD can verify "we have 6 Must, that's the regulator baseline" in 5 seconds.
  • Phase 1 commits on M + best N of S Phase 1 commits on Must (6) + best 2 of Should (4). 第 1 阶段承诺 Must（6）+ Should（4）里最好的 2 个 The commit sentence. Phase 1 = all Must + best N of Should. The PM can lock the FTE plan against this list.
  • Capacity check, FTE and months Capacity check on 8 in Phase 1? 7.5 FTE for 9 months, tight but possible. 第 1 阶段 8 个的容量检查？7.5 FTE 9 个月，紧但可行 Capacity is always in FTE and months. The PM confirms or flags a gap. "Tight but possible" is a yellow light, not a green light; the MD should know.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • MoSCoW n. MoSCoW Must / Should / Could / Won't — the standard prioritisation frame.
  • Regulator-tied n. 监管绑定 A requirement driven by HKMA, MAS, FCA, GDPR, or another regulator. Must-have.
  • Revenue-tied n. 收入绑定 A requirement that drives P&L, contractually committed or forecast.
  • Phase 1 / Phase 2 n. 第 1 阶段 / 第 2 阶段 The first release and the deferred release of the same programme.
  • Capacity check n. 容量检查 The PM's confirmation that the prioritised list fits in the available FTE and timeline.
  • Tight but possible phr. 紧但可行 A yellow light: the work fits, with no slack for surprises. The MD should know.
  • Trade-off n. 权衡 Choosing one item over another because of capacity, regulator, or revenue.
  • Manual workaround n. 人工方案 A non-system way to deliver a customer outcome while the system is built later.
  • BRD v3.4 n. BRD v3.4 The next version of the BRD, updated with the MoSCoW output.
  • Lock the FTE plan phr. 锁定 FTE 计划 Confirming the named resources and start dates against the prioritised list.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Regulator-tied = Must, no debate" — the rule-setting sentence

State the MoSCoW rules at the top of the workshop. Regulator-tied = Must, no debate. The grammar is X-tied = Y, [qualifier]. One sentence per rule. The room can refer back to the rule when an item is contested.

在工作坊开头定 MoSCoW 规则。监管绑定=Must，不讨论。

② "Final MoSCoW: M N, S N, C N, W N" — the locked output

The locked output uses parallel structure: Must N, Should N, Could N, Won't N. Four counts, in that order. The grammar is Category Number, repeated four times. The MD can verify the counts in 5 seconds.

锁定输出使用平行结构：Must N、Should N、Could N、Won't N。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • State the rules at the top of the workshop. "Regulator-tied = Must, no debate" saves 30 minutes. Without the rule, the room relitigates the regulator.
  • Fight at the M/S boundary, not the S/C boundary. The trade-off is in the Must-Should cut. Could and Won't are usually obvious.
  • Revenue is a Should, not a Must, unless contractually committed. FR-02 is contractually committed → Must. FR-07 is forecast $8M → Should.
  • Always name the trade-off winner. "FR-04 wins over FR-13 because of HKMA" is the right sentence. "We'll figure it out" is not.
  • Capacity check at the end, in FTE and months. "7.5 FTE for 9 months, tight but possible" is a yellow light. The MD should know.
  • Lock the MoSCoW with version and sign-off date. BRD v3.4 with MoSCoW by tomorrow EOD. MD sign-off by Monday. The PM holds both dates.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Sort 6 requirements into MoSCoW

You have 6 requirements: (1) regulator-tied, (2) revenue-tied, contractually committed, (3) revenue-tied, forecast, (4) regulator-tied, (5) nice-to-have, (6) explicit defer. Sort them into M/S/C/W. Justify the M/S boundary for items 2 and 3.

Exercise 2 — Name the trade-off winner

You must cut one of two items: A (regulator-tied) or B (revenue-tied, $5M forecast). The room is split. Write the sentence that closes the debate, naming the winner and the reason.

Exercise 3 — Spot the missing capacity check

A PM says: "We've prioritised 10 items, looks good." What is the missing capacity check? Rewrite the PM's update so it includes FTE, months, and a tight/possible flag.
