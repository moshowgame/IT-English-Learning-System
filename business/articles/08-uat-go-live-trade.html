UAT Sign-off vs Go-Live Trade — Defects, Regulator Date, Go/No-Go

Article 08 · Module 2: Requirements & Prioritisation · Business English

⏱ 7 min read 🟠 Advanced 🎯 Severity, regulator date, go/no-go, named decision, slip cost

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday 16:00 HKT. UAT for Digital Onboarding 2.0 closes in 24 hours. The system is functional but has 3 Sev-2 defects that cannot be fixed before the regulator date. The HKMA C-RAF submission deadline is Monday 25 August, 09:00. The bank's reputation with the supervisor depends on hitting the date. The defects: (1) eKYC timeout for non-HK ID affecting 8% of applications, (2) audit log timestamp drift of 2 seconds on high-volume days, (3) FIDO2 fallback path broken in Safari. You have 60 minutes to make the go/no-go call with your MD, Karen, and Group Cyber, Steve.

周五 16:00 HKT，数字开户 2.0的 UAT 还有 24 小时关闭。系统功能正常但有3 个 Sev-2 缺陷无法在监管日期前修复。HKMA C-RAF 提交截止8 月 25 日周一 09:00。银行与监管的声誉取决于能否踩准日期。缺陷：(1) 非香港身份证 eKYC 超时影响 8% 申请，(2) 高流量日审计日志时间戳漂移 2 秒，(3) FIDO2 回退路径在 Safari 中中断。你有 60 分钟和 MD Karen、集团 Cyber Steve 一起做 go/no-go 决定。

The UAT vs go-live trade is the hardest business-IT call: ship with known defects, or slip and lose regulator credibility. The pattern: name the defects, name the customer/regulator impact, name the go/no-go criteria, name the slip cost.

UAT vs 上线权衡是最难的业务-IT 决定：带着已知缺陷上线，还是推迟损失监管信用。模式：列出缺陷、列出客户/监管影响、列出 go/no-go 标准、列出推迟代价。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Customer impact, regulator impact, per defect Customer impact: 8% of applications, manual onboarding, 4-day SLA. Regulator impact: HKMA accepts the fallback as long as it's documented in BRD v3.4. 客户影响：8% 申请、人工开户、4 天 SLA。监管影响：HKMA 接受只要 BRD v3.4 记录 Each defect has two impacts: customer and regulator. State both. "It's a bug" loses. "8% of customers, manual fallback, HKMA accepts if documented" wins.
  • Slip cost, named items Slip cost: HKMA supervisory letter, $4M revenue slip, reputational hit. 推迟代价：HKMA 监管函、400 万收入推迟、声誉影响 The slip cost is always a list: regulator consequence, $ consequence, reputational consequence. The MD buys the list. "It would be bad" loses.
  • Control failure vs UX issue FIDO2 fallback is a UX issue, not a control issue. FIDO2 回退是 UX 问题，不是控制问题 The most important distinction in cyber. A control failure blocks go-live. A UX issue is a backlog item. The Group Cyber lead must name the category.
  • Go / no-go, with named conditions Go for Monday 25 August 09:00 HKMA submission. Conditions: Defect 1 in BRD v3.4 today, Defect 2 fix in 2 weeks, Defect 3 backlog 4 weeks. 周一 8 月 25 日 09:00 HKMA 提交，go。条件：缺陷 1 今日入 BRD v3.4，缺陷 2 两周内修，缺陷 3 backlog 四周 The go/no-go call is always 1 sentence: "Go" or "No-go", with the date. Then 2-4 conditions with named owners and dates.
  • Customer comms, named channel Safari users on Mac get a banner: "For best experience, use Chrome or Edge." Safari on Mac 用户显示横幅："为获得最佳体验，请使用 Chrome 或 Edge" Customer comms is part of the go decision. Always name the channel (banner, email, in-app), the message, the live date, and the owner.
  • Co-sign the closure report Send me the closure report by Sunday 17:00. I'll co-sign with you. 周日 17:00 前把关闭报告发我，我和你联签 For material go/no-go, the MD co-signs the closure report. The MD's signature is the regulator's evidence that the business owns the call.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Go / no-go n. Go / 停止 The call on whether to ship on the planned date.
  • Customer impact n. 客户影响 The effect of a defect on customer experience, segment, manual workaround.
  • Regulator impact n. 监管影响 The effect of a defect on the regulator's view (acceptable, supervisory letter, breach).
  • Slip cost n. 推迟代价 The consequence of missing the date: regulator letter, $ loss, reputation.
  • Control failure n. 控制失效 A defect that breaks a regulator or audit control. Blocks go-live.
  • UX issue n. UX 问题 A defect that affects user experience but not a control. Backlog item.
  • Manual fallback n. 人工回退 A non-system way to handle a customer when the system fails.
  • Customer comms n. 客户沟通 The message, channel, and date for telling customers about a known issue.
  • Closure report n. 关闭报告 The document signed by sponsor and MD confirming the go/no-go call.
  • C-RAF attestation n. C-RAF 合规声明 The formal regulator submission confirming cyber resilience.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Customer impact: X. Regulator impact: Y." — the parallel pair

Each defect has two impacts, stated in parallel: Customer impact: X. Regulator impact: Y. The grammar is Category: detail. Two sentences per defect. The MD reads the customer/regulator split in 5 seconds.

每个缺陷有两个影响，平行陈述：客户影响：X。监管影响：Y。。

② "Go for [date]" — the go/no-go sentence

The call is one sentence: Go for [date] [milestone]. The grammar is Go / No-go for [named date] [named event]. One sentence, with the date and the milestone. The MD co-signs the closure report against this sentence.

决定是一句话：【日期】【里程碑】go。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Always state customer and regulator impact, per defect. Two impacts, two sentences. "It's a bug" loses.
  • Name the slip cost in 3 categories: regulator, $, reputation. "It would be bad" loses. "HKMA supervisory letter, $4M slip, reputational hit" wins.
  • Distinguish control failure from UX issue. Control failure = blocks go-live. UX issue = backlog. The Group Cyber lead names the category.
  • The go/no-go is one sentence, with conditions. "Go for Monday 09:00 HKMA submission" is the call. Conditions are 2-4 numbered items with owners and dates.
  • Customer comms is part of the go decision. Name the channel, message, live date, owner. Safari users get a banner; the message is in the banner.
  • MD co-signs the closure report for material calls. Sponsor signs first, MD co-signs. The MD's signature is regulator evidence.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Customer and regulator impact

A defect breaks the audit log export for transactions over $1M. Write the customer impact and the regulator impact in two parallel sentences.

Exercise 2 — Slip cost

You must decide between go on Friday with 2 Sev-2 defects, or slip to next Friday. Write the slip cost in 3 categories (regulator, $, reputation) for a MAS TRM submission.

Exercise 3 — Control failure vs UX issue

For each defect, say whether it is a control failure or a UX issue, and what the consequence is for the go/no-go call:

  1. Safari users cannot see the confirmation page.
  2. Audit log timestamp drifts by 10 seconds under load.
  3. Typo on the welcome screen.
