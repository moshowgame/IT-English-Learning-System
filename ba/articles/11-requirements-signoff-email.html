Requirements Sign-off Email

Article 11 · Module 3: Written Communication · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Written sign-off, formal approval, and documenting decisions

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday afternoon, 4:45 PM HKT. You are a BA on the RBWM Digital Onboarding squad. The BRD for Mobile Onboarding with eKYC and Facial Biometrics (v1.0) was walked through yesterday. The HK business gatekeeper, the Group DPO, and the GPB Compliance lead have all verbally agreed. You need to lock the approval in writing before the squad can move into sprint planning on Monday morning.

周五下午 4:45，香港时间。你是 RBWM 数字化开户 squad 的 BA。Mobile Onboarding with eKYC and Facial Biometrics (v1.0) 的 BRD 昨天走查完成。香港业务 Gatekeeper、 集团 DPO、GPB 合规负责人都已口头同意。你需要在 squad 周一进 Sprint Planning 之前， 用书面形式锁定签字。

You are drafting the requirements sign-off email. The audience is mixed: a Director in HK, a Compliance VP in London, a Tech Lead in Bangalore, and a PO in Shanghai. The email must set out the decision, the conditions, the open items, the next step, and the deadline — and be self-contained so a reader can approve it without opening the BRD.

你正在起草需求签字邮件。读者很杂：香港的 Director、伦敦的合规 VP、班加罗尔的 Tech Lead、 上海的 PO。这封邮件必须写清楚决定、附加条件、待办项、下一步和截止时间， 并且要 self-contained——读者不开 BRD 也能直接批。

2. Sample Email 范文 / Email 格式

The dialogue below is the actual email body you would send, in a standard To/From/Subject/Body format. Read it as a model for your own sign-off emails.

下面是一封真实的签字邮件，按 To/From/Subject/Body 标准格式呈现。可作为 你自己写签字邮件的模板。

To: David Lau (Head of CMB Ops, HK); Lina Chen (Group Compliance VP, UK); Margaret Yu (Group Head of FCC, HK); Priya Kapoor (PO, London); Ahmed Patel (Tech Lead, Bangalore); Carmen Liu (Compliance BA, Shanghai)David Lau（CMB 运营负责人，香港）；Lina Chen（集团合规 VP，英国）；Margaret Yu（集团 FCC 负责人，香港）；Priya Kapoor（PO，伦敦）；Ahmed Patel（Tech Lead，班加罗尔）；Carmen Liu（合规 BA，上海）
Cc: Sarah Wong (SM, HK); Ravi Menon (QA, Bangalore); Hong Kong Squad DLSarah Wong（SM，香港）；Ravi Menon（QA，班加罗尔）；香港 Squad 邮件组
From: Wei Zhang (BA, HK)Wei Zhang（BA，香港）

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open I'm formally requesting sign-off on the attached BRD v1.0. 我正式请求对附件 BRD v1.0 进行签字 "Formally requesting" is the precise phrase. Avoid "asking for approval" — it sounds casual.
  • Self-contained A summary of the decision, conditions, and open items is below. 决定、条件、待办项的总结如下 Always put a one-screen summary in the email body. Senior readers won't open the attachment.
  • Approved in principle The BRD is approved in principle, subject to the conditions below. BRD 原则通过，以下列条件为准 "Approved in principle" = conditional sign-off. The standard HSBC sign-off language.
  • Subject to Subject to the conditions and open items below. 以下列条件与待办项为准 "Subject to" is the cleanest way to attach conditions. Use it in writing, not "with the condition that".
  • Owner Per-corridor KPI split to be added (David, owner, by Mon EOD). 按通道 KPI 拆分需加入（David 负责，周一 EOD 前完成） Always write "Name, owner, deadline". The triplet is the audit trail.
  • Escalation Otherwise this is a Group-level escalation. 否则升级到集团层面 Reserve "escalation" for real escalations. Putting it in writing makes it stick.
  • Open item Open items (not blocking sign-off). 待办项（不阻塞本次签字） "Not blocking sign-off" is critical. Distinguishes punch-list items from sign-off blockers.
  • Action required Action required: please reply-all with "Approved". 请采取的行动：回复全部，注明"Approved" Always end with an exact verb + an exact format. "Reply-all with X" beats "let me know".
  • Deadline By Mon 9 AM HKT. 周一上午 9 点 HKT 前 Always include the timezone. HKT / GMT / IST / SGT — different stakeholders, different defaults.
  • Attachments Attachments: BRD v1.0, eCAB case reference, RACI v1.0. 附件：BRD v1.0、eCAB 案件号、RACI v1.0 Always list attachments at the bottom with a one-line description. Saves the reader 3 minutes.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Sign-off n. 签字确认 Formal approval, recorded in writing or in a system like eCAB.
  • Sign-off email n. 签字邮件 The email that requests and captures the written approval.
  • Approved in principle n. 原则通过 Conditional sign-off, subject to named conditions.
  • Conditions of sign-off n. 签字条件 Items that must be satisfied for the sign-off to be valid.
  • Open items n. 待办项 Items that don't block sign-off but need a named owner and due date.
  • eCAB n. 电子合规审批 HSBC's electronic Compliance Approval Booking system.
  • DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) n. 数据保护影响评估 A GDPR / PDPO-required document for PII processing.
  • Action required n. 请采取的行动 The header that says "this email needs your reply".
  • Reply-all n. 回复全部 Email verb — sends to all recipients, including CC list.
  • e-sign v. 电子签字 Sign electronically in eCAB or another system of record.
  • Punch list n. 待办清单 Same as "open items" — list of small follow-ups.
  • Audit trail n. 审计痕迹 The written record showing who approved what, when.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Approved in principle" — the "past participle + adverb" phrase

Approved in principle uses the past participle + prepositional phrase construction. It is a nominal phrase that acts as a sentence subject or complement. In sign-off emails, this pattern replaces the verb "to approve" with a state ("the BRD is approved in principle"), which sounds more formal and more final.

过去分词 + 介词短语（如 approved in principle）把"批准"这个动作变成状态，听起来更正式、更有终局感。

② "Reply-all with 'Approved'" — the verb + prepositional phrase pattern

Please reply-all with "Approved" uses the verb + preposition + exact string pattern to specify an action precisely. The exact string ("Approved") goes in quotes so the reader knows exactly what to type. Avoid paraphrases — "let me know" is unactionable.

动词 + 介词 + 精确措辞 给出明确指令，避免 "let me know" 这类含糊说法。

③ "Otherwise this is a Group-level escalation" — the conditional consequence

Otherwise this is a Group-level escalation uses otherwise + declarative statement to attach a consequence to a deadline. It's a softer version of "if you don't, X will happen" but reads as a fact, not a threat. In compliance writing this is the standard pattern.

Otherwise + 陈述句 用陈述事实的方式表达后果，比直接威胁更专业，是合规邮件的常见写法。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • A sign-off email is the artefact, not the conversation. At HSBC, the verbal "ok" from David in a walkthrough is not a sign-off. The sign-off is the email reply + the eCAB case. As BA, you write the email and own the chase.
  • Always state "not blocking sign-off" for open items. Senior stakeholders will read the email looking for what blocks them. Make that line unmistakable — it speeds up approvals dramatically.
  • Use the "Name, owner, deadline" triplet. "(David, owner, by Mon EOD)" is the format. Vague assignments like "David will look at it" don't survive a six-month audit.
  • Self-contain the email. A Director in HK gets 200 emails a day. They will read the first screen and reply. The summary must be in the body, not in the attached BRD. The attachment is for the auditor.
  • Timezone matters. Saying "by Monday morning" is ambiguous across HK / UK / India. Always write "Mon 9 AM HKT" or "Mon 9 AM GMT". The default timezone for HSBC sign-off is HKT for APAC programmes and GMT for global ones.
  • "Reply-all" is the verb, not the action. Many sign-off emails are lost in inboxes because people reply only to the sender. Tell the reader explicitly to "reply-all with X".
  • The eCAB case is the system of record. Don't rely on the email thread as the audit trail. The email is a request; the eCAB case with the named approver is the actual record. BAs who skip eCAB get burned at audit time.
  • Cross-region etiquette: When the email goes to UK, HK, Shanghai, and Bangalore, the deadline in HKT will be 1 AM in the UK. State it explicitly and offer an out-of-hours call for the UK approver — they will respect the consideration.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the sign-off email subject line

Translate the following sign-off email subject line into natural Chinese.

"SIGN-OFF REQUEST — BRD v1.0: Mobile Onboarding with eKYC & Facial Biometrics · Action required by Mon 9 AM HKT"

"签字请求 —— BRD v1.0：Mobile Onboarding with eKYC & Facial Biometrics · 请在周一上午 9 点 HKT 前回复"

Exercise 2 — Spot the weak sign-off email

Which of these sign-off email lines is the strongest and most HSBC-appropriate? Why?

  1. "Hi all, please review the BRD and let me know if you have any comments. Thanks!"
  2. "I'm formally requesting sign-off on the attached BRD v1.0. The BRD is approved in principle, subject to the conditions and open items below. Action required: please reply-all with 'Approved' or 'Approved with comment' by Mon 9 AM HKT."
  3. "Please find attached the BRD for your kind perusal. Awaiting your esteemed feedback."
  4. "As discussed, here's the BRD. David and Lina have already approved, so please sign."

② is the strongest. It uses the precise phrase formally requesting sign-off, states the decision with approved in principle, subject to, lists the conditions, names the exact action (reply-all with), and gives a specific timezone-aware deadline. ① is too casual — "let me know" gives no audit trail. ③ uses "perusal" and "esteemed feedback" — too formal and evasive. ④ is an appeal to authority, not a formal sign-off request — a senior reader will reject it.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're a BA on the CMB Trade Finance squad. You need to send a sign-off email for the FRD v1.0 of "SWIFT MT798 Migration to ISO 20022". The walkthrough was held 2 days ago. The Tech Lead (Ahmed, Bangalore) has approved. The Trade Finance head (David, HK) is on annual leave until Thursday but has approved via WhatsApp. The Compliance BA (Carmen, Shanghai) has 2 conditions: (a) the MT798 field mapping for documentary credits must be confirmed by the SWIFT Standards team, and (b) the data-residency rule for trade messages must be confirmed by Group DPO. Write the sign-off email body, including To, Subject, Decision, Conditions, Open items, and Action. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

To: David Lau (Head of Trade Finance, HK); Ahmed Patel (Tech Lead, Bangalore); Carmen Liu (Compliance BA, Shanghai); Priya Kapoor (PO, London); Lina Chen (Group DPO, UK); Margaret Yu (Group Head of FCC, HK)

Cc: Sarah Wong (SM, HK); Trade Finance Squad DL; Hong Kong Change Mgmt DL

Decision: The FRD v1.0 is approved in principle, subject to the conditions below. The squad will move into Sprint Planning on Friday based on this sign-off.

Conditions of sign-off: (1) The MT798 field mapping for documentary credits to be confirmed by the SWIFT Standards team — Carmen, owner, by Thu noon HKT. (2) The data-residency rule for trade messages to be confirmed by Lina (Group DPO) — by Thu 5 PM HKT. Otherwise this is a Group-level escalation. (3) David's verbal approval via WhatsApp to be confirmed in eCAB on his return from leave — owner: Wei, by Thu 5 PM HKT.

Action required: Please reply-all with "Approved" or "Approved with comment" by Thu 5 PM HKT. If you cannot approve, please state the specific condition that blocks you. Margaret will e-sign the FRD in eCAB on receipt of all approvals.

Attachments: FRD v1.0 (Confluence link), eCAB case reference, RACI v1.0, MT798 field-mapping appendix. Thanks all — please ping me on Teams if you have any questions. — Wei
