Classic BA Standup

Article 01 · Module 1: Daily Standup & Reporting · BA English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 The BA standup rhythm: Stories in flight / clarifications / risks

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 9:30 AM HKT. The HSBC RBWM Digital team holds its daily standup on Microsoft Teams. You are a Business Analyst working on the Wealth Management Onboarding squad. The team includes Sarah, your Scrum Master (Hong Kong), Akash, a developer (Bangalore), and Mei, a developer (Shanghai).

上午 9:30 香港时间，汇丰 RBWM 数字化团队在 Teams 上开每日站会。你是 财富管理客户开户 squad 的业务分析师。团队成员包括香港的 Scrum Master Sarah、班加罗尔的开发 Akash、上海的开发 Mei。

Your turn comes. As a BA, your standup is different from a developer's — you report on story readiness, pending clarifications from the business, and walkthrough demos coming up. Keep it tight: 60 to 90 seconds.

轮到你发言。BA 的站会和开发不同——你汇报的是Story 准备度、待业务方澄清的项、 以及即将进行的 Walkthrough。控制在一分钟左右。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Sarah (SM): Morning, everyone. Let's go round. Wei, you're up first today.大家早上好，按顺序来。Wei，今天你先来。
You (BA): Morning, all. Quick BA update from me.大家早上好，我简单同步一下 BA 这边的进展。
You (BA): Yesterday, I walked through the AC for the KYC refresh story with Jonathan from RBWM Compliance. Three AC items were unclear — I've raised them as comments on the Jira ticket WM-1982 and pinged the business PO.昨天我和 RBWM 合规的 Jonathan 走查了 KYC 信息更新这个 Story 的验收标准，有 3 条 AC 仍不清晰，我已经在 Jira WM-1982 上加评论并 @ 了业务 PO。
You (BA): I also shaped two new stories from the sanctions screening epic — both are ready for refinement in tomorrow's session.另外我把制裁名单筛查 Epic 拆成了 2 个 Story，明天 Grooming 会就可以讨论了。
You (BA): Today, I'll prep the walkthrough deck for the UAT kick-off on Thursday, and book a 30-min slot with the HK business to close the open AC items.今天我会准备周四 UAT 启动会的 Walkthrough 演示稿，并和香港业务方约 30 分钟来把悬而未决的 AC 项收尾。
You (BA): No blockers on the BA side, but I'm flagging a soft dependency on the business PO's input — if I don't hear back by tomorrow EOD, the WM-1982 AC won't be locked in time for refinement.BA 这边没有阻塞，但我提一个软依赖：业务 PO 的输入如果明天 EOD 前没回，WM-1982 的 AC 就赶不上 Grooming 会敲定。
Akash (Dev): Thanks, Wei. Quick one — once the AC is locked, do we still need a separate non-functional requirements review for the screen-out threshold?谢谢 Wei。问一下——AC 敲定后，screen-out 阈值这块还要单独过一次 NFR 评审吗？
You (BA): Good catch. Yes, let me loop in the Performance Lead this afternoon — that's an NFR item, not a functional AC. I'll book a 30-min review before sprint planning.好问题。是的，这块属于 NFR 不属于功能 AC。我下午拉上性能负责人，过 30 分钟评审，定在 Sprint Planning 之前。
Sarah (SM): Perfect. Mei, you're next.好的，Mei 接着。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Quick BA update from me. 我简单同步一下 BA 进展 Marks the start of a BA-led standup slot — different from dev's "quick update".
  • Walkthrough I walked through the AC with… 我和…走查了 AC "Walk through" = step through together. Standard for AC / BRD reviews.
  • Raise I've raised them as comments on the Jira ticket. 我在 Jira 上加了评论 "Raise" = log formally. Use for any open question — creates an audit trail.
  • Shape I shaped two new stories from the epic. 我把 Epic 拆成了 2 个 Story "Shape" = split + draft. A BA term for going from epic → ready-to-refine stories.
  • Ready Ready for refinement. 准备好进入 Grooming "Refinement" = grooming. Story is clear enough to discuss; not yet "ready for sprint".
  • Prep I'll prep the walkthrough deck. 我会准备 Walkthrough 演示稿 "Prep" = prepare. Use for slides, demo scripts, and meeting materials.
  • Soft Flagging a soft dependency on… 提一个软依赖 "Soft dependency" = not blocking today, but a risk. Lighter than "blocker".
  • Blocker No blockers on the BA side. BA 这边没有阻塞 Even when true, say it — gives the team confidence about BA's status.
  • Loop in Let me loop in the Performance Lead. 我把性能负责人拉进来 "Loop in" = bring someone into the conversation. Very common in BAs / PMs.
  • NFR That's an NFR item, not a functional AC. 这是 NFR，不是功能 AC NFR = non-functional requirement (performance, security, scalability). Separate from AC.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Standup n. 站会 Daily 15-min team sync. BA standup focuses on story status, not code.
  • AC (Acceptance Criteria) n. 验收标准 The bullet list of "done" conditions for a story.
  • Jira ticket n. Jira 单 A story / bug / task in Jira (HSBC uses Jira, not ADO).
  • Story n. 故事卡 A unit of user value, sized in points.
  • Epic n. 史诗 A large body of work that spans multiple sprints.
  • Refinement / Grooming n. Story 澄清会 The meeting to discuss and size upcoming stories.
  • Walkthrough n. 走查 A line-by-line review of a document or flow with stakeholders.
  • Demo n. 演示 Showing a working feature to business / stakeholders.
  • NFR (Non-Functional Requirement) n. 非功能需求 Performance, security, scalability, audit.
  • PO (Product Owner) n. 产品负责人 The business voice on the team. Owns backlog priority.
  • Squad n. 小队 HSBC term for a cross-functional delivery team (~6-9 people).
  • Soft dependency n. 软依赖 Not blocking today, but will become a blocker if not addressed.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "I walked through" vs "I have walked through" — present perfect in standups

In standups, the most natural tense is past simple for what you did yesterday (I walked through the AC) and will / present progressive for today (I'll prep the deck, I'm working on…). Reserve have walked through for the same day, just before the standup.

站会里最自然的时态：昨天用一般过去式，今天用 will 或现在进行时。当天上午刚做完用现在完成时。

② "Flagging" — the gerive as a soft announcement

I'm flagging a soft dependency is softer than I have a blocker. Using the -ing form (flagging, raising, pinging) signals "I want you to know, but I'm not demanding action right now". A key BA politeness tool.

"I'm flagging…" 比 "I have a blocker" 语气更软。-ing 形式表示"告知但不强求立刻处理"。

③ "Loop in" / "Ping" / "Raise" — vocabulary for routing attention

Three near-synonyms with different nuance: loop in (bring into a conversation), ping (a quick IM / chat nudge), raise (log formally in a ticket or doc). Use all three in one standup to show you're working the right channels.

三个近义词：loop in 拉入对话 / ping IM 提醒 / raise 正式记录。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • BA standup ≠ dev standup. As a BA, you don't talk about lines of code or test coverage — you talk about story readiness, AC clarity, and business alignment. Reframe your status through the lens of "what does the team need to know to deliver?"
  • HSBC uses "squad" and "tribe". Don't say "team" — say "squad". A tribe is a collection of squads. "Chapter" = functional community of practice (e.g., BA chapter, dev chapter).
  • Always mention the ticket ID. Saying "WM-1982" in standup is non-negotiable — it gives traceability and lets people look it up live.
  • "Soft dependency" is a politeness device. If you say "blocker", Sarah will drop everything to help. If you say "soft dependency", she knows to nudge the PO but won't burn the runway. Save "blocker" for things that genuinely stop your day.
  • End with an action, not a complaint. Bad: "I still don't have the AC from the PO." Good: "I'll book a 30-min with HK business today to close it out." Always pair a problem with the next step you own.
  • Cross-region etiquette: When you have team members in Bangalore, Shanghai, and HK, keep standup updates to 60-90 seconds. They will be on the call at 7 AM or 11 PM — show respect by being concise.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the standup

Translate the following BA standup snippet into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the BA frames the soft dependency.

"Morning. Quick BA update. Yesterday I finished shaping the four stories under the 'Account Closure' epic — three are ready for refinement. Today I'll run the AC walkthrough with the UK business at 3 PM and loop in Compliance on the data-retention NFR. No blockers, but I'm flagging a soft dependency on the data-masking rule from the Risk team — if that doesn't land by Wednesday, we'll have to descope one story from this sprint."

"早上好，简单同步一下 BA 进展。昨天我把'账户关闭' Epic 拆成了 4 个 Story，其中 3 个已准备好进入 Grooming。今天下午 3 点我会和英国业务方过 AC 走查，并把合规部门拉进来讨论数据保留的 NFR。这边没有阻塞，但我提一个软依赖：风险团队的数据脱敏规则如果周三前没到位，这次 Sprint 就得砍一个 Story。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the BA mistake

Which of these standup lines is most "BA-like" and which is most "Dev-like"? Why?

  1. "Yesterday I finished the unit tests for the MT103 parser. Coverage is at 92%."
  2. "Yesterday I walked through the AC for WM-1982 with Compliance. Three AC items are still open."
  3. "Yesterday I fixed the bug on the HSBCnet callback. It's deployed to UAT."
  4. "Yesterday I shaped two new stories under the sanctions screening epic. Both are ready for refinement."

② and ④ are BA-like (AC walkthrough, story shaping). ① and ③ are Dev-like (unit tests, bug fix, deployment). A common mistake new BAs make is dropping into code-status reports — reframe yours as story-level status.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

It's 9:30 AM HKT. You're a BA on the Cards squad. You have: (a) walked through the rewards-recalculation AC with the business PO at 10 AM today, (b) one story "CRD-3020" that is blocked on a Compliance review, (c) prepared a demo for the Friday walkthrough. Write your 60-second BA standup update in English. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Morning, all. Quick BA update. Today I'm walking through the rewards-recalculation AC with the business PO at 10 AM. One story, CRD-3020, is still blocked on the Compliance review — I've raised it as a hard blocker and pinged the Compliance lead. On the positive side, the Friday walkthrough demo is prepped and ready to go. No other soft dependencies on the BA side — Mei, you're next."
