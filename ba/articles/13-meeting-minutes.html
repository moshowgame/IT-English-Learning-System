Meeting Minutes & Action Items

Article 13 · Module 4: Documentation & User Stories · BA English

⏱ 8 min read 🟢 Beginner-friendly 🎯 Capturing decisions, owners, and due dates in clean English

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Wednesday 4:30 PM HKT. You have just finished a 60-minute cross-regional meeting on Microsoft Teams: "FATCA / CRS Reporting — Phase 2 Walkthrough". Attendees include Priya (Tech Lead, UK), Akash (Dev, Bangalore), Mei (Dev, Shanghai), David (Scrum Master, HK), and Jonathan (PO, HK RBWM Compliance). The meeting covered five agenda items, three decisions, and six action items.

周三下午 4:30 香港时间，你刚在 Teams 上结束了一场 60 分钟的跨地区会议：FATCA / CRS 报告——Phase 2 走查。与会人包括英国的技术负责人 Priya、班加罗尔的开发 Akash、上海的开发 Mei、香港的 Scrum Master David 以及香港 RBWM 合规的 PO Jonathan。议程有 5 项，3 个决策，6 个 Action Item。

You are the BA and meeting scribe. The convention at HSBC is that the BA circulates the minutes within 24 hours — anyone who didn't attend should be able to read them and know exactly what was decided, who owns what, and when it's due. A weak minutes email causes the same conversation three weeks later.

你是这场会的 BA 兼会议记录人。汇丰的惯例是 BA 要在 24 小时内发出会议纪要——没参会的人 看一眼就知道决策是什么、谁负责、什么时候交。一封糟糕的纪要邮件会导致 3 周后重复开同样的会。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (BA): Thanks, everyone. Before we close — let me recap the decisions and action items so the minutes are accurate. One minute of your time.谢谢各位。散会前我过一下决策和 Action Item，确保纪要准确。占用大家 1 分钟。
You (BA): Decision one: the FATCA recalcitrant account flag will be calculated nightly, not on-demand. Owner: Akash. Due: end of next sprint.决策一：FATCA 不配合账户标记改为每晚计算，不再按需触发。负责人 Akash，下 Sprint 末交付。
You (BA): Decision two: we are deferring the CRS auto-classification for non-HK entities to Q3. Owner: Priya to confirm with the UK tax counsel.决策二：CRS 非香港实体的自动分类推迟到 Q3。Priya 负责与英国税务顾问确认。
Akash (Dev): Quick one — on the FATCA flag, can I get clarity on the cut-off time? You said end of day; is that HK time or UTC?快问一下——FATCA 标记的截止时间能不能澄清下？你说的"日终"是香港时间还是 UTC？
You (BA): Good catch. Let's lock that down — 23:59 HKT for the batch, with the file landing in the OneWealth share by 02:00 HKT. Jonathan, are you good with that?好问题，确认一下：批次截止 23:59 HKT，文件 02:00 HKT 前落到 OneWealth 共享目录。Jonathan，这样行吗？
Jonathan (PO): Yes, that works for the HK business. Just make sure the file format matches the MAS submission template.香港业务这边没问题。文件格式要符合 MAS 提交模板。
You (BA): Noted. I'll add that to the minutes as a constraint on the action item. Mei, you own the MAS file format check — due Friday EOD HKT. Can you confirm?收到，我加到 Action Item 的约束里。Mei，MAS 文件格式核对由你负责，周五 HKT 17:00 前。能确认吗？
Mei (Dev): Confirmed. I'll drop the spec in Confluence by Friday.确认了。周五前我把规范发到 Confluence 上。
You (BA): Last one. Action on David — circulate the risk rating memo from Risk to the squad by tomorrow 10 AM HKT. David, copy in?最后一条。David 的 Action——明天 10:00 HKT 前把风险部门的评级备忘录转给 squad。David，抄送给你？
David (SM): Done. I'll send it out first thing tomorrow.收到，明天一早发。
You (BA): Perfect. Minutes will be in your inbox within 24 hours — please reply-all if anything is miscaptured. Thanks, all.完美。会议纪要 24 小时内发到大家邮箱，如果记录有误请回复全部。谢谢各位。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Recap Let me recap the decisions and action items. 我回顾一下决策和 Action Item "Recap" = summarize. Always do this before closing a meeting. It surfaces disagreements in real time.
  • Owner Owner: Akash. 负责人：Akash Every action item MUST have one owner. "The team will…" is not an owner. One name, one accountability.
  • Due Due: end of next sprint. 截止：下 Sprint 末 "Due" = deadline. Always pair it with a specific time (EOD, Friday, sprint end) — never "soon".
  • Defer We are deferring the CRS auto-classification to Q3. 我们把 CRS 自动分类推迟到 Q3 "Defer" = move to later. Capture the new date — not just "deferred".
  • Constraint File format must match the MAS template. 文件格式必须符合 MAS 模板 Add constraints to action items, not just the deliverable. "What" + "by when" + "to what spec".
  • Lock Down Let's lock that down — 23:59 HKT. 把这个敲定——23:59 HKT "Lock down" = finalize, no more discussion. Use when ambiguity is causing delay.
  • Circulate Circulate the memo by tomorrow 10 AM. 明天 10 点前把备忘录发出去 "Circulate" = send to all attendees / stakeholders. Common minutes verb.
  • Mis-captured Reply-all if anything is miscaptured. 如果有记录错误请回复全部 "Miscaptured" = recorded wrong. The polite, professional way to invite corrections.
  • AI Action item AI-04: Mei to confirm file format. Action Item AI-04：Mei 确认文件格式 Number every action item. AI-01, AI-02… makes them easy to track in the next meeting's review.
  • 24-hour rule Minutes will be in your inbox within 24 hours. 纪要 24 小时内发到大家邮箱 At HSBC, the BA is expected to send minutes within 24 hours — sometimes 4 hours for urgent squads.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Minutes n. 会议纪要 The written record of a meeting. Distinct from "agenda" (the plan) and "transcript" (verbatim).
  • Action item (AI) n. Action Item 行动项 A specific task with one owner and a due date. The atomic unit of a minutes doc.
  • Decision n. 决策 A choice the group committed to. Should be recorded with rationale, not just the outcome.
  • Owner n. 负责人 The single person accountable for an action item. One name, not a team.
  • Recap v. 回顾 To summarize the key points of a meeting, usually at the end.
  • FATCA abbr. 美国海外账户税务合规法 Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (US). Affects all HSBC entities.
  • CRS abbr. 共同申报准则 Common Reporting Standard (OECD). Global equivalent of FATCA.
  • Recalcitrant account n. 不配合账户 An account that refuses CRS / FATCA self-certification. Triggers reporting.
  • OneWealth n. OneWealth 平台 HSBC's internal wealth platform; common file drop location for batch outputs.
  • MAS abbr. 新加坡金融管理局 Monetary Authority of Singapore. Defines local submission formats.
  • EOD abbr. End Of Day 日终 Common due-date shorthand. Default to the meeting's reference timezone (e.g., HKT).
  • Reply-all v. 全部回复 To respond to a group's email. Used to correct minutes or escalate blockers.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "We are deferring" vs "We defer" — present progressive for live decisions

In a meeting recap, the present progressive (we are deferring…, we are scoping…, we are confirming…) signals a decision that has just been made in this meeting, here and now. The simple present (we defer…) sounds like a standing policy. Use the progressive to mark "this is what we just agreed" — auditors and POs both prefer this in minutes because it is unambiguous about the decision moment.

现在进行时 (we are deferring…) 强调"刚做出的决策"，一般现在时 (we defer) 听起来像既定政策。会议纪要里推荐前者。

② The zero-relative clause: "the memo you sent yesterday"

Minutes are full of reduced relative clauses: "the memo Jonathan sent yesterday", "the file we agreed last time". English allows dropping that / which when the meaning is clear. In minutes, this makes sentences tighter. Compare: "the memo that Jonathan sent yesterday" (formal) vs "the memo Jonathan sent yesterday" (standard business English). The shorter form is preferred in cross-cultural HSBC minutes.

③ "Reply-all if anything is miscaptured" — the conditional for polite corrections

"Reply-all if anything is miscaptured" uses the present simple conditional (if + present, imperative) to invite corrections without sounding defensive. The alternative — "If I got anything wrong, please tell me" — is fine but more personal. The minutes-style version attributes the issue to the document ("miscaptured"), not to you. HSBC BAs use this pattern to keep the email neutral.

"Reply-all if anything is miscaptured" 用 if + 现在时 + 祈使句的句型，把问题归因于"文档"而不是"我"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • One owner per action item. Always. "The team will…" is the most common minutes mistake. If three people are listed, none of them owns it. If Akash owns it but Mei is involved, write "Owner: Akash. Support: Mei." — clear accountability, clear help.
  • Always state the timezone. "Friday" means different things in HK, Shanghai, Bangalore, and London. "Friday 17:00 HKT" is the HSBC default. Audit reviewers and Risk will both look for the timezone on every action item.
  • Recap at the end, not at the start. Some BAs recap the meeting at the beginning of the next meeting. The HSBC standard is the opposite: recap at the end of the meeting so corrections happen in real time, and the minutes are 95% right before you even type them.
  • Number your action items. AI-01, AI-02, AI-03… Sounds bureaucratic, but it makes the next meeting's review trivial: "AI-03 from last week — Akash, status?" Two minutes, done.
  • Use the "decision / owner / due date" triplet in every entry. A good minutes line is: "Decision: X. Owner: Y. Due: Z. Constraint: W." If any of the four is missing, the action item will come back to you.
  • Send within 24 hours — earlier for risk or compliance topics. For a FATCA / CRS walkthrough, the BA should send minutes the same day if possible. Compliance and Risk reviewers both want a fresh record; an old minutes doc invites "I don't remember saying that".
  • Don't transcribe — summarize. Minutes are not a transcript. Capture decisions and action items, not every word. A 12-page minutes doc gets ignored; a 1-page summary gets read.
  • Use the squad email, not your personal one. Send from the squad distribution list (e.g., rbwm-onboarding-squad@hsbc.com), not your personal alias. The minutes then become a team artefact, not your personal note.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the minutes recap

Translate the following recap into natural Chinese, paying attention to the decision/owner/due pattern.

"Three decisions. Decision one: the FATCA recalcitrant account flag will be calculated nightly, not on-demand. Owner: Akash. Due: end of next sprint. Decision two: we are deferring the CRS auto-classification for non-HK entities to Q3. Owner: Priya to confirm with UK tax counsel. Decision three: MAS file format must match the submission template. Owner: Mei. Due: Friday 17:00 HKT. Constraint: drop the spec in Confluence."

"3 个决策。决策一：FATCA 不配合账户标记改为每晚计算，不再按需触发。负责人 Akash，截止下 Sprint 末。决策二：CRS 非香港实体的自动分类推迟到 Q3，负责人 Priya，与英国税务顾问确认。决策三：MAS 文件格式必须符合提交模板，负责人 Mei，截止周五 17:00 HKT。约束：规范文档发到 Confluence。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the bad minutes line

Which of these minutes lines is "audit-ready" and which would Risk reject? Why?

  1. "AI-04: The team will look into the file format issue. Due: soon."
  2. "AI-04: Mei to confirm the MAS submission file format matches template. Due: Friday 17:00 HKT. Constraint: spec to be dropped in Confluence."
  3. "AI-04: Ping Mei about file format."
  4. "AI-04: Mei, file format. Friday."

② is audit-ready: one owner, one due date with timezone, one constraint. ① fails on owner (team = no one) and due date ("soon" = not a date). ③ is a chat message, not a minutes line. ④ is too terse — no verb, no timezone, no constraint. A good minutes line is a contract, not a note to self.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You just finished a 45-min meeting on "PCI-DSS data-masking on the cards statement PDF". Decisions made: (1) card numbers must be masked to first 6 + last 4 by 1 Aug 2026, (2) the mask will be applied at PDF generation, not in storage, (3) HK and SG will pilot; UK and CN are out of scope for now. Three action items: Akash to draft the technical design (due next Wed), Mei to draft the UAT script (due next Fri), Jonathan to confirm the regulator's interpretation letter (due Mon). Write the recap you would deliver at the end of the meeting in English. Use at least 4 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks, everyone. Let me recap the decisions and action items before we close. Decision one: card numbers must be masked to first 6 and last 4 by 1 August 2026, in line with PCI-DSS. Decision two: the mask will be applied at PDF generation, not in storage. Decision three: HK and SG will pilot; UK and CN are out of scope for now. Action item AI-01: Akash to draft the technical design, due next Wednesday 17:00 HKT. AI-02: Mei to draft the UAT script, due next Friday 17:00 HKT. AI-03: Jonathan to confirm the regulator's interpretation letter, due Monday EOD HKT. Minutes will be in your inbox within 24 hours — please reply-all if anything is miscaptured."
