User Story Writing Workshop

Article 07 · Module 2: Discovery & Workshops · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 "As a / I want / so that" + INVEST + split-by-ability

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday afternoon, 3 PM HKT. The RBWM Digital onboarding squad — a Product Owner (Priya, in London), a BA (you, Wei, in Hong Kong), a QA Lead (Ravi, in Bangalore), and a Tech Lead (Mei, in Shanghai) — has just finished a discovery interview with the HK retail-banking business. The team now has a stack of yellow stickies on the Miro board and needs to turn them into well-shaped user stories by end of day.

周二下午 3 点，香港时间。RBWM 数字化开户 squad —— 包括伦敦的 PO Priya、香港的 BA 你（Wei）、 班加罗尔的 QA Lead Ravi，以及上海的 Tech Lead Mei —— 刚刚结束与香港零售银行业务方的需求 访谈会。团队在 Miro 板上一堆黄色便利贴，要在今天 EOD 之前把它们整理成合格的 User Story。

You're facilitating a 90-minute story-shaping workshop. The goal is to leave with at least 6 stories that are INVEST-compliant, split where needed, and ready for refinement. Mei keeps pushing for technical tasks; Ravi wants every story testable. You have to guide the conversation without losing the business value.

你要主持一场 90 分钟的 Story 整理工作坊。目标是至少产出 6 条符合 INVEST 原则、必要时拆分过的 Story，并能进入下一轮 Grooming。Mei 一直想把它写成技术任务；Ravi 要求每条 Story 都可测试。你需要把握方向，不偏离业务价值。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (BA): Right, so let's keep this tight — 90 minutes, 6 stories out. Priya, can you walk us through the top three pains you heard from the HK business?好，我们把时间卡紧——90 分钟，产出 6 条 Story。Priya，你先说说你从香港业务方听到的三大痛点。
Priya (PO): Sure. Number one: customers abandon onboarding when the CDD enhanced due-diligence questionnaire is too long. Number two: relationship managers can't see the KYC status of their book. Number three: PEP hits are flagged too late in the journey.好。第一：客户在 CDD 增强尽职调查问卷太长时直接放弃开户。第二：客户经理看不到自己客户的 KYC 状态。第三：PEP（政治公众人物）命中信号在开户流程中出现得太晚。
You (BA): OK, so that's our three pillars. Let's take pain one first. Mei, I know you want to refactor the questionnaire engine — but let's frame this as a user story first, not a tech task.好，这就是三个支柱。我们先看第一个痛点。Mei，我知道你想重构问卷引擎——但我们先把它写成 User Story，不是技术任务。
Mei (Tech Lead): Hmm, but the engine doesn't support conditional branching at all today. We'd need a spike first.嗯，但现在的引擎根本不支持条件分支，我们得先做一个 Spike。
You (BA): Fair point. Let's split this by ability. Story A: the business authors the questionnaire in a new CMS. Story B: the engine renders conditional questions at runtime. Each one delivers value on its own.有道理。我们按能力拆开。Story A：业务方在新的 CMS 里配置问卷；Story B：引擎运行时渲染条件问题。两条都能独立交付价值。
Ravi (QA): Quick one — can we make the story testable on day one? I want a clear "Given/When/Then" for the abandonment drop.问一下——这条 Story 第一天就能测吗？我想要一个清晰的 Given/When/Then 来验证弃单率下降。
You (BA): Yes. Given a retail customer is mid-onboarding, when they complete the shortened CDD form, then the abandonment rate drops below 15% — we'll baseline that with Priya. That's the AC.可以。Given 零售客户正在开户中，when 完成简化版 CDD 表单，then 弃单率降到 15% 以下——这个基线我和 Priya 一起拉。这条就是 AC。
Priya (PO): Love it. What about the second story — RM visibility? Are we tying this to OneWealth or HSBCnet?很好。第二个 Story——客户经理可见性呢？这个我们接 OneWealth 还是 HSBCnet？
You (BA): Good question — let's call it out as an open assumption in the story description. Default to OneWealth for now, Mei can flag the tech risk tomorrow.好问题——我们在 Story 描述里把它列为开放假设。先默认接 OneWealth，Mei 明天把技术风险点出来。
You (BA): OK, time check — we've got 25 minutes left. Let's lock the story titles, owners, and rough sizing. Ravi, can you capture?好，时间提示——还剩 25 分钟。我们把 Story 标题、负责人、大致点数锁一下。Ravi，你来记录可以吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Right, so let's keep this tight. 好，我们把时间卡紧 Sets the timebox and tone. A workshop opener that signals focus, not pressure.
  • Walk Can you walk us through the top three pains? 你能说说你听到的三大痛点吗 Use "walk us through" with a numbered list — forces the PO to prioritize, not ramble.
  • Frame Let's frame this as a user story first, not a tech task. 我们先按用户故事框架来，不是技术任务 "Frame" = choose the lens. Defends business-value-first thinking against tech-led drift.
  • Split Let's split this by ability. 我们按能力拆 Split-by-ability is a BA standard. Each slice must be independently shippable.
  • Testable Can we make the story testable on day one? 这条 Story 第一天就能测吗 INVEST's "T". If QA can't write a test, the story is too vague or too big.
  • AC That's the AC. 这就是验收标准 Use to anchor a conversation. Once AC is locked, the story is "sprint-ready".
  • Assumption Let's call it out as an open assumption. 我们把它标为开放假设 Open assumptions go in the story description, not the AC. They get resolved in refinement.
  • Baseline We'll baseline that with Priya. 我们和 Priya 一起拉基线 "Baseline" = measure the current value so "improvement" is provable, not vibes-based.
  • Lock Let's lock the story titles, owners, and sizing. 我们把标题、负责人、点数锁一下 "Lock" = commit for now. Avoids endless re-litigation in the workshop.
  • Blocker Time check — we've got 25 minutes left. 时间提示——还剩 25 分钟 A polite timebox nudge. Phrase as a check, not a warning, to keep the room calm.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • User story n. 用户故事 A short description of value from the user's perspective.
  • INVEST n. INVEST 原则 Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable.
  • As a / I want / so that n. 故事三段式 The standard user-story template.
  • Split by ability v. 按能力拆分 Cut a story along ability / capability lines, not layers.
  • Spike n. 探针 A timeboxed research task to reduce a technical unknown.
  • Story shaping n. Story 整理 Workshop where a BA turns raw discovery into ready-to-refine stories.
  • Story readiness n. Story 准备度 A story's maturity: shaped → refined → ready → sprint-ready.
  • Open assumption n. 开放假设 A working hypothesis that must be validated before sprint.
  • PEP (Politically Exposed Person) n. 政治公众人物 A high-risk customer category triggering EDD.
  • CDD / EDD n. 客户/增强尽职调查 Customer / Enhanced Due Diligence screening.
  • OneWealth n. 内部系统 HSBC's relationship manager workstation (RBWM).
  • Discovery n. 需求发现 The phase of gathering business problems before shaping solutions.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Let's …" — soft facilitation language

In a workshop, you are suggesting, not ordering. Let's + base verb (e.g., Let's split this by ability) is the politest way to steer a group. Compare: I'll split this (decides for everyone) vs. Let's split this (we decide together).

工作坊里多用 Let's + 动词原形 引导团队，比 I'll 更礼貌、更能调动参与。

② "We'd need a spike" — conditional past for hypotheticals

We'd need a spike first uses 'd (= would) + base verb to describe a hypothetical or desired next step. It's softer than We need a spike (which sounds like a demand) and softer than We will need a spike (which sounds like a prediction).

would + 动词原形 用于假设/委婉表达，比 "we need" 更柔和。

③ "Given / When / Then" — the imperative mood in AC

Acceptance criteria use the imperative / present-tense form: Given X, when Y, then Z. You don't say "the system will…" or "the user should…" — you say "Given…, when…, then…". This is a deliberate, declarative style that makes the AC unambiguous and test-script-ready.

AC 用 Given/When/Then 陈述式表达，不要用 "the system will" 或 "should"，让 AC 更可测、无歧义。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • "Story" beats "requirement" at HSBC. Even in regulated areas, the squad vocabulary is story-based. Reframe any "the system shall…" requirement language as As a … I want … so that … to fit the team.
  • Spike is a word, not a question. In HSBC squads, a "spike" is a timeboxed research story (e.g., 3 points, 2 days). When the Tech Lead asks for a spike, log it in Jira as a story of type Spike, not a comment.
  • Always anchor AC in a number. "Drop the abandonment rate" is not an AC; "drop it below 15% from a baseline of 28%" is. BAs at HSBC are pushed by the QA chapter to make AC measurable, not aspirational.
  • Split, don't layer. The split-by-ability rule exists because slicing by technical layer (UI / API / DB) creates hidden dependencies. If Mei says "let's just do the backend first", push back with "what's the smallest user-facing slice?"
  • Cross-region story reviews. With Priya in London and Ravi in Bangalore, expect at least one async round of review on Confluence before refinement. Keep story descriptions in plain English, not UK idiom — your Shanghai and Bangalore reviewers will struggle otherwise.
  • Use Miro or Mural, not just Jira. HSBC squads default to a digital board for shaping. "Move the sticky" is a literal instruction. If you can't share the board, send a screenshot — never just type "left of the page" on a call.
  • "Lock" is a verb you should use often. Workshops go off the rails when nothing is committed. As BA, you own the parking lot and the "let's lock this for now" decision. The PO re-prioritises later; you don't re-debate in the workshop.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the workshop opening

Translate the following BA facilitation line into natural Chinese. The phrase is from a story-shaping workshop.

"Right, so let's keep this tight — 90 minutes, 6 stories out. Priya, can you walk us through the top three pains you heard from the HK business? And Mei, I know you want to refactor the questionnaire engine, but let's frame this as a user story first, not a tech task."

"好，我们把时间卡紧——90 分钟，产出 6 条 Story。Priya，你先说说你从香港业务方听到的三大痛点。Mei，我知道你想重构问卷引擎，但我们先按 User Story 框架来，不是技术任务。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the non-INVEST story

Which of these stories is INVEST-compliant? Why?

  1. "As a relationship manager, I want to see a single dashboard of my top 50 clients' KYC status, EDD alerts, and review dates, integrated with HSBCnet, OneWealth, and the eCAB system, so I can prepare for client meetings and stay compliant with HKMA and MAS guidelines."
  2. "As a retail customer, I want to complete a shortened CDD questionnaire, so that I don't abandon onboarding."
  3. "As a Compliance officer, I want to download a daily PEP hits report, so that I can review sanctions matches."
  4. "As a Tech Lead, I want to refactor the questionnaire engine, so that the codebase is clean."

② and ③ are INVEST-compliant: specific user, clear value, testable. ① is too big (multiple systems, multiple value statements — split it). ④ is a tech task in a story costume — the "user" is a Tech Lead and the "value" is codebase cleanliness, not business value. Rephrase as a spike with a learning outcome.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're a BA on the GPB (Global Private Banking) onboarding squad. The HK private bankers want a feature: see the source-of-wealth documentation status for each prospect, integrated with CAB. Write a user story for this feature and split it into at least 2 stories using the split-by-ability rule. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section. Then write one Given/When/Then AC.

Story 1 (Authoring side): "As a Private Banker in HK, I want to upload source-of-wealth documents in CAB, so that I have a single place for prospect due-diligence evidence."

Story 2 (Visibility side): "As a Private Banker, I want to see the source-of-wealth status for each prospect in OneWealth, so that I can prioritise the next conversation with the prospect."

AC for Story 2 (Given/When/Then): Given a prospect has a source-of-wealth record in CAB, when the Private Banker opens the prospect in OneWealth, then the SoW status is displayed within 2 seconds and links to the underlying CAB record.

Workshop phrasing you might use: "Right, so let's frame this as user value first, not a CAB refactor. Let's split by ability — the uploading flow and the read-only view. Priya, can you walk us through which slice your bankers want first? We'll baseline the current onboarding drop-off before we lock the AC."
