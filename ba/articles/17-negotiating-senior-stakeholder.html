Negotiating with Senior Stakeholders

Article 17 · Module 4: Difficult Conversations · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎯 Push back on MD-level asks while keeping them on side

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday afternoon, 3 PM HKT. You are the BA on the HSBCnet Cash Management squad in Global Liquidity & Cash Management (GLCM). A 30-minute meeting has been called at short notice in the MD's office — Margaret, MD of Payments & Receivables APAC, wants to add real-time FX rate display on the corporate client's payment initiation screen. She wants it live by next Friday, ahead of a key client visit from Standard Chartered.

周四下午 3 点香港时间。你是 GLCM（全球流动性与现金管理）HSBCnet 现金管理 squad 的 BA。 MD 办公室临时召集了一个 30 分钟的会议——Margaret，亚太区收付款业务部 MD，希望在企业客户付款 发起页面上加上 实时汇率展示。她要求 下周五前上线，因为渣打银行要来做重要客户拜访。

You know the squad has two stories committed this sprint and the FX rate feed integration involves a downstream dependency on the Group Treasury OneFX service plus a model risk sign-off. You need to push back — but without losing Margaret. The goal: find a workable compromise, not "no".

你知道 squad 这个 sprint 已经承诺了 2 个 Story，而且汇率源接入涉及集团财政部 OneFX 服务的下游 依赖以及 模型风险签字。你需要 push back——但 不能丢掉 Margaret 这个 sponsor。目标： 找到一个可执行的折中方案，而不是简单的"不"。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Margaret (MD): Thanks for coming at short notice. Quick context — Standard Chartered is in next Friday, and they specifically asked about real-time FX on the payment screen. Can we commit to having that live by then?临时召集你过来，谢谢。简单背景——渣打下周五来访，他们特别问了付款页面的实时汇率。我们能承诺那时上线吗？
You (BA): Thanks for the heads-up, Margaret. Absolutely I want to help on this. Let me just be transparent on the constraints first, then let's see if we can shape something workable.Margaret，谢谢提醒。这件事我非常想帮忙。我先把约束条件讲清楚，然后看能不能搞个可执行的方案。
You (BA): From a build perspective, the FX feed itself comes from Group Treasury's OneFX service, and that integration needs a model risk sign-off — typically that's a 10-day cycle, and we're already on day 3.从开发角度看，汇率源是集团财政部的 OneFX 服务，集成需要模型风险签字——通常要 10 个工作日，我们现在已经过 3 天了。
Margaret (MD): Right, but this is a competitive ask. If we don't show it, we lose the deal. What are our options?明白，但这是一个竞争性的诉求。如果我们不展示，就会丢单。我们有哪些选项？
You (BA): Three options, in my view. Option A: we push the live FX to the following sprint — solid, compliant, no risk. Option B: we show indicative FX sourced from a public feed with a clear disclaimer, as a placeholder for the demo — fast, but carries reputational risk if SC reads the fine print. Option C: we cut one of the two stories already in flight this sprint — but that breaks our commitment to Trade Finance.我看到三个选项。方案A：实时 FX 推到下个 sprint——稳妥、合规、无风险。方案B：展示一个公开源的指示性汇率，附带明确的免责声明，作为 demo 占位——快，但渣打如果看小字会有声誉风险。方案C：砍掉本 sprint 已开工的 2 个 Story 之一——但这会破坏我们对贸易融资的承诺。
You (BA): My recommendation is Option B, provided we get a 5-minute sign-off from Compliance and Model Risk first, and we make it explicit to SC that this is indicative, not contractual.我推荐方案B，前提是我们先花 5 分钟拿到合规和模型风险的签字，并向渣打明确说明这是指示性的、不是合同性的。
Margaret (MD): Hmm. I don't love the disclaimer. Clients don't read disclaimers. Is there a way to frame it as a "preview feature" rather than a placeholder?嗯。免责声明我不喜欢，客户不会看的。有没有办法把它框定为"预览功能"而不是占位符？
You (BA): That's a smart reframe. How about we call it an "FX preview widget" in the UI, with a small "Beta — indicative rates" label? That way it's positioned as innovation, not as a workaround. I can have a mock-up to you by tomorrow noon if that works.这个重新定义很聪明。我们在 UI 里叫它"汇率预览小部件"，加一个小小的"Beta — 指示性汇率"标签。这样定位是创新、不是变通。如果可以的话，明天中午前我把 mock-up 发给你。
Margaret (MD): OK, let's go with that. But I want the real OneFX live by end of next sprint. Can you book a checkpoint with me on Monday to confirm the model risk sign-off is on track?好的，就这么定。但下个 sprint 结束前我想要真正的 OneFX 实时汇率上线。能在周一和我约一个 checkpoint 确认模型风险签字的进度吗？
You (BA): Done. I'll send a calendar invite for Monday 10 AM, loop in the OneFX tech lead, and circle back on the mock-up by lunch tomorrow. Thanks for being flexible on the framing, Margaret — I think this is a much stronger story for SC.搞定。我会发周一上午 10 点的会议邀请，拉上 OneFX 的技术负责人，明天中午前把 mock-up 反馈给您。Margaret，谢谢您对表述方式的灵活处理——我觉得这个故事对渣打来说更有力了。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Let me just be transparent on the constraints first. 我先把约束条件讲清楚 A polite way to introduce bad news. Frames honesty as a service, not a refusal.
  • Frame Let's see if we can shape something workable. 看能否搞一个可执行的方案 "Shape" here = co-design. Implies you're problem-solving together, not blocking.
  • Trade-off It carries reputational risk if SC reads the fine print. 如果客户看小字会有声誉风险 Always quantify the risk, not just name it. MDs respond to "reputational risk" + a scenario.
  • Options Three options, in my view. 我看到三个选项 Never give one option. Three feels structured; two feels like a yes/no trap.
  • Recommend My recommendation is Option B, provided… 我推荐方案B，前提是… Lead with a recommendation + conditions. Don't leave the MD to pick from three alone.
  • Reframe How about we call it an "FX preview widget"? 我们把它叫做"FX 预览小部件"如何？ "Reframe" = repackage the same thing in a way that wins buy-in. A senior BA move.
  • Position It's positioned as innovation, not a workaround. 定位为创新、不是变通 Stakeholders care about how things are framed. Same feature, different story.
  • Commit I want the real OneFX live by end of next sprint. 下个 sprint 结束前要真正的 OneFX 上线 When an MD says "I want X by Y", capture it explicitly — it becomes your next checkpoint.
  • Checkpoint Can you book a checkpoint with me on Monday? 周一能约一个 checkpoint 吗？ "Checkpoint" = a 15-min status touchpoint. Slower than a meeting, faster than an email.
  • Close Thanks for being flexible on the framing, Margaret. 谢谢您对表述的灵活处理 Always credit the senior for the win. They "approved" the reframe — that's the story they tell upward.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • MD (Managing Director) n. 总经理 The most senior business rank below the CEO. Decisions are usually final at this level.
  • Sponsor n. 发起人 The senior executive who "owns" the outcome of your squad's work and fights for budget.
  • Squad n. 小队 HSBC's cross-functional delivery team (~6-9 people). One squad = one product slice.
  • OneFX n. 集团外汇服务 Group Treasury's official FX rate service. The only "live" FX feed for client-facing products.
  • Model risk sign-off n. 模型风险签字 Formal approval that a pricing/rate model is fit for production use.
  • Indicative rate n. 指示性汇率 A non-binding market rate shown for reference only — never contractual.
  • Disclaimer n. 免责声明 Legal text that limits liability. MDs dislike them; clients ignore them; Compliance insists on them.
  • Reputational risk n. 声誉风险 Risk that a public-facing decision damages HSBC's brand or trust.
  • Mock-up n. 模型/原型 A static design (Figma / PowerPoint) that shows the look — not the working product.
  • Checkpoint n. 节点会议 A 15-min status touchpoint. Lighter than a steering meeting, heavier than an email.
  • Group Treasury n. 集团财政部 The HSBC function that owns liquidity, FX, and interest rate policy globally.
  • Standard Chartered (SC) n. 渣打银行 HSBC's main regional competitor in APAC corporate banking. Common in war stories.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Provided" / "On the condition that" — conditional softeners

My recommendation is Option B, provided we get a 5-minute sign-off first. "Provided" (and its longer cousin on the condition that) is a subordinate conditional that softens a recommendation. It says "yes, but with a condition" — much more diplomatic than a plain if clause with an MD.

provided 是从属连词，比直接用 if 更有外交感——"可以，但有个前提"。

② "I think / I would say / In my view" — the "hedge" family

When giving an opinion to an MD, always hedge with a personal-stance marker: I think, in my view, I'd say, my take on this is…. Direct statements (Option B is the right choice) sound presumptuous; hedges sound collaborative.

给 MD 提建议时用"对冲"措辞：I think / in my view / I'd say——更协作、更不越界。

③ "If we don't… we lose the deal" — first conditional for risk framing

If we don't show it, we lose the deal. The classic if + present, will + verb structure is the standard pattern for framing business risk. Pair it with a concrete consequence ("lose the deal", "miss the HKMA deadline") — not a vague outcome.

业务风险描述的标准 if 句型：if + 现在时, will + 动词，一定配具体后果。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • MDs respond to options, not single answers. Walking in with one answer invites a yes/no fight. Three options — with a clear recommendation — show you've thought about the problem. Always lead with the recommendation; offer the alternatives as backup.
  • "Reputational risk" is the magic phrase. At MD level, the words that move decisions are risk, revenue, and reputation. Cost comes second. Frame technical constraints in those terms.
  • Never say "no" — say "yes, and". Yes, we can absolutely do this. And here's what it would take to land it well… The "and" reopens the conversation without making the MD feel blocked.
  • Capture commitments in writing — same day. After an MD meeting, send a 3-line Teams message summarising what was agreed, the owner, and the date. If it's not in writing, it didn't happen.
  • Credit the senior for the win. When Margaret re-frames the placeholder as a "preview widget", say "thanks for the framing" in the meeting. Politically, you want her to own the narrative going up.
  • Cross-region MDs (London / HK) have different tolerances. UK MDs often want a written options paper first; HK MDs often want the conversation first, paper after. Read the room and adapt.
  • "Model risk sign-off" is non-negotiable for FX. Even in a rush, never skip it. The Group Model Risk team is the gate; they can hold a release for weeks. Plan around their SLA, not around the demo date.
  • Bring data, not opinions. "I think this is risky" loses. "Last time we showed an indicative rate to a Tier-1 client, the post-demo survey flagged a 22% trust drop" wins. Numbers stick.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the negotiation

Translate the following BA response into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the BA balances push-back with options.

"Thanks for the heads-up, Margaret. I absolutely want to help. From a build perspective, the real-time FX needs Group Treasury sign-off plus a model risk review — typically ten working days. Three options: (A) push to next sprint, solid and compliant; (B) show an indicative rate with a clear 'Beta' label for the demo, with Compliance sign-off first; (C) descope one in-flight story. My recommendation is Option B, provided we get a 5-minute sign-off from Compliance and Model Risk before the demo. I can have a mock-up to you by tomorrow noon."

"Margaret，谢谢提醒。这件事我肯定想帮。从开发角度看，实时 FX 需要集团财政部签字 + 模型风险评审，通常要 10 个工作日。三个方案：(A) 推到下个 sprint，稳妥合规；(B) 展示指示性汇率并打上明确的'Beta'标签用于 demo，事先要合规签字；(C) 砍掉一个已开工的 Story。我推荐方案 B，前提是 demo 前先花 5 分钟拿到合规和模型风险的签字。明天中午前我可以给您一个 mock-up。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the weak response

An MD asks: "Can we go live with the new sanctions screening rules by Monday?" Which response is most "BA-grade" and which is weakest?

  1. "No, that's not possible. Model risk takes 10 days minimum."
  2. "I'll try, but no promises. Maybe."
  3. "Thanks for flagging. Let me be transparent on the constraints — Model Risk needs 10 days, and Compliance needs a parallel review. Three options: A) push to next sprint, B) phase the rules (only the 4 most critical for Monday), C) go live with a manual override flag for exceptions. My recommendation is B, with a checkpoint Wednesday. I can have a draft plan to you by EOD."
  4. "Yes, sure, no problem. I'll have the team work over the weekend."

③ is the strongest — transparent, three options with a clear recommendation, time-bound next step. ① is a flat "no" with no alternatives — will alienate the MD. ② is vague — sounds unconfident. ④ is the worst — saying "yes" without checking the team sets up a weekend crunch and a broken promise. A common BA mistake is over-promising in the moment to please a senior.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're the BA on the OneWealth onboarding squad. An MD from RBWM Asia-Pacific wants to add biometric login (Face ID) as a mandatory field for new sign-ups — by end of next week — to address an MAS observation letter. You know (a) the squad is mid-sprint on a MAS-TRM remediation, (b) biometrics requires Infosec sign-off and a Data Privacy Impact Assessment, (c) the MAS deadline is actually 6 weeks away, not next week. Write a 4-6 sentence BA response that pushes back politely and proposes a workable compromise. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks for raising this, Rachel. I want to help close out the MAS observation — it's a high-priority item for the tribe. Let me just be transparent on the constraints: a mandatory Face ID field needs an Infosec sign-off and a Data Privacy Impact Assessment, which together run about two weeks. Looking at the MAS letter, the actual remediation deadline is six weeks away, so we have headroom. Three options: (A) we ship Face ID as optional in the next release — closes the MAS observation on intent, no sign-off blocker; (B) we go mandatory within the four-week window, gated on the DPIA; (C) we escalate to the Group Privacy Office for a fast-track DPIA. My recommendation is A for the MAS deadline, with B as the roadmap item — that way we meet the regulator without burning the squad's runway. I can have a draft plan to you by EOD, and book a checkpoint on Monday to align."
