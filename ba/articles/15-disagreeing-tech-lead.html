Disagreeing with Tech Lead on Feasibility

Article 15 · Module 5: Difficult Conversations · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎯 When AC conflicts with tech reality — discuss trade-offs in English

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 2:30 PM GMT. You are in a 30-minute refinement session on Microsoft Teams for story GPB-1190 — real-time sanctions screening for cross-border payments. The AC you wrote says: "All MT103 outgoing payments must complete sanctions screening and return a decision within 200 milliseconds p99." Priya, your Tech Lead in the UK, has just said bluntly: "That latency target isn't achievable with the current screening API. We'd need to rebuild the rule engine."

下午 2:30 GMT，你在 Teams 上开 30 分钟的 Grooming，讨论 GPB-1190——跨境支付实时 制裁名单筛查。你写的 AC 是："所有 MT103 发出支付必须在 200 毫秒 p99 内完成制裁名单筛查 并返回决策。"UK 的技术负责人 Priya 刚直接说："这个延迟目标用现在的筛查 API 做不到， 我们得重建规则引擎。"

The tension is real: the AC came from the HK regulator's circular; the latency is non-negotiable for Compliance. Priya is not wrong — the API has a 600ms p99 floor today. You have to disagree politely but firmly, find the trade-off, and keep the relationship with the Tech Lead intact while protecting the regulatory commitment.

矛盾是真实的：AC 来自 HK 监管的通函，延迟对合规是硬要求。Priya 也没错——API 今天的 p99 底线 是 600 毫秒。你要礼貌但坚定地 disagree，找到 trade-off，既维护和 Tech Lead 的关系，又保住监管承诺。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Priya (TL): Wei, I have to push back on the latency AC. 200ms p99 is not achievable with the current sanctions API — its floor is 600ms.Wei，这个延迟 AC 我得 push back 一下。200 毫秒 p99 用现在的制裁 API 做不到，底线就是 600 毫秒。
You (BA): Thanks for being direct, Priya. Let me share the constraint on my side first, and then we can find a middle ground.Priya 谢谢直说。先说下我这边的约束，然后我们找个中间地带。
You (BA): The HKMA circular 2025-04 explicitly says the screening decision must be returned before payment dispatch. The 200ms is not a nice-to-have; it's a regulatory commitment that's been signed off by Jonathan at the PO level.HKMA 通函 2025-04 明确要求筛查决策必须在支付发出前返回。200 毫秒不是 nice-to-have，而是监管承诺，已经由 PO Jonathan 签字。
Priya (TL): I hear the regulatory pressure, but 200ms is not a soft target on my side either — the rule engine itself takes 400ms per single rule match. You'd need to fundamentally rebuild the screening service to hit that.我理解监管压力，但 200 毫秒在我这边也不是软指标——规则引擎每条规则匹配就要 400 毫秒。要达到 200 毫秒必须根本性重建筛查服务。
You (BA): OK, so we're both boxed in by our own constraints. Can I ask — is 200ms impossible, or is it costly?好，我们都被各自的约束困住了。我问一下——200 毫秒是"完全不可能"，还是"代价很高"？
Priya (TL): It's not impossible — it's expensive. We'd need to re-architect the rule engine, bring in a pre-screening cache, and add async risk scoring. Realistic number: 4 sprints of dedicated work, two extra engineers, and roughly USD 400k of infra.不是不可能，但很贵。需要重新架构规则引擎，引入预筛查缓存，加异步风险评分。实际数字：4 个 Sprint 的专项工作，2 个额外工程师，大约 40 万美元基础设施。
You (BA): Right, so the constraint is time and budget, not physics. That changes the conversation. What if we…明白了，那约束是时间和预算，不是物理定律。这就不一样了。我的想法是……
You (BA): What if we split the AC? Phase 1: keep 200ms as the regulatory target and use the pre-screening cache + async scoring to get to 250ms this sprint — which is good enough for the HKMA letter if we document the gap and the roadmap.把 AC 拆开呢？第一阶段：200 毫秒保留为监管目标，用预筛查缓存+异步评分本 Sprint 做到 250 毫秒——这够 HKMA 来函用，只要把差距和路线图写清楚。
Priya (TL): 250ms this sprint is tight but doable. I'd want a spike first to de-risk the cache though.本 Sprint 做到 250 毫秒紧但可行。不过我想要先做个技术 spike把缓存风险降下来。
You (BA): Agreed. Let me shape a 1-point spike story for the cache proof-of-concept, and we lock 250ms as the sprint AC with a clear roadmap to 200ms in the description. Can we take that to Jonathan together?同意。我去成型一个 1 点的 spike Story 做缓存 PoC，把 250 毫秒作为 Sprint AC 锁定，描述里写清楚到 200 毫秒的路线图。我们一起跟 Jonathan 谈行吗？
Priya (TL): Happy to. Good trade-off — I think Jonathan will accept it if we show him the roadmap.乐意。不错的 trade-off——只要我们给他看路线图，Jonathan 应该会接受。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Push Back I have to push back on the latency AC. 我得 push back 一下延迟 AC "Push back" = disagree formally. The standard verb for a polite-but-firm challenge.
  • Constraint Let me share the constraint on my side. 让我说下我这边的约束 "Constraint" = a hard limit you can't move. Naming the constraint (not the person) is the polite form.
  • Middle Ground Can we find a middle ground? 我们能不能找个中间地带？ "Middle ground" = a position both sides can accept. The goal of every negotiation.
  • Boxed In We're both boxed in by our own constraints. 我们都被各自的约束困住了 "Boxed in" = cornered. Acknowledging the other side's corner makes the conversation collaborative.
  • Impossible vs Costly Is 200ms impossible, or is it costly? 200 毫秒是"完全不可能"还是"代价很高"？ A magic question. "Impossible" ends the conversation; "costly" opens it. Always ask this.
  • Trade-off What if we split the AC as a trade-off? 我们能不能把 AC 拆开做个 trade-off？ "Trade-off" = a position where both sides give something. The unit of negotiation.
  • Phase Phase 1: 250ms. Phase 2: 200ms. 第一阶段 250 毫秒，第二阶段 200 毫秒 "Phase" = staged delivery. Lets you ship something this sprint without losing the long-term target.
  • Good Enough 250ms is good enough for the HKMA letter. 250 毫秒对 HKMA 来函够用了 "Good enough" = meets the real requirement, even if not the ideal. Use carefully — never for ACs that are the requirement.
  • Spike Let me shape a 1-point spike for the cache PoC. 我去成型一个 1 点的 spike 做缓存 PoC "Spike" = a time-boxed investigation to de-risk a tech choice. Standard Scrum term.
  • Roadmap Lock 250ms as the sprint AC with a roadmap to 200ms. 锁定 250 毫秒为 Sprint AC，并写明到 200 毫秒的路线图 "Roadmap" = the path to the long-term target. What the regulator wants to see, even if you can't ship it today.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Feasibility n. 可行性 Whether something can be built given the current tech, time, and budget.
  • Latency n. 延迟 The time a system takes to respond. Often measured at p50, p95, p99.
  • p99 abbr. 第 99 百分位 The 99th percentile latency — 99% of requests are faster than this.
  • Spike n. 技术验证 A time-boxed (usually 1-3 day) investigation to de-risk a tech choice.
  • Pre-screening cache n. 预筛查缓存 A store of pre-computed screening decisions to avoid repeating the full check.
  • Async risk scoring n. 异步风险评分 Running the deep risk score in the background, not in the request path.
  • Re-architect v. 重新架构 To redesign the system's structure. Implies a large, expensive change.
  • Proof of concept (PoC) n. 概念验证 A small implementation that proves a tech approach is viable.
  • Constraint n. 约束 A hard limit. A regulatory date, a budget cap, a p99 target. Always name, never blame.
  • Trade-off n. 权衡 A position where both sides give something. The output of a feasibility negotiation.
  • Regulatory commitment n. 监管承诺 A formal date a regulator requires a change to be live. Drives sequencing.
  • Sanctions API n. 制裁名单 API The service that checks a counterparty against OFAC, EU, UN, HMT, HKMA lists.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "It's not impossible — it's expensive" — the contrastive correction

One of the most useful sentences in BA-TL negotiation: "It's not impossible — it's expensive". The structure not X — it's Y is a contrastive correction that reframes a "no" into a "how much". It works because the listener hears the negation first (preserving their point of view) and then the reframing. Compare: "That's not going to work" (dead end) vs "It's not impossible — it's costly" (opens the conversation). Use the contrastive correction when you suspect the other side is conflating difficulty with impossibility.

"It's not X — it's Y" 是一种对比修正句式，把"不可能"重构成"代价大"，给对话留出空间。

② "Is X impossible, or is it costly?" — the disjunction that opens a negotiation

The disjunctive question (Is X A, or is it B?) is the BA's scalpel. It forces the other side to pick a category. "Impossible" ends the conversation; "costly" opens it. By asking, you also signal you suspect the answer is the second one — which is itself a soft form of pressure. Use it when you sense the Tech Lead is inflating the difficulty to avoid the work.

"Is X A, or is it B?" 是个二分式问题，逼对方归类。"不可能" 终止对话，"代价高" 开启谈判。

③ "What if we split the AC?" — the first-person plural suggestion

"What if we split the AC?" uses the first-person plural (we, us, our) to share ownership of the new idea. The contrast is with "What if you split the AC?" (sounds like a demand) or "What if I split the AC?" (sounds like you'll do it alone). We is the negotiation default. Pair with can for politeness: "Can we split the AC?"

"What if we…" 用第一人称复数共享方案归属，比 "you" 听起来更像要求，比 "I" 听起来更协作。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Disagree with the constraint, not the person. "The 200ms is hard to hit" is a fact. "You're wrong about 200ms" is a personal attack. HSBC BAs default to the first form — even when the Tech Lead is, in fact, being pessimistic.
  • Always share your constraint before naming the trade-off. If you skip the "here's my constraint" step, the Tech Lead will feel you're asking them to move without giving anything back. The order is: my constraint → your constraint → the trade-off.
  • Use "what if" to introduce your idea, not "I want" or "you should". "What if we split the AC?" is an invitation. "You should split the AC" is a directive. The former gets the Tech Lead thinking with you; the latter triggers defensiveness.
  • Be willing to be wrong. If after the discussion the Tech Lead still says 200ms is impossible, the right answer is "Happy to be wrong — let's spike it." A spike is cheap insurance; ego is expensive.
  • Take the trade-off to the PO together. Don't make the BA-TL decision in isolation. The PO needs to see both the constraint and the trade-off, and the BA-TL pair presenting a unified position is much stronger than either presenting separately.
  • Document the trade-off in the AC. "AC: 250ms p99 this sprint. Roadmap to 200ms in Sprint 17 (depends on re-architect of rule engine). Signed off by Priya (TL) and Jonathan (PO)." The signature line is gold for audit and for next sprint's planner.
  • Cross-region timing matters. When the Tech Lead is in the UK and the PO is in HK, the "fast" feedback loop is broken. Be explicit about response times: "Jonathan will reply by tomorrow 10 AM HKT — that's your morning." Sets expectations and avoids the 24-hour silence trap.
  • End on the relationship. "Appreciate you digging into this, Priya — let's catch up over coffee next sprint review." Disagreement + warmth is the long game. Tech Leads who trust their BAs will move mountains on real blockers.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the trade-off conversation

Translate the following BA-TL exchange into natural Chinese, preserving the constraint-trade-off structure.

"Priya, let me share the constraint on my side first. The HKMA circular 2025-04 says the screening decision must be returned before payment dispatch. 200ms is not a nice-to-have; it's a regulatory commitment signed off by Jonathan. I hear the rule engine constraint on your side. So we're both boxed in. Can I ask — is 200ms impossible, or is it costly? If it's costly, can we split the AC? Phase 1: 250ms this sprint. Phase 2: 200ms in Sprint 17 with a roadmap. Let's take the trade-off to Jonathan together."

"Priya，先说下我这边的约束。HKMA 通函 2025-04 要求筛查决策必须在支付发出前返回。200 毫秒不是 nice-to-have，而是 Jonathan 签字的监管承诺。你那边的规则引擎约束我听到了，所以我们都被困住了。我问一下——200 毫秒是'完全不可能'还是'代价很高'？如果是'代价很高'，我们能不能把 AC 拆开？第一阶段：本 Sprint 250 毫秒；第二阶段：Sprint 17 到 200 毫秒，并写明路线图。Trade-off 我们一起带给 Jonathan。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the bad disagreement

Which of these responses is most likely to resolve the disagreement AND keep the Tech Lead on side? Why?

  1. "200ms is the AC, the regulator signed it off. Make it work."
  2. "You don't understand the regulatory pressure. We have to hit 200ms."
  3. "I hear the rule engine constraint. Here's my constraint too — the HKMA letter is dated. So we're both boxed in. Is 200ms impossible, or is it costly? If it's costly, can we split the AC as a trade-off and take it to Jonathan together?"
  4. "OK fine, let's just descope the latency AC."

③ is the textbook answer: shares constraint, names mutual box, asks the magic "impossible vs costly" question, proposes a trade-off, loops the PO. ① sounds like a directive, no acknowledgment, no alternative. ② is personal ("you don't understand") — relationship-damaging. ④ caves on the regulator's commitment — the PO will not be happy. The skill is firmness and curiosity.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're a BA on the GPB onboarding squad. Priya, your UK Tech Lead, says: "Wei, the eKYC selfie-liveness AC is not achievable. The third-party liveness vendor we use has a 4-second floor for the deep check. You wrote 2 seconds in the AC." The AC came from the HKMA eKYC standard, which says liveness must complete within 3 seconds for the customer experience. You need to push back politely, find a trade-off (maybe split AC: happy-path 2s, deep-check 4s async?), and ask Priya to take it to Jonathan with you. Write a 6-8 line Teams message in English. Use at least 4 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks for flagging, Priya. Let me share my constraint: the HKMA eKYC standard sets a 3-second customer experience budget for liveness, which is why the AC says 2 seconds. I hear the vendor floor of 4 seconds on your side. So we're both boxed in. Quick question — is the 2-second target impossible, or is it costly? If we can split the AC, my idea: Phase 1 — happy-path liveness in 2 seconds (vendor's fast lane), with the deep check moved to async and logged out-of-band. The customer sees 2 seconds; the regulator sees the full 4-second deep check on record. Can we spike the vendor's fast lane together, then take the trade-off to Jonathan as a pair? Happy to be wrong on the split if you see a cleaner option."
