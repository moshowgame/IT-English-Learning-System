Walkthrough / Demo Prep

Article 05 · Module 5: Stakeholder Demos · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Rehearsing the demo, anticipating business questions, and not freezing when the CDD screen shows up

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Friday, 10:00 AM HKT. The HSBC GPB (Global Private Banking) Onboarding squad is 30 minutes from a stakeholder walkthrough for the new EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence) journey for high-net-worth clients in the OneWealth platform. The audience is the HK GPB Head of Compliance (Mrs. Cheung), the UK Head of Private Banking Products (Mr. Hartley, joining from London via Teams), and the Singapore Front-Office Champion (Mei Lin).

周五上午 10 点香港时间。HSBC GPB（全球私人银行） 开户 squad 离 OneWealth 平台新 EDD（强化尽调） 流程的 Stakeholder 走查还有 30 分钟。 听众包括：香港 GPB 合规负责人 Cheung 女士、英国（伦敦 Teams 加入）私行产品 负责人 Hartley 先生、新加坡前线 Champion Mei Lin。

You are the BA leading the walkthrough. Mei, the Shanghai developer, has finished deploying the build to the UAT environment. The dev team is sitting in the back of the HK conference room on standby. Your job in the next 30 minutes is to rehearse the demo flow, anticipate the 5 hardest business questions, and prepare graceful answers for the bits that don't work yet (the source-of-wealth upload field, the in-progress PEP second-line review screen).

你是走查主持人。上海开发 Mei 已把构建部署到 UAT 环境。开发团队在香港会议室后方 待命。接下来的 30 分钟你的任务是：演练演示流程、预判 5 个最难的业务问题、对还没做好的部分（资金来源上传字段、PEP 二线 复核进行中页面）准备得体的回答。

I'll be honest about it — "this is a pre-recorded clip, the live field is being stabilised". Agreed?好，记下。Plan B——如果 Cheung 女士要我现场上传 SOW 文档，我就跳过现场上传，放我昨天导出的预录点击演示。坦诚说明——"这是预录片段，现场字段在稳定中"。同意吗？

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Rehearse Quick dry-run, team. I'm going to talk through the demo flow. 快速彩排一下。我按语气走一遍演示流程 Always rehearse the demo with the dev team 30 mins before, not at the door. The dev team knows the failure modes.
  • Plan B Plan B — if she asks for a live upload, I'll show the pre-recorded clip. Plan B——如果她要求现场上传，我放预录片段 Every demo has 2-3 Plan Bs for the bits that don't work. Decide the Plan B in the dry-run, not live.
  • Honest This is a pre-recorded clip, the live field is being stabilised. 这是预录片段，现场字段在稳定中 Honesty builds trust with senior stakeholders. Never fake a working demo — it backfires in the Q&A.
  • Placeholder This screen is intentionally in placeholder state for v1. 这个页面在 v1 演示里是有意占位状态 "Intentionally" + "placeholder" pre-empts the "is this broken?" question. Stops the audience from assuming bug.
  • Predict The 5 hardest questions I'm expecting — quick brainstorm. 我预判的 5 个最难问题——头脑风暴一下 Always brainstorm the 5 hardest business questions before a senior demo. Senior stakeholders always have a "favourite" question.
  • Delegation Anyone want to take that one? 谁来接？ In a demo prep, assign each question to the person who'll answer it. The BA doesn't have to answer all of them.
  • Phrase Mrs. Cheung loves the phrase "single source of truth". Cheung 女士很喜欢"单一可信数据源"这个说法 Stakeholders have buzzwords they love. Learn them from the SM or PO, and weave them in. Cheap win, big impact.
  • Probable Mr. Hartley will very likely ask about GDPR. Hartley 先生很可能问 GDPR Use "very likely" / "almost certainly" / "probably" to rank the questions by probability. Drill the top 3.
  • Data Based on the last UAT cycle with 12 test users. 基于上一轮 UAT 的 12 个测试用户 Always cite the data source for any number. "12 users" beats "we tested it" because it shows traceability.
  • Honest cap The 12-user UAT is a directional sample — we're piloting with 200 real customers post-launch. 12 个 UAT 是方向性样本——上线后会用 200 个真实客户做试点 Always cap a small sample explicitly. Pre-empts the "but is this real?" follow-up. Show you know the limit.
  • Hook I'll open with the "single source of truth" line for Mrs. Cheung. 开场我先用"单一可信数据源"勾住 Cheung 女士 Plan your opening line per stakeholder. The first 60 seconds set the tone for the whole demo.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Walkthrough / Demo n. 走查 / 演示 A live show-and-tell of a working build to stakeholders.
  • Dry-run n. 彩排 A private rehearsal of the demo with the dev team before going live.
  • Plan B n. 备用方案 A pre-planned fallback for a part of the demo that may not work live.
  • Pre-recorded click-through n. 预录点击演示 A video / animated capture of the user flow, used as a Plan B.
  • Placeholder n. 占位符 A stub UI element that's intentionally not the real implementation.
  • Single source of truth (SSOT) n. 单一可信数据源 One system owns a piece of data; everything else reads from it.
  • EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence) n. 强化尽调 Extra KYC for high-risk / high-net-worth customers (source-of-wealth, second-line review).
  • Source of wealth (SOW) n. 资金来源证明 Documents proving where a customer's wealth originated.
  • PEP (Politically Exposed Person) n. 政治敏感人物 High-risk customer category requiring second-line review.
  • 4-eyes approval n. 四眼原则 Two-person sign-off for sensitive actions (RM + Compliance).
  • Regional cluster (EU / APAC) n. 区域集群 A data residency group — EU cluster keeps data inside EU, APAC inside Asia.
  • Directional sample n. 方向性样本 A small test result used for direction, not statistical significance. Always flag it.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Will almost certainly" — modal adverbs for probability ranking

In demo prep, ranking questions by probability is essential. The pattern is will + almost certainly / very likely / probably / possibly: Mrs. Cheung will almost certainly ask about CDD (95%), Mr. Hartley will very likely ask about GDPR (75%), Mei Lin will probably ask about timing (60%). The adverbs calibrate the room's mental probability — and your prep time.

问题概率排序：will almost certainly（95%）/ will very likely（75%）/ will probably（60%）。

② "Anyone want to take that one?" — elliptical questions for fast meetings

In fast dry-runs, native speakers drop auxiliary verbs: Anyone want to… instead of Does anyone want to…, That work? instead of Does that work?. This is standard in agile teams. The full form sounds overly formal; the elliptical form sounds collegial. Use the short form in team dry-runs, the full form in writing.

快节奏的彩排里省助动词：Anyone want to… 比 Does anyone want to… 更亲切。团队彩排用短形，书面用完整形。

③ "If pushed" — passive participle as a conditional shortcut

If pushed, if asked, if challenged are passive participle shortcuts for "if a stakeholder pushes / asks / challenges me on this". They are extremely common in senior-stakeholder meetings. The pattern is if + past participle: a full conditional compressed to 2 words.

If pushed / asked / challenged 是过去分词条件式的简写，资深 Stakeholder 会议里高频使用。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Rehearse 30 mins before, not at the door. The dry-run is the single highest-ROI activity in a demo. Catches the broken click-path, the missing data, the placeholder button. Never skip it.
  • Assign each hard question to a person. The BA doesn't have to answer GDPR (Akash does), the dev doesn't have to answer timing (BA does). In the dry-run, write the names against the questions. No one freezes on the day.
  • Learn the stakeholder's favourite phrase. Mrs. Cheung loves "single source of truth". Mr. Hartley loves "data residency". Mei Lin loves "pilot numbers". Use their own language back to them — it signals you listened.
  • Honesty > polish. "This screen is intentionally a placeholder" is 10x more credible than faking a working button. Senior stakeholders respect a BA who knows the build's limits.
  • Always cite the data source for any number. "12 users in UAT" beats "we tested it". HSBC stakeholders are auditors at heart — they want the provenance of every figure.
  • Cap small samples explicitly. A 12-user test is "directional", not "definitive". Naming the limit pre-empts the "but is this statistically valid?" question and earns trust.
  • Plan the opening 60 seconds per stakeholder. Mrs. Cheung wants SSOT. Mr. Hartley wants GDPR. Mei Lin wants timing. A demo for 3 senior stakeholders has 3 opening lines, not 1.
  • Cross-region rehearsal matters. When Mr. Hartley joins from London, the time-zone factor means you can't "rehearse live with him". Rehearse with the SM, who proxies his likely questions. The SM is your stakeholder translator.

Exercise 2 — Spot the BA mistake

Which of these demo-prep lines would land worst with a senior stakeholder, and which would land best? Why?

  1. "Everything works perfectly, no need to worry, please don't click the SOW upload field."
  2. "This screen is intentionally in placeholder state for v1 demo — the workflow is in scope but the buttons land in next sprint."
  3. "Uh… I don't know why that didn't work. The dev probably didn't finish it."
  4. "Average is 35 minutes for EDD based on the last UAT cycle with 12 test users. That's a directional sample — we're piloting with 200 real customers in HK post-launch, and I'll share the post-pilot data in the QBR."

② and ④ land best — ② is honest, framed as intentional, with the next-step; ④ cites the data source, names the limit, and promises a follow-up. ① is the worst — saying "everything works perfectly" and then telling them not to click something is a credibility time-bomb. ③ blames the dev publicly — a senior stakeholder will not forget this. Always own the answer or attribute it cleanly to the team.
