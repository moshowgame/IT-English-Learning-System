Requirements Clarification

Article 04 · Module 4: Requirements & Backlog · BA English

⏱ 9 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Live Jira-comment Q&A: pinning down AC between BA, dev, and PO — without ping-pong

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is 2:30 PM HKT, Tuesday. The HSBC RBWM Digital Onboarding squad is mid-refinement for next sprint's Jira ticket WM-1982 — "Refresh KYC for high-value retail customers". The ticket has 9 acceptance criteria items, but 3 of them are still yellow-flagged in the comments. The conversation has moved to the Jira ticket itself — a live thread between you (BA, Hong Kong), Akash (Dev, Bangalore), and Priya (PO, Singapore business).

周二下午 2:30 香港时间。HSBC RBWM 数字化开户 squad 正在做下个 Sprint Jira WM-1982——"高净值零售客户 KYC 信息更新" 的 Refinement。这张 Ticket 有 9 条验收标准，3 条还在黄标状态。讨论已转到 Jira Ticket 评论区—— 是你（BA，香港）、Akash（开发，班加罗尔）和 Priya（业务 PO，新加坡）三方实时 留言。

The three open items are: (1) what triggers the KYC refresh — calendar anniversary or risk-score change? (2) what documents does the customer need to re-upload? and (3) does CDD have to be re-run by Operations, or is the digital refresh enough? Your job is to convert ambiguity into testable AC before the sprint planning on Thursday — without bouncing the PO between you and the dev.

3 个开放项：(1) KYC 更新的触发条件——是周年日期还是风险评分变化？(2) 客户需要 重新上传哪些文件？(3) 运营是否需要重跑 CDD，还是数字端更新就够？你的任务是在 周四 Sprint Planning 之前把模糊项变成可测的 AC，同时不让 PO 在 你和开发之间被踢皮球。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Frame Let's go one by one so we don't ping-pong. 一条一条来，避免踢皮球 "Ping-pong" = bouncing a question back and forth. The BA's job is to kill ping-pong with structure.
  • Confirm Just to confirm my understanding — am I right? 确认一下我的理解——我对吗？ "Just to confirm" + "am I right?" = double softener. Restate what you heard and ask for a yes/no. Forces a clear answer.
  • Precise Just to be precise — the change should only trigger if… 明确一下——变化只在…时触发 "Just to be precise" = polite way to add a qualifier that might contradict a previous statement. Use freely in PO conversations.
  • Format Given… when… then the system shall… 假设… 当… 则系统应… The Gherkin format (Given/When/Then). Universal AC template. Turns ambiguity into a testable sentence.
  • Scope I'd say defer the force-logout to a follow-up story. 我建议强制下线延到后续 Story "Defer to a follow-up" = polite way to say no without saying no. Captures the need without burning this sprint's points.
  • Ask Does that match what RBWM Compliance told us? 这和 RBWM 合规说的一致吗？ Cross-check pattern: ask the PO to validate their own statement against another stakeholder. Builds in audit accuracy.
  • Differentiate That's a separate rule, separate from the EDD requirement. 这是独立规则，独立于 EDD 要求 Always separate regulatory drivers (FATCA/CRS, KYC, EDD, CDD, AML) — different rules, different audit trails.
  • Force Is the answer 'yes' or 'no'? 答案是 yes 还是 no？ A dev's natural question style. BAs learn this trick from devs — force a binary answer when stakeholders waffle.
  • Recommend My recommendation — for v1, we do NOT trigger… 我的建议——v1 不触发… Lead with "my recommendation" before the decision. Lets the PO disagree with the rec, not the question.
  • Close Locked. I'll update the ticket and close the yellow flags. 锁定。我会更新 Ticket 并关掉黄标 "Locked" = the AC is final. Always explicitly close the loop in writing, not just in chat.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Acceptance Criteria (AC) n. 验收标准 The bulleted testable conditions that define "done" for a story.
  • Gherkin (Given/When/Then) n. 行为驱动格式 A standard format for writing AC: Given a state, when an action, then an outcome.
  • Yellow flag n. 黄标 A Jira label signalling an open question or unresolved AC item.
  • Refinement (Grooming) n. Refinement 会 The meeting to discuss and size upcoming stories.
  • KYC n. 了解你的客户 Know Your Customer — identity & risk verification.
  • CDD / EDD n. 客户尽职调查 / 强化尽调 Customer / Enhanced Due Diligence.
  • PEP n. 政治敏感人物 Politically Exposed Person — high-risk customer category.
  • FATCA / CRS n. 美国 / OECD 税务信息申报 Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act / Common Reporting Standard.
  • Event bus / Kafka n. 事件总线 A publish-subscribe messaging system for real-time events.
  • Case-management integration n. 案件管理集成 A link between the digital channel and the Operations case tool.
  • Source-of-wealth (SOW) n. 资金来源证明 Documents proving where a customer's wealth originated.
  • Tag / @mention v. @ 提及 A Jira / Teams feature to pull a specific person into a thread.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Am I right?" — the tag question that locks the AC

After you restate your understanding of a requirement, end with a tag question: am I right?, is that correct?, does that match?. The tag forces the other person to say "yes" or "no" — not to waffle. In Jira comments, this is the difference between a closed thread and a 30-comment ping-pong.

复述需求后，加反义疑问句（am I right? / is that correct?）逼对方给"是/否"，避免来回。

② "Should" for soft requirements vs "shall" for hard AC

In HSBC requirements docs, should means "recommended" and shall means "mandatory, testable". Compare: The system should warn the user (nice to have) vs The system shall block access (testable AC). Use shall in AC items; use should in design notes.

HSBC 需求文档里：should = 建议，shall = 强制可测。AC 用 shall，设计备注用 should。

③ "I'd say defer" — "I'd" + verb as a soft recommendation

I'd say defer, I'd recommend…, I'd suggest… all use the contracted form I'd (I would) to soften a recommendation. The pattern is I'd + recommend / say / suggest + base verb. It's the politeness prefix for any BA recommendation in writing.

I'd say / recommend / suggest 是书面推荐的礼貌前缀。结构：I'd + recommend/say/suggest + 动词原形。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Written AC beats chat AC. "I thought we agreed" is the most expensive phrase in a sprint. Everything agreed in chat must be written into the Jira ticket AC before refinement closes. The ticket is the source of truth.
  • One thread, one topic. Don't let a Jira comment thread become a 20-message debate on 9 different things. Restructure ("let's go one by one") and assign each item a number. Saves hours.
  • The BA is the translator. Devs speak in code and events (Kafka, APIs). POs speak in business rules (KYC, EDD). The BA is the only one who hears both. When Akash asks "how fresh is fresh?", your job is to translate the answer into a business rule, not to bounce the question to the PO.
  • Tax residency is a separate compliance stream. Don't fold FATCA/CRS into KYC. They have different regulators, different audit trails, different schedules. Always separate the compliance drivers in the AC.
  • Singapore POs are time-conscious. Priya is in Singapore. She will give a 1-line answer, expect you to act on it, and move on. Don't ask 3 follow-ups in the same comment — ask the most important one, get the answer, ask the next.
  • Bangalore devs are detail-oriented. Akash will probe edge cases (force-logout, event freshness). Don't dismiss — fold the question into a "defer to follow-up story" or "yes, in scope, will size". It shows the BA respects the engineering view.
  • "Locked" is the magic closing word. When the AC is final, say locked explicitly. It signals the end of the conversation, the start of the implementation, and gives the dev permission to start sizing.
  • Tag the right people, not everyone. A Jira thread @-mentioning the whole squad creates noise. Only @ the people whose decision is needed. Be respectful of attention.

Exercise 2 — Spot the BA mistake

Which of these Jira comment styles would lead to a "ping-pong" thread and which would close the loop? Why?

  1. "Hi @Priya, can you clarify the documents thing? Also the trigger. And the CDD question. Thanks!"
  2. "@Priya @Akash — three open items. Let's go one by one. Item 1 (trigger): my understanding is X. Am I right? Item 2 (documents): the spec says Y. Does that match Compliance? Item 3 (CDD): my recommendation is Z. Please confirm."
  3. "Hey, just checking in on WM-1982 — any update on those AC items?"
  4. "@Priya — quick one. I rewrote AC#1 to add the FATCA/CRS tax-residency self-cert (separate from the EDD docs). Please review. AC#1 now reads: 'Given a customer's KYC anniversary is within 30 days OR their risk score moved low to high, when the customer logs in, then the system shall block access and request KYC refresh + tax-residency self-cert.' OK to lock?"

② and ④ close the loop — ② pre-frames and numbers items, ④ proposes the exact wording and asks for a yes/no. ① and ③ are vague and will generate ping-pong. ① crams 3 questions into one message; ③ is the worst — vague nudge with no proposal, forcing the PO to think from scratch.
