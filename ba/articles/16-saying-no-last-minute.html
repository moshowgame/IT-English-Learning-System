Saying "No" to Last-Minute Requests

Article 16 · Module 5: Difficult Conversations · BA English

⏱ 8 min read 🔴 Advanced 🎯 "We can do that, but not in this sprint" — offering alternatives

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Thursday 5:18 PM HKT. You are wrapping up the day when Vincent, a senior Product Owner from GPB (based in London), calls you on Teams. It's 9:18 AM in London. "Hi Wei, sorry to drop this on you last minute — we have a major client onboarding in Singapore on Monday, and the GPB COO wants a real-time risk-rating widget on the relationship manager dashboard by then. Can your squad add it this sprint? I know it's late, but…"

周四下午 5:18 HKT，你正要收工。GPB（伦敦）的高级 Product Owner Vincent 在 Teams 上打给你。伦敦 那边是上午 9:18。"Wei，抱歉这么晚才说——我们周一在新加坡有个大客户开户，GPB 的 COO 想要在客户 经理仪表盘上加一个实时风险评级小部件。你们 squad 这个 Sprint 能加吗？我知道很晚，但是……"

The sprint ends on Friday at noon HKT. The squad is fully booked — Akash on the MAS file format fix, Mei on the FATCA recalcitrant flag, QA locked for the HK pilot. There is literally no engineering capacity this sprint. But "no" without an alternative damages the relationship with GPB. The skill is: "We can do that — but not in this sprint."

Sprint 在周五中午 HKT 结束。squad 满载——Akash 在做 MAS 文件格式修复，Mei 在做 FATCA 不配合标记，QA 锁定给 HK 试点。本 Sprint 真的没有工程产能。但"不行"而不给替代方案会 损坏和 GPB 的关系。要点是："我们可以做——但不是这个 Sprint。"

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Vincent (Sr PO, GPB): Hi Wei, sorry to drop this on you last minute — we have a major client onboarding in Singapore on Monday, and the GPB COO wants a real-time risk-rating widget on the RM dashboard by then. Can your squad add it this sprint?Wei 抱歉这么晚才说——周一新加坡有大客户开户，GPB 的 COO 想要在 RM 仪表盘上加一个实时风险评级小部件。Squad 这个 Sprint 能加吗？
You (BA): Hey Vincent, thanks for the heads-up — and I can hear this is time-critical. Let me think out loud for a second.Vincent 谢谢提前说——我能听出这很紧急。让我捋一下。
You (BA): We can absolutely do the real-time risk-rating widget — but not in this sprint. The sprint closes Friday at noon HKT, and the squad is at full capacity with the MAS file format and the FATCA flag both going into UAT.实时风险评级小部件我们肯定能做——但不是这个 Sprint。本周五中午 HKT Sprint 结束，squad 满载，MAS 文件格式和 FATCA 标记都在进 UAT。
Vincent (Sr PO, GPB): But it's Monday. The COO is breathing down my neck.但是周一就要，COO 一直在催我。
You (BA): I really get the pressure. Let me offer you two alternatives — and you can pick the one that works best for the COO.我完全理解压力。我给你两个替代方案，你挑一个对 COO 最合适的。
You (BA): Option A: we shape and groom the widget story this afternoon, get it refined tomorrow morning, and prioritise it as Sprint 14's first story. The widget ships end of Sprint 14 — that's two weeks from Monday.方案 A：我们今天下午就把 Story 成型并过 Grooming，明天上午Refinement，锁定为 Sprint 14 的第一个 Story。小部件Sprint 14 末交付——距今两周。
You (BA): Option B: a read-only risk snapshot based on OnePay / eCAB data — we can hack something together over the weekend and have a basic version live by Monday morning HKT. It's not real-time, but it shows the COO a working widget for the client meeting, with the real-time version coming two weeks later.方案 B：一个只读的风险快照，基于 OnePay / eCAB 数据——我们周末赶一赶，周一上午 HKT 前有一个基础版。不是实时的，但客户会议上能给 COO 看到一个能用的小部件，两周后再上实时版。
Vincent (Sr PO, GPB): Option B might work for the client optics. But I'd need to check the data sources with Priya — can you loop her in?方案 B 在客户面前可能够用。但我得和 Priya 确认数据源——你能拉她进来吗？
You (BA): Of course. I'll set up a 30-min call with you, Priya, and Mei for tomorrow 10 AM HKT — that's 2 AM your time, so no pressure if you'd rather do 3 PM HKT.当然。我来约一个 30 分钟会议，你、Priya、Mei 一起参加，明天 10:00 HKT——你那会是凌晨 2 点，不急的话也可以约 15:00 HKT。
You (BA): If the data sources check out, we'll have a basic widget live by Monday morning, with the real-time version locked into Sprint 14. Does that work for the COO's ask?如果数据源OK，周一上午前基础版上线，实时版锁进 Sprint 14。这样对 COO 的要求 OK 吗？
Vincent (Sr PO, GPB): That's a solid plan. Let's go with Option B and lock the meeting for tomorrow 10 AM HKT. Appreciate the flexibility, Wei.这个方案很扎实。走方案 B，明天 10 点 HKT 锁会议。Wei 感谢你的灵活。
You (BA): Glad to help. I'll send the calendar invite in the next 10 minutes and raise the widget story in Jira as a Sprint 14 candidate. Speak tomorrow.乐意帮忙。我 10 分钟内发出会议邀请，并在 Jira 里把 Story 提到 Sprint 14 候选。明天见。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • But Not We can do that — but not in this sprint. 我们可以做——但不是这个 Sprint The single most useful sentence in BA. Says yes to the work, no to the timing. Keeps the relationship.
  • Time-critical I can hear this is time-critical. 我能听出这很紧急 "Time-critical" = more urgent than "important". Acknowledge before pivoting.
  • Full Capacity The squad is at full capacity. Squad 满载 "Full capacity" is the polite, factual way of saying "we can't". Pair with what's already committed.
  • Alternative Let me offer you two alternatives. 我给你两个替代方案 Always give at least two. One is "no in disguise"; two is a choice. The PO has to pick, not negotiate.
  • Option A / Option B Option A: shape and groom. Option B: hack a basic version. 方案 A / 方案 B Label your alternatives. Option A = "the right way". Option B = "the emergency way". Let the PO choose.
  • Prioritise Prioritise it as Sprint 14's first story. 锁定为 Sprint 14 的第一个 Story "First story" of the next sprint = the highest priority. A real concession the PO can take to the COO.
  • Hack together We can hack something together over the weekend. 我们周末赶一赶 "Hack together" = quick and dirty. Use for Option B only — never for the real version.
  • Basic Version Have a basic version live by Monday morning. 周一上午前有一个基础版 "Basic version" = MVP. Lower expectations, deliver something, follow up with the real thing.
  • Loop In Can you loop Priya in? 你能把 Priya 拉进来吗？ "Loop in" = include. The right move when feasibility or data ownership is in question.
  • Solid Plan That's a solid plan. 这个方案很扎实 "Solid" = well-grounded. The senior PO's verbal handshake. Use it yourself when you hear it from the PO.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Last-minute request n. 临时请求 A ask that arrives after the sprint has been planned and committed. The classic BA test.
  • Full capacity n. 满载状态 No engineering bandwidth left for new work this sprint.
  • Time-critical adj. 时间紧迫 Hard deadline, often regulatory or client-facing. Stronger than "important".
  • Alternative n. 替代方案 A different way to deliver value when the original path is blocked.
  • Hack together v. 拼凑搭建 To build a quick-and-dirty version, accepting lower quality. For Option B only.
  • Basic version / MVP n. 基础版 / 最小可行产品 A minimal, working version. Lower expectations, faster delivery.
  • Real-time adj. 实时的 Updated continuously, not in batch. Often the unstated ask in "real-time widget".
  • Read-only adj. 只读 Display-only, no writes. A common constraint that lets you ship faster.
  • OnePay / eCAB n. HSBC 内部平台 Internal HSBC platforms that surface client / risk data.
  • RM dashboard n. 客户经理仪表盘 The relationship manager's view of a client's portfolio and risk.
  • Risk-rating widget n. 风险评级小部件 A UI element showing a client's current risk score.
  • Sprint 14 candidate n. Sprint 14 候选 A story being considered for the next sprint, raised early to make prioritisation visible.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "We can do that — but not in this sprint" — the dash for the polite pivot

The em dash (—) is the BA's politeness engine. "We can do that — but not in this sprint." The dash creates a beat, a micro-pause, that turns a flat "but" into a structured concession. Read aloud: "We can do that [pause] but not in this sprint." The pause is what makes it land as collaborative, not contradictory. The same sentence without the dash — "We can do that but not in this sprint" — sounds like a single flat refusal. In written Teams messages, the dash is doing the work that a real conversation would do with tone of voice.

破折号 (—) 是 BA 的礼貌引擎。它制造一个"微停顿"，把"拒绝"变成"让步+条件"。

② "Can your squad add it this sprint?" — the indirect question

Senior stakeholders rarely ask a flat "Can you do this?" — they ask a leading question with the answer baked in: "Can your squad add it this sprint?" The BA's job is to not just answer the question as asked — that would be a yes or no. Instead, respond to the underlying ask (the widget) and reframe the boundary (the timing). Compare: "No, we can't" (answers the question) vs "We can do that — but not in this sprint" (answers the ask). The first is a closed door; the second is a redirected path.

不要只回答"问题" (squad 能加吗)，要回答"诉求" (怎么把 widget 做好)。用破折号重新框定时间。

③ "Option A… Option B…" — the binary choice as a polite ending

Offering two labelled alternatives (Option A, Option B) is the politest form of saying "this is your decision, not mine". The labels make the choice feel structured. Option A is conventionally the "right way" (slower, more complete); Option B is the "emergency way" (faster, hacky). The PO picks — and the BA is no longer the bottleneck. If only one option is offered, the PO can negotiate; with two, they have to choose.

提供两个明确标记的方案 (Option A / Option B) 是最礼貌的"决定权在你"。A 是"正确方式"，B 是"应急方式"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • "Yes — but" is always better than "no". "We can do that — but not in this sprint" protects the relationship with the senior PO. "No, we can't" burns it. The same outcome — the widget ships in Sprint 14 — is delivered with totally different downstream effects on the next 5 requests.
  • Always offer at least two alternatives. One alternative is a "no" in disguise. Two is a real choice. The senior PO needs something to take back to the COO; give them a menu, not a single answer.
  • Label your alternatives "A" and "B", not "good" and "bad". Option A = the right way. Option B = the emergency way. The PO chooses; you don't moralise.
  • Be careful with time zones when looping people in. "That's 2 AM your time, so no pressure if you'd rather do 3 PM HKT." The skill of offering a more humane slot, even if the senior accepts the early one, signals respect.
  • Raise the Sprint 14 candidate in Jira now. Don't wait for the meeting tomorrow. Putting it in the backlog as a Sprint 14 candidate makes the priority visible to the whole squad and locks the commitment in writing.
  • Document the trade-off in the meeting minutes. "Decision: GPB risk-rating widget to be shipped in two phases — basic version by Monday 15 June (read-only, OnePay data), real-time version end of Sprint 14 (week of 28 June). Signed off by Vincent (Sr PO) and Wei (BA)." Protects you if the COO forgets.
  • Don't apologise for sprint boundaries. "Sorry, the sprint is closed" sounds weak. "The sprint closes Friday at noon HKT" is factual. Apologising invites the PO to push harder; stating the fact invites them to choose an alternative.
  • End on the relationship. "Appreciate you flagging it early, Vincent — even if it was 5 PM on a Thursday." A senior PO who gets the "yes — but" pattern from their BA will keep coming back, even when the answer is "we can't do that either". Trust is built by being honest and helpful.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the polite "no"

Translate the following BA response into natural Chinese, preserving the "yes — but" and the two-alternatives structure.

"Vincent, thanks for the heads-up — and I can hear this is time-critical. We can absolutely do the real-time risk-rating widget — but not in this sprint. The sprint closes Friday at noon HKT and the squad is at full capacity with MAS and FATCA both going into UAT. Let me offer you two alternatives. Option A: shape and groom this afternoon, refine tomorrow, prioritise as Sprint 14's first story — real-time version ships end of Sprint 14. Option B: hack a basic read-only snapshot together this weekend using OnePay data — Monday morning HKT it's live for the client meeting, real-time version follows two weeks later. Which works for the COO?"

"Vincent 谢谢提前说——我能听出这很紧急。实时风险评级小部件我们肯定能做——但不是这个 Sprint。本周五中午 HKT Sprint 结束，squad 满载，MAS 和 FATCA 都在进 UAT。我给你两个方案。方案 A：今天下午成型并过 Grooming，明天 Refinement，锁定为 Sprint 14 的第一个 Story——实时版 Sprint 14 末交付。方案 B：周末基于 OnePay 数据赶一个只读快照版——周一上午 HKT 前能赶上客户会议，实时版两周后再上。哪个对 COO 合适？"

Exercise 2 — Spot the best "yes — but"

Which of these responses is most likely to deliver the widget, keep the sprint, AND keep the relationship with Vincent? Why?

  1. "No, the sprint is closed. We can't add it."
  2. "Sorry, no can do. The squad is full."
  3. "We can do that — but not in this sprint. Let me offer you two alternatives. Option A: shape and groom today, refine tomorrow, prioritise as Sprint 14's first story. Option B: hack a basic read-only version together this weekend for the Monday meeting, with the real-time version following in Sprint 14. Which works for the COO?"
  4. "I can ask Akash, but he's already overloaded. Maybe Mei can help? Actually, no, she's busy too. Probably next sprint."

③ is the textbook answer: "yes — but" + two clear alternatives + asks the PO to choose. ① is a flat no — relationship-damaging, no path forward. ② apologises and gives no alternative — Vincent has nothing to take to the COO. ④ waffles, asks people without permission, and ends in a vague "probably next sprint" — Vincent will not trust you again. The skill is: yes, clear constraint, two real choices, ask for the decision.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

It's Friday 4:30 PM HKT. Akash's standup just finished. Priya messages you on Teams: "Wei, the UK regulator (FCA) has just sent an urgent guidance update — we need to add a 'high-risk jurisdiction' warning banner to the HSBCnet trade screen for UK corporate clients by Monday morning UK time. The squad would need to come in over the weekend. Can we do it?" The squad is finishing the MAS file format and FATCA flag. The UK business is the requester. You need to respond with a "yes — but" structure and two alternatives. Write the Teams message in English (8-10 lines). Use at least 4 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks for flagging, Priya — and the FCA guidance is a real priority. We can do the high-risk jurisdiction banner — but not in this sprint. The sprint closes at noon HKT tomorrow and the squad is at full capacity with MAS and FATCA going into UAT. Two options for you. Option A: shape the banner story this afternoon, refine it Monday 10 AM HKT (that's 2 AM your time, so we can shift to 3 PM HKT if easier), prioritise as Sprint 14's first story — banner ships end of Sprint 14, roughly 10 days from Monday. Option B: hack a basic hard-coded banner list this weekend using the existing OFAC list as a starting point, with a real-time data-driven version in Sprint 14. The hard-coded version is read-only and shows the COO something for Monday morning UK. I'll raise the Sprint 14 candidate in Jira now. Which works for the FCA ask?"
