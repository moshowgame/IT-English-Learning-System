Talking to Compliance & Regulators

Article 18 · Module 5: HSBC Banking Context · BA English

⏱ 10 min read 🔴 Advanced 🏦 GDPR, MAS TRM, HKMA, PCI-DSS — audit findings and exceptions

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Tuesday morning, 10 AM SGT. You are the BA on the WealthView Client Portal squad (RBWM, Singapore). The MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) has issued an observation letter citing weaknesses in the platform's sanctions screening re-evaluation frequency and the handling of data subject access requests (DSARs) under the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). Internal Audit has logged it as a RIB (Risk Issue in Box) P1, and the squad has 30 days to deliver a remediation plan.

周二上午 10 点新加坡时间。你是 WealthView 客户门户 squad（RBWM，新加坡）的 BA。新加坡金融管理局 （MAS）发来一份 观察函，指出平台在 制裁名单复评频率 和处理 个人数据访问请求（DSAR）（依据《个人数据保护法》PDPA）方面存在薄弱环节。集团内审将其 记为 RIB（盒中风险事项）P1，squad 有 30 天时间提交整改方案。

You are in a 45-minute meeting with Sundara, Head of APAC Compliance, and Helen, the regional Data Privacy Officer (DPO). The goal: align on the AC for the remediation story, agree on the evidence package for MAS, and confirm the PCI-DSS scope for the upcoming release. Every word in this meeting is potentially auditable.

你和 Sundara，亚太区合规负责人 以及 Helen，区域数据隐私官（DPO） 开了一个 45 分钟的会议。目标：对齐整改 Story 的 AC、就提交给 MAS 的证据材料达成一致、并确认 即将发布的版本的 PCI-DSS 范围。这次会议上的每句话未来都可能被 审计 调取。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

Sundara (Compliance APAC): Thanks for joining, Wei. Quick one — MAS has flagged two findings under the TRM (Technology Risk Management) Guidelines. First, the screening re-eval is running every 24 hours, but the control standard requires at least every 12 hours. Second, DSAR turnaround is sitting at 14 days as per current runbook, but PDPA allows only 30 calendar days, and our internal SLA is 7. Where's the gap?Wei，谢谢你过来。简单说一下——MAS 在 TRM（技术风险管理）指引下提出两项发现。第一，筛查复评目前是 24 小时一次，但控制标准要求至少 12 小时一次。第二，DSAR 处理周期目前按 runbook 是 14 天，但 PDPA 允许 30 个日历日，而我们内部 SLA 是 7 天。差距在哪里？
You (BA): Thanks for laying that out so clearly, Sundara. On the screening re-eval: the batch job in the legacy ACME screen is hard-coded at 24h. We're working on moving it to the OneScreening platform with a 12h cadence — that's in flight in the next sprint. In the meantime, I'm proposing an interim manual control: a 12h secondary check by the Operations team, logged in the audit trail.Sundara，谢谢您讲得这么清楚。筛查复评这块：老 ACME 屏的批处理作业硬编码为 24 小时。我们正在迁移到 OneScreening 平台，频次改为 12 小时——下个 sprint 进行中。在此期间，我建议一个过渡性的人工控制：运营团队每 12 小时做一次二次检查，记录在审计追踪中。
Helen (DPO): On the DSAR point — the 14-day turnaround is the median, not the ceiling, right? We are within the regulatory maximum, but exceeding the internal SLA. Just to confirm, the constraint is on the manual data extraction step in the WealthView API.关于 DSAR 这一点——14 天是中位数，不是上限对吧？我们没超过监管上限，但超出了内部 SLA。确认一下，瓶颈是 WealthView API 里人工数据抽取这一步。
You (BA): That's right. 80% of the cycle is spent on cross-system reconciliation — pulling client data from HSBCnet, OneWealth, and the legacy CRM. We're scoping a self-service DSAR portal in the next PI, but for this MAS cycle, we can commit to automating the two highest-volume client segments — that's ~70% of requests.是的。80% 的时间花在跨系统对账上——从 HSBCnet、OneWealth 和老 CRM 拉客户数据。我们下个 PI 正在规划一个自助式 DSAR 门户，但针对本次 MAS 周期，我们可以承诺把两个最高量的客群自动化，约占请求量的 70%。
Sundara (Compliance APAC): OK, so the MAS evidence pack should include: (1) the interim control for screening, with an expiry date; (2) the automated DSAR coverage percentage; and (3) a target operating model with timelines. Does that work for you, Wei?好，那 MAS 证据包应包括：(1) 筛查的过渡性控制，附过期日期；(2) DSAR 自动化覆盖率；(3) 目标运营模型和时间表。Wei，这样可以吗？
You (BA): Yes, agreed. One thing to flag — the interim control needs a waiver reference number from Group Risk. Can I ask the Compliance team to raise that this week? Otherwise the audit trail will show an uncontrolled manual step, which might invite a follow-up finding.同意。提一个事项——过渡性控制需要集团风险出具一个豁免编号。能否请合规团队本周内申请？否则审计追踪会显示一个未受控的人工步骤，可能引来后续发现。
Sundara (Compliance APAC): Good catch. I'll have my team raise the waiver by Thursday. Last point — is the upcoming release in PCI-DSS scope? I need to know whether the QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) should be looped in.好问题。我会让我团队周四前把豁免申请提上去。最后一点——即将发布的版本是否在 PCI-DSS 范围内？我需要知道是否要把 QSA（合格安全评估师）拉进来。
You (BA): Quick check — the release touches the card-on-file tokenisation flow, but does not store or transmit the PAN. As far as I know, it stays in scope for the SAQ A but out of scope for the full ROC. I'll confirm with Infosec and circle back to you in writing by EOD.快速确认——这次发布涉及卡档令牌化流程，但不存储或传输 PAN。据我所知，它属于 SAQ A 范围，但不在完整 ROC 范围内。我会和信息安全确认后，今天 EOD 前书面回复您。
Helen (DPO): Perfect. Send me the user stories once they're ready, and I'll sign off on the privacy AC. And Wei — please make sure every DSAR-related field is captured in the consent ledger, not just the audit log. That's been a recurring finding in HKMA reviews.好的。准备好之后把 user story 发给我，我会在隐私 AC 上签字。Wei，请确保每个 DSAR 相关字段都记入同意台账，而不只是审计日志。这是 HKMA 审查的常见发现项。

Thanks both — I'll send the minutes and the updated story by tomorrow EOD.Helen，记下了。我会把它作为显式 AC 加进去——"所有 DSAR 同意事件写入同意台账，附时间戳、请求方和法律依据"。谢谢两位——我会在明天 EOD 前发出会议纪要和更新后的 Story。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Open Thanks for laying that out so clearly. 谢谢您讲得这么清楚 Acknowledges the regulator's framing. Critical for compliance meetings — opens the conversation in a register that matches theirs.
  • Cite MAS has flagged two findings under the TRM Guidelines. MAS 在 TRM 指引下提出两项发现 Always cite the regulation, not the regulator. "Under the TRM Guidelines" sounds more rigorous than "MAS said".
  • In-flight That's in flight in the next sprint. 下个 sprint 正在进行 "In flight" = actively being worked on. Distinguishes from "on the roadmap" or "being scoped".
  • Interim I'm proposing an interim manual control. 我建议一个过渡性人工控制 "Interim control" = a temporary mitigation while the permanent fix is in development. Always pair with an "expiry date".
  • Commit We can commit to automating the two highest-volume segments. 我们可以承诺把最高量的两个客群自动化 Be specific. "We can commit to X% of Y" is auditable; "we'll improve it" is not.
  • Flag One thing to flag — the interim control needs a waiver reference. 提一个事项——过渡性控制需要豁免编号 "Flag" = raise a concern politely. A favourite compliance word. Always pair with the consequence.
  • Scope Is the upcoming release in PCI-DSS scope? 即将发布的版本是否在 PCI-DSS 范围内？ "In scope" / "out of scope" — the regulator's mental model. Default to "I'll confirm in writing".
  • Confirm I'll confirm with Infosec and circle back in writing by EOD. 我会和信安确认后，今天 EOD 前书面回复 "In writing" is a defensive phrase. Creates the email trail that protects you in the next audit.
  • Sign off I'll sign off on the privacy AC. 我会在隐私 AC 上签字 "Sign off" = formal approval. Get it captured in writing — Slack is not a sign-off channel.
  • Recurring finding That's been a recurring finding in HKMA reviews. 这是 HKMA 审查的常见发现项 "Recurring finding" = something the regulator has flagged before. Politically charged — solve it before the next review.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) n. 新加坡金融管理局 Singapore's integrated regulator for banking, insurance, and securities.
  • TRM (Technology Risk Management) Guidelines n. 技术风险管理指引 MAS's binding standard for IT risk in financial institutions. Revised in 2021.
  • HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority) n. 香港金融管理局 Hong Kong's banking regulator. Issues its own SA-2 (Supervisory Policy) circulars.
  • Observation letter n. 观察函 Formal written communication from a regulator flagging concerns. Not a fine, but precedes one.
  • RIB (Risk Issue in Box) n. 盒中风险事项 HSBC's internal risk register entry. P1 = highest severity; P2 = high; P3 = medium.
  • DSAR (Data Subject Access Request) n. 数据主体访问请求 A formal request by an individual to access or delete their personal data, under PDPA / GDPR.
  • PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act) n. 《个人数据保护法》 Singapore's data protection law. 30-day maximum response window.
  • GDPR n. 欧盟通用数据保护条例 EU data protection regulation. 30-day maximum, with possible 60-day extension.
  • PCI-DSS n. 支付卡行业数据安全标准 The global standard for protecting cardholder data. 12 requirements.
  • SAQ A / ROC n. 自评问卷/合规报告 SAQ A = self-assessment for card-not-present merchants. ROC = Report on Compliance, the full audit report.
  • QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) n. 合格安全评估师 A PCI-SSC certified assessor. Required for full ROC.
  • Waiver n. 豁免 A formal documented exception to a control, with an expiry and an owner. The Compliance team's bread and butter.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "We're working on / We're scoping / We're committing to" — the BA's present continuous toolkit

Compliance conversations love the present continuous tense because it conveys active, in-flight work rather than a vague plan. Compare: We will improve DSAR turnaround (vague future) vs We're scoping a self-service DSAR portal in the next PI (specific, ongoing, owned). Always lead with the latter.

合规对话里多用现在进行时，强调"在做事"而不是"将来做"。

② "As far as I know / I believe / To my understanding" — epistemic modality

When you are not 100% sure of a regulatory detail, use an epistemic hedge: as far as I know, I believe, to my understanding, if I recall correctly. This protects you if the answer turns out to be wrong — you qualified it. Compliance officers respect this register; lawyers require it.

对监管细节不确定时用认识情态：as far as I know / I believe——既是保护，也是合规官尊重的说话方式。

③ "It stays in scope for SAQ A but out of scope for the full ROC" — the parallel structure

The pattern X for A but Y for B is the standard way to express scope segmentation. Compliance and Infosec think in scope buckets — mirror their language. Always follow with I'll confirm in writing.

X for A but Y for B 是表达范围切分的标准句型，对齐合规和信安的思维方式。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Compliance officers write everything down. So should you. If a Compliance lead says something in a meeting that affects scope, follow up with a 3-sentence email: "Per our discussion, agreed X, owner Y, date Z." Treat every compliance meeting as a future audit exhibit.
  • Never use "I think" or "probably" with a regulator's rule. Compliance will remember the uncertainty and re-question it. Use "as far as I know" + "I'll confirm in writing by EOD" — that pattern buys you time without losing credibility.
  • MAS, HKMA, FCA, GDPR — they don't overlap neatly. A control that satisfies MAS TRM might not satisfy HKMA SPM. Always ask "which regulator's standard are we aligning to?" before promising a date. Compliance teams love this question.
  • The "interim control" is your best friend. Regulators accept a temporary fix if it has (a) a clear expiry date, (b) a documented waiver, and (c) a permanent remediation plan. The trio is non-negotiable. Skip the waiver and you have a finding, not a fix.
  • Recurring findings are career-altering. If HKMA has flagged the same control weakness twice, the second time will get escalated to the regional CRO. Treat every repeat finding as a P1 — it will eventually be one.
  • PCI-DSS scope questions belong to Infosec, not to you alone. When in doubt, say "I'll confirm with Infosec and circle back in writing." Never commit on PCI scope without their sign-off — the consequences of a wrong answer are measurable in millions.
  • Data Privacy Officers (DPOs) think in "legal basis". When writing DSAR-related AC, use the phrase "with timestamp, requester, and legal basis" — that's the DPO's mental model. They'll sign off faster when you speak their language.
  • Different regions, different paces. HKMA is methodical and paper-heavy; MAS is fast and outcome-focused; FCA in the UK is principle-based and trusts judgement. Adapt your meeting style — but never your rigour.

Exercise 1 — Translate the compliance call

Translate the following BA response into natural Chinese. Pay attention to the "interim control + waiver + expiry" trio.

"Thanks for laying that out so clearly, Sundara. On the screening re-eval: the batch job in the legacy ACME screen is hard-coded at 24h. We're working on moving it to OneScreening with a 12h cadence — that's in flight next sprint. One thing to flag — the interim control needs a waiver reference number from Group Risk. Can I ask Compliance to raise that this week? Otherwise the audit trail will show an uncontrolled manual step, which might invite a follow-up finding. I'll confirm the PCI-DSS scope with Infosec and circle back in writing by EOD."

"Sundara，谢谢您讲得这么清楚。筛查复评这块：老 ACME 屏的批处理作业硬编码为 24 小时。我们正在迁移到 OneScreening，频次改为 12 小时——下个 sprint 进行中。在此期间，我建议一个过渡性人工控制：运营团队每 12 小时做一次二次检查，记录在审计追踪中。提一个事项——过渡性控制需要集团风险出具豁免编号。能否请合规本周内申请？否则审计追踪会显示一个未受控的人工步骤，可能引来后续发现。PCI-DSS 范围我会和信安确认后，今天 EOD 前书面回复。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the compliance risk

A BA gives a verbal answer in a meeting with the regional DPO. Which line is most likely to cause a follow-up audit finding?

  1. "I'll add that as an explicit AC: all DSAR consent events written to the consent ledger with timestamp, requester, and legal basis."
  2. "Yeah, I think we already log that somewhere. Probably in the audit log."
  3. "Let me confirm with Infosec and circle back in writing by EOD."
  4. "Noted, Helen. I'll send the minutes and the updated story by tomorrow EOD."

② is the dangerous one. "I think", "probably", "somewhere" — three hedges stacked together. A DPO will write that down, and at the next audit the BA will be asked to prove it. ① is the gold standard (explicit AC + specific fields). ③ and ④ are professional hedges — owning the answer with a time-bound commitment.

You are in a meeting with the regional Head of Compliance. Write a 5-7 sentence BA response that acknowledges both findings, proposes an interim control for the KYC cycle, and commits the consent-ledger fix as a story. Use at least 3 patterns from the Key Phrases section.

"Thanks for the heads-up, Joyce. Let me address both findings. On the KYC refresh cycle: you're right that the current platform runs annually, but SPM CR-1 requires six-monthly for HKRBWM high-risk. I'll commit it as a story: 'Add legal basis field to consent ledger entries, with timestamp, requester, and source.' I'll have a draft story and the interim-control memo to you by Friday EOD.
