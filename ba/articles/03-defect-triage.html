Defect Triage Meeting

Article 03 · Module 3: Defect & Risk Management · BA English

⏱ 10 min read 🟡 Intermediate 🎯 Prioritizing P1/P2 defects and pushing back on "must fix in this sprint" — with evidence

1. Scenario 场景背景

It is Wednesday, 11:00 AM HKT. The HSBC CMB (Commercial Banking) Trade Finance squad is running its weekly defect triage on Microsoft Teams. You are the BA based in Hong Kong. In the room: Daniel, the QA Lead (Bangalore), Lina, the Tech Lead (Shanghai), and Henry from the Singapore business who has just dialled in 5 minutes late and is already pushing for "everything to be fixed before the UAT on Friday".

周三上午 11 点香港时间，汇丰 CMB（公司银行） 贸易融资 squad 在 Teams 上开 周度缺陷评审。你是 BA（base 香港）。在场的有：班加罗尔的 QA Lead Daniel、 上海的技术负责人 Lina，以及迟到 5 分钟刚上线的 Henry（新加坡业务方），他一上来就 催"周五 UAT 前必须全部修完"。

There are 14 open defects on the board: 2 P1, 5 P2, 7 P3. The squad has 4 developer-days left this sprint. Your job as BA is to facilitate the prioritisation conversation, push back on Henry with evidence (severity × business impact × effort), and protect the sprint goal — which is the SWIFT MT202 cover-payment validation, not the cosmetic dashboard issues.

看板上有 14 个开放缺陷：2 个 P1、5 个 P2、7 个 P3。本 Sprint 还剩 4 个开发人天。 你作为 BA 要主持优先级讨论，用证据（严重度 × 业务影响 × 修复成本）顶回 Henry 的 压力，并守住 Sprint Goal（SWIFT MT202 同行报文校验，不是仪表盘的 UI 小问题）。

2. Dialogue 英文对话

You (BA): Right, let's get started. We have 14 defects on the board — 2 P1, 5 P2, 7 P3. We have 4 dev-days left this sprint, and our sprint goal is the MT202 validation release. Let's triage by severity first, then we'll talk about scope.好，开始。看板上有 14 个缺陷——2 个 P1、5 个 P2、7 个 P3。本 Sprint 还剩 4 个开发人天，Sprint Goal 是 MT202 校验上线。先按严重度评审，再讨论范围。
Daniel (QA): Quick one — the P1s first. DEF-4421, the MT202 cover-payment field is truncating the IBAN after 30 characters. We've reproduced it in UAT with 3 different client data sets.先说 P1。DEF-4421，MT202 同行报文字段在 IBAN 超过 30 字符后被截断。UAT 上 3 套客户数据都复现了。
Lina (TL): Yeah, I saw that one. It's a schema validation gap. One day to fix and a half-day for regression. Lina: this one is non-negotiable — we cannot ship without it.看到了。这是字段校验缺失。1 天修，半天回归。这个不能让——没修好不能上线。
You (BA): Agreed. DEF-4421 is in for this sprint, and it's flagged as a release blocker. Daniel, can you log the RCA template so we have it for the audit trail?同意。DEF-4421 本 Sprint 修，标记为上线阻塞。Daniel，能开一下 RCA 模板吗？要留审计痕迹。
Henry (Business): Sorry I'm late. Quick question — are we going to fix all 14 before Friday UAT? The Singapore desk is on my back about this.不好意思迟到了。问一下——周五 UAT 之前14 个全修完吗？新加坡那边在催我。
You (BA): Honest answer — no, Henry, we can't. We have 4 dev-days left, 1 is locked to DEF-4421. That leaves 3 days for the remaining 13. Let me walk you through the prioritisation so you can take it back to the desk with a clear picture.实话——修不完，Henry。还剩 4 个开发人天，1 天锁给 DEF-4421，剩 3 天给其他 13 个。我把优先级过一遍，你好带着清晰画面回新加坡那边。
You (BA): Out of the 13, I'd recommend 2 more in-sprint: DEF-4430 (P2, sanctions screening false-negative on Turkish IBAN — 2 days, regulatory risk) and DEF-4445 (P2, MAS report timestamp off by 1 hour — half-day, audit risk). The other 11 are P3 cosmetic / UX issues, deferred to sprint +1.13 个里我建议再加 2 个本 Sprint 修：DEF-4430（P2，土耳其 IBAN 制裁名单漏报——2 天，监管风险）和 DEF-4445（P2，MAS 报告时间戳差 1 小时——半天，审计风险）。其余 11 个是 P3 体验/UI 问题，延到下个 Sprint。
Henry (Business): But DEF-4460 — the dashboard label mis-alignment — the Singapore desk flagged it as must-fix. We had a customer complaint last week.但 DEF-4460——仪表盘标签错位——新加坡那边标了必做。上周有客户投诉了。
You (BA): I hear you on the complaint — let me push back gently. DEF-4460 is a cosmetic UI issue, it doesn't block any trade or report. Realistically, 1 dev-day to fix, and if we pull that in, we have to descope either DEF-4430 or DEF-4445 — and those are regulatory, not cosmetic. Which one would you like me to drop?理解你那边的投诉——我委婉顶一下。DEF-4460 是 UI 体验问题，不影响任何交易或申报。实际上 1 个开发人天，如果拉进来，就必须砍掉 DEF-4430 或 DEF-4445 其中之一——那两条是监管问题，不是 UI 问题。你想让我砍哪一条？
Henry (Business): Hmm, fair point. Keep the regulatory ones in. Can we at least get a workaround note for DEF-4460 out to the desk by Friday?嗯，有道理。监管的留着。DEF-4460 能否至少周五前给业务方一个临时说明？
You (BA): Yes, I can do that. I'll draft a 1-page workaround note today and loop you in before I send it out. Right, anything else on the board before we close?可以。今天草拟 1 页临时说明，发之前拉你确认。好，看板上的其他项有要说的吗？
Lina (TL): One more — DEF-4450, the eCAB-raised PCI-DSS logging one. Just to confirm that's in scope for this sprint as a regulatory must-have?还有一条——DEF-4450，eCAB 提的 PCI-DSS 日志。确认一下这条是本 Sprint 监管必做项？
You (BA): Confirmed — in scope, regulator-raised via eCAB last Friday. That's the 4th dev-day, so the board is now fully loaded. Thanks, all. I'll send the prioritised list and the workaround note by EOD.确认——在上周范围里，周五 eCAB 走监管流程进来的。第 4 个开发人天用在这里，看板已满负荷。感谢各位。我 EOD 前发优先级列表和临时说明。

3. Key Phrases 关键句型

  • Frame Let's triage by severity first, then we'll talk about scope. 先按严重度评审，再讨论范围 Sets the rules of engagement: severity first, scope second. Stops the meeting becoming a "fix everything" debate.
  • P1 Flagged as a release blocker. 标记为上线阻塞 "Release blocker" = cannot ship. Strongest triage label. Use sparingly — once a sprint is the most.
  • Honest Honest answer — no, we can't. 实话——修不完 "Honest answer" = politeness wrapper for saying no. BAs need to say no with data, not vibes.
  • Quantify 1 is locked to DEF-4421. That leaves 3 days for the remaining 13. DEF-4421 锁 1 天，剩 3 天给其他 13 个 Always do the math out loud. Capacity + priorities = the conversation. Maths kills "fix everything" arguments.
  • Push back Let me push back gently. 我委婉顶一下 "Push back" = disagree. "Gently" = softens. Always pair push-back with data (severity, effort, regulatory exposure).
  • Trade-off Which one would you like me to drop? 你想让我砍哪一条？ The classic "force the choice" move. Don't accept "fix everything" — make them choose what to drop.
  • Acknowledge I hear you on the complaint. 理解你那边的投诉 Always acknowledge the stakeholder's concern first, before pushing back. Acknowledgement ≠ agreement.
  • Defer Deferred to sprint +1. 延到下个 Sprint "Sprint +1" = next sprint. "Sprint +2" = the one after. Standard agile shorthand for deferral.
  • Workaround I'll draft a 1-page workaround note. 我草拟 1 页临时说明 A workaround acknowledges the pain without committing the dev time. Critical tool for de-escalating cosmetic defects.
  • Confirm Just to confirm that's in scope… 确认一下这条在范围内 "Just to confirm" = polite, no-judgement way to surface a disagreement in a meeting. Lets you reset the record.

4. Vocabulary 词汇表

  • Defect triage n. 缺陷评审 The meeting to review, prioritise, and assign defects.
  • P1 / P2 / P3 n. 优先级 1/2/3 Severity classification. P1 = release blocker, P2 = high, P3 = cosmetic.
  • Release blocker n. 上线阻塞 A defect severe enough to prevent production release.
  • Severity vs Priority n. 严重度 vs 优先级 Severity = technical impact. Priority = business urgency. They differ!
  • RCA (Root Cause Analysis) n. 根本原因分析 A structured investigation into why a defect occurred (often "5 whys").
  • SWIFT MT202 n. SWIFT MT202 报文 Cover payment message for bank-to-bank transfers.
  • IBAN n. 国际银行账号 International Bank Account Number — up to 34 characters.
  • Sanctions screening false-negative n. 制裁名单漏报 A match that should have been caught but wasn't — a regulatory risk.
  • MAS n. 新加坡金管局 Monetary Authority of Singapore — Singapore's banking regulator.
  • PCI-DSS n. 支付卡行业数据安全标准 Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard.
  • Sprint +1 n. 下个 Sprint The next sprint in sequence. +2 = two sprints away.
  • Workaround n. 临时方案 A manual or interim solution that avoids the defect's impact without fixing the root cause.

5. Grammar Notes 语法点

① "Let me push back gently" — gerund as a soft announcement

Saying let me push back (verb) is direct, almost confrontational. Saying let me push back gently adds the adverb, signalling "I disagree but I'm doing it with care". The adverb is doing the politeness work. Same trick works with let me flag this carefully or let me raise this honestly.

直接说 push back 有点冲，加 gently 副词就软了。同样的技巧：flag this carefully、raise this honestly。

② "We can't" vs "We won't" — modality in pushback

We can't fix all 14 says it's impossible (capacity-bound). We won't fix all 14 says we've chosen not to (priority-bound). Use can't with stakeholders — it removes the "you don't want to" reading and replaces it with "we literally don't have the days". Can't is the BA's politeness tool.

Can't 表示客观能力不足，won't 表示主观选择。对业务方用 can't，避免被认为"你不想做"。

③ "Which one would you like me to drop?" — the question that ends the debate

When a stakeholder says "fix everything", the correct response is a forced-choice question: Which one would you like me to drop? This shifts the conversation from "can we add more?" to "what are we willing to lose?". The structure is which + noun + would you like + me + to + verb: polite, hypothetical, and uncatchable.

当业务方说"全做"时，正确回应是强制选择题：Which one would you like me to drop?。把对话从"加"转到"放弃什么"。

6. Cultural Tips 文化 / 职场 Tips

  • Severity ≠ priority. A P1 might still be P3 priority if it doesn't affect a release. A P3 might be P1 priority if a regulator is calling. Train the room to separate "how bad is it technically" from "how urgent is it for the business". HSBC audits love this distinction.
  • "Must-fix" needs evidence, not volume. When a business says "this is a must-fix", ask: "What's the regulatory or revenue impact?" If they can't quantify it, it's a "want-fix", not a "must-fix". Politely push back with the question.
  • Singapore vs HK: be culturally careful. Henry is from Singapore business. Singapore colleagues are direct, time-conscious, and will accept a "no" with reasons. Don't soften so much that they think you haven't decided — be clear and quick.
  • Regulatory beats cosmetic, always. Sanctions, MAS, HKMA, FCA, PCI-DSS — once a regulator is mentioned, the conversation is over. Cosmetic UI issues are "next sprint" until proven otherwise.
  • Workarounds are your friend. A 1-page workaround note is 30 minutes of BA work that resolves a customer complaint without burning 1 dev-day. Always offer the workaround as the third option: fix it / defer it / workaround it.
  • Don't accept late arrivals changing scope. Henry dialled in 5 minutes late and immediately pushed for "fix everything". As BA, you reset: "we've already triaged, let me walk you through the prioritisation". Don't re-open the whole board for a latecomer.
  • Always confirm in writing after the meeting. Send the prioritised list by EOD. If it's not in writing, the "must-fix" list grows by Monday morning. The triage meeting's output is the email that follows.
  • Numbers beat volume. "1 P1, 2 P2 in-sprint, 11 P3 deferred" beats "we're working hard on quality". HSBC PMs and BAs speak in numbers. Train yourself to count out loud.

7. Practice 练习题

Exercise 1 — Translate the BA pushback

Translate the following BA pushback into natural Chinese. Pay attention to how the BA acknowledges first, then quantifies, then forces the choice.

"I hear you on the customer complaint. Let me push back gently. DEF-4460 is a cosmetic UI issue — it doesn't block any trade or report. Realistically, 1 dev-day to fix. If we pull that in, we have to descope either DEF-4430 (sanctions screening false-negative) or DEF-4445 (MAS report timestamp) — and those are regulatory, not cosmetic. Which one would you like me to drop? As a workaround, I can draft a 1-page note for the desk by Friday EOD."

"理解你那边的客户投诉。我委婉顶一下。DEF-4460 是 UI 体验问题——不影响任何交易或申报。实际上 1 个开发人天。如果拉进来，就必须砍掉 DEF-4430（土耳其 IBAN 制裁名单漏报）或 DEF-4445（MAS 报告时间戳）其中之一——那两条是监管问题，不是 UI 问题。你想让我砍哪一条？作为临时方案，周五 EOD 前我可以给业务方草拟 1 页说明。"

Exercise 2 — Spot the BA mistake

Which of these triage lines is the most "BA-polished" and which would damage your credibility in the meeting? Why?

  1. "Yeah, sure Henry, we'll just work harder and fix everything by Friday."
  2. "We can't fix all 14. We have 4 dev-days left, 1 is locked to DEF-4421. That leaves 3 days for 13. I'd recommend 2 more in-sprint: DEF-4430 (regulatory) and DEF-4445 (audit). The other 11 P3s are deferred to sprint +1. Which P3 would you like me to swap in instead of one of those two?"
  3. "Henry, you're late. Catch up."
  4. "I'll draft a 1-page workaround note for DEF-4460 and loop you in before sending. That gets the customer-facing pain resolved without burning 1 dev-day."

② and ④ are BA-polished — ② quantifies and forces a choice, ④ offers a workaround. ① is the worst — agreeing to fix everything destroys your credibility and the sprint goal. ③ is rude and will be remembered. A BA's job in triage is to be the calm, evidence-based adult in the room.

Exercise 3 — Your turn

You're a BA on the GPB Tax Reporting squad. Weekly defect triage. Facts: (a) 2 P1s — DEF-5101 (CRS report XML schema mismatch, regulator-raised, 1 dev-day) and DEF-5102 (PEP false-positive on UK MPs, 2 dev-days); (b) 3 P2s — DEF-5110 (tax-residency dropdown missing 2 countries, 1 dev-day), DEF-5115 (OneWealth dashboard slow on first load, half-day), DEF-5120 (CRS report PDF footer wrong year, quarter-day); (c) 4 dev-days left. The PO is pushing for everything to be done before MAS audit on Friday. Write the triage facilitation. Acknowledge the PO, do the maths, recommend an in-sprint set, and offer a workaround for at least one deferred item.

"Right, let's triage. We have 5 open defects — 2 P1, 3 P2 — and 4 dev-days left. Honest answer: we cannot fix all 5 by Friday. The maths: 2 P1s alone are 3 dev-days, which leaves 1 day for the P2s. I'd recommend DEF-5101 and DEF-5102 in-sprint as the regulator and compliance P1s. For the P2s, my recommendation is to defer DEF-5115 and DEF-5120 to sprint +1, and pick up DEF-5110 (tax-residency dropdown) — that one has a clear 1-day fix and a real customer impact. So the in-sprint set is 5101, 5102, and 5110. For the deferred items, I'll draft a 1-page workaround note for DEF-5115 and a holding email to the regulator for DEF-5120, both out by Friday. Push-back welcome — but we'll have to drop one of the P1s to add more, and I'd rather not do that given the MAS audit. Sound good?"
